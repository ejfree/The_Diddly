# Solstice of the Horde-Trees (Dragon University Holiday Encounter)

A winter-solstice tradition at Dragon University that‚Äîif you ask the baby dragons‚Äîsymbolizes one sacred principle:

**Break things to get prizes.**

The **Horde-Trees** (called **Hoard-Trees** by faculty trying to sound dignified) are semi-living Lamannian automaton/elemental trees hung with jingling ornaments. Hatchlings are encouraged to chase them, slam them, and knock ornaments off. Each ornament is redeemable for a **real prize**.

This year, the grand prize tree wakes up mean and starts defending ‚Äúits‚Äù loot with elementals.

## What This Is

- **Location**: The Commons (Water Quad), spilling into the **Grand Diddly Amphitheater**
- **Length**: 30‚Äì60 minutes
- **Core fantasy**: daycare chaos + fundraiser carnival + elemental outbreak
- **Win condition**: keep hatchlings safe, keep prizes flowing, and stop the grand prize Horde-Tree from escalating into a campus disaster

## Quick Hooks (Why The Party Is Here)

Pick one:

- **Hired help**: Safety Marshal Prim paid the party to ‚Äúkeep the solstice crowd safe.‚Äù
- **Club favor**: Mirecrown asked the party to attend as ‚Äúresponsible adults‚Äù because Horde-Trees get unpredictable around excited hatchlings.
- **Free food, bad timing**: Chef Gristle comped the party‚Äôs tickets/snacks; they‚Äôre closest when things go sideways.
- **Investigation lead**: the party is tracking a trinket vendor; the fundraiser is their next stop.

### Tone Note

This encounter plays best if the party is encouraged to **wrangle, divert, and de-escalate** rather than razing the venue. The university cares about casualties *and* property damage, and the hatchlings are very, very fragile (emotionally).

## The Holiday (In-World): ‚ÄúBonk For Blessings‚Äù

On the longest night, dragons mark the **Turn of the Great Scale**. The official story is about fortune and growth.

The actual tradition is:

- Io'lokar families drop off hatchlings for the bonk arena while adults socialize and bid at auction.
- Hatchlings chase and body-check the Horde-Trees to knock down ornaments.
- Handlers and older students keep the arena ‚Äúsafe-ish.‚Äù
- Everyone eats snacks and cheers like it‚Äôs a sporting event.

## The Fundraiser: The Horde-Tree Prize Drive

Run by the **Lamannia Enthusiasts Society** (trees + arena wards) with booths/food support from **The Draconic Gastronomy Guild**.

- **Goal**: raise funds for the **Hearthroot Scholarship** (tuition aid + house repairs + expedition grants).
- **How it works**:
  - Visitors buy **prize tags** (each tag = 1 redemption).
  - Horde-Trees ‚Äúdrop‚Äù tagged ornaments when struck (or when the warding decides the kid ‚Äúearned it‚Äù).
  - Staff at redemption booths swap the ornament for a prize (roll on the d100 table).
- **What goes wrong**: the grand prize tree was built around a Lamannian feral-spirit knot. When hundreds of hatchlings start bonking it, the knot decides it‚Äôs being hunted‚Ä¶ and fights back.

### Fundraiser Stakes (Optional Tracker)

Start at **6 Fundraiser Points**.

- -1 when a hatchling is put in real danger (fire/cider, stampede, falling decor, etc.).
- -1 when a booth is wrecked or a crowd surge gets out of hand.
- +1 when the party completes a mini-game cleanly (below) or saves a major donor/auction lot.

End result:
- **5‚Äì7**: the benefit is a success; the party gets a public shout-out and first pick of a ‚Äúthank you‚Äù prize.
- **3‚Äì4**: the benefit limps along; rewards are quieter (a favor, not a ceremony).
- **0‚Äì2**: the university is embarrassed; the party is still thanked, but someone starts auditing *everyone*.

### Mini-Game 1: ‚ÄúNo Biting!‚Äù
One hatchling has grabbed another hatchling‚Äôs tail-fin/ear-frill and won‚Äôt let go.

- **Check**: Diplomacy, Intimidation, Nature, or Performance
- **Success**: separate them; gain **+1 Fundraiser Point**
- **Critical Success**: as success, and you recover a dropped ornament (roll prize)
- **Failure**: somebody gets nipped; **-1 Fundraiser Point**

### Mini-Game 2: Snack Herding
A pack of hatchlings is stampeding toward the snack stalls (and the boiling cider).

- **Check**: Athletics (herd), Acrobatics (cut off safely), Survival (predict movement), or Cooking Lore (bait by smell)
- **Success**: redirect them into a safe corral; gain **+1 Fundraiser Point**
- **Failure**: stall damage or singed whiskers; **-1 Fundraiser Point**

### Mini-Game 3: Lost Eggie, Found Eggie
A tiny one has gotten separated and is trying to climb the Horde-Tree like it‚Äôs a parent.

- **Check**: Perception, Nature, Stealth, or Society
- **Success**: recover hatchling; gain **+1 Fundraiser Point**
- **Critical Success**: the hatchling ‚Äúpays you‚Äù with a stolen ornament (roll prize)
- **Failure**: hatchling gets too close to the fight; **-1 Fundraiser Point**

### Mini-Game 4: Harness Fiasco
A harness/leash tangles around a hatchling‚Äôs wings right as it tries to pounce.

- **Check**: Thievery (untie quickly), Medicine (gentle handling), Crafting (fix the buckle), or Athletics (hold steady)
- **Success**: hatchling is freed and not frightened; gain **+1 Fundraiser Point**
- **Failure**: hatchling panics and breathes; creatures adjacent must attempt a **basic Reflex save** (standard DC by level) or take **1d6** damage (fire/cold/electricity‚Äîpick what matches the hatchling).

### Mini-Game 5: ‚ÄúThat One Likes You Now‚Äù
A hatchling decides you are its favorite person and refuses to leave your shoulder/back.

- **Check**: Acrobatics (keep balance), Athletics (gently remove), Diplomacy (trade attention), or Nature (calming signals)
- **Success**: hand off to a handler; gain **+1 Fundraiser Point**
- **Critical Success**: the hatchling also drops an ornament in your hand (roll prize)
- **Failure**: you‚Äôre off-guard until end of your next turn (distracted).

## Encounter Structure (Waves + Single Boss)

### Scene 1: ‚ÄúDoors Open, Kids Unleashed‚Äù (5‚Äì10 min)

Read-aloud vibe: Io'lokar parents hand over hatchlings, the announcer shouts rules, and the first Horde-Tree clanks into the arena‚Äîbranches jingling with prizes. The babies charge. The crowd cheers.

Then the ornaments rattle wrong. A few ‚Äúdrops‚Äù sprout legs, wings, and spite.

Immediate problems (pick 2):

- A hatchling is mesmerized by a jingling elemental in the arena.
- A snack stall is getting swarmed (and it‚Äôs hot).
- A donation runner is being mugged by ribbons and coins.

**Wave 1**: 2‚Äì6 small elementals (mix 2 types).

### Scene 2: ‚ÄúKeep It Fun‚Äù (5‚Äì10 min)

Optional skill challenge (3 successes before 2 failures; Arcana/Nature/Occultism/Society): stabilize the arena wards.

- **Success**: gain **+1 Fundraiser Point** and the party gets **2 prize tags** to hand out (roll twice).
- **Critical success** (if you‚Äôre tracking): the boss starts with **-15 HP** (or loses resistances for its first round).
- **Failure**: **-1 Fundraiser Point** and Wave 2 begins with +1 extra small elemental.

### Scene 3: ‚ÄúThe Grand Prize Tree Takes The Field‚Äù (10‚Äì20 min)

**Wave 2**: 2‚Äì4 small elementals (one type you haven‚Äôt used yet). Keep it quick.

Then the **grand prize Horde-Tree** stomps into the arena.

**Boss fight**: run **only** the **Hearthbough Warden** (above).

Boss win options:

- **Combat**: defeat the Warden; the wards snap back and the tree ‚Äúplays dead.‚Äù
- **Re-key mid-fight**: once per round, one PC can spend **[two-actions]** to attempt Arcana/Nature/Occultism (standard DC by level). On success, reduce the Warden‚Äôs HP by **10** (or remove its resistances for 1 round).
- **Crowd advantage**: if Fundraiser Points are **5+**, handlers keep hatchlings out of the arena; PCs gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Re-key checks.

### Prize Drops During Combat

Prizes are the whole point. Make them visible and immediate.

- When a small elemental is defeated, it drops 1 ornament at the arena edge.
- When the boss takes a critical hit (or drops below half HP), it drops 1 ornament.
- A PC can use **Interact** to scoop an ornament and toss it to a handler to roll on the Prize Table.
