# Excessive Eggie Deaths

## Root Cause

Two forces are converging on the university's egg-year population simultaneously, and they are inseparable — but the faculty only perceive one of them.

**1. The Pale Choir (Insanity Plague) — hidden cause**
Faculty are in various stages of Pale Choir progression (see [insanity-plague.md](../../setting/background/insanity-plague.md)). The disease originated through hoard contamination and breath adjacency in shared lecture vaults — the university is a perfect incubation environment. Affected professors present as increasingly intense, rigid, and intolerant of "noise." Internally they frame this as *clarity*. The escalating bloodlust reads to them as righteous focus.

**The faculty do not know they have the plague.** They are unaware the Pale Choir exists, unaware it connects to the prophecy, and unaware their own judgment is compromised. To them, everything feels sharper, more urgent, more correct.

**2. The Drow Uprising — the only thing they see**
The faculty are aware of Lolth's giant-chain campaign building toward a surface war. This is the threat they believe they are responding to. Their increasing intensity, their culling of weaker eggies, their obsessive drive to produce the strongest possible flight — all of it is, in their minds, a rational response to an existential surface threat. They are preparing the next generation for war.

The professors are not aware that this graduation flight is the prophesied antecedent of the Fourth Age. They know the prophecy exists. They do not know they are inside it.

## What Is Actually Happening

The plague's "clarity" + the real drow threat = a feedback loop. The threat is genuine. Their response is genuine. But the Pale Choir has removed their capacity to weigh cost against gain. The eggie deaths are not a strategy — they are what happens when bloodlust is given a righteous justification.

- Professors are pushing eggies harder than ever before
- They are rationalizing early deaths as "the weak culling themselves"
- Small faculty cells are synchronizing positions without realizing it (Pale Choir "Choirs" phase)
- The sick days and abscesses in eggie wings are partly the plague vectoring into the young through breath adjacency

## For the GM

- Each eggie wing is at risk. Have each PC roll a d100 to determine which wing takes losses this semester.
- Write specific NPC absences and deaths into the plot as they occur — these are not random; each is the result of a faculty decision made under degraded judgment.
- The professors are not the enemy in a simple sense. They are tragic: responding to a real threat, but with a mind that can no longer calibrate correctly.
- The PCs may be the "Young Wings" of the prophecy — the ones who can fulfill the Fourth Age *without* the culling — but the faculty will never frame it that way. To the professors, the PCs are just another cohort being tested.

## The Positive Vision (What the Fourth Age Could Be)

The professors' goals are real even if their methods are broken. If the PCs can survive and choose to act:

- Draconic re-integration with mortal society — dragons walking openly as partners and stewards
- Re-opening of the Mournlands — the dual-rift overlay stabilized; the Mournlands redeemed and resettled
- The lightning rail transformed into a high-speed dragonmark monorail, the symbol of a new age of shared civilization

The prophecy favors those who *choose* the path rather than those who survive it.
