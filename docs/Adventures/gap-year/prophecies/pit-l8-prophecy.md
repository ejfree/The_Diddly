# The Pit Prophecy

*Found scratched into the stone of a dungeon wall in the Underdark. It is elvish, the script is Drow, but the hand is wrong — too many legs, the strokes uneven, some letters doubled as if written by a mind that cannot hold a single thought. A drider's work. The inscription loops back on itself in places, and sections have been scratched out and rewritten. Whatever wrote this was compelled.*

---

## 📜 Inscription on the Wall of the Drider Seer — Known in Drow as "The Counted Silence"

*She spins below what flies above,*
*And the high ones do not feel the thread.*
*Praise Her. Praise Her.*
*The Pale Mouth opens in the House of Wings.*

*When the Fractured Song finds the lecture-stone,*
*When the breath breaks between the thought and the flame,*
*The hoard-gift passes jaw to jaw without a name—*
*And She Who Waits Below counts each one.*

*The worm-mind seeded it in the deep before memory.*
*She of Eight Arms harvests what the mad dark planted.*
*The high ones call it sickness. They call it loss.*
*She calls it the key.*

*Five seals. Five flames.*
*Cracked voices cannot hold them.*
*When the wing-singers cannot sing,*
*Who guards the chains of the Bone Queen?*

*Let the Chalky Jaw spread from desk to den.*
*Let the old roar splinter in the throat.*
*Let the Pit stand empty on the day of sending.*
*Then she rises. Then she rises. Then—*

*Praise Her.*
*Praise Her.*
*Praise Her.*

*(Here the inscription becomes illegible, looping the same glyph for "mother" forty-seven times before the marks degrade into raw gouges.)*

## GM Notes

**What this is:** A drider seer — one of Lloth's compelled prophet-slaves — was granted a vision of the full shape of Lloth's plan and ordered to inscribe it. The drider's fractured mind could only hold fragments of the truth, producing a prophecy that is accurate but incomplete, written in a fever of devotion and madness.

**Key references:**

- **"The House of Wings"** — The Diddly, the dragon university on the ground beneath the floating city of Io'Lokar.

- **"The Fractured Song" / "The Pale Mouth" / "The Chalky Jaw"** — The Pale Choir plague (Wyrmblight). The plague spreads through shared breath in lecture halls, through hoard-gifts exchanged between students, and through contaminated relics. The drider knows it by its effect, not its biological name.

- **"The hoard-gift passes jaw to jaw"** — The primary vector at the university is hoard-exchange: coins given as gifts, ceremonial items, relics passed between students and faculty in academic traditions. The drider sees this clearly.


- **"The worm-mind seeded it"** — Xoriat. The Pale Choir is Xoriat-origin biological, not Lloth's creation. She did not make it. She simply recognized it as a tool and began directing its spread.

- **"Five seals. Five flames. / Cracked voices cannot hold them"** — Tiamat is bound by five seals maintained in part through draconic resonance and concord. Lloth's theory: if the elder dragons lose their voice to the Pale Choir — if the Draconic Council fractures under madness — the harmonic lock on Tiamat's prison weakens. The "Bone Queen" is Tiamat.

- **"The Bone Queen"** — Tiamat. Called "bone" here because the drider's Drow mind reaches for the image of something ancient, imprisoned, and hungry beneath the earth.

- **"Then she rises"** — Lloth's endgame: not to free Tiamat herself, but to so thoroughly destabilize draconic governance that the seals fail on their own. She does not need to act. She only needs the singing to stop.

**What the players can learn from this:**

- The plague is not an accident or a natural outbreak — it is being deliberately spread.
- The university is the primary target, not a coincidence.
- The goal is not to kill dragons. It is to silence them. The deaths are a side effect Lloth finds acceptable.
- Someone in or near the university is facilitating hoard contamination. The drider does not know who — but the players might.

**Where to find this:** Scratched into a wall in a dungeon with Drow or drider occupation — a shrine room, a seer's cell, or a passage that dead-ends into a spiral of obsessive carvings. Lighting it with a torch reveals the looping glyph pattern at the end. A character who speaks Drow and succeeds on a moderate Occultism or Religion check can recognize the prophetic form and the name "Lloth" embedded in the repeated mother-glyph.
