# D3 — Svartjet Crossing (Thoopshib's Ford)

## **Svartjet Crossing**
Thoopshib is the encounter. He is not hostile by default but is genuinely unhinged, and every interaction risks tipping into violence. The party almost certainly needs to cross. He is the only practical crossing. His gar is in the water at all times.

### **Description**
The tunnel opens onto a wide underground river — the Svartjet — running fast through a channel over 80 feet deep. The surface is glassy smooth, deceptively calm. An 8' × 14' flat-bottomed barge is moored in a cove on the far bank, tied to an iron ring. A massive kuo-toa is either asleep on the barge, working the sculling oar, or crouching motionless in the shadows of the far cove.

The cave ceiling rises 30 feet above the water. The river roar is loud enough to mask normal conversation at distance but not enough to hide bright light.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: No natural light. The river surface catches any light source and carries it; anything lit on either bank is visible from across the water. Thoopshib is sensitive to bright light — it draws his attention immediately.

- **Surfaces**: Smooth stone banks, 3–4 feet above water level. The near bank has no handholds for climbing back up if someone falls in — the current will carry them downstream before they can recover without Athletics DC 20.

- **Objects**: The barge (far bank cove). A sculling oar, 12 feet long, lashed to the barge. An iron mooring ring on each bank. A pile of dried seaweed in the side cave (Thoopshib's bed).

- **Doors & Exits**: Far bank connects to the continuation of the Descent Passages. A side cave off the far cove is Thoopshib's den (see Loot). Walking along the riverside is not possible — the channel walls are sheer. Traveling upriver by barge is impossible against the current.

- **Special Interactions**: If the barge is unmanned and someone attempts to scull it, they cross in roughly 12 rounds but drift diagonally downstream; the barge cannot be moored without Thoopshib's technique and will be carried off at 9 mph. If Thoopshib sculls, the crossing takes 6 rounds and lands cleanly in the far cove.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Brine, fish, wet stone. The side cave smells strongly of rot and dried seaweed.

- **Sounds**: The river is loud — DC 16 Perception to hear anything at normal speaking volume across the water. Thoopshib's repeated Undercommon offers sound like rhythmic croaking to anyone without the language. The giant gar creates a slow, heavy wake sound just upstream; DC 18 Perception to distinguish it from the current.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures**: Koronnah, a giant gar over 30 feet long, patrols just upstream. It is bonded to Thoopshib and responds to his call. It does not attack unless Thoopshib summons it or someone enters the water.

- **Hostile Threats**: Thoopshib himself if the encounter goes wrong (see Unique NPCs). Koronnah once summoned.

- **Unique NPCs**:

  **Thoopshib** — Kuo-Toa Monitor, effectively Creature 13. Exceptional Strength and Dexterity. 4 attacks per round, always targets vitals (strikes to kill, not subdue). Chaotic and unstable but functionally sly — he has survived alone here for decades by knowing exactly when to escalate.

  Default behavior: rows travelers across for 1 pp per person (or equivalent value), no questions, no conversation beyond the repeated Undercommon offer. He does not care about faction, species, or cargo. He repeats the offer if not understood; each repetition after the first has a 10% cumulative chance of triggering a berserk attack.

  If the barge is attacked or threatened: leaps into the river, surfaces on Koronnah's back, directs the gar against the barge from the water.

  If forced to ferry under coercion (magically compelled, held hostage, etc.): complies until mid-river, then takes the sculling oar and jumps in. The barge drifts downstream at 9 mph; 70% chance it grounds roughly 2 miles downstream, otherwise it reaches the Sunless Sea.

  **Koronnah** — Giant Gar, ~30 feet, Creature 10 equivalent. Attacks anyone in the water 80% of the time per round. 25% chance to attack someone walking on the water surface. 10% chance to leap and attack someone levitating or flying up to 20 feet above the water.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: The Current — environmental

- **Trigger**: Falling into the Svartjet or attempting to swim

- **Effect**: DC 20 Athletics each round to avoid being swept downstream. Downstream leads toward the Sunless Sea with no safe exit points.

- **Disarm**: None. Avoid entering the water.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot**: Nothing on Thoopshib's person beyond his equipment. The side cave contains only crude gear (worthless) and a large dried seaweed pile.

- **Hidden Loot**: A natural pot in the side cave floor, sealed with a stalactite that appears to be a natural formation (DC 18 Perception to identify as a stopper). Inside:
  - 1,420 gp
  - 691 pp
  - 77 gems, 10 gp each
  - Two potions of greater healing

- **Magical Items**:
  - *Black metal medallion* — spider relief on one face, female drow visage on the other. Likely a House token or Lolth-cult insignia; its specific faction and function are unclear. Worth investigating before using.
  - *Poisonous cloak* — appears as a fine traveling cloak. Cursed: the wearer is poisoned (persistent) upon donning it and cannot voluntarily remove it. Detect Magic reads as transmutation. No obvious sign of the curse without Identify.
  - *Gold sphere* — a bead from a Necklace of Missiles. This one is a 9-die fireball. Single use.

### **Additional Notes**
Thoopshib has been here long enough that every regular caravan operator knows the crossing fee and protocol. The drow pass through routinely; Thoopshib has ferried House Dravun patrols, Velouryn merchants, and at least one illithid without incident. His neutrality is genuine — it is not loyalty to anyone, it is indifference to everyone.

The medallion is the most significant item in the hoard. A drow who recognizes it will react. It should not be worn casually.

The poisonous cloak has no lore marking it as cursed. Thoopshib presumably took it off a traveler who put it on and collapsed.
