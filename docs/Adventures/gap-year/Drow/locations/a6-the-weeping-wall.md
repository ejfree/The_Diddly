### **Description**
A stretch of tunnel where mineral-rich groundwater seeps through a 40-foot section of porous limestone, leaving pale mineral deposits and a constant slow drip. The wet stone glitters faintly in torchlight. The passage is uncomfortably narrow — single file for larger creatures — and the sound of dripping masks footsteps from both directions. The dripping creates a persistent white-noise that makes the passage feel isolated from both directions. PCs who stop here will find it oddly peaceful — which is exactly why the orog chose it.

- **Unique NPCs**: A wounded orog — lone survivor of a surface raiding party that went wrong, not affiliated with the troglodytes. Stopped here to drink and dress wounds. Hostile by default but desperate — a successful DC 12 Persuasion or Medicine offer can shift him to wary neutrality. He knows a route around the troglodyte warrens entirely (a low crawl-tunnel he found by accident). He will trade that route for rations and a clean bandage.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Yellow Mold — environmental hazard

- **Trigger**: Disturbing the mold patch (10 feet across) colonizing the wettest section of the wall, mid-passage

- **Effect**: Yellow mold spore release

- **Disarm**: DC 15 Perception or Nature to identify before disturbing it; the mold is the same pale color as the mineral deposits
