# Encounter Area D-3 — Drow Checkpoint

## **Drow Checkpoint — Dual Patrol Groups**
Two rival patrol contingents hold this passage: males to the southwest, females to the northeast. Despite internal rivalry, both groups cooperate fully in a combined, intelligent attack and defense. The two male pickets are actively watching both directions of the passage — the party is likely spotted before they spot anyone here.

### **Description**
The primary passage runs southeast to northwest through this area (use encounter piece II facing either end). The tunnel walls glow faintly with patches of phosphorescent lichen, casting a dim blue-green light across the stone floor. The passage is wide enough to accommodate two distinct groups holding their respective ends: a male contingent clustered to the southwest and a female contingent to the northeast. Two male picket guards are posted at either end of the passage, nearly invisible against the rock, watching for any movement along the corridor.

Side caves open off the main area and hold bedrolls of sleeping silks, small boxes of provisions, and individual kits (eating utensils, cups, and similar gear).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Dimly illuminated by phosphorescent lichens on the walls and ceiling. Drow suffer no penalty. Other creatures treat this as near-darkness.

- **Surfaces**: Worn stone floor, relatively smooth from long use. The passage curves slightly, limiting sightlines from each end.

- **Objects**: Sleeping silks and provision boxes in the side caves. Two black metal (adamantium alloy) medallions on fine black-chain loops — one side shows a spider, the other a female drow — stored among the personal effects in the caves.

- **Doors & Exits**: Open passage running southeast-northwest. Side cave alcoves off the main area (no doors). No secret doors noted.

- **Special Interactions**: The two male pickets will immediately report any creature moving along the passage to their respective commanders. The medallions are valuable trade/identification tokens among drow; knowledgeable PCs may recognize their significance.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Faint mineral dampness from the lichen-covered stone. A hint of lamp oil or torch resin from the drow's gear. The provision boxes emit a faint smell of preserved fungus-bread and dried meat.

- **Sounds**: Quiet — drow are disciplined. Occasional low murmur between the two groups. The soft scrape of boots on stone from the pickets shifting position. No wind through this section of tunnel.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Drow poison (bolts and javelins).

- **Trigger**: Any creature struck by a poisoned hand crossbow bolt or poisoned javelin.

- **Effect**: Standard drow sleep poison — save vs. poison or fall unconscious. Duration per standard D1-2 rules.

- **Disarm**: Cannot be disarmed; avoid being hit. Antitoxin or neutralize poison counters the effect after the fact.

### **Additional Notes**

Tactics: The two male pickets will alert both contingents the moment any creature is spotted moving along the passage. Despite the ongoing rivalry between the male and female contingents, both groups mount a coordinated, intelligent defense — they will not squabble when their lives are at stake. Expect the two groups to execute a pincer movement: the females pressing from the northeast while the males close from the southwest.

Vlondril's priority is intelligence, not heroism. If the drow are clearly losing, she will slip into the side cave, activate the lurker cloak to polymorph into a lurker above, and fly off unnoticed under cover of the melee. She carries critical information for the Fane and will not throw her life away.

Jeggred's rope of entanglement makes him extremely dangerous in tight tunnel confines. He will likely open with ice storm or lightning bolt if the party is bunched, then use the rope on survivors.

Akordia's glass globes are crowd-control weapons — she will throw them into tight clusters of attackers to blind and incapacitate multiple targets before pressing with hold person.

Political note: The brooch of House Aleval (Jeggred) and the scarab brooch of House Despana (Akordia) are politically significant items. Anyone who reaches drow noble society will find these identifying tokens carry weight — possibly dangerous weight.
