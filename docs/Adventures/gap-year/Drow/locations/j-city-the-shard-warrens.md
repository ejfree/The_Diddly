## The Shard Warrens — Khyber Free-Port

### Overview

The Shard Warrens is the eastern Underdark's open secret: a city that officially does not exist, is not recognized by any faction, appears on no drow census, and has been continuously occupied for at least three hundred years. Everyone uses it. No one defends it publicly.

The city grew around a major Khyber dragonshard vein — a seam of raw shard crystal running through the cavern walls for nearly a mile. Dragonshard mining requires knowledge most Underdark races don't have; it attracted **derro** first, then everyone else. What began as a mining camp became a supply depot, then a slave market, then a black market, then something large enough to have a mayor (they call the position the **Warden**), a town watch (they call it the **Lash**, which tells you something about its priorities), and three factions all competing for control of the shard output.

The Warrens sit on the eastern side of the Underdark corridor between the Sunless Sea shore to the north and the main tunnel approach to Xil to the west. Its position makes it impossible to avoid if you are moving goods through the eastern corridor.

---

### Location

**Eastern Underdark, southeastern shore of the Sunless Sea.** One major approach tunnel from the south (connects to unexplored deep passages, origin of most new arrivals and goods); one from the west (toward Xil and the Slave Road); one water-route from the north (Sunless Sea, connects to kuo-toa piers and the Mindspire's trade vessels). The city is built into a cavern cluster that the original derro miners expanded aggressively — rooms cut directly into the dragonshard seam, which means the walls in the older districts literally glow faintly with contained psionic energy.

---

### Scale

The Shard Warrens is harder to count than most cities because the population is in constant flux.

- **Permanent residents:** ~2,800 (roughly 60% derro, 20% assorted Underdark races, 20% surface-world long-term fugitives)
- **Transient population at any given time:** 500–1,500 (traders, caravan crew, slaves in transit, travelers, people hiding)
- **Slave pens (inventory):** 200–600 at any given time — the single largest variable

The racial breakdown of permanent residents:

- Derro (~1,700): the founding population, still dominant in numbers but fractured into three factions
- Duergar (~300): Grimstone Holds' trade agents plus independent operators
- Drow (~150): mostly House Velouryn agents, some Thalmyr
- Mixed humanoids (~650): grimlocks, goblins, kobolds, surface-world exiles, a handful of deep gnomes who left Blingdenstone for reasons they don't discuss

### Government — The Warden System

The **Warden** is the city's nominal authority, elected by a council of the six largest trade houses operating in the Warrens. "Elected" means bribed, threatened, or maneuvered into position. The current Warden has held the title for eleven years, which is exceptionally long and suggests either genuine competence or a very effective threat apparatus.

**Warden Thysk Crael** (derro, Lvl 11 Rogue with Mastermind archetype) — Small, colorless, and so consistently underestimated that he has started to find it offensive. He is not mad in the way most derro are. His particular psychic disorder manifests as an absolute inability to feel fear — not courage, just the complete absence of the fear response. He has walked into rooms that would stop anyone else and simply done what needed doing, which over eleven years has created a reputation that serves him better than any army would.

He knows what the Shard Warrens is. He does not pretend it is something it isn't. He will deal with anyone — drow, surface-worlders, illithid agents, the Overking's diplomats — as long as they observe the Warrens' three rules:

1. **No psionic attacks inside the city.** (The dragonshard walls amplify psionic damage unpredictably; this rule is self-interested, not principled.)
2. **Disputes are settled at the Lash post or outside the walls.** (Murder is permitted outside; inside it creates paperwork.)
3. **Shard output belongs to the Warrens.** (The seam is not for sale and not for theft. Several entities have tested this rule. They are no longer in the Warrens, or anywhere else.)

#### The Three Derro Factions

The derro population is split into three factions that were once one clan and have been fracturing slowly for a century:

**The Seam-Keepers** — Control the mining operations directly. Loyal to Warden Crael. Their saboteur culture means they are extraordinary at underground engineering: collapsing tunnels, opening new passages, building in the dark. They keep the city running. They are also paranoid about everyone else in the city touching the shard seam.

**The Pale Choir Acolytes** — A religious faction that has organized around the Xoriat resonance in the older shard chambers. The walls in the deepest mining areas pulse with faint psychic energy; the Pale Choir acolytes believe this is a voice speaking to them. (It is. It is the Mindspire's broadcast. They interpret it as divine revelation from "the Eye-That-Floats," which is apparently their name for the Elder Brain.) They are not violent yet. They are becoming harder to reason with.

**The Freeblades** — The mercenary arm, nominally independent, in practice controlled by whoever pays best. Currently under contract to House Velouryn as security for the slave pens and trade routes. Thirty-eight fighters, eight alchemist-saboteurs, two derro savants (psionic specialists). They have a sideline in providing "consultation" to anyone who needs someone found, removed, or frightened — in that order of preference.

### The City Districts

#### The Exchange

The Warrens' commercial center — a large, column-supported cavern that functions as market, auction house, and customs checkpoint. Every commodity that passes through the city is registered here (for a fee). Every fee goes to the Warden's treasury. The shard seam runs along the north wall, behind iron grating, and the faint glow it casts makes the Exchange feel like a perpetual dim twilight.

Goods regularly available:

- Khyber dragonshard fragments (raw and semi-processed)
- Grimstone steel (rebranded and sold at markup)
- Poisons and alchemical compounds (Velouryn surplus)
- Slaves — predominantly captured surface-worlders but also duergar debt-slaves and the occasional non-human
- Surface contraband (food, cloth, worked metal, magic items stripped from victims)
- Information — the Warrens' most valuable export

#### The Slave Pens

The least subtle part of the city. Stone pens, iron doors, minimal water and food. The Freeblades manage security. Clan Vrusk from Grimstone Holds audits the inventory monthly and takes delivery of purchases via the Sunless Sea water-route. House Velouryn runs the books.

The pens also hold an indeterminate number of people who are not technically slaves but have been "secured pending payment of debts" — a legal distinction the Warden maintains with a straight face.

#### The Deep Quarter

The oldest section — where the derro originally settled, carved directly into and around the dragonshard seam. The walls glow. The air has a faint metallic taste. The Pale Choir acolytes cluster here in their increasingly large prayer cells. Seam-Keeper engineers maintain the actual mining apparatus: shard-extraction tools, ore carts, a primitive crushing operation.

Non-derro residents avoid the Deep Quarter unless they have business there. The dragonshard energy has a mild psychic effect on unshielded visitors: vivid intrusive thoughts, an unsettling sense of being observed, occasional brief hallucinations. The Pale Choir acolytes say this means you are being noticed by the Eye-That-Floats and should be grateful.

#### The Warden's Hall

Thysk Crael's base of operations — a heavily fortified cluster of rooms near the center of the city, accessible through two tunnels that can be sealed in seconds. The hall serves as court, prison, treasury, and the Warden's personal residence. Crael's desk is covered at all times in ledgers, contracts, and correspondence. He sleeps four hours a night. He is always awake when you don't expect him to be.

### Subsettlements & Encounter Areas

#### Large Subsettlements

##### The Shore Market — Sunless Sea Waterfront District

A quarter-mile of Sunless Sea shoreline at the Warrens' northern edge has grown into its own semi-regulated district, distinct from the Exchange in the city's interior. Where the Exchange is customs-controlled and indoor, the Shore Market is open, chaotic, and governed primarily by whoever is physically present. Water-borne goods arrive here first. Kuo-toa fish-traders beach their boats here. Mindspire trade vessels anchor off the dock and send indoctrinated servants ashore with manifests.

**Population:** 200–400 at any given time (highly variable with boat traffic)

**Structure:**

- Three wooden docks extending into the lake — maintained by the Warrens at significant expense, constantly in need of repair from the Sunless Sea's fluctuating water levels
- A fish market (open-air, permanent stalls) selling kuo-toa catch, cave shrimp, and aquatic creatures unrecognizable to surface-worlders
- A row of warehouses on the shore where bulk water-borne cargo is staged before movement to the Exchange
- A harbormaster's post — a stone hut occupied by **Dockmaster Vreth** (derro, Lvl 5 Rogue), who collects dock fees and maintains a log of everything that arrives by water. His log is technically Warden Crael's property; Vreth has been selling copies to Clan Solvrak (Grimstone Holds) for six months.

**The Mindspire Presence:** On the third and seventh day of each tenday, a Mindspire vessel arrives with a sealed cargo — Khyber shard fragments purchased from the Seam-Keepers in a transaction that bypasses the Exchange entirely and goes through Vreth's dock fees. Warden Crael knows about this arrangement. The Pale Choir acolytes know the Elder Brain is buying shards and have strong opinions about it they have not expressed publicly yet.

**Role in Play:** The Shore Market is the easiest entry point into the Warrens by water — arriving by boat draws less scrutiny than coming through the southern tunnel with no visible cargo. It is also the place to arrange passage back across the Sunless Sea, find a kuo-toa who will take passengers, or intercept Mindspire communications before they reach the main city.

##### The Lower Seam — Deep Shard Excavation (below the Deep Quarter)

Below the Deep Quarter, through a series of increasingly narrow passages that require crawling for the last hundred feet, the dragonshard vein reaches its most concentrated deposit. This is where the oldest shard crystal is — formations centuries in growth, as tall as a person, glowing with contained psionic energy that would make a surface-world wizard's instruments scream.

The Pale Choir acolytes claimed this area three months ago. The Seam-Keepers have not been able to operate here since. What the acolytes are doing in the Lower Seam is difficult to assess because they don't report to anyone and have stopped responding to conventional communication.

**What is known from Seam-Keeper observation:**

- The acolytes have stopped sleeping, eating, and speaking aloud
- The crystal formations have been growing faster since the acolytes arrived — measurably, with Seam-Keeper instruments
- Three Seam-Keepers who entered to recover equipment last month returned without the equipment and without memory of approximately four hours
- The psionic pressure in the Lower Seam is now detectable two tunnels away without instruments

**What is actually happening:** The acolytes have achieved sustained psionic resonance with the shard seam — they are functioning as biological amplifiers, supplementing the Mindspire's broadcast with their own psychic output. They did not intend this and do not fully understand it. They believe they are in communion with "the Eye-That-Floats" and are receiving divine instruction. The Elder Brain is dimly aware of them as a new signal in its broadcast environment and has been adjusting its output to interact with them. This is a feedback loop that will become dangerous within weeks.

**Role in Play:** The Lower Seam is the physical center of the Pale Choir problem in the Warrens. Entering it is possible but extremely disorienting — all psionic abilities behave unpredictably (including magic items with psionic components), and spending more than 10 minutes inside without protection requires Will saves every minute. Destroying the key crystal formations would collapse the resonance immediately but would also cause significant structural damage to the Deep Quarter above and would end the Seam-Keepers' livelihood. There is a more surgical solution that requires understanding the broadcast frequency first — which means someone needs to talk to the Mindspire.

#### Smaller Encounter Areas

##### 1. The Lash Post

The Warden's enforcement station — a hardened stone room near the Exchange's main entrance, staffed at all times by eight Freeblades on rotating watch. Has a cell block with four holding cells (currently: one duergar accused of short-weighing a shard purchase; one unknown species in deep psychic fugue, brought in from the Shore Market three days ago; two empty). The Freeblades' on-duty officer is **Sergeant Keth** (derro, Lvl 6 Fighter) — professionally brutal, completely transactional. She enforces the Warden's three rules with absolute consistency and has no opinion whatsoever about anything that happens outside them.

The Lash Post also functions as the Warrens' information clearinghouse: anyone who wants to report something, or wants to know if something has been reported, comes here first. The logs are public record. Keth charges two copper pieces per page for access.

---

##### 2. Malryk's Office

House Velouryn's official presence occupies three connected rooms in the Exchange's northeast corner — the most defensible commercial position in the building, which was not an accident. The outer room is a standard trade office with a clerk (actually a Thalmyr informant, but Malryk knows and finds it convenient to let them report back selectively). The middle room is a secure meeting space with sound-deadening stone walls and a door that requires both a physical key and a verbal pass-code to open. The back room is locked at all times and entered only by Malryk personally.

**Trade Captain Malryk Velouryn** (Lvl 8 Rogue with Mastermind archetype) keeps the ledgers in the back room. Not all the ledgers — the most sensitive material is encrypted and stored in a location only he knows — but enough to prove the Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route, two cases of falsified slave-origin paperwork, and one transaction that, if it became public in Xil, would end the Velouryn-Veythrae alliance immediately and possibly House Velouryn's existence shortly afterward.

Malryk is professional, charming, and will be aware the PCs are in the Warrens within two hours of their arrival. He will make contact on his timeline.

---

##### 3. The Pilgrim Cells

Eight rooms rented to travelers who need to stay more than a day — the closest thing the Warrens has to an inn. Located in the Warrens' central district, above the Exchange. The rooms have stone floors, straw bedding, a locked door (the lock is functional; the wall beside it has a crack large enough to look through), and a shared cistern in the corridor. Cost: 2 silver per night, paid in advance to a derro who introduces himself only as "the Key."

Current occupants: a duergar trader waiting for a Grimstone Holds delivery that is three days late; two surface-worlders (siblings, clearly out of their depth) who arrived through the south tunnel and have been trying to quietly find passage back to the surface for a week; a kuo-toa pilgrim who missed the last boat to the Mindspire and refuses to explain what was in the sealed package it was carrying; an empty room with a broken lock that "the Key" has not fixed.

The Pilgrim Cells are where the PCs are most likely to find people who know things they shouldn't and are willing to talk.

---

##### 4. The Forge Annex

A small Grimstone Holds workshop in the northeastern corner of the Exchange — technically Clan Solvrak's trading post, in practice a buying operation for raw shard output before it moves west. Two duergar processors work here sorting and rough-grading shard fragments; a Clan Solvrak factor named **Grund** (Lvl 4 Merchant NPC) manages the paperwork and the relationship with the Seam-Keepers.

The Seam-Keepers resent the Forge Annex's existence. The Warden approved it because Clan Solvrak pays triple the standard trading license fee. The Pale Choir acolytes consider it an insult — outsiders profiting from the seam — and have been leaving acolyte symbols carved into the Annex's doorframe, which Grund covers with plaster every morning.

Grund has noticed that the raw shard output has been decreasing for three months (the acolytes are reducing Seam-Keeper access to the Lower Seam). He has filed reports with Clan Solvrak. The reports have been acknowledged and not acted on. He is starting to wonder if there is something Clan Solvrak knows that would explain the inaction.

---

##### 5. The Speaking Wall

In the Deep Quarter's oldest section, where the shard seam emerges directly through the floor into a shallow pool of crystalline energy roughly 20 feet across, the Pale Choir acolytes conduct their central ritual. The floor glows blue-white. The air carries a metallic charge that makes hair stand on end and tastes of lightning.

The pool is not liquid — the crystal surface is solid — but it behaves like liquid in psionic terms: it ripples when someone projects psychic energy into it and reflects that energy outward amplified. This is where the acolyte who walked into the Exchange three weeks ago was "charged" before her involuntary broadcast.

**Mechanical effect:** Any creature with psionic abilities, spell-like abilities, or psychic-adjacent magic (GM discretion) that touches the pool surface must immediately attempt a Will save. Failure means their ability fires involuntarily, directed outward, at maximum range. Success means they experience the Elder Brain's broadcast as a clear image: an open palm, rising from dark water, very slowly closing.

Non-psionic creatures can stand here with no mechanical effect, which the acolytes find profoundly confusing about the PCs.

### The Shard Seam — What It Actually Is

The dragonshard vein is approximately 280 years old — formed when a Khyber manifest zone crystallized in the cavern rock. Khyber dragonshards hold demonic and binding energies in Eberron lore; this seam is no different. The raw shards are useful for arcane binding, containment rituals, and demon-summoning — which is why House Veythrae (Xil's arcane house) buys them in quantity through House Velouryn intermediaries.

The seam has been mined for three centuries and should be exhausted. It is not. The Seam-Keepers know this. They do not know why. Deepmistress Yurra from Grimstone Holds suspects the east passage connects to this seam's source, which would mean the seam is not a deposit but a conduit — something generating Khyber energy from deeper down is feeding it.

The Pale Choir acolytes believe the seam is alive. This may be less metaphorical than it sounds.

---

### House Velouryn's Operation Here

House Velouryn (Xil's mercantile/poison house) runs its entire external supply chain through the Shard Warrens. Trade Captain Malryk Velouryn keeps a permanent office in the Exchange and pays the Freeblades' contract personally. Velouryn's operations here:

- **Slave acquisition:** Velouryn buys from the Warrens' pens and supplies to Xil's slave registry
- **Contraband flow:** Surface goods that would require explanation in Xil pass through Velouryn's Shard Warrens office first, laundered through Exchange paperwork
- **Poison supply:** The Warrens source rare alchemical components Xil's own production can't match; Velouryn's Poisonmistress Thivra has standing orders here
- **The Veythrae-kuo-toa deal:** Trade Captain Malryk is the one routing Veythrae's arcane supply through the Sunless Sea water-route, bypassing Xilrath's checkpoint (D3). He does not advertise this.

If the PCs want leverage over House Velouryn, the Shard Warrens is where to find it. If House Velouryn learns the PCs are in the Warrens, Malryk will take an interest in them.

### Adventure Hooks

#### The Free Port

The Shard Warrens is the one place in the eastern Underdark where the PCs can move around without drow paperwork — as long as they have coin and don't cause trouble. It is the logical place to resupply, gather intelligence, and make contacts before approaching Xil. Warden Crael will receive them with professional neutrality. He has no particular stake in whether they succeed or fail at whatever they're doing, but he does have a price for everything he knows.

#### The Pale Choir Problem

The Pale Choir acolytes in the Deep Quarter have been getting louder. Three weeks ago one of them walked into the Exchange during market hours, stood in the center of the room, and broadcast a psionic wave that left everyone present with a three-day headache and a persistent image of an open hand reaching from deep water. Warden Crael wants this handled quietly — not violently, because the Seam-Keepers would object, but *handled*. He is looking for outside assistance.

This is directly connected to the Mindspire's amplification broadcast. Stopping the broadcast (or shielding the seam) would stop the acolytes' escalation. The Warden doesn't know this. The PCs might piece it together.

#### Malryk's Ledger

House Velouryn's trade books in the Shard Warrens contain evidence of the Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route and several other transactions that powerful people in Xil would not want documented. Malryk knows where the books are. Thalmyr agent at the Ashen Cup (Grimstone Holds) also knows they exist and has been trying to access them for six months. The PCs arriving is a new variable in an existing operation.

#### The Conduit

Deepmistress Yurra (Grimstone Holds) finally made contact with the Shard Warrens Seam-Keepers, asking about the east passage. The Seam-Keepers were not cooperative. Yurra is now convinced the east passage connects to the shard seam, which means something deeper is generating Khyber energy from below both cities. She wants help investigating — people who are not duergar, not derro, and not connected to the Warden's political interests.
