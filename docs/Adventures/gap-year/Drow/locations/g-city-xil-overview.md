## D3 Chapter 12 — The Vault of the Drow (Xil)

The approach tunnels from the Shrine of the Kuo-Toans narrow and then open dramatically into the Vault: a cavern roughly 40 miles across, 10–20 miles wide, and 300–1,000 feet high. A purplish-violet phosphorescent light suffuses the entire space — dim but constant, emanating from crystalline formations, glowing fungi forests, and mineral-laced stone. This is not darkness. It is another kind of light, alien to surface races but as natural to the drow as sunlight is to elves.

The city of **Xil** rises at the center of the Vault's southern half — a tiered, stalactite-hung metropolis strung between massive column formations with hanging bridges and carved warrens. It was built as a trading hub: the crossroads of the deep Underdark where poisons, slaves, beasts, surface contraband, and arcane components moved through eight noble houses that competed more through commerce and assassination than open war. That city still exists beneath the surface — the Great Bazaar still runs, the Velouryn trade routes still flow, and House Vorryn still records every transaction — but it has been subordinated to a purpose that has been building for two decades.

Xil is now the command center for Lolth's proxy war against the surface. The eight houses are not merely competing merchants; they are the logistics, intelligence, arcane, and assault divisions of a coordinated campaign. The giant clans — hill, frost, and fire — are not acting independently. They are receiving orders, materiel, and strategic direction from Xil. Thalmyr intelligence cells are embedded in surface cities. Velouryn smuggling routes move weapons and poisons upward. Veythrae battle-wizards consult with giant war-chiefs through ritual sending. The surface folk see isolated giant raids. What is actually happening is a phased campaign with Xil at its nerve center.

The surrounding Vault is no safe approach: predators, drow patrols, fungal forests inhabited by poison-spore growths, and the great rivers and lakes teeming with dangerous aquatic life stand between any entering force and the city gates.

**Grakk's Chamber:** Iron trunk (locked, Thievery DC 22): 200 pp, 14 gems (50 gp each), 6 gems (100 gp each). Hook near sleeping pallet: *ring of fire resistance* (taken from a surface adventurer). ### Area 1 — The Gate of Xil

A massive bronze-clad stone archway, 30 feet high, flanked by two carved spider columns (each 40 feet tall). Two iron portcullises with a guard chamber between them.

**Gate guard:** 16 Black Blade soldiers (House Xilrath), 2 patrol leaders, 1 gate captain. Full plate; armed with spear, hand crossbow, and sleep-poison bolts. Two giant spiders (Lvl 2; AC 16; HP 30; SA venom; XP 30 each) chained to the outer arch columns.

- **Black Blade soldier:** Lvl 4; AC 21; HP 58; Fort +11, Ref +11, Will +9; Speed 25 ft; Melee longsword +12 [+7/+2], 1d8+6 slashing; Ranged hand crossbow +11 (range 60 ft), 1d6+4 piercing + sleep poison (Fort DC 18 or Unconscious 1 min); XP 80 each
- **Gate Captain:** Lvl 6; AC 24; HP 90; Fort +15, Ref +14, Will +12; Melee longsword +16 [+11/+6], 1d8+9 slashing; XP 240

**Entry requirements:** All travelers must state house affiliation and purpose. Merchant trains pay 1 gp per person gate tax plus 10% customs on declared goods value. Those without house affiliation may purchase a 24-hour passage token for 5 gp each. Forgery is a capital offense.

**Guard chamber:** Locked strongbox (Thievery DC 24) with 340 gp (week's gate tax) and the day's passage ledger.

### Area 2 — The Great Bazaar

The largest open space inside the city walls, ringed by permanent merchant stalls carved into the cavern wall. Drow of every house mingle here alongside non-drow traders, slaves being evaluated, and Thalmyr informants watching everything.

**Scale:** 300 feet across, roughly circular. Central raised pool serves as gathering point. Market stalls sell edible fungi, preserved meats, crafted goods, slaves, weapons, and exotic substances.

**Key vendors:**
- **Velouryn Consortium Stall** (northwest quadrant): Venom Syndicate enforcers visible. Sells poisons, alchemical goods, and surface-world curiosities. Matriarch Ulvrae Velouryn sometimes appears in person.
- **Vorryn Registry Booth:** Handles slave registration, contract recording, and the sale of forged documents (at premium, in private).
- **Dravun Beast Handlers** (southeast edge): Caged quaggoths, tethered lizards, and bred spiders for sale.

**Encounter note:** The Bazaar is nominally neutral ground. Open violence results in immediate Black Blade response (1d4 rounds). Slow poison, knife-in-crowd, and arranged accidents are business as usual.

**Connection to D2:** The Puffball Mushroom clan pin (found in D2 Area 9, cell 9) grants a private meeting with Trade Captain Malryk Velouryn — he pays 2,000 gp per drow broadleaf fungus sample and can arrange passage out of the city at 500 gp per person.

### Area 3 — City Guard House

House Xilrath's primary enforcement post. Maintains order, investigates gate log breaches, holds prisoners awaiting house judgment. Two levels: ground floor (barracks, interrogation rooms), lower level (holding cells).

**Ground floor:** 12 soldiers on duty at all times (rotating shifts). One sergeant per shift. A *sending stone* (communicates with the Gate and the Fane's security office) is kept in the captain's desk.

**Holding cells (lower level):** 6 cells, each 10' × 10'. Current occupants (DM's discretion): 1–3 slaves, 1 drow commoner accused of "disrespect to a priestess," 0–2 empty.

- **Guard soldier:** Lvl 3; AC 19; HP 42; Fort +10, Ref +9, Will +8; XP 60 each
- **Guard sergeant:** Lvl 6; AC 23; HP 88; Fort +14, Ref +13, Will +11; XP 240

### Area 4 — Slave Processing Pens

The city's intake facility for newly arrived slaves. 12 holding pens, medical triage area, branding station, and small auction block.

**Current inventory:** 40–60 mixed humanoids. Six bugbear handlers (Lvl 3). Two Draethi clerks. One Velouryn assessor conducting valuations.

**Special holding pen:** One cell locked with a heavy iron bar and a *glyph of warding* (Lvl 5 spell; triggers *blindness* on breach). Currently holds a **4th level human fighter** (imprisoned under suspicious circumstances). He has detailed memory of the Bazaar and gate layout from observation — useful to rescuers.

### Area 5 — Temple of Lolth (Outer Precinct)

The outer courtyard and public-facing portion of the Temple-Fortress. Open to all drow for worship; closed to non-drow except as sacrificial offerings. A 60-foot stone idol of Lolth dominates the central courtyard.

**Outer precinct:** 8 Zhaelor warrior-priests (Lvl 4 Fighter/Divine Caster; AC 21; HP 56; Fort +13, Ref +11, Will +12; Melee war flail +12 [+7/+2], 1d8+6; Divine Spells DC 20; XP 80 each) posted at each quarter. Two Lash of Lolth zealots patrol constantly.

**Public altar:** Worshippers may sacrifice slaves here (each sacrifice appeases Lolth's interest; DM notes 1–4 days of favorable omen). The offering box contains 1d100 × 10 gp (emptied twice daily by Zhaelor attendants).

**Entry to Inner Fane:** Requires invitation from the Grand Priestess or valid noble rank. See Areas 18–20.

### Area 6 — The Wharf Cavern

## City Districts (Reference)

**1. The Noble Plateau** — Two major houses (Xilrath and Veythrae) occupy massive stalactite/column complexes with hanging bridges. The four minor houses cluster slightly lower in elevation — a constant reminder of rank.

**2. The Lower Warrens** — Dense cavern neighborhoods for artisans, soldiers' families, and laborers. House Thalmyr maintains hidden bolt-holes and passageways throughout.

**3. The Slave Pens** — Expanded well beyond standard norms. Quaggoths, goblins, kobolds, duergar, and captured surface folk are kept in regimented pens. House Dravun's beast pits connect here.

**4. The Great Market** — Dominated by House Velouryn. Exotic poisons, Veythrae-enchanted weapons, slaves, fungus-based food, surface contraband. Back-channel deals with duergar and Khyber smugglers happen here.

**5. The Temple-Fortress of Lolth** — The centerpiece, carved into a stalagmite cluster, bridges spanning to stalactites above. Serves as holy site, war council hall, and reliquary. The Council of Houses meets in the obsidian chamber within.

**6. The War Tunnels** — Fortified network of caverns leading upward toward the surface. Guard towers, ambush chambers, and choke points. The staging ground for surface assaults; soldiers bivouac and drill here constantly. The Tunnels are also the primary conduit for communication with the giant clans — sealed courier pouches, bound-messenger constructs, and occasional Thalmyr agents pass through under escort daily. The surface is closer than it appears from the city proper.

**7. The Shadowfire Forge** — House Veythrae-controlled. Weapons research compound, demonic binding pits, and arcane testing grounds. Develops the tactical magic for the surface campaign. The "Academy" label is an old name; what the Forge actually produces is war doctrine and bound fiends.

## Tone

- Less decadence, more discipline. Streets are patrolled, curfews exist, soldiers drill openly. The Great Bazaar still runs but trade disputes are resolved faster and more violently than in peacetime.
- The surface war looms over every district. Food stockpiles, war banners, priests preaching conquest. Soldiers in kit are as common a sight as merchants.
- The giant proxy campaign is visible if you know what to look for: courier riders bearing sealed orders heading upward through the War Tunnels, Thalmyr agents returning from surface reconnaissance, Veythrae messengers entering giant-marked safe houses. The surface folk think the giants are acting on their own initiative. They are not.
- Paranoia. Houses plot constantly, but none dare sabotage the war effort — yet. The council deadlock is an open secret. What breaks the deadlock may break the campaign.

## How the PCs Arrive Here

Xil is the D3 endpoint of the giant-chain arc. The PCs enter after descending through:

- G1 (Hill Giants) → G2 (Frost Giants) → G3 (Fire Giants)
- D1 (troglodyte-held Underdark entry) → D2 (kuo-toa shrine)
- D3: Xil

Their arrival fulfills the third step of the prophecy's descent into the Spiral of Despair.
