## D1 Chapter 8 — Warrens of the Troglodytes

**Encounter area q-18/19** — The Grand Cavern. A massive underground complex 200–300 feet wide, 200 feet long, 175–250 feet high. Ceiling drips with stalactites, floor with stalagmites, broken stone throughout. Multiple factions (drow, bugbears, troglodytes, trolls) coexist under drow authority.

Wandering monster check: **1 in 12 per turn.** Roll 1d4 on the appropriate half-table below (western or eastern side of the cavern).

## Encounter Areas 1–40

### Area 1 — Entrance to the Grand Cavern

Terraced ledges lead down to a glistening floor. Ceiling 175–250 feet. Thick columns, protruding ledges, broken stalactites throughout. Sentries at positions B and D.

**B (3 positions):** 3 bugbear sentries (hp 15 each, XPV 195 each). If intruder detected: two attack, one runs to alert area 19–23. Equipped: heavy morning star, 2 heavy throwing spears.

**D (3 positions):** 3 Drow Level 2 Fighters (male). On sighting intruders, cast *dancing lights* to mark position. Each can see at least one other sentry. Reporting sentry goes to area 6; others hold position.

- Drow male sentry: MR 54%; AC 1; HD 2 (hp 8); THAC0 20; chain +1, buckler +1, shortsword +1, dagger +1, poisoned javelin; XPV 134 each

### Area 3 — Glittering Cave

Ceiling ~8 feet. Walls covered in crystals that reflect and refract light. Crystals have no monetary value. Scattered across the northern quarter: **121 crystal gems (10 gp each)** — these are bait for a trapper. The drow recently looted the real treasure.

- **Trapper:** hp 73; XPV 4,018. No other treasure.

---

### Area 4 — Side Cavern

Ceiling 90 feet. Ledges and shelves along the south, east, and north walls house **15 gargoyles** roosting at 65–80 feet, swooping down to attack from behind. Will pursue northward (high ceiling) but not eastward. No treasure.

- Gargoyles (15): hp 22 each; XPV 275 each (4,125 total)

---

### Area 5 — Spur Cavern

A very large **purple worm** (hp 118; XPV 7,260) recently moved in to lay eggs. Six eggs in the northeast finger, hatching in 3 turns. Mother is resting. No treasure inside the worm; eight castings elsewhere — the sixth contains **11 gems (100 gp each)** and **four vials of holy water.**

### Area 6 — Cave (Drow Oversight Post)

Fourteen drow oversee cavern activities, aid merchants, and scout for invaders. If position appears threatened, the female commander retreats northwest to inform her superiors and House Noquar.

Each drow carries 1–4 pp per level. Female commander has two 100 gp gems and a bronze pin (nightmare's head, *Noquar* inscribed in drow).

**Male Fighters (4):** MR 54%; AC 1; HD 2 (hp 9 each); THAC0 20; chain +1, buckler +1, shortsword +1, dagger +1, hand crossbow w/ 10 poisoned bolts; XPV 136 each (544 total)

**Male Commander:** MR 58%; AC −2; HD 4 (hp 18); THAC0 18; chain +2, buckler +2, shortsword +2, dagger +2, hand crossbow w/ 10 poisoned bolts; XPV 312

**Female Fighters (8):** MR 56%; AC 0; HD 3 (hp 15 each); THAC0 20; chain +1, buckler +1, shortsword +1, dagger +1, atlatl w/ 3 poisoned javelins; XPV 235 each (1,880 total)

**Female Commander:** MR 62%; AC −4; HD 6 (hp 30); THAC0 16; chain +2, buckler +2, shortsword +2, dagger +2, *death lance* (Dmg 1–6, plus 3–12 negative energy, plus save vs. poison or 1–4 level drain); mounted on **nightmare** (hp 32; XPV 856); XPV 805

**Treasure (hidden in nightmare stall, lead-lined hollow, stone lid with secret catch):**

- Wooden chest (1,000 sp)
- Iron cup (empty; if poison from leather case mixed with wine here, imbiber gains total poison immunity for 6 turns)
- 10 leather bags (100 gp each)

- Pouch (20 gems, 100 gp each)
- 2 pieces of jewelry (5,000 gp each)
- Ivory case (250 gp; black metal chain & medallion with spider and drow figures)
- Leather case (4 potions: extra-healing, healing, poison, polymorph self)

### Area 7 — Sunken Cave (Lich Lair)

Two narrow stair-like terraced flights descend ~20 feet into a low cave that **appears totally dark and deserted** — permanent illusion. Lair of **Asberdies the Lich** (20th level MU). Will not bother passers-by; attacks only if entered.

**Strategy:** Prefers ventriloquism deception, *repulsion*, *reverse gravity*, *limited wish* to deter; uses *monster summoning* and *invisible stalker* to avoid personal melee. Prefers *dimension door*/*teleport* invisibly to flee rather than fight. Will erect *wall of force* and use *time stop* to strip spell components and holy symbols.

**Asberdies the Lich:** AL N(E); INT Supra-Gen; SZ M; MV 6"; AC 0; HD 20 (hp 66); THAC0 7; #AT 1; Dmg 1–10 or by spell; SA fear, paralysis touch; SD immune to normal weapons, charm, cold, death magic, electricity, enfeeblement, insanity, polymorph, sleep; XPV 23,256; wears *ring of protection +3*

Spells:
- **1st:** burning hands, charm person, magic missile ×2, ventriloquism
- **2nd:** invisibility, mirror image, ray of enfeeblement, web ×2
- **3rd:** dispel magic, fireball, fly, lightning bolt, phantasmal force
- **4th:** charm monster, confusion, dimension door, fumble, polymorph other
- **5th:** cloudkill, cone of cold, hold monster, teleport, wall of force
- **6th:** flesh to stone, globe of invulnerability, invisible stalker, repulsion
- **7th:** limited wish, Mordenkainen's sword, reverse gravity
- **8th:** Bigby's clenched fist, monster summoning VI, Otto's irresistible dance
- **9th:** power word kill, time stop

600 *magic mouth* spells cast throughout the lair. Hollow where he rests is silk-lined — no treasure here. On a small northwest ledge behind a broken stone: **folded-up portable hole.**

**Portable hole contents (cursed — turns living taker into a wight):**

- Jeweled crown (80,000 gp)
- Gem-set orb (50,000 gp)
- Gem-encrusted scepter (65,000 gp)
- 1,000 gp
- 10 garnets (10 gp each)
- Bag of *dust of sneezing and choking*
- *Staff of striking*
- *Potion of longevity*
- *Scarab of protection from evil clerics*
- Scroll of 7 MU spells: *knock, tongues, minor globe of invulnerability, wall of ice, stone to flesh, charm plants, symbol of fear*

### Area 8 — Side Cavern (Gargoyle Roost)

Similar to area 4. Dozens of ledges and shelves 70+ feet above the floor house **23 gargoyles** (hp 22 each; XPV 275 each; 6,050 total). These creatures **greatly fear the drow** and will not attack anyone wearing drow garb. Attack all others freely. No treasure.

### Area 9 — Cavern Stable

Six **pack lizards** tethered near a mound of fungus fodder. Used by merchants. Will attack anyone unchaining them unless controlled with a hooked goad (which they recognize as sign of mastership).

- Pack Lizards (6): hp 33 each; XPV 489 each (2,934 total)

### Areas 10–13 — Complex of Low Caves (Ghoul/Ghast Den)

Temporary residence of 10 ghasts and 32 ghouls traveling northwest to serve the drow. Waiting for the giant slug at area 14 to move on before using the northeast passage.

**Area 10:** 32 ghouls (hp 10 each; XPV 85 each), 1–6 gp each.

**Area 11:** 4 ghasts (hp 19 each; XPV 266 each) with 7 gems (100 gp each).

**Area 12:** 2 ghasts (hp 24 each; XPV 286 each) with a sack containing:

- 3 pieces of jewelry (5,000 gp, 3,000 gp, 1,800 gp)
- Tube 1: scroll of 3 cleric spells (*cure light wounds, tongues, conjure animals* — last produces 3 brown bears, hp 28 each)
- Tube 2: map showing northeast passage to tertiary route leading to area S/T-17 (draw onto players' map)

**Area 13:** 4 ghasts (hp 20 each; XPV 270 each) with 300 gp and 18 gems (10 gp each).

### Area 14 — Giant Slug Niche

A **giant slug** (hp 87; XPV 3,392) blended into a niche beside the northeast passageway. Stayed for the excellent food supply. Drow intend to remove it soon. No treasure.

### Area 15 — Hundred-Foot High Shelf (Hieracosphinx Nest)

Two **hieracosphinx** (hp 49, 43; XPV 1,188, 1,116) nested here awaiting their drow mistress and master. Will not attack unless a creature comes within 20 feet; respond only to name command. No treasure — but one wears a medallion: **staff of copper set with eight matched emeralds (24,000 gp)**, *Eilservs* engraved on reverse in drow.

### Areas 16–18 — Troll Caves

Rank stench noticeable from 10 feet outside. Caves filled with rotting carcasses, bones, excrement, pelts. All treasure moved to tribal chief's lair at area 32. Trolls will support each other, the trogs, and bugbears if alerted.

- **Area 16:** 10 trolls (hp 29 each; XPV 757 each)
- **Area 17:** 16 trolls (hp 30 each; XPV 773 each)
- **Area 18:** 18 trolls (hp 31 each; XPV 789 each)

### Areas 28–30 — Troglodyte Caves

All one tribe; work in cooperation with trolls and bugbears under drow directive (and now willingly, having prospered from mutual aid). Tunnel ceilings only ~6 feet; caves not much more spacious. Most possessions are stone or bone; wood/leather/cloth items are rare.

**T. Unit Dens (throughout):** 1 male (hp 11, stone club), 1 female (hp 7), 2 hatchlings (hp 5, 2). No treasure.

**Area 28 — Long Cave:** 36 young male trogs quartered here. No treasure.

- Trog males (36): hp 10; metal-tipped javelin (THAC0 13, Dmg 2–8) and metal battle axe (THAC0 16, Dmg 1–8); XPV 56 each (2,016 total)

**Area 29 — Gen Leaders:** 4 large trogs (forepart) + 2 huge elder trogs (northernmost). Each leader wears a gold medallion hammered into a human skull likeness (20 gp); elders have rough gems (10 gp each) set in theirs. On back shelf: **human skull sheathed in gold (230 gp) with two huge garnets (2,000 gp each) in eye sockets.** Also 4 sheaves of javelins (48 total).

- Large leader trogs (4): hp 17 each; THAC0 16; metal bardiche (Dmg 2–8); XPV 116 each (464 total)
- Huge elder trogs (2): hp 22 each; THAC0 15; metal bardiche (Dmg 2–8); XPV 198 each

**Area 30 — Communal Cave:** Normally empty except during tribal feasts, councils, or muster. 8 immature females tend the area at all times.

- Young female trogs (8): hp 6 each; XPV 13 each (104 total)

### Area 31 — Eastern Cavern (Fungi Fields)

Troglodytes tend extensive fungi patches here — the primary food source for the cavern's inhabitants. Narrow paths wind through fields of mushrooms and strange gigantic growths. Dim phosphorescent lichen light; flame reveals whites, grays, yellows, tans, browns, reds, oranges, pinks, mauves, violets.

- **S positions:** 1–4 shriekers each
- **V positions:** 1–4 violet fungi each
- **Beneath the underlined violet fungi patch (V):** hidden **gem (10,000 gp)** and a ***jug of alchemy***

**Troll tribal chief:** hp 49; Dmg 7–10/7–10/4–14; XPV 917

### Area 39 — Side Gallery (Wyvern Stable)

Two **wyverns** (hp 49, 41; XPV 1,415, 1,335) chained at position A — raised from eggs by the trog chief (#38). He rides the larger in battle; the other fights beside its sibling. They obey the trog chief only; he and they have a strange mutual liking. On a shelf at position B: a **huge flail** the chief uses when mounted (+2 to hit, +3 damage — only for him). No treasure. If the wyverns are slain, the chief goes berserk.

---

### Area 40 — Magic Pool

A broad, clear pool (~8 feet deep at center), fed by an underground stream. Cavern inhabitants drink from it regularly. **Drinking has no effect.** Pool does not communicate. Pool radiates a dim dweomer; gems inside it do not.

**At the center (under light sand layer):** 89 gems (10 gp each).

**Gem mechanics:**

- If any gems are removed from the pool for more than 1 turn → they turn a like number of gems within a 30-foot radius into worthless quartz (1 gp).
- If a character tosses 1–4 gems *into* the pool → a like number of gems *in their possession* improve by one quality category (flaws cleared; e.g., 10 gp → 50 gp, or 50 gp → 100 gp).
- Drow merchants who know the secret always toss only 10 gp gems.
