# I5 — The Ironbell Warren

## **The Ironbell Warren**
Major Outpost. ~3 hours east of Blingdenstone, in a secondary cavern system off the main D1 corridor. Population: ~45 svirfneblin (garrison, families, and a handful of refugees). Commander: Sergeant Durwick Hammerstone. The only outpost with permanent housing — stone-carved quarters for the garrison plus a dozen family burrows. Blingdenstone's first military response to anything moving up the D1 approach.

### **Description**
A natural cavern with two defensible chokepoints and a widened evacuation shaft. Permanent stone-carved quarters line the walls. Family burrows branch off the main chamber. At the center: a cracked iron bell salvaged from the destroyed gnome enclave of Copperdeep. The bell hangs on a stone bracket; it has not been rung since it was hung, but it is maintained in working condition.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Oil lamps throughout the permanent quarters. The chokepoints are dark by default — defenders fight with darkvision advantage.

- **Surfaces**: Natural cavern floor, worked smooth in the main chamber. The chokepoints are narrow cut-stone passages, barely wide enough for two abreast.

- **Objects**: The Ironbell (cracked iron, salvaged from Copperdeep). The resonance chamber disguised as a gnomish earth-prayer alcove — two tuned stone columns, looks like a stonecutter's adaptation. Three-month ration stockpile. Water-filtration cistern. Crossbows and tunnel-collapse charges at the chokepoints.

- **Doors & Exits**: Two chokepoints (defensible). Collapsed tertiary shaft, deliberately widened by Lisa into an emergency evacuation route — only the garrison knows it.

- **Special Interactions**: The resonance chamber transmits coded vibration signals to Blingdenstone faster than a runner. Lisa designed it using Archano-Mechanical principles; the gnomes believe she adapted an old stonecutter's trick. She did not.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Cook-fire, oil lamps, stone. The cistern: cold clean water smell.

- **Sounds**: The Warren is active — voices, movement, children in the family burrows. The two tuned stone columns in the earth-prayer alcove produce a faint sympathetic hum when signals are being sent or received.

- **Passive Creatures**:

- **Hostile Threats**:

- **Unique NPCs**: **Sergeant Durwick Hammerstone** — blocky, methodical, fought in the old militia and survived the raids that killed the council. Loyal to Grissel Deepmantle to the point of personal devotion; she promoted him when old officers treated him as furniture. He will not be easily misled about the PCs' intentions.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Tunnel-collapse charges — defensive

- **Trigger**: Durwick's order

- **Effect**: Either chokepoint can be collapsed, sealing the Warren from approach. Irreversible without significant excavation.

- **Disarm**: Durwick controls deployment. The charges are not accessible from outside the garrison positions.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot**: Three months of preserved rations. Equipment stockpile (crossbows, bolts, tunnel-collapse charges).

- **Hidden Loot**:

- **Magical Items**: The resonance chamber columns — not removable, but identifiable as arcane construction (not gnomish) with a successful Arcana check, DC 18.

### **Additional Notes**
**The Ironbell:** Ringing it was Durwick's idea — "so the dead know someone's still keeping watch." Lisa said nothing. She found it unexpectedly moving.

**The Warren's functions:** Forward garrison (Durwick's 20-person guard detail, standing orders to hold for one hour then fall back), supply depot (three months of preserved rations, functioning cistern, first rally point in an evacuation), relay station (resonance chamber to Blingdenstone).

**GM note:** If the PCs arrive at Blingdenstone via the D1 approach, they may encounter the Warren first. Durwick will have information about the party before they reach the city gate — what Mira logged at the Crevice Watch (I1) will have been relayed here.
