# I4 — The Dunbarrow Stone

## **The Dunbarrow Stone**
Smaller Outpost 4. ~1 hour west of Blingdenstone, in a small alcove cavern off a secondary passage. Population: ~22 gnomes (refugees, no militia affiliation). Not a military outpost. A refugee camp that assembled itself around a cairn Lisa built in her second month in Blingdenstone — before she made contact, still hiding, mourning her dead.

### **Description**
A small alcove cavern off a secondary passage, dry and sheltered. A natural granite column stands at the center, river stones stacked around its base. The community's living arrangement is minimal but organized — sleeping areas against the walls, a shared food cache, a central space around the cairn that has become informal common ground.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Small oil lamps. The community maintains low light — enough to work by, not enough to be visible from the main passage.

- **Surfaces**: Natural stone floor, smoothed by foot traffic. The cairn base: stacked river stones, each placed deliberately.

- **Objects**: The granite column and stacked stone cairn. Community food stores. Sleeping arrangements. Offerings left by the community — small stones, dried fungi, objects of personal significance — arranged around the cairn base.

- **Doors & Exits**: Single entrance from the secondary passage. No other exits.

- **Special Interactions**: The underside of the granite column has three names carved into it in Draconic — names that do not match any gnomish naming convention. DC 14 Perception to notice the carved surface; reading requires Draconic or a translation.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Cook-fire smoke (carefully ventilated). Dried food. The faint mineral smell of the stone column.

- **Sounds**: Quiet community life. Children. Low conversation. The sound of Bressid moving at night.

### **Additional Notes**
**The cairn's origin:** Lisa found the granite column when she was hiding, in her second month in the Underdark. She stacked river stones around its base and etched nothing on it. She named it Dunbarrow because that was the name she had already decided for her false homeland, and she needed somewhere to put the grief. The grief was real — it is for Kessavan, Drexa, and Pell, the three members of her flight who died in the ambush. Their names are carved in Draconic on the underside of the stone.

**The community's relationship to the cairn:** Survivors from three destroyed svirfneblin enclaves found the cairn first, then each other, then settled because it felt like someone had been there and survived. Bressid's community has begun leaving offerings. They believe it marks something. They are not wrong, but not in any way they could articulate.

**Lisa's visits:** She comes alone every two or three weeks, in the predawn hours, in the shape she was born with. She has not told anyone.

**The Dunbarrow Stone community** is not formally part of Blingdenstone, does not vote, and receives no garrison protection — only irregular food deliveries that Lisa arranged quietly and has never officially acknowledged.

**GM note:** If the PCs find this community before reaching Blingdenstone, Bressid will describe Burrow Warden Grissel as "the one who made sure we didn't starve" without any apparent suspicion that she is anything other than what she appears. The cairn itself is a potential reveal: if a PC examines the underside and reads Draconic, they find three names that do not match any gnomish naming convention.
