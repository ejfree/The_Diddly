# H3 — The Debt Cells

## **The Debt Cells**
A separate wing of Cagehold dedicated to duergar debt-slaves — kept rigorously separated from non-duergar slaves because mixing them causes violent incidents. The Debt Cells are cleaner and better-provisioned than the general slave pens; the duergar treat their own indentured as expensive equipment rather than disposable labor.

Currently holds 43 duergar in various states of indenture: 11 from Clan Thannax (production shortfall), 18 from Clan Murthaen (a disputed contract that has been in Steelcourt arbitration for four months), and 14 from smaller family groups. The Murthaen group is particularly bitter because their case should have been resolved by now; the delay is because Clan Solvrak is profiting from their labor assignment and has been quietly stalling arbitration. Deepmistress Yurra does not know 14 of her clan's people are in the Debt Cells because of Solvrak's interference. If she finds out, the Steelcourt will need to hold an emergency session.

### **Description**
A dedicated wing of Cagehold, cleanly maintained and better-provisioned than the general slave pens. Cells are individual stone rooms with iron doors. The wing is physically separated from non-duergar slave quarters. The separation is enforced rigidly — mixing produces violent incidents.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Torch sconces in the corridor, maintained regularly.

- **Surfaces**: Cut stone, swept floors. Noticeably cleaner than the general pens.

- **Objects**: Iron cell doors. Provision storage at the wing entrance. A Steelcourt registry ledger recording each indenture's terms and assigned labor.

- **Doors & Exits**: One main entrance to the wing, guarded. Individual cell doors are locked.

- **Special Interactions**: The registry ledger contains the official record of assignments — but Clan Murthaen's 18 indentured are recorded with a labor assignment that does not reflect the actual delay caused by Solvrak's interference.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Stone, oil lamps, the muted smell of confined quarters — but without the rot and fear-smell of the general slave pens.

- **Sounds**: Quiet. The Murthaen group in particular has gone silent — bitter waiting rather than protest.
