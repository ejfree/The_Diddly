# Encounter Area A5 — Illithid Spy Post

## **Illithid Spy Post — Active Intelligence Operation**
The westernmost spy post of the illithid colony. The drow know it exists but have not dealt with it due to recent reverses. An active interrogation is underway: the stronger illithid is questioning a bound drow merchant captive near the pool. If the illithids sense the party, the captive will be killed first — he knows too much to be left alive for rescue. The wererats will ambush; the illithids will open with mind blasts.

### **Description**
Uses encounter piece II (reversed). The cave area opens off the main tunnel passage; sparse phosphorescent lichen on the walls means normal vision functions only up to 10 feet — beyond that, darkness. The cave contains a dark pool of still water in one area. An alcove off the pool area serves as the illithids' camp. Opposite the pool, small side caves — tight enough that even a gnome must crawl — house the wererat nest.

A bound drow merchant sits near the pool, wrists tied, bruised but alive. The stronger illithid looms over him, conducting a methodical interrogation about drow clan alliances, power groups, and ongoing feuds between noble houses. The weaker illithid stands further back, ostensibly watching the tunnel approaches. Wererats in rat form act as watchers roughly 60 feet up and down the tunnel from the cave entrance. The remaining wererats are stationed to either side of the passageway, guarding the illithids.

A large boulder blocks the entrance to a nearby nook in the cave wall.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Sparse phosphorescent lichen — normal vision works only up to 10 feet. Beyond that: full darkness. Darkvision/infravision functions normally. The illithids have no light sources; this is deliberate.

- **Surfaces**: Wet stone near the pool; the pool itself is dark and still. The side caves for the wererat nest are very low — gnome-sized or smaller to crawl through.

- **Objects**: Folding cots, small table, two folding stools, utensils, and food hampers in the illithid alcove camp. Two small coffers on the table (see Traps). Iron chest in the blocked nook. Boulder blocking the nook entrance.

- **Doors & Exits**: Open passage. Secret tertiary exit to the northeast — the illithids know its location and will flee through it if the battle turns against their servants. The wererats do not necessarily know this exit.

- **Special Interactions**: The drow prisoner can be rescued — if alive, he is a source of information and potentially a guide or ally deeper in the Underdark. The olive cloak in the wererat nest is not magical but is highly valuable as social camouflage if the party reaches the Vault of the Drow (it was among the merchant's possessions). The iron tube in the chest handle is easily missed — describe it as part of the handle unless players specifically examine it.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Damp stone and stagnant pool water. A faint musky animal smell from the wererat nest caves. Something faintly organic and unpleasant near the illithids — the smell of a mind flayer at work.

- **Sounds**: Near silence, deliberately maintained. The quiet lap of pool water. If the party listens carefully before entering: a low, almost-subsonic hum (the stronger illithid's psionic activity), and occasional stifled sounds from the bound drow.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures**: The bound drow merchant captive — currently helpless, near the pool with the stronger illithid. He will not act unless freed. If freed, he will attempt to escape but may help the party if they give him a clear reason to trust them.

- **Hostile Threats**:

**Wererats (12)** — Four in rat form as tunnel watchers (~60 feet from the cave center, two in each direction). Eight guarding the illithids, four to each side of the passageway. In rat form the watchers are easily overlooked.
hp 17 each; XPV 218 each (2,616 total).
Equipment: drow shortswords +1 each, stored in baldrics/hangers kept to either side of the passage (accessible when they shift to humanoid or hybrid form). Each also carries 30 gp and a gold ring set with a moonstone worth 400 gp — tokens of service to the illithids.

- **Unique NPCs**:

**Illithid (stronger)** — Near the pool, conducting the interrogation. Will kill the drow prisoner at the first opportunity if the party is detected. Flees northwest then through the secret northeast exit if the battle goes badly.
hp 43; XPV 2,316.
Psionic abilities per standard illithid stat block. Opens combat with mind blast.

**Illithid (weaker)** — Surveying the tunnel approaches. Positioned to observe the melee once combat starts; uses psionic domination on any character who approaches it directly. Flees with the stronger illithid when their servants are losing.
hp 38; XPV 2,256.
Psionic abilities per standard illithid stat block. Opens combat with mind blast.

Combat sequence when party is sighted:
1. Wererat watchers rush to warn their masters.
2. All available wererats prepare an ambush.
3. Each illithid delivers an opening mind blast.
4. The weaker illithid surveys the melee, using psionic domination on any PC who moves toward it.
5. The stronger illithid moves back toward the pool, ready to kill the drow prisoner.
6. When the battle turns against their servants, both illithids flee northwest, then exit through the secret tertiary passage to the northeast.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Poisoned needle latches — both small coffers in the illithid camp alcove.

- **Trigger**: Opening either coffer without disarming the latch. The needle is concealed in the latch mechanism.

- **Effect**: Poison needle — save vs. poison per standard rules. Likely incapacitating or lethal depending on system specifics.

- **Disarm**: A thief examining the latch carefully (find/remove traps) can locate and disarm the needle before opening. Wearing thick gloves does not protect against the mechanism.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot**: On each wererat: 30 gp and a gold ring set with a moonstone (worth 400 gp) — 12 rings total across the group. Drow shortswords +1 (12 total, stored on baldrics by the passage wall). Illithid camp gear (cots, table, stools, utensils, food hampers — mundane but recoverable).

- **Hidden Loot**:
  - Wererat nest (small crawl-through caves opposite the pool): nest of animal skin, old cloth, and bones. Under the nest — a dull olive cloak. Not magical, but extremely valuable as social camouflage if the party later reaches the Vault of the Drow (it was among the drow merchant's possessions and will help them pass as legitimate travelers).
  - Blocked nook (boulder must be moved): iron chest. The handle of the chest lid is actually an iron tube. Inside the chest: 300 copper ingots and 300 silver ingots (10 pounds each — used by the illithids as bribes to subvert drow servants). Iron tube in the handle contains: scroll of protection from undead. Hidden in the inner lining of the chest: scroll of protection from demons.

- **Magical Items**:
  - Drow shortswords +1 ×12 (wererats)
  - Coffer 1 (after disarming needle): potion of healing, 200 gp, black sapphire worth 5,000 gp.
  - Coffer 2 (after disarming needle): 150 gp, 58 pp, potion of polymorph self, scarab of death, small silver crescent brooch of the Aleval merchant clan.
  - Iron tube (chest handle): scroll of protection from undead.
  - Chest inner lining: scroll of protection from demons.

### **Additional Notes**

The scarab of death in Coffer 2 is a cursed item — any PC who handles it without identifying it first is at risk. Make sure to describe it matter-of-factly alongside the other contents; it should not be obviously labeled.

The black sapphire in Coffer 1 (5,000 gp) is the single most valuable portable item in this area. The illithids were using it as a high-value bribe reserve.

The silver crescent brooch of the Aleval merchant clan (Coffer 2) connects to House Aleval, which also appears in the nearby drow checkpoint (D-3, Jeggred's brass wand brooch). Players who notice this connection may infer that the drow merchant being interrogated had dealings with House Aleval — or is himself an Aleval agent.

The olive cloak is a sleeper reward. It has no game-mechanical effect here, but flag it for later: when the party reaches the Vault of the Drow, having a legitimate drow merchant's traveling cloak can smooth over an encounter that would otherwise go very badly.

The illithids are gathering intelligence specifically on drow power dynamics — current alliances, feuds, and the fallout from Eclavdra's failure at the fire giant halls. They are not here by accident. The drow know about this post and have chosen to tolerate it for now; how long that tolerance lasts is a DM variable.

The secret northeast exit is the illithids' ace in the hole. If the party does not find it during this encounter, the illithids may reappear deeper in the campaign using information gathered here.
