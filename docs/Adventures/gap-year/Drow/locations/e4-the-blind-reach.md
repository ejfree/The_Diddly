# E4 — The Blind Reach

## **The Blind Reach**
Drow special operations forward post. A sea cave on the western shore of the Sunless Sea, accessible by swimming a submerged entry channel or by a narrow unmarked ledge path from E1's eastern edge. Population: 7 drow (6 operatives + 1 commander), 1 kuo-toa guide. No light visible from the water. No sound escapes. House Veythrae has been using it for three weeks as a forward staging post. It does not appear in G1's patrol coordination records, any official Xil documentation, or the Zhaelor inquisitors' awareness. This is intentional.

### **Description**
A natural sea cave system — three chambers, the innermost sitting 30 feet above the waterline and dry for centuries. The entry channel is twelve feet underwater and twenty feet long: manageable for a prepared swimmer, lethal in the dark without a contained light source.

Chamber 1 (flooded antechamber): Six feet of water, entry channel at the far end. Pliprik has marked the channel's ceiling with a scrape of bioluminescent lichen. The Shroud uses this chamber to stage supply drops — watertight containers with rations and equipment.

Chamber 2 (transition chamber): A sloped floor rising out of the water, 15 feet of dry stone, low ceiling. The operatives sleep here in rotation. Simple camp: bedrolls, a shielded fire-bowl venting through a crack above, the arcanist's work surface (a flat-topped rock covered in wax-tablet notes and two small resonance instruments).

Chamber 3 (upper chamber): Reached by a tight chimney climb, 20 feet. Dry, cool, 40 feet across. Velith's workspace and sleeping quarters (four hours at a time, twice a day). A carved stone shelf along the far wall — drow work done since arrival — holds six sealed message cylinders, a detailed map of the E-sector shoreline, and Velith's personal weapons rack. From the upper chamber's northern window — a natural crack in the stone — the entrance to the Drowned Vault is visible as a faint double-spire rock formation across 200 feet of dark water.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: No light visible from the water. The bioluminescent lichen mark on the entry channel ceiling is the only deliberate light source in the lower chambers. Chamber 3 has a shielded lamp Velith uses for map work.

- **Surfaces**: Chamber 1: six feet of cold water, entry channel at depth. Chamber 2: sloped wet-to-dry stone floor, low ceiling. Chamber 3: dry stone, carved shelf, chimney access.

- **Objects**: Watertight supply containers (Chamber 1). Bedrolls, shielded fire-bowl, arcanist's wax-tablet notes, two resonance instruments (Chamber 2). Six sealed message cylinders, E-sector shoreline map, weapons rack (Chamber 3). The ledge path from E1's eastern edge: requires DC 18 Perception to find from outside, or a guide who knows it.

- **Doors & Exits**: Submerged entry channel (Chamber 1). Chimney climb between Chambers 2 and 3. The ledge path to E1. No other exits.

- **Special Interactions**: The arcanist's wax-tablet notes in Chamber 2 describe the Drowned Vault's resonance key clearly enough that a high-Arcana PC can attempt to open the vault door. The sealed message cylinders in Chamber 3 are Velith's reports to Vardeth — unopened, awaiting the runner who has not returned.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Salt and cold water in Chamber 1. Damp stone and fire-bowl smoke in Chamber 2. Dry stone and lamp oil in Chamber 3.

- **Sounds**: Water movement in Chamber 1. The chimney between chambers creates faint air movement audible in both. From Chamber 3's northern window: the faint sound of the Sunless Sea and, every four days approximately, something surfacing near the vault entrance.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures**: Pliprik (kuo-toa guide, Chamber 1 or nearby water). The unidentified thing in the water — surfaces near the vault entrance approximately every four days, last surfacing two days ago.

- **Hostile Threats**: The Shroud operatives if the encounter goes wrong. Four drow fighters (Vesketh, Rhovar, Illyn, Serrath-Tane), Lvl 5, dual-blade, optimized for close-quarters cave combat. Bored and careful, which is a dangerous combination. They will not start a fight. They will finish one very efficiently.

- **Unique NPCs**:

  **Velith Veythrae — Commander** (Lvl 8 Gloom Stalker Ranger). Female, early middle age by drow reckoning, built for sustained low-light operations. Has run three previous Shroud operations. Current state: frustrated, controlled. Found the Drowned Vault in week one; the arcanist cannot open the inner door; the runner sent for a specific archive text two weeks ago has not returned. Giving it three more days before attempting forced entry, which the arcanist says will likely destroy the chest's contents. She is considering whether she trusts that assessment. Does not bluff. Does not threaten unless she has already decided to act. Will offer a clean transaction if the PCs seem useful. Will honor any deal she makes. Will not explain the mission beyond "recovery work."

  What she knows: the vault contains something old; the matriarch wants it specifically; the matriarch described it as "a key that was mislaid before the houses were formed." Velith has not been told what it unlocks. She has not asked.

  What she doesn't know: the runner was intercepted — not by Zhaelor, not by another house, but by something that has been watching the E-sector since the Shroud arrived. She suspects the runner is dead. She is not yet acting on that suspicion.

  **Dethrix vel Veythrae — The Arcanist** (Lvl 6 Divination Wizard). Male, young for the mission's stakes, visibly nervous in a way he is trying to conceal behind technical language. Specializes in structural resonance analysis. Has never been on a field operation of this type before. Conclusions from his first week of analysis: the vault door predates current deep gnome stonework conventions, was designed to respond to a specific resonance frequency rather than a physical key, and the text that would specify that frequency is what Velith sent the runner to retrieve. Without the text, brute-forcing the resonance has a high probability of destroying the chest's contents — and possibly him in an enclosed space.

  Will talk to anyone who seems technically knowledgeable, more than Velith would want. Not disloyal — genuinely interested in the vault and unable to maintain operational silence when someone asks the right question. Knows things Velith doesn't: the vault's design matches pre-Xil construction, older than any current drow house, and the resonance signature is consistent with deep gnome workmanship deliberately masked. Whatever built the vault wanted it to look more recent than it is.

  Also knows something he hasn't told Velith: the thing in the water has been responding to his resonance tests, not randomly but in a pattern.

  **Pliprik — The Guide** (kuo-toa, not from the main E3 settlement). From a smaller outlying family group on the western shore, maintaining deliberate distance from High Servant Kloarek's religious authority. Hired before the Shroud entered the E-sector, paid in surface-forged silver. Knows every submerged channel in the western shore. Terrified of Velith. Has not been mistreated. Will continue cooperating until the job is done, then return to his family group and not speak of this. If the PCs speak kuo-toa or approach him without the Shroud present, he will confirm the drow are looking for something in a drowned cave and cannot open a door. Nothing else. He is done being in the middle of things bigger than him.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Submerged entry channel — environmental

- **Trigger**: Attempting to enter or exit without a light source

- **Effect**: The channel is twelve feet underwater and twenty feet long. Without a contained light source in total darkness, orientation is lost. Lethal without preparation.

- **Disarm**: A contained light source and a guide (Pliprik, or the bioluminescent lichen mark he placed) make the crossing manageable.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot**: The supply containers in Chamber 1 (rations, basic equipment). The arcanist's wax-tablet notes (intelligence value regarding the vault).

- **Hidden Loot**: The six sealed message cylinders (Velith's reports to Vardeth — intelligence value regarding House Veythrae's operation and the matriarch's interest in the vault).

- **Magical Items**: The two resonance instruments in Chamber 2 — specialized arcanist's tools, not combat items, but potentially useful for vault work or identifying resonance-keyed stonework.

### **Additional Notes**
**The Unit — The Shroud:** Not a standard patrol or house guard detail. A dedicated retrieval cell, seven specialists assembled and deployed directly by Veythrae's senior weaponmaster Vardeth vel Veythrae, under the authority of the matriarch and no one else. No house insignia. Unmarked equipment, un-house-stamped rations, untraceable weapons. If found and killed, Veythrae will deny them. They know this. They accepted the terms.

**What the Shroud has observed:** Three weeks of quiet observation:

- Kuo-toa patrol routes around the western shore (complete, accurate to the day)
- The location of Shlipik's secondary contact — a lower-ranked whip named Thrissik who works the night dock and handles Veythrae cargo shipments (not Shlipik himself; Shlipik does not know about Thrissik)
- Something moving in the deep water that is not the eel, is not any kuo-toa creature they recognize, and surfaces briefly near the vault entrance approximately every four days
- A drow runner who passed through E1 six days ago moving toward the G-sector, not from it — someone heading away from Xil through the main approach, which is wrong

The last entry may be relevant to the PCs depending on their timeline.

**Adventure hooks:**

If the PCs can open the vault door: Velith will offer safe E-sector passage and the kuo-toa patrol intelligence in exchange. She will not tell them what is in the chest. She will attempt to retrieve it before they can examine it closely.

If the PCs have Serath's intelligence from G4: they know the Shroud exists before they find it. They know the manifests connect to E-sector. They do not know the vault. Arriving with that context changes what Velith is willing to offer — she realizes her operation has a leak.

If the PCs reach the vault first: they find the arcanist's work notes cached in the outer chamber. The notes describe the resonance key clearly enough that a high-Arcana PC can attempt the opening. Velith will arrive within 20 minutes of any disturbance at the vault — she has been watching the formation.

The thing in the water: whatever surfaces near the vault every four days has been responding to the arcanist's resonance tests in a pattern. Dethrix has not told Velith this yet.

**GM Notes:**

- The Shroud is not a combat encounter unless the PCs force it. Velith's default is observe-assess-transact. What she cannot afford is exposure.
- The sealed chest — what the matriarch called "a key that was mislaid" — is for the GM to define. Possible directions: a resonance instrument that can read or disrupt the speaking-stone prophecy system, a pre-house artifact proving a disputed claim about Xil's founding, something genuinely dangerous that no current house should have.
- The missing runner is a thread. If the PCs investigate, they find no body — the runner was taken, not killed. What took them is the thing in the water.
- Pliprik, if treated decently, will tell the PCs the ledge path back to E1 without alerting the Shroud. He genuinely does not care who gets the chest as long as he gets to go home.
