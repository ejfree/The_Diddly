# I3 — The Whispering Shelf

## **The Whispering Shelf**
Smaller Outpost 3. ~2.5 hours north of Blingdenstone, above the underground river that runs between D1 and D2. Population: 4 gnomes. A natural stone shelf overlooking a 60-foot-wide underground river — the same waterway the kuo-toa use for transit between their settlements. The four gnomes here do nothing but watch and listen: river traffic, raft movements, anything unusual surfacing from the kuo-toa direction.

### **Description**
A natural stone shelf set high above a 60-foot-wide underground river. The shelf has exceptional acoustic properties — sound carries oddly across the water, and voices on the shelf travel parallel to the stone rather than downward. The gnomes here maintain total sound discipline, communicating only in hand-signals.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: No artificial light. The river surface carries any ambient luminescence.

- **Surfaces**: Smooth stone shelf, naturally formed. The edge is ungated — a fall from the shelf into the river would be serious.

- **Objects**: Watch bedrolls. Signal equipment (stone-tap relay). Hand-signal reference charts carved into the shelf surface.

- **Doors & Exits**: Access via a narrow side passage from the main tunnel system above.

- **Special Interactions**: The shelf's acoustic properties make it ideal for surveillance but dangerous for speech — voices carry unpredictably across the water. The gnomes use no spoken language while on station.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Cold water, wet stone. Faint mineral smell from the river.

- **Sounds**: River current. Kuo-toa raft movement (if present). Low, rhythmic sounds from below that the Shelf gnomes have logged but cannot classify — not mechanical, not any known creature.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Acoustic exposure — environmental

- **Trigger**: Speaking aloud on the shelf

- **Effect**: Voices carry downstream; anything said at normal volume may be audible to creatures on the river surface up to 200 feet away.

- **Disarm**: Maintain hand-signal discipline. The gnomes here have done this successfully for months.

### **Additional Notes**
Lisa established this station after Fragment Four of the speaking-stone prophecies made her reconsider every approach vector, not just the drow ones. The kuo-toa are nominally neutral — but their god-calling rituals have been intensifying, and something is moving in the water below. The Shelf gnomes have not yet identified what.

The unclassified sounds — low, rhythmic, not mechanical — have been logged for three weeks. Lisa's working theory is something large is using the river channel below the kuo-toa pier. She has not shared this with the Shelf gnomes or with anyone in Blingdenstone.
