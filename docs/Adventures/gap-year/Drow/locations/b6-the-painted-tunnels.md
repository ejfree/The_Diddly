# B6 — The Painted Tunnels

## **The Painted Tunnels**
Grimlock settlement and cultural site. A 300-foot stretch of worked and natural tunnel in the deep grimlock range, branching from the main B-sector passage via a wide archway framed with stacked stones. Population: ~35 grimlocks (permanent residents), plus transient workers from the Eyeless Village (B2). The Painted Tunnels are the oldest continuous site in the grimlock range — predating Elder Vrakt's community by at least four generations, and predating any drow presence in this sector by longer than the grimlocks can accurately track. The paintings are not decoration. They are record, memory, law, and genealogy. Elder Vrakt defers to this community on matters of tribal memory. What they record becomes true. What they omit becomes forgotten.

### **Description**
The Archway: a natural tunnel mouth widened and framed with stacked and mortared stone — the only worked masonry in the B-sector. The framing stones are themselves painted, with a repeating border motif translating roughly as "here the record is kept."

The Main Gallery: 300 feet of tunnel with both walls, the ceiling, and sections of the floor covered in layered pigment going back generations. Oldest layers near the archway (faded, partially obscured by newer work); newest work concentrated at the far end. The bioluminescent paste used in the most recent century of paintings glows faint blue-green in total darkness — the gallery is dimly visible to darkvision without any external light source.

The Work Alcoves: four short side-passages, 15–20 feet deep, where active painting work is conducted. Pigment stores, bone scrapers, moss-padded kneeling surfaces.

The Reading Chamber: a roughly circular cavern at the far end, ~40 feet across. The floor is smooth — generations of feet have worn it flat. A low stone platform in the center is the seat of the chief interpreter. The walls have been inscribed and re-inscribed so many times that the pigment layers have built up a measurable physical texture, readable entirely by touch.

The Settlement Proper: behind the reading chamber, a short natural passage opens into small caves used as living quarters. Sleeping niches, communal eating areas, food cache.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: No external light source needed in the main gallery — bioluminescent paste from the most recent century of work glows faint blue-green. The work alcoves and reading chamber are darker. The settlement proper is completely dark.

- **Surfaces**: Gallery walls and ceiling: layers of pigment, the most recent of which has measurable physical texture. Work alcove floors: moss-padded kneeling surfaces. Reading chamber floor: worn smooth by foot traffic over generations.

- **Objects**: Pigment stores in the work alcoves (mineral grounds, dried fungi, small bladders of bioluminescent extract). Bone scrapers. The stone platform in the reading chamber. The new addition at the far end of the main gallery — two weeks old, still tacky in places.

- **Doors & Exits**: The archway entrance from the B-sector passage. No other exits. The settlement quarters are accessible through the reading chamber.

- **Special Interactions**: The new addition at the gallery's far end: eight tall figures moving toward the archway. One is circled. The circled figure has a secondary mark beneath the circle that does not appear elsewhere in the record. Grel inscribed this section herself despite her tremor. She will not discuss it with outsiders.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Mineral dust and something organic and sweet — the bioluminescent paste, made from a fungal extract harvested from C3. In the work alcoves the smell is strongest.

- **Sounds**: Near-silence. The painters work in total darkness by touch and spatial memory; the only sounds are the soft scrape of bone tools on stone and the occasional low vocalization from Grel narrating the record. Any foreign footstep is immediately audible — Grel registers arrivals before they reach the archway.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures**:

- **Hostile Threats**: The settlement's protected status is informal but real — outsiders who have damaged the paintings in the past have subsequently had accidents in B-sector that the grimlocks did not discourage.

- **Unique NPCs**:

  **Grel (no other name)** — The Master Inscriber. Oldest painter, female, age indeterminate. May be the oldest individual in the B-sector range. Sits at the reading chamber's central platform most of each day, running her fingers across old inscriptions and narrating them in the grimlock tactile-vocalization language. Does not inscribe new content herself anymore — her hands tremor. She dictates; the younger painters execute precisely. She is aware of the party before they enter the archway. She will not react overtly. She will wait.

  **The Senior Painters (x3): Dusk, Notch, Pallor** — Names assigned by community convention (Dusk has darker skin than average; Notch lost part of an ear; Pallor has albino coloring rare even among grimlocks). All three are skilled, fast, and completely uninterested in anything outside the work. They will stop and assess rather than attack. They understand the Painted Tunnels' protected status.

  **The Junior Painters (x4)** — Younger workers in their first decade of inscription practice. More reactive, less controlled. Most likely to raise an alarm if the party behaves strangely.

  **Support Workers (~20)** — Pigment grinders, surface preparers, supply runners to/from B2, food handlers, children being trained in the tactile reading tradition. Not combatants. Will scatter to the living quarters if a fight begins.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Damaged paintings — social/faction consequence

- **Trigger**: Any physical damage to the painted surfaces

- **Effect**: Grel will do nothing visible in the moment. The three senior painters will not be in the gallery when the party leaves. B-sector will become significantly more hostile — the grimlocks will not announce this or explain it.

- **Disarm**: Do not damage the paintings.

### **Additional Notes**
**The Record — periods:**

*The Old Record (archway to midpoint):* Pre-drow. Hunting scenes, territorial maps of a much larger grimlock range than currently exists, genealogical webs. Evidence of contact with svirfneblin (depicted as trading partners, "exchange without threat"). Evidence of contact with something else — a recurring figure larger than any humanoid type, depicted only in profile, always moving in the direction of the deep.

*The Middle Record (midpoint to three-quarters):* The arrival of the drow. Depicted without drama — they simply appear, large elegant figures with the vertical "cannot-be-killed" marker. The grimlock range shrinks across subsequent panels. Trade with the svirfneblin stops abruptly and does not resume. The large deep-moving figure disappears from the record at approximately the same period.

*The Recent Record (three-quarters to the far end):* The current era. Drow figures appear with house-symbol variants — Grel can distinguish at least six house-marks. The grimlock community's relationship to each house is noted in a coding system of line weights and adjacency patterns. House Dravun: "tolerated exchange." House Zhaelor: "unpredictable — distance maintained."

*The New Addition:* At the very end of the main gallery, still tacky in places — two weeks old. Eight tall figures moving toward the archway (toward the surface). One is circled. The circled figure has a secondary mark beneath the circle that does not appear elsewhere in the record. Grel inscribed this section herself, overriding the tremor through concentration. She will not discuss it with outsiders or with Elder Vrakt, though he has asked twice.

**Encounter dynamics:**

Entering without light: painters register the party acoustically but will not immediately react. Grel will speak — a single low vocalization — and work continues. PCs who move carefully and make no aggressive gesture will be tolerated.

Entering with a light source: activity pauses. Every grimlock in the gallery orients toward the light. Not hostility — involuntary attention. Junior painters back toward the alcoves. Senior painters hold position. Grel does not move.

Approaching Grel: She will not turn toward the party. She will speak in Undercommon if she detects the party has it. First words, if she speaks: *"You are not from below. You are going below. Sit."* This is not a command. It is an offer.

**What Grel can offer:**
- A reading of relevant record portions — she can identify which drow houses are currently active in the B–D corridor by their recent appearance in the record.
- The deep-moving figure from the Old Record. She knows more about it than she will volunteer. If pressed specifically about the thing in the water at E-sector, she will go still and then say: *"It is in the record. It was always going to come back."*
- The new addition's secondary mark — if asked specifically about the symbol under the circle, she will touch it and say it means "carries two names." She will not say whose.

**What Grel will not do:** Explain the new addition. Show anyone the reading chamber without an escort. Permit the paintings to be damaged.

**GM Notes:**
- The "large figure moving deep" in the Old Record is the GM's to define. Potential connections: an ancient creature connected to the Drowned Vault (E2), the thing in the water responding to Dethrix's resonance tests (E4), or something older that the speaking-stone prophecies at Blingdenstone also reference.
- Grel's phrase "carries two names" — if the PCs have any context about Lisa/Grissel, this lands differently. Grel has not seen Lisa. She is reading from the record. The symbol predates Lisa's arrival by centuries.
- The Dravun patrol sergeant who buys from the Blind Market (B4) appears in the recent record with a Dravun house marker and an additional notation Grel calls "operates outside her house's knowledge." She inscribed this from observation, not from being told.
- The supply runner relationship with B2 means Grel has current intelligence on the Eyeless Village — including Vrakt's state of mind — that she will share selectively.

**Relationship to B2:** The Painted Tunnels community is not under Vrakt's authority — it predates his position and operates by a different legitimacy. He consults them; he does not command them. Supply runs from the Eyeless Village bring food and raw materials. Grel and Vrakt have a complicated relationship — contemporaries, both old enough that neither remembers when the other was not a community figure. Vrakt's accommodation with the drow is recorded in the recent panels in terms that are observational but not flattering. He has not demanded it be changed, because the record cannot be changed — only added to.
