## D2 Chapters 9–10 — The Shrine of the Kuo-Toans

The route from the Grand Cavern (Chapter 8) to the Kuo-Toan shrine passes through two set encounters before reaching the shrine cavern itself. Where the main passage nears the shrine, phosphorescent lichen grows thickly on walls and ceiling, giving a dim but constant light. Monsters act with intelligence appropriate to their kind; the kuo-toans in particular have a highly structured society and react accordingly.

## Chapter 9 — The Journey to the Shrine

### Encounter Area W-27 — Svartjet River Crossing

**Encounter piece IV.** A broad, very smooth river channel over 80 feet deep. On the far bank, moored in a cove, is an 8' × 14' barge with a sculling oar.

The barge is operated by **Thoopshib**, a rogue kuo-toan monitor of great size — extraordinary Dexterity and Strength. He is chaotic and somewhat insane, but very sly. He attacks relentlessly, always to kill. Normally, anyone acquainted with his service will whistle or call, pay **1 pp per person**, and be ferried across. He does not care who or what he transports.

**Crossing mechanics:**
- Thoopshib sculls across in **6 rounds**, mooring cleanly on the opposite bank.
- Other scullers take **12 rounds**, drift diagonally downstream, and cannot moor — the barge is carried off downstream by current.
- If forced to ferry, Thoopshib leaps into the river midstream, taking the sculling oar with him to summon Koronnah.
- **Barge carried downstream at 9 mph.** 70% chance of grounding at hex B2-24; otherwise continues at ~5 mph all the way to the Sunless Sea. Travel upriver is impossible. Walking along the riverside is not possible.

**If the barge is threatened or attacked:** Thoopshib leaps into the Svartjet and summons Koronnah, his giant gar companion (30+ feet long, stays just upstream).
- Each round Koronnah is urged on by Thoopshib: **Athletics DC 18** for barge occupants each round or the barge is destabilized (all passengers make DC 15 Reflex or fall in).
- Koronnah's bite: automatically targets any creature in the water; 25% chance to target creatures walking on water; 10% chance to leap and target levitating/flying creatures up to 20 feet above the surface.

**Communication:** Thoopshib offers his service in Undercommon. Without magical translation, the party cannot understand him. Each time he repeats the offer (ununderstood): cumulative Diplomacy DC 10 check or he becomes hostile (starts at a +10 to the DC; DM tracks escalation). River noise masks normal sounds, but bright light will draw his attention.

### Encounter Area A2-31 — Svirfnebli Scout Band

**Encounter piece V.** Multiple passage openings; area faintly lit by glowing lichens and phosphorescent mineral streaks. The party will see no creatures and hear no noise — but eight **svirfnebli** are watching from hiding (Stealth +14).

**Composition:** 5 × Lvl 3 deep gnome fighters, 2 × Lvl 4 fighters, 1 × Lvl 5 leader (**Trosli Garnetgetter**).

If the party stops and searches (or calls out in friendly terms), Trosli shows himself and offers the peace sign — he recognizes the party as surface-worlders. He converses in sign language, or normally if magical communication is available.

**Alliance terms:** If offered not fewer than **one 100 gp gem per deep gnome** and "halfies" on any gems taken, the svirfnebli will accompany the party to the shrine cavern. They hate the kuo-toa as much as they despise the drow, and have been spying on the shrine. They know the passages well and have small secret passages allowing them to observe the shrine from high walls or above.

### Area 1 — Entrance to the Low Cavity

Greenish phosphorescence from lichens, plus grayish luminosity from man's-fist-sized slug-like creatures crawling everywhere (walls, ceilings, floors) give the area an undersea appearance. A strange salt tang is in the air. Directly north: a huge dark green stone idol, like a giant lobster-headed woman with one pincer raised and the right extended ahead and open (detailed at area 4). Walls and pavement are well-made but very worn — old, alien, foreboding.

Looking left (west) reveals an archway; looking right (east) reveals a 20-foot-wide, 40-foot-high opening. Stairs lead up to the west; slits pierce the east wall of the entranceway. The bend of the eastern corridor is visible.

**At this point, intelligent observers ignore the party.** Traffic here is not uncommon; entrants are permitted to approach the shrine, make obeisance to the Sea Mother, obtain the required passes, and move on (see area 3). The shrine community is organized only with respect to its guards and hierarchy, not its pilgrims or passersby.

### Area 2 — Ramp and Stairs

The ramp leads to broad steps descending into the pool surrounding the idol. From here the arena seating is visible — capacity approximately **2,000 creatures of human size.** The pool's depth cannot be determined by sight (translucent quality). The 20-foot-tall idol atop the dais is stone. An opening in the northern wall of the place can be made out.

**The pool:** Filled with leeches everywhere except the direct route to area 3 (the steps to the first tier of the ziggurat). Any warm-blooded creature straying off the path is attacked at **1–4 leech strikes per 10 feet traveled.** Kuo-toans pass unmolested.

- **Leech (each):** Lvl −1; AC 10; HP 4; Fort +3, Ref +5, Will +0; Speed swim 15 ft; Melee bite +3, 1 piercing + **blood drain** (attached; deals 1 piercing damage per round automatically until removed; Athletics DC 12 to remove); XP 10 each

### Area 3 — Steps of the Ziggurat

All creatures passing through must ascend these steps to pay homage to the Goddess, make an offering, and receive a pass. Groups arriving from other tunnels are held in waiting areas while representatives perform the required obeisance.

**First Altar** (white stone, green splotches; 2'×2'×4'): Two basin-sized sea shells. Left: 337 gp. Right: fresh water and snail shells. *Pay 1 gp per person (not slaves/lowly servants), take a snail shell pass, and present to guards.*

**Second Altar** (blue stone, twice as large): Two beautiful shells with silver chasing. Left: brackish water and crab claws. Right: **71 pp and 18 gems (10 gp each).** Worshippers of the kuo-toan goddess pay here for a crab claw pass, which grants friendly treatment and escort for 1 mile. Requires the secret sign/countersign of Sea Mother's worshippers (fore- and middle fingers raised as a "V", mirrored from the left with fingers pointing ahead), plus utterance of her true name: **Blibdoolpoolp** (pronounced *Blib-doool-pooolp*).

**Third Altar** (green stone, 3'×3'×6'): Two gold-inlayed shells, mother of pearl insides. Left: salt water and live lobsters. Right: **24 white pearls (100 gp each) and 16 black pearls (500 gp each).** Supplicants for a special favor take a lobster to the pool at area 15 and place it prayerfully there — any other use is sacrilege (full alarm and attack). Approach to this tier must be by the **north ramp only**; any other approach is seen as defilement.

### Area 4 — Idol of Blibdoolpoolp, Sea Mother

A **20-foot malachite statue** atop the ziggurat summit: nude human female body, articulated shell covering the shoulders, lobster head and claws in place of the expected human head and arms. Right claw open and raised; left open and held out about 8 feet above the tier floor.

**The idol will not move or come to life**, but grasping the extended left claw while standing upon the altar and pronouncing her name correctly triggers a **plane shift** to Blibdoolpoolp's court on the Elemental Plane of Water (Will DC 25 to resist if unwilling; willing creatures are transported automatically).

**Audience with Blibdoolpoolp:**
- Must offer **10,000–60,000 gp in pearls**, or double that in other gems.
- She will grant a small favor and return the individual to the altar.
- Those without offerings are *geased* (7th-rank divine; Will DC 28) not to harm or aid in harming any of her worshippers, and must contribute **60,000 gp in gems** to the shrine (or deliver drow whose combined levels total 600 — she hates the dark elves but cannot oppose Lolth directly). The individual is then returned, able to speak Kuo-Toan, and secretly marked so all kuo-toans recognize them as in her service.

*Note: From the upper tier of the ziggurat, the guards at area 5 can be distinguished.*

### Area 10 — Meditation Rooms

Provided for pilgrims and shrine inhabitants. Each room: four stone tables, two stone benches, eight bone stools. Each holds **27 scrolls and 18 books** (worth 1,000 gp and 2,500 gp respectively if sold to a book dealer or religious college on the surface).

Both rooms contain a tract-like sheet with writings in **Elven and Kuo-Toan**. The latter is a translation of an account of an elf who escaped from the drow homeland. The fragment reads (in part):

> *"…and these degenerate filth continue to consort with all manner of foul things and d— (smear destroys several words) but this does not mean that they are particularly watchful for trespassers. Any wearing a cloak of sickening (another smear here) can move freely about, and the merchant clans and noble houses employ all sorts of servants and slaves who roam through the black and debauched City of (smear) —ng the brooches. Most of these abominations worship the disgusting creature they call Mother of Lusts, and if I could only…"*

The kuo-toans use this fragment to emphasize the badness of the drow who do not worship Sea Mother.

Passage and chamber ceiling ~7 feet. Only the priest-prince knows of this place — a secret passed from ruler to ruler. **The accumulated wealth of the shrine**, from decades of contribution, sacrifice, taxes, and trade. Seven large chests (~3' high × 3.5' deep × 4.5' long).

**Chest #1** (bronze, DC 24 lock, DC 28 Thievery): Filled with **8,124 shells** (precious to kuo-toans, otherwise valueless). **Chest #2** (heavy wood and iron, DC 22 lock): **48,912 gp.**

**Chest #3** (iron, secret catch lock — DC 28 Thievery to find the latch): **2,440 pp** and a great silk altar cloth wrapped around three gold service pieces set with pearls (**60,000 gp for the set, or 36,000 gp for the pearls alone**).

**Chest #4** (bronze and iron, two iron bars on back side; DC 22 lock): When the lid is unlocked and lifted, the iron bars trigger a **10' wide × 20' long trapdoor** that swings down (Perception DC 26 to notice mechanism before opening; Reflex DC 24 to avoid). Falls 70 feet; last 10 feet are water (reduces fall damage to 1d6 bludgeoning, but Armored victims begin drowning in 2 rounds unless they have *water breathing* or can climb/fly out). Chest holds: **5,320 sp, 2,100 ep**, 12 *potions of water breathing* (Lvl 3; 1 hour duration), a *helm of underwater action* (Lvl 7; grants water breathing, low-light vision, and +10 ft swim Speed), and *gloves of swimming and climbing* (Lvl 7; +4 item bonus to Athletics for swimming and climbing).

**Chest #5** (iron-bound wooden trunk, unlocked): **100 copper ingots (10 lbs each)**, atop which is a coral coffer (1,000 gp) containing **188 gems (10 gp each)**. The twenty ingots at the bottom are **platinum with copper plating** (25 lbs each) — not apparent unless most ingots are lifted out (Perception DC 20 to notice unusual weight; Society DC 22 to identify the plating).

**Chest #6** (locked bronze; DC 22 Thievery): Trapped identically to #1 (moving seals the room). Contents: **12 pieces of jewelry (500 gp each)**, a bone tube containing a scroll of 3 divine spells (*control water* Rank 5, *true seeing* Rank 6, *restoration* Rank 4), and an **icon of Blibdoolpoolp** set with 10 white pearls (100 gp each). Sea Mother will be angry at those looting her servant's treasure room.

**Chest #7** (locked, plated silver over iron — worth 3,000 gp; all seams sealed with silver solder; DC 26 Thievery to open without breaking the seal): Inside: a **jeweled medallion (25,000 gp; 15,000 gp for gems alone)** and a **Specter** trapped inside by the silver solder. Breaking or opening the chest releases it; it attacks the first creature it sees.

**Specter** — Lvl 7; **AC** 23; **HP** 105; **Fort** +13, **Ref** +16, **Will** +15; Speed fly 40 ft; Resistances cold 5, electricity 5; **Immunities** death effects, disease, paralyzed, poison, precision, unconscious; **Weakness** positive 10

### Area 35 — Locked Room
