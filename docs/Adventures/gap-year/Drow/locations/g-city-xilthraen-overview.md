## Xilthraen — The War City

A forward Underdark city built and maintained for one purpose: projecting force toward the surface. Smaller than Menzoberranzan but far more militarized — less decadence, more discipline.

## Scale

- **Drow population:** 8,000–12,000
- **Non-drow residents:** 3,000–6,000 (thralls, mercenaries, slaves — duergar, goblinoids, quaggoths, grimlocks, captured surface races)
- **Total:** ~12,000–18,000

## Drow Demographic Breakdown

| Segment | % | Count |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Noble houses & retainers | ~10% | 800–1,200 |
| Priestesses of Lolth & clergy | ~5% | 400–600 |
| Professional soldiers & raiders | ~25% | 2,000–3,000 |
| Mage cadre (battle-wizards, students) | ~5–8% | 400–1,000 |
| Artisans, fungus farmers, laborers | ~40% | 3,200–4,800 |
| Criminals, houseless, underclass | ~10% | 800–1,200 |

## Military Standing Force

Two decades of stockpiling for war make the military disproportionately large relative to population:

- **Standing drow soldiers:** 2,500–3,000
- **Slave/auxiliary troops:** 1,500–2,000
- **Reserve (militia, half-trained commoners):** ~2,000 if mobilized
- **Immediate war band:** ~4,000–5,000 deployable; more if the city empties itself

## City Districts

Based on the D3: Vault of the Drow map layout, re-skinned for Eberron.

**1. The Noble Plateau**
The two major houses (Xilrath and Veythrae) occupy massive stalactite/column complexes with hanging bridges. The four minor houses cluster slightly lower in elevation — a constant reminder of rank. Barracks and weapons forges are integrated into noble estates.

**2. The Lower Warrens**
Dense cavern neighborhoods for artisans, soldiers' families, and laborers. House Thalmyr maintains hidden bolt-holes and passageways throughout.

**3. The Slave Pens**
Expanded well beyond standard norms. Quaggoths, goblins, kobolds, duergar, and captured surface folk are kept in regimented pens. House Dravun's beast pits connect here.

**4. The Great Market**
Dominated by House Velouryn. Exotic poisons, Veythrae-enchanted weapons, slaves, fungus-based food, surface contraband. Back-channel deals with duergar and Khyber smugglers happen here.

**5. The Temple-Fortress of Lolth**
The centerpiece — carved into a stalagmite cluster, bridges spanning to stalactites above. Serves as holy site, war council hall, and reliquary. The Council of Houses meets in the obsidian chamber within. Spider motifs, hanging sacrifice cages, obsidian altar.

**6. The War Tunnels**
A fortified network of caverns leading upward toward the surface. Guard towers, ambush chambers, and choke points. The staging ground for surface assaults; soldiers bivouac and drill here constantly.

**7. The Academy of Shadowfire**
House Veythrae-controlled equivalent of Sorcere. Half wizard-academy, half war laboratory. Demonic binding pits, shadowforges, testing grounds for experimental spells. Apprentices serve as expendable shock mages.

## Tone

- Less decadence, more discipline. Streets are patrolled, curfews exist, soldiers drill openly.
- The surface war looms over every district. Food stockpiles, war banners, priests preaching conquest.
- Paranoia. Houses plot constantly, but none dare sabotage the war effort — yet.

## How the PCs Arrive Here

Xilthraen is the D3 endpoint of the giant-chain arc. The PCs enter after descending through:

- G1 (Hill Giants) → G2 (Frost Giants) → G3 (Fire Giants)
- D1 (troglodyte-held Underdark entry) → D2 (kuo-toa warrens)
- D3: Xilthraen

Their arrival fulfills the third step of the prophecy's descent into the Spiral of Despair. See [council-of-houses.md](council-of-houses.md) for the full arc breakdown and political map of the city.
