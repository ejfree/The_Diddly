# E1 — The Shore of Black Sand

## **The Shore of Black Sand**
The Slave Road terminates at a wide crescent beach of black volcanic sand at the edge of the Sunless Sea. What greets the PCs is not a beach — it is a functioning port town, roughly 400 inhabitants, built from the collision of two cultures that need each other and trust each other not at all.

### **Description**
The beach divides naturally by use and by faction:

**Southwest — The Drow Quay.** A permanent drow installation: stone-cut dock structures, iron mooring rings, a fortified harbormaster's post flying House Xilrath colors. Three to five slave barges are in berth at any given time, off-loading surface captives or taking on cargo for the return run. The quay runs under a permanent torchlight regime — the drow do not share the kuo-toa's comfort in total dark. A garrison of 12 drow soldiers (Xilrath rotation), 2 drow harbormasters, and a shifting population of house agents, merchants, and slave handlers occupy the structures cut into the southern cliff. The Quay has its own gate that can be barred against the rest of the beach. It is barred at night.

**Northeast — The Kuo-Toa Fishery.** A sprawling tangle of racks, nets, weirs, drying platforms, and low mud-brick structures that smell powerfully of fish and brine. The kuo-toa population here (roughly 120 permanent residents, plus transients) operates a significant fishery supplying the main settlement across the water. They maintain three shrines to Blibdoolpoolp along the waterline, each attended by a junior priest who blesses each catch. The eastern dock is entirely kuo-toa — outsiders permitted only by invitation or escort.

**The Middle — The Neutral Strand.** Roughly 200 feet of beach between the two installations where neither side claims authority. In practice this is where everything interesting happens: a chaotic market of mixed traders (grimlock labor-brokers, a small derro alchemical stall, surface-world contraband moving in both directions), two rival tavern-tents serving something that is technically alcohol, and a semicircle of permanent stalls run by a family of deep gnome traders who arrived three years ago and have somehow made themselves indispensable to both sides by being aggressively neutral and extremely good at logistics.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Permanent torchlight regime on the Drow Quay; bioluminescent algae in the waterline gives the beach an eerie cold glow at night

- **Surfaces**: Wide crescent beach of black volcanic sand; stone-cut docks on the southwest; mud-brick and wooden structures on the northeast

- **Objects**: The Double-Spire Rocks — visible from the Neutral Strand, roughly 80 feet out in the shallows to the east, marking the Drowned Vault entry (E2). Local knowledge says it is bad luck to swim near them. Both factions agree on this. Neither has a coherent explanation why.

- **Doors & Exits**: Slave Road terminates here; Sunless Sea crossing; Kuo-Toa Fishery eastern dock (by invitation or escort only); Drow Quay gate (barred at night)

- **Special Interactions**: The Quay's iron pens hold 30–80 captives sorted by destination house. A Velouryn clerk (seconded from G2) handles documentation inside the harbormaster's post. The same clerk filters Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route manifests before they reach Thrissik at the dock.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Brine, fish oil, cookfires, slave-pens

- **Sounds**: Two languages spoken simultaneously across a space where neither side quite turns its back on the other
