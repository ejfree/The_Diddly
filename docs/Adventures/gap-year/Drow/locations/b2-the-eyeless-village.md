# The Eyeless Village — Grimlock Farming Hamlet

## **The Eyeless Village**
A satellite hamlet of the Blind Warrens, positioned roughly half a mile south along the active caravan corridor. Where the main Warrens is a hunter's settlement — political, martial, predatory — the Eyeless Village is quiet, slow, and organized around cultivation. Eighty grimlocks live here alongside a small number of thralls, tending fungal groves, maintaining fish-traps in the underground stream that runs through the lowest cavern, and herding the knot of blind cave-rothé that provide the settlement's primary protein supplement. The village sits on the caravan route by design — the grimlocks listen, and Grove-Warden Sketh trades what she hears.

### **Description**
A branching cave system off the main passage in Sector B, between the Blind Warrens (north) and the Echoing Chasm (south). Three connected chambers: the Upper Grove (fungal cultivation), the Stream Floor (fish-trapping, thrall quarters), and the Pen Cavern (cave-rothé, food storage). The main caravan route passes within 80 feet of the hamlet entrance. Grimlock sound-markers — carved tone-stones that produce a low hum when struck by passing air movement — indicate claimed territory to anyone who can read them. Almost no one passing through can.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: No artificial light. Bioluminescent fungi in the Upper Grove produce faint ambient glow. The Stream Floor and Pen Cavern are completely dark.

- **Surfaces**: Upper Grove: carefully managed fungal beds, zurkhwood columns from tended root-mounds. Stream Floor: cold fast stream cutting through rock, zurkhwood-fiber fish traps along both banks. Pen Cavern: sealed side-chamber through a low squeeze-passage.

- **Objects**: Tone-stones at the entrance (territorial markers). Fish traps. Cave-rothé pen. Food storage. Thrall quarters (dry alcove above the flood line in the Stream Floor). Dried meat and fungi stores in the Pen Cavern.

- **Doors & Exits**: One entrance from the main caravan passage. Three connected internal chambers. No secondary exits — the stream drops back below the rock after 200 feet.

- **Special Interactions**: The tone-stones activate on passing air movement — anyone moving quickly past the entrance will trigger a low hum audible to the grimlocks. DC 14 Perception for a traveler to notice the stones and recognize them as territorial markers. Sketh will detect any group approaching the entrance by their sound signature before they arrive.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Upper Grove: rich loamy spore smell, unlike anything else in the corridor — distinctive enough that grimlocks can smell the hamlet from 300 feet in still air. Stream Floor: cold water, fish, damp stone. Pen Cavern: animal musk, dried meat.

- **Sounds**: Upper Grove: near-silence — grove work is quiet work. The hamlet runs at a different pace than the hunter culture of the Warrens. Stream Floor: fast water over stone. A faint irregular sound from somewhere upstream that Sketh has heard for years and considers unremarkable; Aldric heard it last week and found it deeply unsettling.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures**: 6 blind cave-rothé (congenitally blind, bred in the Underdark for at least ten generations; handled exclusively by Vursh). Indeterminate fish stock in the stream cavern.

- **Hostile Threats**: The hamlet's grimlocks will not attack travelers who stop at the entrance — but Sketh will assess, and travelers who enter uninvited and behave aggressively will be met with the full hamlet population.

- **Unique NPCs**:

  **Grove-Warden Sketh** (Lvl 5 Druid with Animal Companion) — Older female grimlock, heavily scarred hands from decades of fungus-cutting. Manages all food production for both the hamlet and the Blind Warrens. Reports to Elder Vrakt; one of three grimlocks he trusts without reservation. Not a fighter, but extraordinarily observant — she can estimate caravan size, cargo weight, and species composition from footfall patterns and load-creak. Has noted drow patrol frequency has increased sharply in the past three weeks. Reported this to Vrakt. He has not explained why. Treats her skilled thralls better than most grimlock keepers — not from sentiment, from efficiency.

  **Aldric** — Surface human, formerly a traveling merchant, blinded on capture roughly two years ago. Has adapted to sensory life in the dark to a remarkable degree; is genuinely useful to Sketh as agricultural labor. Knows the passage schedule for at least six regular caravan operators by ear. Has been quietly mapping the sounds of the settlement in his memory. Is waiting for an opportunity he has not yet identified.

  **Vursh** — Grimlock cave-rothé tender. Has worked with these animals since childhood; communicates with them through touch and low-frequency vibration. Does not interact with outsiders and cannot be easily convinced to do so.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Stream current — environmental

- **Trigger**: Falling into the Stream Floor's fast-running channel

- **Effect**: The stream is cold and fast; the channel walls offer no easy grip. Anyone swept into the current will be carried into the subterranean passage where the stream drops back below the rock — no recovery point exists downstream.

- **Disarm**: Stay out of the stream. The fish traps are accessed from the banks.

### **Additional Notes**
**Scale:** ~80 grimlocks, ~12 thralls (blinded surface humans and one goblinoid family group), 6 blind cave-rothé.

**Chamber breakdown:**

| Chamber | Function |
| --- | --- |
| The Upper Grove | Fungus cultivation — zurkhwood columns, edible shelf fungi, spore-beds |
| The Stream Floor | Fish-trapping, water management, thrall quarters |
| The Pen Cavern | Cave-rothé enclosure, hide-working, food storage |

**As an information source:** Sketh has heard a great deal. She knows the D3 checkpoint rotation schedule by caravan sound-patterns and can confirm that drow patrol composition has changed recently (more soldiers, different house insignia based on armor-sound). She will share this with the PCs if they can demonstrate value to her or to the Warrens — or if Vrakt has already told her to assist them.

**Aldric as potential ally:** If the PCs are looking to free a captive, Aldric is the most likely candidate. He is functional, intelligent, and has been mentally preparing for extraction for two years. He knows things about recent caravan traffic that the PCs will find useful — but he cannot navigate without assistance and will be a liability in combat.

**The unidentified stream sound:** Faint, irregular, not identifiable as any known creature. Sketh has heard it for years and considers it unremarkable. Aldric heard it last week for the first time and found it deeply unsettling. He cannot explain why.

**Relationship to the Blind Warrens:** The Eyeless Village supplies roughly 40% of the Blind Warrens' food. The hunters provide the rest through active hunting in the B1 grounds. Without the Village's fungus and fish production, the Warrens would need to significantly expand its hunting range — into territory that would create friction with the troglodytes to the north and potentially with the drow caravan corridor to the south.

Sketh and Vrakt have a quiet understanding: she runs the hamlet without interference, delivers the agreed food quota, and passes him any intelligence she gathers. He does not send Huntmistress Grezak's people here. This has been a point of low-grade tension since Grezak's rise — she sees the hamlet as soft, and the food supply as something the Warrens could secure by force if the arrangement ever failed.
