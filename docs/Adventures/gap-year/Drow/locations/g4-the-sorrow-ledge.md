# G4 — The Sorrow Ledge

## **The Sorrow Ledge**
Punishment post and intelligence encounter. A wide stone shelf carved 15 feet above the G-sector approach tunnel, between G1 and G2. Visible from below only as a row of iron rings bolted into the ceiling — the chains that hang from them pass through slots in the shelf's edge, keeping prisoners suspended over open air while still standing on stone. PCs with darkvision or who look up will notice the ledge and the silhouettes. PCs moving through quickly may not. Two drow are currently chained on it. They have been here six days. One is dead. One is not.

### **Description**
A wide stone shelf, 15 feet above the tunnel floor, carved into the passage wall. Access via an iron ladder bolted to the tunnel wall behind a natural stone buttress — easy to miss, not locked. No one authorized to use the ledge would need a lock, and no slave is going to climb it. From the ledge, the G2 terminus is partially visible: specifically, the entrance alcove and the iron pen yard. Serath has been watching it for six days and has detailed intelligence on patrol schedules, clerk shift changes, and the Zhaelor inquisitor team's movements.

The ledge is not on any official map. It predates the current G1 way-station by at least a century. Its current use is informal: House Zhaelor punishes personnel here when it wants a sentence public enough to be a warning and quiet enough not to require House Council acknowledgment. Caravans passing below see the chained figures. The message is received. No paperwork.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Whatever ambient light the tunnel provides. The ledge itself has no dedicated lighting.

- **Surfaces**: Stone shelf, wide enough for two people to stand abreast. Slots cut in the shelf's forward edge where the chains pass through. A small natural water seep in the ledge stone — this is what has kept Serath alive.

- **Objects**: Iron rings bolted to the ceiling above (six, only two currently used). Chains running through the shelf-edge slots. The iron ladder behind the buttress. Naleth's body (chained, left side). Serath vel Velouryn (chained, right side, alive).

- **Doors & Exits**: The iron ladder down to the tunnel floor. No other access. The ledge is a dead end.

- **Special Interactions**: The view from the ledge toward G2 is partial but useful — Serath has mapped patrol schedules and shift changes from it. A PC who takes time to observe from the ledge can acquire the same intelligence with a DC 12 Perception check after 30 minutes of watching.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: The smell of Naleth's body — noticeable at close range on the ledge, not yet strong enough to be obvious at tunnel level. The water seep: cold mineral smell.

- **Sounds**: Tunnel traffic below (echoes up to the ledge). Serath's breathing — controlled, deliberate. Chains shift when she moves.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures**:

- **Hostile Threats**: The Zhaelor sergeant who placed them here has no fixed return schedule. 1-in-6 chance per hour the PCs spend on or near the ledge that he appears below with two soldiers. If Serath is gone when he arrives, he begins asking questions at G1.

- **Unique NPCs**:

  **Naleth** — Dead. Low-ranked Zhaelor soldier who spoke to the wrong person about something he witnessed at D3. Has been dead roughly two days. Still chained. The Zhaelor sergeant who put them here has not returned to remove him. He is not a story — he is a warning about what Serath is looking at if no one comes. If the PCs examine the body: DC 14 Perception finds a dried smear of ink on Naleth's right palm — the kind left by a message-stick. He had recently written something. Whatever it was is gone.

  **Serath vel Velouryn** — Alive. Mid-ranked operative of House Velouryn, seconded to G2 clerk work eight weeks ago. Her actual assignment was courier — handling unofficial transit documents for goods moving through the Slave Road Terminus that do not appear in G2's official tally. Specifically: the Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route. She made the mistake of asking Velouryn's G2 liaison why the Veythrae cargo was being logged with Xilrath house-marks. The liaison did not answer. Three days later, a Zhaelor squad put her here. She has spent six days thinking about why it was Zhaelor and has not yet reached the conclusion she should have: that Zhaelor is protecting something Veythrae needs, and Velouryn's G2 liaison is Zhaelor's asset.

  Physical state: weakened — no food in six days; the water seep has kept her alive. Not delirious yet but moving in that direction. Will not ask for help from anyone she does not believe can actually provide it.

  Personality: cold, calculating, genuinely angry in a way she has learned to keep off her face. She is Velouryn — she trades in information and obligation. She will not offer gratitude. She will offer a deal.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: The Zhaelor sergeant — timed complication

- **Trigger**: 1-in-6 chance per hour spent on or near the ledge

- **Effect**: Sergeant appears below with two soldiers. If Serath is missing, he raises questions at G1 and begins an investigation.

- **Disarm**: Move quickly. Alternatively, deal with the sergeant directly.

### **Additional Notes**
**What Serath offers:**

- The Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route documentation — she memorized the contents of three manifest cycles. Ship times, cargo types, the kuo-toa dock contact at the Pier (not Shlipik — a second contact Shlipik does not know about).
- The location of Velouryn's G2 dead drop: a loose stone in the wall of G2's clerk alcove, third from the corner, behind a water-staining mark. Contains the current cycle's unofficial manifests.
- The name of the Zhaelor asset inside Velouryn's G2 operation: **Liaison Orrath vel Velouryn.** She is not certain, but she is close enough to certain that she will say it.

**What she wants:**

1. Off this ledge, alive.
2. Safe passage out of the G-sector approach — she is not going into Xil, where she will be found and finished properly.
3. Word sent to Velouryn's upper house that her silence was purchased rather than broken — she wants it on record that she did not talk to Zhaelor. (She did not. She is talking to the PCs, which is different, and she is managing this distinction carefully.)

**What she will not do:** Tell the PCs anything about Velouryn's surface operations. Trust them without a contingency — if the PCs agree to help and then try to bring her to Zhaelor, she will scream and take everyone down with her. Pretend she is grateful. She will honor the deal. She will not pretend it was kindness.

**Encounter variations:**

If the PCs pass below without looking up: nothing happens. Serath sees them, assesses, says nothing. If they have drow insignia she watches more carefully. If they look like surface-worlders in bad disguise, she debates thirty seconds and then says quietly, in Common: *"You are not drow. And you are going the wrong direction if you want to survive past G2."* She is not being altruistic. She is buying an audience.

If the PCs notice the ledge and climb up: Serath is watching them come. By the time they reach the top she has already decided what she is willing to trade. She opens with: *"I have been up here six days. I am past the point of pride. Tell me what you need and I will tell you if I have it."* She will not beg. She will not cry. She will not thank them for listening.

If the PCs try to take her to Xil or G2: she knows she cannot fight them in her current state. She will go quietly until she has an opportunity. She will create the opportunity.

If the PCs free her and let her go: she disappears into a side tunnel she has been studying from the ledge for six days and has already mapped by sound. Before she goes, she does one thing she did not agree to do: she tells them which stone in the G2 clerk alcove to check. She frames it as "paying forward a debt I prefer not to carry." It is the closest she will come to thank you.

**The intelligence value:**

The G2 dead drop contains manifests showing 11 cargo movements in the past 6 weeks not in official G2 records. Cargo types include alchemical reagents, architectural diagrams, and two manifests listing cargo only as "perishables — Veythrae consignment." The diagrams are for Xil's outer wall.

The Veythrae-kuo-toa route connects directly to what Lisa/Grissel has been tracking from Blingdenstone — the increased river traffic at the Whispering Shelf. The route passes through the underground river. The kuo-toa secondary contact is being paid in something other than metal.

Liaison Orrath vel Velouryn — if the PCs reach Xil and make contact with Velouryn, knowing Orrath is a Zhaelor plant is significant leverage. It is also dangerous: Orrath will know something went wrong at the ledge the moment Serath's absence is noticed.

**Naleth's missing message:** If pursued, this connects to something the D3 checkpoint commander (Overseer Nerath Dravun) was trying to report and was silenced for. This is a deeper thread than G4 can hold; the GM should decide if that thread is worth pulling.

**GM Notes:**

- Serath is not a hero and should not be played as one. She is a professional in a bad situation making the most rational available trade. She will honor her commitments precisely and no further.
- The Zhaelor-Velouryn-Veythrae connection is a thread into Xil's house politics. The Sorrow Ledge encounter is the first place the PCs can pull it.
- If the PCs skip this entirely, Serath dies in approximately two more days. Her body is found by a maintenance crew three weeks later and logged as a disciplinary matter. The dead drop at G2 is cleared in the next manifest cycle. Orrath continues operating.
