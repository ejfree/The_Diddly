# Blingdenstone — The City of Speaking Stones

## Overview

Blingdenstone is the canonical svirfneblin (deep gnome) city from the D-series modules, located in the Underdark between D1 (the troglodyte caverns) and D2 (the kuo-toa warrens). It sits in a large natural cavern system off the main route, reachable via a hidden side-passage that most travelers miss.

The city is currently run by its **Burrow Warden**, a deep gnome named **Grissel Deepmantle** — a quiet, sharp-eyed woman who emerged from the chaos following a drow raid that killed most of the old council. In nine months she has reorganized the city's defenses, expanded food production, and positioned Blingdenstone as a genuine sanctuary in the D1-D2 corridor.

Grissel Deepmantle does not exist. She is **Lisa Siviridion** — Lyrriman d'Sivis, emerald dragon, RA of the Diddly's flight wing — running the deepest cover of her life.

---

## The Real Story: What Happened to Lisa

During the Drow War, Lisa's reconnaissance flight was ambushed by a large drow war party in the upper passages. The rest of her flight was killed. Lisa survived using every dirty trick available — shapeshifting, misdirection, feigned death — and fled deeper into the Underdark rather than back toward the surface.

She spent roughly a month in the tunnels, hiding, healing, and observing. She found Blingdenstone before Blingdenstone found her — spending weeks watching from the shadows, learning names, relationships, grievances, the power vacuum left by the dead council, who was trusted and who was not.

When she finally made her move, she did not appear as a threat. She appeared as a survivor.

She transformed into a deep gnome — a refugee from a smaller svirfneblin enclave, wiped out by the same drow war pressing on all sides. A skilled stonecutter. Quiet. Capable. Someone who asked good questions and never overreached. She did not seize power. She made herself useful, built trust person by person, and let the community hand her authority because they needed someone and she was there.

Nine months later, she is Burrow Warden.

## Lisa's Disguise

**Presented identity:** Grissel Deepmantle, refugee stonecutter and former militia captain from a destroyed svirfneblin enclave called Dunbarrow (small enough that no one in Blingdenstone would have contacts there).

**Cover construction:**

- Her espionage training let her study the gnomes for weeks before making contact — she arrived already knowing their dialect patterns, common names, tunnel customs, and which faces to approach first
- She presented a single verifiable skill (stonework analysis) as her entry point — something she could actually do, grounded in her Archano-Mechanics knowledge
- She built relationships laterally before ever moving vertically — she was trusted by a dozen citizens before anyone in authority noticed her
- She volunteered for dangerous work (tunnel patrols during a period of heavy drow probing) and performed well, which accelerated credibility faster than any manipulation

**Dragon build equivalent:** Level 14 fighter (samurai discipline). She does not fight unless she has to. When she does, she wins fast and changes the subject.

**How she maintains the cover:**

- Emerald dragons are innately talented at sustained illusion and subtle telepathic influence — she can hold the physical form indefinitely
- Her cover identity has a complete backstory she has rehearsed to the point of emotional reflex
- She deflects questions about Dunbarrow with grief that is entirely real (she is grieving her actual flight companions)
- She has positioned herself as a leader who listens rather than one who commands, which means fewer people are carefully scrutinizing her

**Cracks in the cover:**

- Her stonework knowledge is technically precise in ways that feel slightly "academic" rather than learned by hand — a veteran stonecutter might notice
- Her combat instincts are samurai, not brawler — clean and economical in a way that is distinctly not gnomish
- She is too good at reading people; experienced spies or empaths may sense she is always processing, never fully at rest
- She has developed genuine affection for several gnomes, which pulls against the calculated detachment good cover requires
- She has not sent any word to the surface — no messages, no signals — and nine months is a long time to stay completely dark

## Blingdenstone Today

### Scale

- **Population:** ~1,800 svirfneblin (reduced from a pre-war peak of ~3,200 — raids, refugees departed, some dead)
- **Burrow Guard:** 40-person rotating militia that Lisa reorganized from scratch; 8 function as her de facto command unit

### Political Structure Under Lisa

Blingdenstone nominally operates by council — five district wardens who vote on major decisions. Lisa, as Burrow Warden, holds a tie-breaking vote and sets agenda. In practice, she has shaped the council into people who broadly trust her read of a situation.

Key changes she has driven in nine months:

- **Defensive restructuring.** Collapse-tunnel traps at four approach corridors, rotating patrol schedules, early-warning wards with a distinctly mechanical logic woven through gnomish earth-magic. The city has repelled two drow probing actions since she took over.
- **Food surplus.** She pushed hard for expanded fungus cultivation and rationing reform. The city now has a three-month reserve — the first time in years.
- **Information network.** She established a network of observers in the D1-D2 tunnel approaches — gnomes who report drow movement patterns. She has framed this as "community scouts" but it is a functional intelligence operation.
- **Strict neutrality.** Blingdenstone has refused contact with Xilthraen, duergar traders, and surface factions alike. Lisa sells this as survival pragmatism. It is also how she keeps anyone from asking questions about Grissel Deepmantle that she cannot answer.

### What the Gnomes Think

Most of Blingdenstone thinks Grissel Deepmantle is the best thing to happen to them since the raids started. She is calm under pressure, she does not play favorites, and she is demonstrably right about threats — twice now she has ordered defensive preparations that the council thought were excessive, and twice the drow came anyway.

A few are uneasy in ways they cannot articulate. The gemcutter elder **Tarvix** has said nothing aloud but watches her with an expression he usually reserves for seismic anomalies: *something there that should not be, not yet wrong, but worth watching.* His granddaughter **Senna** has simply decided that Grissel is strange and interesting and has begun following her around like an apprentice, which Lisa finds both touching and operationally inconvenient.

## Adventure Hooks

### Finding Blingdenstone

The PCs may encounter leads in D1 or the tunnels between D1 and D2:

- A gnome refugee who fled before Lisa arrived mentions that Blingdenstone was barely surviving — but a traveler who passed through two months ago says it seems to be thriving now under new leadership
- Drow scouts in D1 report the gnome city has gotten harder to probe; their intelligence on its layout is now outdated
- A coded message scratched into a tunnel wall that uses university shorthand — something only a Diddly student would recognize

### The Reveal

The PCs arrive in Blingdenstone and are received carefully by Burrow Warden Grissel Deepmantle — a gnome who asks precise, intelligent questions and watches them with a stillness that does not feel quite gnomish.

Lisa will almost certainly recognize the PCs before they recognize her. She will not break cover in public. If she trusts them enough, she will arrange a private meeting, probably framed as a security debrief. In private she has a choice: hold cover or let them in.

**If she holds cover:** She assists the PCs covertly — better information, safer passage, supplies — without explaining why. She is not ready to give up what she has built.

**If she breaks cover:** She is honest and exhausted. She wants help, but she will not leave until the gnomes have a viable path — either the war ends, or she gets the city evacuated, or she finds a way to hand it off to someone she trusts.

### Lisa's Request

Lisa wants:

1. Word sent to the Diddly that she is alive — she has not been able to do this safely
2. A plan for Blingdenstone that does not end with drow reprisals the moment she leaves
3. Something she will not ask for directly: she has been alone with this for nine months and she is not doing as well as her cover suggests

## The Prophecies of the Speaking Stones

Blingdenstone's oldest tunnels predate the current city by centuries. The svirfneblin built over and through them, incorporating their passages into the lower warrens, but few gnomes go there anymore — the resonance feels wrong, the stone too old, the echoes too long.

Lisa found them during her second month in the city, when she was still hiding and still trying to understand where she had landed. She has been deciphering them in fragments ever since, using her Archano-Mechanics training to read the structural patterns of the inscriptions alongside the linguistic content.

They are not written in any gnomish script she recognizes. They are written in something older.

And they are about her.

---

### Fragment One — *The Stone Remembers the Face That Changes*

*"She will come wearing a skin that is not her own,*
*green beneath the grey, fire beneath the stone.*
*She will not know the road has been waiting.*
*The city of speaking stones does not choose its wardens — it remembers them."*

**What Lisa understands:** The deep gnome tradition of "stone-memory" holds that significant events leave impressions in geological strata — not metaphor, but literal information encoded in crystalline structure over time. This inscription was not written *after* a prophecy was fulfilled. The stone formed around it.

She has not told anyone she found this.

---

### Fragment Two — *The Web-Ward and the Shield Beneath the Hill*

*"When the spider's silk reaches for the speaking city,*
*it shall find not gnome-flesh but dragon-breath.*
*The shield is not kin to the city it guards.*
*The shield came lost. It did not know it was a shield."*

**What Lisa understands:** The "spider's silk" is clearly the drow — the Lolth iconography is unmistakable even in a pre-gnomish script. The city has been here before, in some form. And whatever force or awareness encoded these words knew that its protector would arrive not as a savior but as a fugitive.

The phrase *"did not know it was a shield"* is the one she returns to most often.

---

### Fragment Three — *The Two-Named Thing and the Stone That Sings*

*"She carries two names and has lost both.*
*She carries two faces and trusts neither.*
*When she speaks the true name aloud in the deep place,*
*the stone will answer, and the path below will open."*

**What Lisa understands:** "Two names" — Lyrriman d'Sivis, Lisa Siviridion. "Two faces" — dragon, gnome. And before that, elf. The phrase *"has lost both"* hits differently since the ambush. Her flight is dead. The university thinks she is dead. She has been Grissel Deepmantle for nine months.

The "path below" is the part she cannot decipher. Below Blingdenstone there is nothing mapped. She has not looked.

---

### Fragment Four — *The Plague Before the Spider*

*"First the rot comes up through the roots,*
*the invisible war that the surface fights and does not name.*
*The plague is the spider's first thread — cast wide, cast early,*
*to weaken the weave before the fangs descend.*
*The shield who knows this can name the thread to those who climb.*
*Name it, and the descent becomes a rescue.*
*Do not name it, and the descent becomes a funeral."*

**What Lisa understands:** The plague that preceded the Drow War was not incidental. It was a deliberate opening move — Lolth's first thread. Lisa does not yet know who the "those who climb" are. She suspects, from the context of the other fragments, that they are coming through the giant chain.

She has been preparing a summary of everything she knows — the Aelindra intelligence, the war preparations, the tunnel scout network, the plague connection — on the assumption that someone will eventually come down who needs it.

### GM Notes on the Prophecies

- Lisa has partial translations only. Fragments Two and Three she has mostly decoded. Fragment Four she deciphered two months ago and it shook her badly.
- She has not found the source inscription yet — the four fragments are scattered across the deep tunnel walls. Somewhere lower there is likely a complete text.
- The "path below" in Fragment Three may connect to a sub-level of Blingdenstone that opens into a passage toward D3, bypassing D2 entirely — a significant tactical asset if real.
- The prophecies tie directly to the main arc in [prophecy.md](../prophecy.md). The "Young Wings" of the Black Coil fragment and the "shield who names the thread" are the same event, viewed from different angles.
- Lisa bringing the plague intelligence to the PCs — Fragment Four's *"name it"* — is potentially the moment she breaks cover.

---

## GM Notes

- Lisa's **Archano-Mechanics** training is visible in the city's defenses if anyone examines them — the wards have a mechanical precision that feels designed rather than grown, which is unusual for svirfneblin earth-magic
- Her **samurai sword form** has been maintained through daily private practice in a sealed tunnel section she has claimed as a "contemplation space"
- She has **not contacted her grandmother** Lysse d'Sivis, and the guilt is a live wire — any mention of the tea shop or House Sivis will hit harder than she intends
- **Tarvix** will approach PCs privately if he has any reason to think they know more than they are saying; he does not know what Grissel is, only that she is not what she appears
- **Senna** (his granddaughter) has formed a genuine bond with Lisa and will be a complication if the truth comes out — she will feel betrayed before she feels relieved
- Blingdenstone's location makes it a viable **safe house and intelligence hub** for the D1-D3 arc, which Lisa will offer freely if the PCs earn her trust
