# The Blind Warrens — The Grimlock Settlement

## Overview

The Blind Warrens is the largest grimlock settlement in this region of the Underdark — a sprawling cave complex that predates every other civilization currently squatting in these tunnels. The grimlocks did not build it so much as occupy it, expand it by smell and touch over generations, and fill every passage with meaning that outsiders cannot perceive.

There is no light here. The grimlocks have no use for it and treat its presence as an act of aggression. Every inch of the Warrens is mapped in the grimlocks' collective memory by sound, texture, smell, and air current. They can hear a stranger breathing from fifty feet away. They can track a caravan by the vibration it leaves in the stone.

The Warrens occupies Sector B of the Underdark corridor — a network of mid-descent passages linking the troglodyte entry zones to the drow-controlled lower tunnels. This location was not an accident. The grimlocks control the chokepoint at the Echoing Chasm (B3), and for generations this gave them leverage. The drow eventually noticed. The current arrangement — caravans pass unmolested, the grimlocks are not exterminated — is the result.

Elder Vrakt has maintained this arrangement for thirty years. He believes it is the only reason his people are still alive. He may be right.

---

## Location

**Mid-upper Underdark**, Sector B. The Warrens occupies three interconnected cave clusters radiating from the Echoing Chasm:

- **Upper Passages** — toward Sector A (troglodyte territory); grimlock hunting grounds extend into B1
- **The Chasm Approach** — the Echoing Chasm (B3) is the settlement's natural southern boundary and primary defensible position
- **Deep Burrows** — carved and expanded passages descending below the main cave level; the oldest parts of the settlement

---

## Scale

- **Grimlock population:** ~280
- **Thrall population:** ~30 (surface folk, blinded; some goblinoids taken as labor)
- **Total:** ~310

The Warrens is organized not by clans or districts but by **function-clusters** — groups of passages and caverns that share an acoustic signature and serve a related purpose. Grimlocks navigate by sound-memory; they name locations by the sounds they make, not by geography.

| Cluster | Grimlock Name (approx. translation) | Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Deep Hum | "Vrumkrak" | Elder's seat, council cave, ancestor-shrines |
| The Wet Stones | "Dripkall" | Water source, bathing, fish-trapping in underground streams |
| The Kill Floor | "Blutrak" | Butchering, food preparation, hide-working |
| The Nest Passages | "Skarrow" | Sleeping burrows, child-rearing, storage |
| The Hunt Mouth | "Grethval" | Armory (crude), staging area for hunting parties |
| The Hollow Bridge | "Echoval" | Echoing Chasm approaches — ambush points, caravan-watching posts |

## Government — Elder Rule and the Voice-Circle

The Blind Warrens has no formal government. Authority is held by the **Elder** — currently **Elder Vrakt** — whose word is final on matters of survival, territory, and dealings with outsiders. The Elder is not chosen by vote or birth but by demonstrated competence over time. Vrakt has simply outlived and out-performed every challenger for three decades.

Below Vrakt is the **Voice-Circle** — the seven most capable hunters and the three oldest females, who advise on resource allocation and raiding decisions. They meet in the Deep Hum cavern, where the acoustics allow every voice to be heard equally. Decisions are made by consensus of the Circle, except in matters where Vrakt invokes Elder's Right and rules alone. He uses this sparingly and always explains himself afterward.

**Key figures:**

- **Elder Vrakt** (Lvl 7 Ranger with Ranger's Warden dedication) — Ancient, scarred, missing his left ear from a skirmish with a drow patrol decades ago. Blind from birth like all his kind. Navigates with absolute confidence in complete darkness and is acutely disoriented by light of any kind — magical illumination is a meaningful weapon against him. Vrakt has one priority: the Warrens survives. He will deal with drow, surface-worlders, or anyone else if it serves that purpose. He does not trust the drow. He trusts the arrangement he has with them, which is different.

- **Huntmistress Grezak** (Lvl 6 Fighter) — Commands the B1 hunting parties. Young by grimlock standards, ambitious, and quietly resentful of Vrakt's cooperative stance toward the drow. She believes the Warrens should be predators, not passage-keepers. She is not wrong about the settlement's capabilities. She is wrong about what would happen next.

- **Stonebinder Thrull** (Lvl 5 Cleric of Torog) — The settlement's spiritual authority. Maintains the ancestor-shrines in the Deep Hum, performs the rituals that bind the dead to the stone (grimlocks believe their ancestors' awareness passes into the rock of the Underdark). Thrull is old, calm, and the only grimlock in the Warrens who seems unbothered by outside visitors — he has seen enough to stop being surprised. He reads the stone's vibrations as omens and recently told Vrakt that "something large is moving through the deep passages, and it has no sound."

- **Kalla the Pale** (Lvl 4 Rogue) — Manages the thralls. She is responsible for their feeding, working, and the blinding of new captives. Not cruel by grimlock standards — thralls who work are fed; thralls who cannot work are released (into the tunnels, without guides). She speaks a crude version of Common learned from captives over years, making her the settlement's de facto translator for surface visitors.

## The Settlement

### The Deep Hum (Vrumkrak)

The political and spiritual heart of the Warrens — a large oval cavern where the rock itself produces a low, barely perceptible resonance from an underground stream running beneath the floor. Every sound in this cave carries with extraordinary clarity. Grimlocks can detect the heartbeat of a standing creature from across the room.

The ancestor-shrines line the walls — not carvings or images, but stones that have been struck, shaped, and tuned to produce specific tones when touched. Each tone corresponds to a dead elder or great hunter. Stonebinder Thrull reads the harmony of the shrines to interpret the will of the dead.

Vrakt's place in this cave is not a throne — it is a specific point in the floor where the resonance is strongest. He sits there when addressing the Voice-Circle.

### The Kill Floor (Blutrak)

Organized for maximum efficiency in complete darkness — every surface has a specific textural marker indicating its purpose. Blood trenches are cut into the floor leading to a lower drain-cavern. The smell here is overwhelming to any surface creature but unremarkable to grimlocks. Hides, fat, sinew, and bone are processed and stored here. The grimlock diet is entirely carnivorous with some fungal supplement; they have eaten well since establishing the caravan arrangement (drow caravans occasionally "lose" food stocks to grimlock taxation).

### The Hollow Bridge (Echoval)

The Echoing Chasm approaches — the B3 position. This is not housing but a permanent tactical installation. Grimlock sentries maintain a continuous presence here in rotating three-hour shifts. They do not guard it in any visible sense; they simply sit, motionless, and listen. They can detect an approaching party by footfall vibration a full ten minutes before visual contact would be possible.

The Chasm itself is used as an acoustic weapon. Grimlocks can communicate across it at whisper-volume. They can also, by striking specific stones on their side of the arch, generate sounds that disorient creatures attempting to cross — not enough to stun, but enough to make footing uncertain.

### The Nest Passages (Skarrow)

The domestic core of the Warrens — narrow burrows dug laterally into the cave walls, just large enough for one adult grimlock to sleep in. No two burrows are adjacent; sound-privacy is important. Children sleep in communal creches guarded by elder females. The entire nest area is marked with a scent-boundary that every grimlock recognizes instinctively as *home territory* — outsiders crossing it without a grimlock escort trigger an immediate, non-negotiable attack response.

The thrall quarters are adjacent to Skarrow but separated by a textured floor marker. Thralls sleep communally, chained to iron spikes driven into the floor. The chains are long enough to allow movement for work but not for escape.

## Subsettlements & Encounter Areas

### Large Subsettlements

#### The Eyeless Village — Satellite Farming Hamlet (B2)

A smaller grimlock hamlet roughly half a mile south of the main Warrens, positioned along the active caravan corridor. Population ~80. Specializes in fungus cultivation, cave creature herding, and underground stream fishing — the food production that the Warrens depends on. Managed by Grove-Warden Sketh under Vrakt's broader authority. See [b2-the-eyeless-village.md](b2-the-eyeless-village.md) for full detail.

#### The Deep Burrows — Old Settlement Below the Main Caves

The oldest portion of the Warrens, accessed through a narrow shaft in the Vrumkrak floor. Pre-dates current grimlock occupation — the passage markings here are not grimlock work, and Stonebinder Thrull believes they were made by something that came before. Currently used as cold storage and as a refuge of last resort. Vrakt has never fully explored its deepest chambers.

#### The Hunt Grounds — B1 Territory

Not a settlement but an administered zone — a stretch of upper passages where Huntmistress Grezak maintains organized hunting rotations. The grimlocks here have memorized every echo-point and ambush location over generations. They are extraordinarily effective in this terrain and almost helpless outside of it.

---

### Smaller Encounter Areas

#### 1. The Ancestor Stones

Stonebinder Thrull's collection of tuned memorial stones, arranged by seniority. Touching them without permission is the most profound social violation in grimlock culture. One stone — the oldest, placed against the far wall — produces a tone that Thrull cannot identify. He did not make it. It was here when the grimlocks arrived.

#### 2. The Blind Pens

The thrall quarters. Currently housing 12 blinded surface humans and 8 goblinoids. The surface humans range from recent captures (still in shock, functional) to long-term residents who have adapted to life in permanent darkness. One, a half-elf named Serris who has been here roughly four years, has learned enough grimlock speech-signs (touch-based) to function as an informal interpreter. She has no particular desire to leave — the surface world she came from was not much better.

#### 3. The Sounding Wall

A flat, dense section of cave wall near the Echoing Chasm where grimlocks practice echolocation by striking patterns and listening to the returns. Young grimlocks train here. The wall is marked by ten thousand small impact points from generations of use. A visitor with a keen ear can hear what sounds like a faint, rhythmic tapping coming from *the other side of the wall* — from within the rock itself — with no identifiable source.

#### 4. The Water Cache

A deep basin in the Wet Stones cluster where an underground stream surfaces. Fresh, cold, reliable. The grimlocks have built no structure around it but maintain it carefully — removing blockages, keeping the banks clear. It is the settlement's most defended resource; three experienced sentries are here at all times, sleeping in rotation.

#### 5. The South Watch

The forward position on the caravan route toward Sector C and the drow checkpoints. Two grimlocks are always posted here, 400 feet ahead of the Chasm. Their job is to listen, count, and report — not to engage. They can distinguish a lightly-loaded trade caravan from a military patrol by the sound profile alone.

## Relationship to Other Factions

**Xilthraen (Drow):**
A tolerance arrangement, not an alliance. The drow allow the Blind Warrens to exist; the grimlocks allow drow caravans to pass unmolested. The drow also provide occasional "tribute" — cast-off slaves and food surplus that would otherwise be wasted. Vrakt is under no illusions about what this means: the drow could end the arrangement any time they found it inconvenient. He has spent thirty years making himself inconvenient to remove.

House Dravun handles the practical relationship — a standing arrangement managed through infrequent courier contact and a simple token system for authorized caravans. House Xilrath ignores the grimlocks entirely, which Vrakt considers a safe relationship.

**Grimstone Holds (Duergar):**
No formal relationship. The duergar routes do not pass through Sector B. Occasional contact occurs when duergar prospecting parties wander into the wrong tunnels; they are escorted out. The duergar have never attempted to establish a slave relationship with the Blind Warrens, possibly because grimlocks make poor forced laborers — they work only when they choose to, and their cooperation cannot be compelled without constant and expensive violence.

**The Troglodytes (Sector A):**
Territorial boundary maintained by mutual avoidance. The troglodytes and grimlocks have raided each other periodically for generations. Currently at a cold truce — neither side has enough surplus aggression to make war profitable. The troglodytes' musk is immediately detectable to grimlock hunters; the grimlocks regard the A2 tribe with contempt and stay out of their passages.

**The Mindspire (Illithid):**
The grimlocks know something hunts minds from the northeast. Several grimlocks have disappeared over the decades — no bodies found. Stonebinder Thrull has performed rites to protect the Warrens from "the silent devouring." Whether these rites work or whether the illithids simply find 280 grimlocks insufficient to bother with, no one has tested.

**Blingdenstone (Deep Gnomes):**
Unknown to most grimlocks, the deep gnome carving at B3 is a genuine artifact — the Echoing Chasm was a svirfneblin trade route before the grimlocks arrived. The gnomes know this and have occasionally sent observers to watch the Warrens from a distance. No contact has been attempted. Elder Vrakt once heard an unfamiliar step-pattern near the South Watch and correctly identified it as "something small, careful, and not trying to be followed." He has not reported this to the Voice-Circle.

## Adventure Hooks

### The Passage Price

The PCs need to pass through Sector B to reach the lower tunnels. Standard caravan tokens do not apply here — the grimlocks run their own system, and it requires a direct conversation with Elder Vrakt. He will talk, but he wants something in return: information. Specifically, he wants to know why drow patrol frequency through Sector D has doubled in the past three weeks. He already knows the PCs likely have a guess. He is willing to negotiate passage, intelligence about the tunnels ahead, and safe escort to the Chasm for the right exchange.

### Something in the Stone

Stonebinder Thrull is hearing the tapping from the Ancestor Stones again — it started three days ago and has been getting louder. He needs someone who is not grimlock to go into the Deep Burrows and find the source. He cannot send his own people; the Burrows are sacred space and entering them without cause violates the ancestor-covenant. Non-grimlocks are technically exempt from the covenant. The tapping is coming from a sealed chamber that Thrull's ancestors bricked up four generations ago and left no record of why.

### Kalla Has a Problem

Kalla the Pale's most valuable thrall — Serris the half-elf, the de facto translator — has been showing signs of awakening hearing ability beyond normal range. She is beginning to understand grimlock sound-signs without being taught. This is disturbing to Vrakt (only grimlocks should be able to do this) and interesting to Stonebinder Thrull (it suggests she has underdark lineage she does not know about). If the PCs arrive while this is being discovered, they walk into a political situation where Serris's status is suddenly contested and she may be moved from thrall quarters to something with significantly different implications.
