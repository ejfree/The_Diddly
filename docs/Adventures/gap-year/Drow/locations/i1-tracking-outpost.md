# I1 — The Crevice Watch

## **The Crevice Watch**
Scout post / early-warning station. High fissure above the D1 main tunnel junction, ~1.5 hours east of Blingdenstone. Population: 6 scouts (rotating 3-day shifts). Reports to Burrow Warden Grissel Deepmantle (i.e., Lisa). A narrow stone ledge, naturally formed in a fissure that splits the cavern ceiling above the most-traveled junction in the D1 approach corridor. Forty feet below, three tunnel mouths meet. From the shelf, a patient observer can track torch-glow from a quarter mile out in two directions simultaneously.

### **Description**
The ledge itself is two feet wide at its narrowest, six at its widest, and about thirty feet long. It does not connect to any other passage — access is via a near-vertical crack in the south wall, requiring climbing gear or practiced gnomish tunnel movement. The scouts haul a rope behind them.

There is no fire. No light. The gnomes sleep in shifts on bedrolls wedged against the stone and eat cold rations. The acoustic properties of the fissure are such that sound from below rises clearly, but voices on the ledge travel parallel to the stone and dissipate before reaching the junction.

Mira discovered this last property by accident in her first week and immediately reported it. Lisa called it an ideal natural feature. Mira started calling the ledge "the Whispermouse" — the name stuck among the scouts, though it never made it into any official report.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: None. The gnomes operate in total darkness.

- **Surfaces**: Narrow stone ledge, two to six feet wide, thirty feet long. No connecting passages. Vertical crack access in the south wall.

- **Objects**: Scout bedrolls. Cold ration stores. Signal equipment (stone-tap relay). The rope the scouts haul up behind them after climbing — the ledge is inaccessible without it or climbing gear.

- **Doors & Exits**: One access point: the near-vertical crack in the south wall. No other exits. The ledge is a dead end above a 40-foot drop to the tunnel junction.

- **Special Interactions**: Acoustic property — sound from below rises clearly to the ledge, but voices on the ledge dissipate before reaching the junction. The scouts exploit this for continuous surveillance without sound discipline risk. The junction below is the most-traveled point in the D1 approach; torch-glow is visible from a quarter mile in two directions.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Cold stone. Faint smell of cold rations. Nothing to distinguish this fissure from any other.

- **Sounds**: Everything from the junction below rises clearly — footfalls, cart wheels, conversation at normal volume. Patrol sounds, load-creak, species-specific movement patterns. Mira can extract patrol size, movement speed, and burden load from these sounds alone.

### **Traps & Hazards**

- **Trap Name & Type**: Ledge drop — environmental

- **Trigger**: Moving near the edge without care

- **Effect**: 40-foot fall to the tunnel junction below. The fall itself is survivable; landing in the junction with a drow patrol present is not.

- **Disarm**: The ledge is wide enough to move carefully. The scouts have worked here for months without incident.

### **Additional Notes**
**Operations:** The Watch maintains a continuous two-person rotation: one observer, one recorder. Every patrol, cart, or foot traffic movement below is logged in a shorthand system Mira developed. Reports are relayed by stone-tap signal to a relay point, and from there to the Ironbell Warren or directly to Blingdenstone depending on urgency.

**Signal codes (abbreviated):**
- 2 taps: standard drow patrol, standard size
- 3 taps: oversized patrol or escort formation
- Sustained tap: unknown formation, all-hands alert
- 5-tap-pause-2: civilian or merchant movement

Lisa wrote the original code. Mira has since expanded it to 34 distinct signals. Lisa reviewed the additions without comment and approved all of them.

**Current Intelligence:** As of the most recent report cycle, the Watch has noted:
- Drow patrol frequency on the D1 main approach has increased by roughly 40% over the past six weeks
- A new patrol configuration: two scouts ahead of the main body rather than one, suggesting the drow are expecting contact
- Three instances of a patrol stopping at the junction and stationing briefly before moving on — behavior consistent with relay-point use, meaning the drow have reorganized their corridor communications
- One unconfirmed sighting of a figure that did not move like a drow, did not move like a gnome, and was gone before Mira could characterize it further

The last entry is flagged and underlined in Mira's report. Lisa has not responded to it yet.

**Adventure Hooks:**
- The unconfirmed sighting: what was it? (Drow shapechanger? Something else using the tunnel? A PC's earlier passage that Mira caught a glimpse of?)
- The Watch is the station that first spotted the PCs' approach. Whatever Mira observed — size, equipment, behavior — is in Lisa's hands before the party reaches Blingdenstone's gate.
- If the PCs are trying to move through D1 undetected, someone in the party needs to get past the junction without being logged.

**Lisa's history with this position:** She identified it during her second week in the Underdark — before she made contact with any gnome, while she was still hiding and still deciding who she was going to become. The fissure was already there. She simply noted it as the best observation point in the approach corridor and filed it away. When she established the Listening Net, the Crevice Watch was the first station she placed.
