## Drow House Overview — Xil

Quick reference for all eight noble houses. Six hold council seats; two (Vorryn and Draethi) operate outside the council by mutual necessity. For full detail — TOEs, doctrine, personalities, and cross-references — see each house's individual file.

---

## Council Houses

### House Xilrath — "The Black Blades"

*The war engine of Xil.*

- **Council rank:** Major (2 votes; Vel'dryn holds the chair)
- **D3 mapping:** House Kilsek (rank 2 — military powerhouse)
- **Specialty:** Heavy infantry, logistics, and two decades of pre-positioned supplies
- **Command:** Matron Xilrath Vel'dryn (Lvl 16 Cleric/Warpriest) · First Sword Captain Veynar Xilrath (Lvl 12 Fighter with Champion dedication) · Logistics Mistress Shynrae Xilrath (Lvl 10 Cleric with Investigator dedication)
- **Force:** 650 drow + 900 auxiliaries = **1,550 total**
- **Council stance:** Immediate war. The public rationale is Lolth's mandate; the personal reason is Vel'dryn's catastrophic entanglement with the royalty of Arenal, which ended when she discovered the "envoy" she had confided in was a foreign intelligence operative. Lolth approves of a personal wound that accelerates a genuine conquest.
- **Allies:** Dravun (beast leases), Zhaelor (shared aggression timeline)
- **Rivals:** Veythrae (military doctrine vs. arcane supremacy)
- **Doctrine:** Blade Cohorts as anvil, Quaggoth shock as hammer, Void-Archers maintaining poison pressure throughout. Weakness: if the archers are neutralized, advances stall.

→ [house-xilrath-the-black-blades.md](house-xilrath-the-black-blades.md)

### House Veythrae — "The Shadowfire Cadres"

*Half wizard academy, half war laboratory.*

- **Council rank:** Major (2 votes)
- **D3 mapping:** House Eilservs (rank 1 — dominant arcane/political)
- **Specialty:** Shadowfire battle-magic, fiend-binding, enchantment production
- **Command:** Matron Thalara Veythrae (Lvl 17 Wizard with Cleric dedication) · Archmage Sybrynn Veythrae (Lvl 12 Wizard, Evocation) · Demonmistress Elvrae Veythrae (Lvl 11 Wizard with Summoner dedication)
- **Force:** 520 drow + 680 auxiliaries = **1,200 total**
- **Council stance:** War, but on arcane terms — bombardment first, infantry second. Thalara has spent two decades making Veythrae's magic indispensable to every planned operation.
- **Allies:** Thalmyr (intelligence-for-magic exchange), Zhaelor (theological cover for experimental casualties), Velouryn (kuo-toa corridor funds research)
- **Rivals:** Xilrath (Vel'dryn views magic as support; Thalara views infantry as expendable screens)
- **Doctrine:** Area-denial first (darkness fields, web walls, fireball corridors), then fiend release into the broken line. Weakness: devastating in the first hour, exhausted by the third; fiend containment breaches are uncommon but not unknown.

→ [house-veythrae-the-shadowfire-cadres.md](house-veythrae-the-shadowfire-cadres.md)

### House Dravun — "The Fang Legion"

*A service provider that is also very difficult to kill.*

- **Council rank:** Minor (1 vote)
- **D3 mapping:** House Noquar (rank 4 — beastmasters/merchants)
- **Specialty:** Monopoly on trained Underdark creatures — quaggoths, giant spiders, shadow weasels, rothé herds, pack lizards
- **Command:** Matron Yvra Dravun (Lvl 13 Cleric with Ranger dedication) · Beastlord Krael Dravun (Lvl 9 Ranger with Animal companion) · Mistress of Fangs Nelyth Dravun (Lvl 8 Rogue with Alchemist dedication)
- **Force:** 400 drow + 1,200 auxiliaries = **1,600 total**
- **Council stance:** Immediate war — revenue begins on deployment, not on victory. Xilrath (40 giant spiders currently on lease) and Zhaelor (quaggoth assault column committed) are both paying clients.
- **Allies:** Xilrath (primary beast-lease contract), Zhaelor (quaggoth assault commitments)
- **Rivals:** None structural — Thalmyr has attempted to plant informants inside the beast pens; Nelyth has caught two.
- **Doctrine:** Beasts hit first and absorb fire; Spear Cohort follows into the disorder; Nightfangs take the commanders. Does not hold ground. Weakness: vulnerable to morale collapse if quaggoths are routed early.

→ [house-dravun-the-fang-legion.md](house-dravun-the-fang-legion.md)

### House Thalmyr — "The Silent Blades"

*The house that makes every other house's battles go the way Thalmyr requires.*

- **Council rank:** Minor (1 vote; swing vote in deadlocks)
- **D3 mapping:** House Everhate (rank 5 — spies and assassins)
- **Specialty:** Informant networks, assassin cells, surface infiltration, cipher operations
- **Command:** Matron Lythrae Thalmyr (Lvl 12 Cleric with Rogue dedication) · Shadow Captain Drevan Thalmyr (Lvl 9 Rogue, Assassin) · Whisper Adept Kyssil Thalmyr (Lvl 7 Investigator with Rogue dedication)
- **Force:** 350 drow + 500 auxiliaries = **850 total**
- **Council stance:** Cautious support for war. The campaign creates intelligence-collection opportunities that peacetime does not; chaos is Thalmyr's optimal operating environment.
- **Allies:** Veythrae (intelligence-for-magic exchange), Dravun (information-for-access exchange)
- **Rivals:** Velouryn (competing courier and outer-passage networks; two Velouryn couriers have disappeared — Kyssil is running that investigation)
- **Doctrine:** No open battle. Eliminate command before the first official sword is drawn. Poison supply lines. Vanish. Weakness: no heavy frontline capacity whatsoever.

→ [house-thalmyr-the-silent-blades.md](house-thalmyr-the-silent-blades.md)

### House Velouryn — "The Venom Syndicate"

*They do not fight wars; they decide who can afford to.*

- **Council rank:** Minor (1 vote)
- **D3 mapping:** House Godeep (rank 6 — craftsmen/merchants)
- **Specialty:** Trade networks (Underdark and surface), smuggling, alchemical poisons, surface contraband
- **Command:** Matron Ulvrae Velouryn (Lvl 11 Cleric with Rogue dedication) · Trade Captain Malryk Velouryn (Lvl 8 Rogue, Mastermind; currently stationed at the Shard Warrens) · Poisonmistress Thivra Velouryn (Lvl 7 Alchemist, Bomber/Poisoner)
- **Force:** 300 drow + 400 auxiliaries = **700 total**
- **Council stance:** Conditional war support. Ulvrae has demands: first-access rights to surface trade routes post-conquest, caravan protection from military requisition, a seat at the logistics table.
- **Allies:** Veythrae (kuo-toa corridor smuggling funds both houses), Xilrath (surface-access promise)
- **Rivals:** Thalmyr (overlapping courier and intelligence networks in the outer passages)
- **Doctrine:** Avoid combat. Pre-planted explosive kegs, poison gas through ventilation shafts, hired mercenaries absorb contact while drow extract personnel and ledgers. Weakness: militarily the weakest house — any force that closes to melee overwhelms them.

→ [house-velouryn-the-venom-syndicate.md](house-velouryn-the-venom-syndicate.md)

### House Zhaelor — "The Lash of Lolth"

*The house no one mocks, even in private.*

- **Council rank:** Minor (1 vote)
- **D3 mapping:** House Despana (rank 3 — clerical fanatics)
- **Specialty:** Religious authority — controls the great temple, inquisitions, and public ceremonies; decides what is heresy
- **Command:** Matron Zarila Zhaelor (Lvl 14 Cleric/Warpriest with Champion dedication) · Zealot Commander Rhazek Zhaelor (Lvl 9 Champion with Cleric dedication) · Firebrand Cleric Syrris Zhaelor (Lvl 7 Cleric/Warpriest)
- **Force:** 250 drow + 200 auxiliaries = **450 total**
- **Council stance:** Immediate and total war. Zarila believes. This sincerity is more unsettling than cynicism would be — she cannot be bought off with trade concessions.
- **Allies:** Xilrath (shared "now" timeline), Dravun (quaggoth units committed for shock assault column)
- **Rivals:** Veythrae (competing claims to be Lolth's truest instrument — divine supremacy vs. arcane supremacy)
- **Doctrine:** Zealot Warband charges first, Indoctrinated Fanatics flood the second wave, Clerical Core supports with mass-morale and divine curses. Burns hot and exhausts fast. Weakness: low staying power beyond the opening assault; effective only against targets that can be broken quickly.

→ [house-zhaelor-the-lash-of-lolth.md](house-zhaelor-the-lash-of-lolth.md)

## Non-Council Houses

These houses hold no seats and cast no votes. They are outside the power structure by design — and protected by it for the same reason.

---

### House Vorryn — "The Grey Ledger"

*Every house needs them; no house can destroy them.*

- **Council rank:** Non-council (no vote)
- **D3 mapping:** House Aleval (rank 7 — scribes/archivists). House colors: gray and pale violet. Sigil: open book on a web.
- **Specialty:** City archives — slave registries, contract records, census, legal arbitration, founding charter custody
- **Command:** Matron Syrn Vorryn (Lvl 7 Cleric, neutral doctrine) · Archive Mistress Vaeryn Vorryn (Lvl 5 Wizard with Rogue dedication) · Registry Warden Thressil Vorryn (Lvl 4 Investigator with Rogue dedication)
- **Force:** 100 drow + 100 auxiliaries = **200 total**
- **Political position:** Genuine neutrality. Velouryn uses Vorryn manifests as shipping cover (at a premium Syrn logs in her cipher journal). Vorryn scribes update the Grand Priestess's war council model weekly. Syrn maintains a private cipher journal of house corruption — never threatened, universally understood to exist.
- **Allies / Rivals:** None declared. Transactional relationships with all six council houses.

→ [house-vorryn-the-grey-ledger.md](house-vorryn-the-grey-ledger.md)

### House Draethi — "The Black Warrant"

*Feared by commoners, useful to every house, trusted by none.*

- **Council rank:** Non-council (no vote)
- **D3 mapping:** House Tormtor (rank 8 — torturers/executioners). House colors: black and rust-red. Sigil: iron spider on a rack.
- **Specialty:** Judicial execution, warrant delivery, official torture program, Pit Cell administration beneath the Arena
- **Command:** Matron Rhevnis Draethi (Lvl 8 Cleric with Rogue dedication) · Chief Executioner Vorath Draethi (Lvl 6 Fighter with Champion dedication) · Warrant-Keeper Drezmyr Draethi (Lvl 5 Rogue with Investigator dedication)
- **Force:** 110 drow + 100 auxiliaries = **210 total**
- **Political position:** Executes council orders. Holds no opinion on policy publicly. Privately holds leverage over three houses via the sub-basement: a captured Thalmyr agent, two diverted Velouryn humans, and sealed documentation of a Zhaelor financial corruption scheme — none of which Rhevnis has used yet.
- **Allies / Rivals:** None declared. Zhaelor commissions inquisition support most often; Xilrath submits the highest warrant volume.

→ [house-draethi-the-black-warrant.md](house-draethi-the-black-warrant.md)
