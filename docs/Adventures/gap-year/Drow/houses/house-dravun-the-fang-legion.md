## House Dravun — "The Fang Legion"

The beastmaster house. Dravun's wealth and influence rest entirely on their monopoly over the Underdark's most dangerous creatures — quaggoths, giant spiders, shadow weasels, rothé herds, and the pack lizards that keep armies fed on long marches. They do not posture about ideology. They lease muscle, collect fees, and cultivate the only asset that every house needs and none can replicate quickly: trained beasts. The Fang Legion is less an army than a service provider that also happens to be very difficult to kill.

---

## Politics & Council Position

Dravun votes for war because war is their best market. Every house must lease beasts for a campaign of this scale, and Dravun sets the terms. Matron Yvra has already signed contracts with Xilrath (spiders, currently 40 on lease) and verbally committed Quaggoth units to Zhaelor's shock assault column.

The house stays deliberately neutral in the Xilrath-Veythrae rivalry. Taking sides would cost them contracts. They have leverage over everyone and intend to preserve it.

**Council Stance:** Immediate war — revenue begins on deployment, not on victory.

**Allies:** Xilrath (current primary beast-lease contract), Zhaelor (quaggoth assault commitments)

**Rivals:** None structurally — Dravun maintains transactional neutrality. Thalmyr has attempted to place informants inside Dravun's beast pens; Nelyth has caught two.

## Doctrine

**Beasts first. Drow follow. Nightfangs take the commanders.**

The standard Dravun engagement: quaggoth packs and giant spiders hit the front, absorbing fire and breaking formation. Web-Archers suppress flanks and prevent retreat. Once the enemy line is disordered, the Spear Cohort pushes through. The Nightfangs use the chaos to eliminate command.

The house does not attempt to hold ground. They break things and leave.

**Weakness:** Beast-heavy formations are vulnerable to morale collapse. If the quaggoths are routed, the handlers cannot hold the line alone. Dravun forces do not fight prolonged sieges well — their greatest advantage, the quaggoth charge, only works in open cavern space.

**Darkness Tactics:** Shadow weasels and giant spiders operate normally in total darkness; quaggoths have limited darkvision but fight by smell and sound as effectively. Handlers carry signal drums to redirect beast units without visibility.

## Personality & Politics

Pragmatic to the point of cynicism. Yvra Dravun does not believe in Lolth's mandate or Vel'dryn's grievances. She believes in contract terms, breeding records, and the knowledge that every house in Xil needs her cooperation more than she needs any single one of them.

Nelyth is the house's edge — her venom programs have produced toxins that can drop a quaggoth in six seconds, which she uses as both a product and a demonstration. She also runs counter-intelligence. Thalmyr wants eyes inside Dravun's pens; Nelyth has made that consistently expensive.

Krael is quieter than his sister and more dangerous in the field. He communicates with quaggoth pack leaders through a system of drum codes and scent markers that he developed over twenty years. The Nightfangs regard him as a god.
