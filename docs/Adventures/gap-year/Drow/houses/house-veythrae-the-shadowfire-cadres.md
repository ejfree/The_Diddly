## House Veythrae — "The Shadowfire Cadres"

The arcane powerhouse of Xil. Half wizard academy, half laboratory, the house obsesses over shadowfire research — a proprietary fusion of flame and darkness magic that no other house can replicate. They disdain brute force as a failure of imagination, but their battle-mages, bound fiends, and enchantment-laced soldiers give them battlefield impact that the sword-houses cannot match. The problem is that they cannot sustain a war alone, which is why they need Xilrath — and why they resent Xilrath for it.

---

## Politics & Council Position

Veythrae votes for war but insists on controlling the magical assets. Matron Thalara has spent two decades positioning the house as indispensable: every siege requires her shadowfire cadres, every ritual requires her arcane council. This dependence is leverage, and she intends to collect on it once the surface burns.

The rivalry with Xilrath is structural. Vel'dryn commands larger armies; Thalara commands the magic that makes those armies lethal. Neither house can win without the other, and both know it. The alliance holds through mutual need and mutual contempt.

**Council Stance:** War, but fought on Veythrae's terms — arcane bombardment first, infantry second.

**Allies:** Thalmyr (intelligence sharing; Thalmyr's dossiers feed Veythrae's targeting), Zhaelor (shared theological cover for experimental casualties), Velouryn (kuo-toa corridor smuggling funds Veythrae's research; a financial relationship neither house advertises)

**Rivals:** Xilrath (doctrine disputes; Vel'dryn views magic as support, Thalara views infantry as expendable screens)

## Doctrine

**Soften with magic, then shatter with fiends.**

The Shadowfire Cadres open with area-denial: darkness fields, web walls, fireball corridors. The Goblin Skirmishers absorb the enemy's first volley and disorder their line. Once the enemy is suppressed and disorganized, Elvrae releases the bound demons into the collapse.

The Shadowguard protects the mages throughout — their job is to keep the cadres alive, not to fight the enemy directly. If a cadre is isolated, the whole formation becomes fragile.

**Weakness:** The fiend-binding is not always stable. Containment breaches during battle are uncommon but not unknown. If the Arcane Council is disrupted before the demons are deployed, the whole sequence fails. Extended attrition grinds the house down — they are devastating in the first hour and exhausted by the third.

**Darkness Tactics:** All Shadowfire spells layered over magical darkness gain enhanced penetration. Shadowguard are trained to fight effectively in zero-visibility conditions; goblin skirmishers are not.

## Personality & Politics

Arrogant, precise, and convinced of their own indispensability. Thalara does not posture — she calculates. She has spent years accumulating knowledge of every house's vulnerabilities, and she shares that knowledge selectively through the Thalmyr intelligence network in exchange for targeting data.

The house treats experimental casualties as acceptable. Thralls, goblins, apprentices who survive insufficient doses — these are data. The priesthood of Lolth has never objected, which Thalara regards as implicit endorsement.

She believes, with some justification, that the war only goes well if she controls the arcane component. She is prepared to let Xilrath take the glory so long as Veythrae takes the profit.
