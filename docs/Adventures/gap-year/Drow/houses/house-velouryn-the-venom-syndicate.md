## House Velouryn — "The Venom Syndicate"

The merchant house. Every house in Xil ultimately depends on trade that flows through Velouryn-controlled routes — fungal reagents, surface metals, exotic poisons, slave-stock, and the alchemical components that Veythrae's labs require. The Venom Syndicate is named for its original specialty but has expanded into a full-service logistics operation. They do not fight wars; they decide who can afford to.

Trade Captain Malryk Velouryn is currently stationed at the Shard Warrens on the Sunless Sea, running the external supply chain through the kuo-toa smuggling corridor that bypasses Xilrath's D3 checkpoint. His ledgers document the Veythrae-kuo-toa arrangement in enough detail to be politically explosive.

---

## Politics & Council Position

Velouryn holds a swing vote and sells it to whoever offers the best trade concession that cycle. The house does not have ideological commitments. Matron Ulvrae supports the war because conquest opens new trade territories and because Vel'dryn has promised Velouryn first-access rights to surface trade routes once the campaign succeeds.

The tension with Thalmyr is ongoing — both houses run courier networks and informant pools in the outer passages, and their agents occasionally find themselves watching each other rather than the target. Two Velouryn couriers have disappeared in circumstances that Kyssil Thalmyr investigated with suspicious efficiency.

**Council Stance:** Conditional war support. Ulvrae has demands: trade route guarantees post-conquest, protection of Velouryn caravans from military requisition, and a seat at the logistics planning table.

**Allies:** Veythrae (the smuggling arrangement through the kuo-toa corridor funds both houses), Xilrath (surface-access promise)

**Rivals:** Thalmyr (overlapping courier and intelligence networks)

## Doctrine

**Avoid combat. Poison clouds. Hired blades. Tunnel geometry.**

Velouryn does not field armies. When forced to fight, they collapse tunnel approaches with pre-planted explosive powder kegs, release poison gas through ventilation shafts, and let hired mercenaries absorb contact while the drow extract personnel and ledgers. The house has standing extraction protocols for every waystation on their route network.

The alchemical corps maintains stockpiles of contact poisons and inhalants calibrated for confined-space deployment. A Velouryn ambush in a trade tunnel is extremely effective against a numerically superior force that cannot maneuver.

**Weakness:** Militarily the weakest house in Xil. Any force that can weather the initial poison deployment and close to melee overwhelms them. The house knows this and does not allow confrontations to reach that stage if they can avoid it.

**Darkness Tactics:** Enforcers carry pre-mixed smokepots — substances that foul darkvision and create mixed darkness/smoke environments. The effect is disorienting to drow and lethal to most other Underdark races at close range.

## Personality & Politics

Transactional and unsentimental. Ulvrae has no personal stake in the war beyond the commercial opportunity it represents. She is not cruel for pleasure, but she is entirely indifferent to suffering that does not affect her margins.

Malryk is the house's most capable operative and its greatest liability. He is comfortable enough at the Shard Warrens that there are questions about whether he is managing the Velouryn operation there or has become part of the Warrens' ecosystem. His ledgers are meticulous. His personal loyalties are opaque.

Thivra is the one house member who enjoys her work in a way that makes the others slightly uncomfortable. Her poison variants are innovations, and she treats each new formula as a creative achievement.
