## House Thalmyr — "The Silent Blades"

The spy house. Thalmyr fields the smallest military force on the council and the longest reach. Where Xilrath counts swords and Veythrae counts spells, Thalmyr counts secrets. They maintain a network of informants, sleeper agents, and paid eyes that extends from the outer passages (see D1→F2 in the hex map) through every major faction in the Underdark. The house's military assets exist not to win pitched battles but to ensure that every other house's battles go the way Thalmyr requires.

---

## Politics & Council Position

Thalmyr holds the balance of the council. Their vote is the swing vote in any three-way deadlock, and every other house has, at some point, paid for it. Matron Lythrae does not announce her price — she lets others arrive at the conclusion that cooperation is cheaper than opposition.

The house has dossiers on every senior commander in Xil. They have shared selected contents with Veythrae in exchange for magical support for their surveillance operations. They have sold redacted versions to Dravun in exchange for priority beast leases. They have burned nothing on Xilrath, because Vel'dryn's grief-driven recklessness is currently more useful than leverage.

**Council Stance:** Cautious support for war. The campaign creates intelligence-collection opportunities that peacetime does not, and the chaos of a war is Thalmyr's optimal operating environment.

**Allies:** Veythrae (intelligence-for-magic exchange), Dravun (information-for-access exchange)

**Rivals:** Velouryn (competing smuggling and courier networks; overlapping informant pools in the outer passages)

## Doctrine

**No open battle. Eliminate command. Poison supply lines. Vanish.**

Thalmyr does not fight wars — they ensure the enemy cannot fight theirs. A campaign begins with targeted assassination of opposition leadership, disruption of their logistics, and the introduction of disinformation into their communications. The assassin cells operate before the first official sword is drawn.

If forced into open battle, Thalmyr ambushes. They will not hold a line. They will not assault a fortified position. They retreat, regroup, and find the gap.

**Weakness:** No heavy frontline capacity whatsoever. In a direct siege or open-cavern engagement, they collapse. The house is aware of this and plans around it — any situation that forces Thalmyr into direct battle is a Thalmyr failure, not a Thalmyr tactic.

**Darkness Tactics:** All assassin cells operate in total darkness as a baseline. Hand signal codes replace verbal coordination. Poison delivery is calibrated for close-quarters dark tunnel work.

## Personality & Politics

Patient, methodical, and genuinely dangerous in a way that sword-houses are not. Lythrae has never raised her voice in council. She does not need to. Everyone in that room knows she has read their correspondence.

Kyssil is younger than her reputation suggests and considerably more ruthless. She is responsible for two unsolved disappearances among Velouryn's courier staff — unsolved because Thalmyr runs the investigation.

Drevan is the one who scares the other houses. He does not threaten. He acts, and then he attends the next council meeting as though nothing happened.

The house's outer-passage spy trail runs from Sector D1 through F2 on the D1-2 hex map — a relay of embedded informants tracking surface approach vectors and reporting to Kyssil's archive staff.
