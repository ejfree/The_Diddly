## House Draethi — "The Black Warrant"

The executioner house. Draethi carries out the council's death sentences, manages the Pit Cells beneath the Arena, runs the city's official torture program, and holds the keys to every prisoner the council would rather the public not know about. No house likes them. Every house has needed them. That asymmetry is the foundation of Draethi's position: feared by commoners, useful to every matriarch, trusted by none — which is exactly how Rhevnis prefers it. Trust creates obligations. Usefulness creates fees.

D3 mapping: **House Tormtor** (rank 8 — torturers and executioners).
House colors: black and rust-red. Sigil: iron spider on a rack.

---

## Political Position

Draethi holds no council seat. They are the city's instruments, not its rulers — or so the council tells itself. In practice, Rhevnis has accumulated enough leverage to make several matriarchs uncomfortable. She holds it quietly, uses it selectively, and does not announce it. The execution warrants she carries are issued by council authority; the sub-basement beneath her estate operates on her authority alone, and no other house has been invited to inspect it.

The house operates at the intersection of force and legality. Draethi enforcers deliver warrants, escort condemned prisoners, and staff the Pit Cell management. Draethi torturers provide interrogation services to any house willing to pay the rate, and the rate is not negotiable. The clerks process intake, log prisoner status, and file completion reports. Everything is documented. Rhevnis is meticulous about this.

**Council Stance:** No seat, no vote. Executes council orders. Does not offer opinions on policy. Does offer, on occasion, a quiet reminder that the warrant for a particular individual was never formally closed.

**Allies:** No formal alliances. Zhaelor commissions inquisition support most frequently (Zarila's heresy hunts require arrests and interrogations that Draethi provides). Xilrath submits the highest volume of warrants. Both relationships are transactional and undisguised.

**Rivals:** No declared rivals. Thalmyr's attempt to place an informant inside the Draethi estate was unwelcome; the agent is currently in the sub-basement. Rhevnis has not contacted Thalmyr about this. She is deciding the right moment to do so.

## Doctrine

**Legal mandate. Net first. Everything is documented.**

Draethi enforcers do not improvise. Every action is predicated on a warrant, an order, or an established tariff. This is not squeamishness — it is the legal architecture that allows Draethi to operate inside every other house's space without being treated as an invader. A Draethi enforcer arriving at your door with a warrant is the council arriving at your door. Resisting a Draethi enforcer is resisting a council order. No house wants to establish that precedent.

In physical encounters, the doctrine is net-and-subdue. The contact poison loaded on torturer saps induces Clumsy 2 on a failed Fortitude save — enough to end most resistance without killing a subject that may have information. Enforcers carry restraints, not execution implements; those are reserved for the formal execution ground.

The sub-basement is outside the doctrine. What happens there is Rhevnis's business and no one else's.

**Weakness:** Draethi's authority is entirely institutional. Strip the legal mandate — if the council collapses, or if a house successfully delegitimizes their warrant authority — and the enforcers are just 40 drow with nets. Rhevnis is aware of this. Her leverage over multiple houses is partly insurance against exactly this scenario.

**Darkness Tactics:** Enforcers are trained for corridor work — narrow passages, controlled entrances, dragging a subject out of a doorway without letting them flee into the city. They do not fight open-cavern engagements.

## Personality & Politics

Rhevnis does not raise her voice. She has the flat patience of someone who has watched a great many people run out of options. She holds three things of value simultaneously: execution warrants for named drow not yet in custody (potentially saleable to the subjects themselves), a captured Thalmyr intelligence operative, and documented evidence of a Zhaelor financial corruption scheme. She has used none of these cards. She is waiting for the moment when playing one of them solves a problem she currently has, rather than one she might someday have.

She is not cruel by preference. She is precise. Pain is a tool, like a ledger or a net. She calibrates it for the result required and stops when the result is achieved. This professionalism is, in its way, more unsettling than sadism would be.

Vorath is the public face of the house — the one drow who carries execution warrants in person to subjects who might resist. He has done this forty-one times. Thirty-eight subjects came with him to the execution ground. He considers this an excellent rate.

Drezmyr runs the warrant room with the disposition of a clerk who knows that every document in his custody represents leverage. He has never misplaced a warrant. He has, on occasion, noticed that certain warrants were processed more slowly than others, for reasons that are not recorded in the intake log.
