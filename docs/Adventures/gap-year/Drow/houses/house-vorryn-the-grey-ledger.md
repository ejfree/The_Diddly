## House Vorryn — "The Grey Ledger"

The archivist house. Vorryn keeps the city's memory — every slave registry, every founding contract, every execution warrant, every deed and census and debt ledger. They have no council seat and no army. They have something more durable: every other house needs them, which means no house can afford to destroy them. The Grey Ledger is the name for both their force and their function — quiet, gray, indispensable, and watching everything.

D3 mapping: **House Aleval** (rank 7 — scribes and archivists).
House colors: gray and pale violet. Sigil: open book on a web.

---

## Political Position

Vorryn holds no council seat. They are deliberately outside the council's structure, which is the source of their safety. When six houses are fighting for power, the house that keeps the records of all six is untouchable as long as it remains genuinely neutral. Matron Syrn has refused bribes, declined alliance offers, and returned political favors uncashed. Her neutrality is the product, not a limitation.

The house maintains official registry booths in the Great Market (slave registration, contract recording) and harbor masters at the Docks. Vorryn scribes update the war council's stone model of the Vault weekly on the Grand Priestess's commission. Every house matriarch has, at some point, asked Vorryn to produce a document that required a flexible interpretation of the facts. Syrn obliges at premium pricing and logs the request in her cipher journal.

**Council Stance:** No seat, no vote. Serves whoever holds power. Will continue to serve the winning side regardless of which house that turns out to be.

**Allies:** Transactional relationships with all six houses. Velouryn uses Vorryn manifests as cover for surface goods shipments (Syrn knows; the fee is high). The Grand Priestess commissions weekly map updates (the only non-house entity Vorryn formally serves).

**Rivals:** None declared. Draethi and Vorryn operate in adjacent spaces (warrants and records) and maintain a careful professional distance. Neither house has reason to make an enemy of the other.

## Doctrine

**Neutrality as protection. Records as leverage. Never draw a weapon first.**

Vorryn's security model is political, not military. Their garrison exists to deter opportunistic theft — not to fight a house war. If a house threatens Vorryn directly, Syrn's response is to remind the other five houses that their records are in Vorryn's vaults and that destabilizing Vorryn destabilizes the city's legal architecture. This has always worked.

If the estate is physically attacked, the garrison buys time for Syrn and the Archive Mistress to invoke the emergency cipher protocol: vault contents are locked under Ward of Vorryn (a permanent institutional seal that requires a sitting council majority to break), and a sealed copy of the most politically explosive records is deposited with the Grand Priestess. The message: *whatever you're looking for, it's now the Grand Priestess's problem too.*

**Weakness:** The garrison cannot hold against a coordinated assault. A house that decides the records are worth destroying regardless of cost could take the estate by force. No house has yet concluded that this calculation works in their favor. If one ever does, Vorryn has no military answer.

**The cipher journal:** Syrn's private record of house corruption is the nuclear option. She has never threatened to release it. Every house knows it exists. This is sufficient.

## Personality & Politics

Syrn Vorryn is the quietest person in any room she enters. She does not perform power. She does not need to — everyone in Xil knows that she has read documents they have never seen, knows agreements they have tried to bury, and has written down things they would prefer did not exist in ink.

She is lawful evil in the strict sense: she operates within whatever legal structure exists, enforces it consistently, and benefits from being the entity that defines what "legal" means in this city. She has no particular loyalty to Lolth's agenda. She has considerable loyalty to institutional continuity.

Vaeryn is the house's edge — she runs forgery detection for the city's official documents, which means she also knows how every known forgery was constructed. She has declined three offers from Thalmyr to share this knowledge and one offer from Velouryn to participate in a manifest operation more directly than the standard arrangement allows.

Thressil is younger, field-practical, and increasingly aware that the war will make the records more valuable and the house more exposed. She has begun quietly building the Registry Warden role into something with more physical security capacity — carefully, within budget, without alarming the Matron.
