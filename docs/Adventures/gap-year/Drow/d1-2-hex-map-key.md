# D1-2 Hex Map Key — Descent into the Depths

The hex map covers the Underdark journey from the G3 (Fire Giant Lair) exit to the outer gates of Xilthraen. Organized into seven sectors by depth and dominant faction. Travel is roughly linear, but side branches exist at C1, F2, and G1.

| Sector | Region | Dominant Threat |

## Sector A — The Entry Warrens
*Just below the G3 fire giant lair exit. Troglodyte territory.*

### A1 — The Shattered Gate
The tunnel collapse that marks the boundary between giant country and the Underdark proper. Collapsed masonry from a dwarf-built passage predating the giants. Troglodyte sentries lurk in the rubble, reporting to a warchief further in. Strong smell of musk — the first warning.

- **Hazard:** Unstable ceiling in the western branch; loud combat risks a rockfall
- **Creature:** 4–6 troglodyte scouts, 1 warchief (at A2)
- **Sign of Drow:** Old iron shackle bolted to the wall — slave caravan anchor point, abandoned
- **See:** [a1-the-shattered-gate.md](locations/a1-the-shattered-gate.md)

### A2 — The Stench Tunnels
The main troglodyte warrens — a series of low-ceilinged passages thick with musk, rot, and the smell of raw meat. The tribe here is substantial: 40–60 adults plus young. They treat the drow as gods and gods as things to be feared. They will not willingly aid travelers unless intimidated or bribed with surface flesh.

- **Key NPC:** Warchief Gruthlak — stupid, aggressive, but will parley if dominated or offered tribute
- **Captive:** One surface human, half-mad, shackled in the pen. Knows nothing useful but may become a liability
- **Loot:** Crude spears, stinking hides, one drow signal horn (taken from a dead patrol)
- **See:** [a2-the-stench-tunnels.md](locations/a2-the-stench-tunnels.md)

### A3 — The Bone Heap
A massive refuse midden at the edge of troglodyte territory — bones, shell, broken armor, and the remains of everything the tribe has eaten or killed in the past decade. Also serves as a territorial marker: anything beyond is not theirs.

- **Hazard:** Carrion crawler nesting in the deeper refuse; the smell masks its approach
- **Discovery:** Among the bones — pieces of a drow patrol slaughtered here roughly one month ago. One body still has legible House insignia: House Thalmyr. This was not a random kill.
- **Transition:** Past the Bone Heap, the tunnels widen and the air becomes notably cooler
- **See:** [a3-the-bone-heap.md](locations/a3-the-bone-heap.md)

### A4 — Drow Checkpoint
Two rival patrol contingents hold this passage: males to the southwest, females to the northeast. Despite internal rivalry, both groups cooperate fully in a combined, intelligent attack and defense. The two male pickets are actively watching both directions of the passage — the party is likely spotted before they spot anyone here.

**Description**
The primary passage runs southeast to northwest through this area (use encounter piece II facing either end). The tunnel walls glow faintly with patches of phosphorescent lichen, casting a dim blue-green light across the stone floor. The passage is wide enough to accommodate two distinct groups holding their respective ends: a male contingent clustered to the southwest and a female contingent to the northeast. Two male picket guards are posted at either end of the passage, nearly invisible against the rock, watching for any movement along the corridor.

Side caves open off the main area and hold bedrolls of sleeping silks, small boxes of provisions, and individual kits (eating utensils, cups, and similar gear).

- **See:** [a4-drow-checkpoint.md](locations/a4-drow-checkpoint.md)

### A5 — Illithid Spy Post
The westernmost spy post of the illithid colony. The drow know it exists but have not dealt with it due to recent reverses. An active interrogation is underway: the stronger illithid is questioning a bound drow merchant captive near the pool. If the illithids sense the party, the captive will be killed first — he knows too much to be left alive for rescue. The wererats will ambush; the illithids will open with mind blasts.

**Description**
Uses encounter piece II (reversed). The cave area opens off the main tunnel passage; sparse phosphorescent lichen on the walls means normal vision functions only up to 10 feet — beyond that, darkness. The cave contains a dark pool of still water in one area. An alcove off the pool area serves as the illithids' camp. Opposite the pool, small side caves — tight enough that even a gnome must crawl — house the wererat nest.

A bound drow merchant sits near the pool, wrists tied, bruised but alive. The stronger illithid looms over him, conducting a methodical interrogation about drow clan alliances, power groups, and ongoing feuds between noble houses. The weaker illithid stands further back, ostensibly watching the tunnel approaches. Wererats in rat form act as watchers roughly 60 feet up and down the tunnel from the cave entrance. The remaining wererats are stationed to either side of the passageway, guarding the illithids.

- **See:** [a5-illithid-spy-post.md](locations/a5-illithid-spy-post.md)

### A6 — The Weeping Wall
A stretch of tunnel where mineral-rich groundwater seeps through a 40-foot section of porous limestone, leaving pale mineral deposits and a constant slow drip. The wet stone glitters faintly in torchlight. The passage is uncomfortably narrow — single file for larger creatures — and the sound of dripping masks footsteps from both directions.

- **Hazard:** Patch of yellow mold (10 feet across) colonizing the wettest section of the wall, mid-passage. It is the same pale color as the mineral deposits. DC 15 Perception or Nature to identify before disturbing it.
- **Encounter:** A wounded orog (lone survivor of a surface raiding party that went wrong, not affiliated with the troglodytes) has stopped here to drink and dress wounds. He is hostile by default but desperate — a successful DC 12 Persuasion or Medicine offer can shift him to wary neutrality. He knows a route around the troglodyte warrens entirely (a low crawl-tunnel he found by accident). He will trade that route for rations and a clean bandage.
- **Atmosphere:** The dripping creates a persistent white-noise that makes the passage feel isolated from both directions. PCs who stop here will find it oddly peaceful — which is exactly why the orog chose it.
- **See:** [a6-the-weeping-wall.md](locations/a6-the-weeping-wall.md)

### A7 — The Split Passage
The tunnel forks into three branches for the first time since the Shattered Gate. Two of the branches rejoin within 200 feet and lead toward Sector B. The third descends sharply and does not rejoin — it goes somewhere else entirely.

- **Navigation:** No markings. The troglodytes know to take the left or center fork and avoid the right. They will not say why if asked.
- **The Right Fork:** Descends at roughly 30 degrees for 400 feet before opening into a small cavern occupied by a **rust monster** and the corroded remains of whoever last came down here with metal equipment. The cavern has no other exits. Loot accessible only if the rust monster can be dealt with: a ceramic flask (intact, contains a potion of climbing), two non-metal weapons (a bone shortbow, a stone-headed war pick), and a leather satchel containing a partial map of the Sector B grimlock territory drawn by someone who did not survive to use it.
- **The Correct Forks:** Both are safe. The center fork is slightly faster; the left fork passes close to a troglodyte watch-post (2 sentries, drowsy) that can be avoided with DC 13 Stealth.
- **See:** [a7-the-split-passage.md](locations/a7-the-split-passage.md)

### A8 — The Fungal Shelf
A naturally terraced section of tunnel where a step-change in geology has created three broad stone shelves, each about waist height, running along the left wall for 60 feet. The shelves are carpeted in dense fungal growth — zurkhwood caps, glowing puffballs, and several clusters of edible cave mushrooms. The air here is noticeably different: warmer, more humid, faintly sweet.

- **Resource:** 4 days of rations worth of edible fungi accessible with a DC 10 Nature check to identify safe from harmful. On a failure, the forager harvests 1d4 violet fungus clusters mixed in with the good ones.
- **Settlement:** A pair of **myconid sprouts** tend the shelf, docile and largely unaware of travelers unless directly approached. Their adult (a single myconid adult, non-violent) resides in a hollow at the back of the third shelf. It communicates through rapport spores and will share a single useful fact about the passage ahead — it has observed everything that passes through here for years and remembers all of it.
- **What the Myconid Knows:** Its most recent memory of note is a group of eight humanoids moving fast toward the surface three days ago — not drow, not troglodyte, moving in formation, carrying something wrapped in cloth that was approximately the size and shape of a person. They did not stop. (GM: adapt this detail to whatever is relevant to the campaign.)
- **See:** [a8-the-fungal-shelf.md](locations/a8-the-fungal-shelf.md)

## Sector B — Grimlock Depths
*Mid-upper descent. No natural light survives here. Grimlock hunting grounds.*

### B1 — The Blind Hunt
A network of passages where grimlocks maintain active hunting grounds. They have mapped every inch by sound and touch. Any light source is immediately detectable — grimlocks will not approach light directly but will circle and wait for it to fail or move into a choke point.

- **Tactical Note:** Combat here in darkness or dim light strongly favors grimlocks. PCs without darkvision or echolocation are heavily disadvantaged.
- **Creature:** 8–12 grimlock hunters in rotating pairs; they withdraw to alert the village at B2 if overmatched
- **Discovery:** Scattered evidence of a caravan that passed recently — snapped chains, blood, a dropped surface-world coin
- **See:** [b1-the-blind-hunt.md](locations/b1-the-blind-hunt.md)

### B2 — The Eyeless Village
The grimlock settlement proper — roughly 80 individuals packed into a labyrinthine cave cluster. Low stone walls, crude totems made of bone and stone, no light of any kind. They keep a handful of enslaved surface folk as laborers, blinded. The village elder is ancient and has a rudimentary relationship with the drow: they pass caravans through unmolested in exchange for being left alone.

- **Key NPC:** Elder Vrakt — blind, scarred, pragmatic. Knows the path to D3 and will tell PCs if convinced they are drow-sanctioned (or sufficiently threatening)
- **Captive:** 2–3 blinded surface laborers. Will need assistance navigating if freed.
- **Intel:** Elder Vrakt knows the drow checkpoint at D3 rotates patrols every three days. Current rotation is "the Spider-marked ones" (House Dravun handlers with quaggoths)
- **See:** [b2-the-eyeless-village.md](locations/b2-the-eyeless-village.md)

### B3 — The Echoing Chasm
A long natural chasm with a 60-foot drop to unseen water below. The only crossing is a natural stone arch, 4 feet wide. The acoustics are extraordinary — a whisper at one end is audible as a murmur at the other. Grimlocks use it as an ambush point.

- **Hazard:** Rock piercers clinging to the ceiling above the arch; movement on the bridge may dislodge 1d4
- **Encounter:** If the PCs made noise at B1/B2, a grimlock ambush waits at the far side of the arch
- **Feature:** Carvings in the stone — pre-grimlock, possibly deep gnome (Svirfneblin). Symbols suggest this was once a trade route
- **See:** [b3-the-echoing-chasm.md](locations/b3-the-echoing-chasm.md)

### B4 — The Blind Market
A secondary grimlock camp operating at the edge of Elder Vrakt's authority — a splinter group of 15–20 younger grimlocks who have broken from the Eyeless Village's drow-accommodation policy and set up their own operation in a wide crack off the main passage. They are not at war with Vrakt's group, but they are not bound by the truce either.

- **What they do:** Trade in stolen drow equipment — signal horns, torch-chalk, manacle keys, a small hoard of Undercommon-inscribed tablets taken from ambushed couriers. They do not speak to outsiders as a rule but will trade with anyone who finds them by sound (DC 14 Perception to hear the tapping signals they use for communication).
- **Key figure: Scratch** — a grimlock who lost his primary echolocation in a rockfall and compensates through exceptionally fine touch-sensing. He deals in written material he cannot read himself, trading by weight and apparent value. He has three tablets in current stock that are Velouryn courier communications — unopened, wax-sealed. He has no idea what they contain.
- **Hazard:** The crack entrance is a tight squeeze (Medium creatures fit; anything larger requires a DC 13 Athletics check and makes noise). The grimlocks inside will hear any failed check.
- **Drow Threat:** Elder Vrakt does not officially know this market exists. A Dravun patrol sergeant does — she has been quietly purchasing recovered equipment here for six weeks, off-manifest, paying in raw iron. If the PCs are here when she arrives (1-in-8 chance per hour), the situation becomes complicated.
- **See:** [b4-the-blind-market.md](locations/b4-the-blind-market.md)

### B5 — The Cold Seep
A section of passage where a sub-zero underground current vents through fissures in the floor, dropping the ambient temperature sharply and coating the stone in a thin layer of condensation that becomes near-invisible ice in the cold zone. The cold zone extends roughly 80 feet along the passage.

- **Hazard:** The ice is DC 15 Acrobatics to cross at speed; failure means falling prone and sliding 1d6 × 5 feet toward the nearest fissure. The fissures are 2 feet wide — too narrow to fall into, but a prone creature sliding into one takes 1d6 cold damage and is restrained until pulled free.
- **Creature:** A **cloaker** has taken up residence against the ceiling of the cold zone, benefiting from the temperature's effect on its prey's perception. It has been feeding on grimlocks that use this passage and has learned that cold-slowed prey is easier to manage. It will not attack a group of four or more — it has learned caution.
- **Discovery:** Three frozen grimlock bodies are visible in the fissures, partially preserved by the cold. One clutches a wrapped bundle — a grimlock-made map, crude but accurate, showing the passage layout between B3 and C1 with four locations marked by a scratched symbol that appears to mean "food here." Two of the four locations correspond to known encounter sites. Two do not.
- **See:** [b5-the-cold-seep.md](locations/b5-the-cold-seep.md)

### B6 — The Painted Tunnels
A 300-foot gallery tunnel where grimlock pictographs cover every surface floor to ceiling — mineral pigments and bioluminescent paste layered over centuries, glowing faint blue-green in darkvision. A small permanent settlement of ~35 grimlocks has formed around the site: seven active painters, support workers, children in training, and the community's chief interpreter, an ancient female called **Grel** who has been reading and dictating the record longer than anyone can recall.

- **The Settlement:** Living quarters behind the reading chamber at the far end. Organized and modest — sleeping niches, food cache, pigment stores in four work alcoves off the main gallery. Not a warrior community; they scatter if combat starts.
- **The Record:** Three periods — pre-drow (large grimlock range, trade with svirfneblin, and a recurring large figure always moving deeper), the drow arrival (range contracts, gnome contact stops, the deep figure vanishes), and the current era (six house-marks distinguishable; Dravun marked "tolerated"; Zhaelor marked "unpredictable — distance maintained").
- **The Recent Addition:** Fresh pigment at the gallery's far end — two weeks old, inscribed by Grel herself despite her tremored hands. Eight figures moving toward the surface; one circled; beneath the circle, a symbol that appears nowhere else in the record. Grel translates it as *"carries two names."* She will not say whose.
- **Grel:** Knows the party is present before they reach the archway. Will tolerate them if no light source is used and no aggressive gesture made. Speaks Undercommon. First words, if she speaks: *"You are not from below. You are going below. Sit."* Will trade intelligence on the B–D corridor (house patrol patterns, the thing in the deep water at E-sector, the Dravun sergeant from B4) for patience and stillness.
- **Mechanical Value:** DC 14 History or Insight after studying the paintings — advantage on Charisma checks with grimlocks for the rest of the descent.
- **See:** [b6-the-painted-tunnels.md](locations/b6-the-painted-tunnels.md)

###  B7 — The Rendering Pits
A wide natural depression in the cavern floor roughly two hours past the D3 checkpoint, where the tunnel opens briefly before narrowing again toward the E-sector descent. The pits themselves are former mineral pools, long since drained and repurposed — eight stone basins, each 15 feet across and 6 feet deep, currently used by House Dravun as a field processing station for quaggoth handlers and slave-conditioning crews.

The official function is logistical: slaves who arrive in poor condition after the surface journey are assessed here, medically stabilized if worth the cost, or culled. Quaggoths that have become erratic or uncontrollable are reconditioned here using methods that Dravun's handlers prefer to conduct away from Xilthraen's more fastidious houses.

- **Population:** 2 Dravun handlers (Lvl 4 Rangers, beast-master configuration), 6 quaggoths in varying states of conditioning, 14–22 slaves in the assessment pens, 1 Dravun apothecary managing the medicinal side
- **The Apothecary — Thessivir Dravun:** Female, Lvl 5 Cleric (Death domain), pragmatic to the point of clinical detachment. She is the most knowledgeable person at the Pits and the most dangerous to cross. She maintains a field formulary of sedatives, pain compounds, and accelerants that she has developed personally over twenty years of handler work. She will trade apothecary supplies for information about surface-world medicinal sources — she is always looking for new inputs.
- **What's Unusual:** One of the pits has been sealed with a stone cover and iron bolts, recently. The handlers do not discuss what is inside. The quaggoths give it a wide berth. Sounds emerge from it approximately twice per day — rhythmic, low, not distress. Thessivir sealed it herself four days ago and has been checking on it every six hours. She will not explain.
- **Encounter Dynamic:** The Pits are not a checkpoint — they do not log traffic. Caravans that know the route pass through or around without stopping. The handlers will wave through anyone displaying valid house credentials without much scrutiny. PCs without credentials can attempt to pass as a conditioning crew or a medical detail (Deception DC 14) — Thessivir is the one likely to notice a discrepancy.
- **Intel Available:** The handlers know D3's current patrol rotation precisely (they deal with Nerath Dravun regularly and do not like her). Thessivir knows something is wrong at the E1 port — supply shipments for the Pits have been inconsistent for six weeks, and the irregularities track with the Veythrae cargo route disruptions, though she has not connected those dots yet.
- **See:** [b7-the-rendering-pits.md](locations/b7-the-rendering-pits.md)

## Sector C — The Deep River
*Mid-descent. Underground river system. Terrain is the primary threat.*

### C1 — The River Fork
The first major navigation decision. An underground river splits here — the left fork flows toward the Sunless Sea (sector E); the right fork leads into a dead-end kuo-toa fishing tunnel used for smuggling. Carved drow arrows (decades old) point left.

- **Terrain:** Wet stone, slippery. The river runs fast — falling in means a DC 18 Athletics check to grab a ledge within 60 feet or be carried toward C2 with force
- **Encounter:** A drow slave caravan may be camped here — 2 drow handlers, 8 goblinoid slaves, 1 quaggoth. They are returning to Xilthraen and are weary. Could be ambushed or possibly infiltrated.
- **Side Branch:** The right fork — if explored — leads to a hidden kuo-toa cache (dried fish, a water-breathing potion, crude silver idol)
- **See:** [c1-the-river-fork.md](locations/c1-the-river-fork.md)

### C2 — The Ford of Pale Stone
The only reliable river crossing for miles. White stone slabs create a natural ford — knee-deep in the center but with fast current. Lurkers above cling to the ceiling, waiting for creatures to slow in the water. Drow have carved warning glyphs into the approach stone (in Undercommon).

- **Hazard:** 2 lurkers above; they drop when creatures are mid-crossing
- **Discovery:** Drow toll-carving on the far bank — a count of slaves passing through, last updated roughly two weeks ago. High traffic recently.
- **Loot (on a Lurker):** A digested drow arm still gripping a +1 hand crossbow
- **See:** [c2-the-ford-of-the-pale-stone.md](<locations/c2-the-ford-of-the-pale-stone
- .md>)

### C3 — The Fungal Shore
The riverbank widens into a massive fungal forest — towering zurkhwood columns, glowing shriekers, violet fungus clusters. Beautiful in an alien way. The fungi provide dim phosphorescent light, the first natural illumination since Sector A. Spore clouds are a hazard; sound triggers shriekers which alert everything within 500 feet.

- **Hazard:** Violet fungus (2d4 within the deeper grove); shriekers at B and C density throughout
- **Encounter:** A hunting party of 4 quaggoths (House Dravun) is harvesting zurkhwood here. They carry a carved Dravun token and will not attack if shown a drow insignia. Otherwise extremely aggressive.
- **Resource:** Zurkhwood planks can be fashioned into a raft or simple weapons. Dried fungi from here are edible (basic rations, 3 days' worth accessible with a Nature check)
- **See:** [c3-the-fungal-shore.md](locations/c3-the-fungal-shore.md)

## Sector D — The Descent Passages
*Lower Underdark. Drow presence begins. The tunnel narrows toward controlled territory.*

### D1 — The Slave Road
The main corridor becomes noticeably more worked — the rough tunnel widens into a deliberate passage, walls smoothed by decades of traffic. Iron rings bolted at intervals for slave-chains. Drow directional markings in Undercommon every 200 feet. This is a recognized route.

- **Atmosphere:** The sound of distant chains, faint rhythmic chanting (a drow work-song echoing from far ahead)
- **Encounter:** A slave caravan coming the other direction — 3 drow soldiers, 12 mixed humanoid slaves, 1 drider outrider. The drider is the authority figure and will demand papers from the PCs if challenged.
- **Discovery:** A dead slave left by the road — recently deceased, shackled to a ring. On a small scrap of hide hidden in the sole of their boot: a crude map drawn in blood, marked in Elvish with one word: *Thalmyr.* (House Thalmyr plant among surface captives)
- **See:** [d1-the-slave-road.md](locations/d1-the-slave-road.md)

### D2 — The Pillared Hall
A vast natural cavern the size of a cathedral, filled with enormous stalagmites and stalactites that have partially fused into floor-to-ceiling pillars. A traditional waypoint on the Slave Road — the stone floor around the central cluster shows centuries of campfire scars, discarded rope, and food refuse.

- **Scale:** Roughly 200 feet across; multiple entrances/exits including two secret side passages (DC 20 Perception to spot)
- **Encounter:** A resting drow patrol — 4 soldiers, 1 priestess, 2 giant lizard mounts. They are on their way back to Xilthraen after a surface raid and are in good humor. They will be suspicious of the PCs but can be bluffed with convincing cover.
- **Secret:** One of the side passages leads 300 feet to a House Thalmyr drop-point — a hollowed stalagmite containing a wax-sealed message cylinder and a vial of antitoxin
- **See:** [d2-the-pillared-hall.md](locations/d2-the-pillared-hall.md)

### D3 — Drow Checkpoint *(Prophecy: "the stink of the Deep Hunger" passes this gate)*
The first permanent fortification in the descent — a fortified post controlling a narrow choke point where the Slave Road passes through a natural stone gate (two massive columns with a crossbow platform above). House Dravun rotation currently on duty.

- **Garrison:** 1 checkpoint commander (Dravun priestess, Lvl 6), 6 drow soldiers, 2 quaggoths on leash, 1 giant spider patrolling the ceiling
- **Fortification:** Stone barracks cut into the cavern wall (right side); iron portcullis that can be dropped from above; a signal bell connected by rope to Xilthraen (D3 arrival city)
- **Routine:** All traffic is logged. Caravans pass with tokens; new drow require House insignia and explanation of business. Non-drow slaves pass in irons only.
- **Challenge:** The commander, Overseer Nerath Dravun, is meticulous and paranoid. She runs random strip-searches of caravans. She dislikes House Thalmyr operatives intensely and will hold anyone who smells of spy-work.
- **Bypass Options:** A maintenance tunnel above the portcullis (DC 22 Athletics to climb, requires disabling the spider); a smugglers' side passage known to House Velouryn operatives (requires intel from C1 encounter or earlier)
- **See:** [d3-svartjet-crossing.md](locations/d3-svartjet-crossing.md)

## Sector E — The Sunless Shore
*The underground lake. D2 territory begins here. Kuo-Toa presence.*

### E1 — The Shore of Black Sand
The Slave Road terminates at a wide crescent beach of black volcanic sand at the edge of the Sunless Sea. What greets the PCs is not a beach — it is a functioning port town, roughly 400 inhabitants, built from the collision of two cultures that need each other and trust each other not at all.

The beach divides naturally by use and by faction:

**Southwest — The Drow Quay.** A permanent drow installation: stone-cut dock structures, iron mooring rings, a fortified harbormaster's post flying House Xilrath colors. Three to five slave barges are in berth at any given time, off-loading surface captives or taking on cargo for the return run. The quay runs under a permanent torchlight regime — the drow do not share the kuo-toa's comfort in total dark. A garrison of 12 drow soldiers (Xilrath rotation), 2 drow harbormasters, and a shifting population of house agents, merchants, and slave handlers occupy the structures cut into the southern cliff. The Quay has its own gate that can be barred against the rest of the beach. It is barred at night.

**Northeast — The Kuo-Toa Fishery.** A sprawling tangle of racks, nets, weirs, drying platforms, and low mud-brick structures that smell powerfully of fish and brine. The kuo-toa population here (roughly 120 permanent residents, plus transients) operates a significant fishery supplying the main settlement across the water. They maintain three shrines to Blibdoolpoolp along the waterline, each attended by a junior priest who blesses each catch. The eastern dock is entirely kuo-toa — outsiders permitted only by invitation or escort.

**The Middle — The Neutral Strand.** Roughly 200 feet of beach between the two installations where neither side claims authority. In practice this is where everything interesting happens: a chaotic market of mixed traders (grimlock labor-brokers, a small derro alchemical stall, surface-world contraband moving in both directions), two rival tavern-tents serving something that is technically alcohol, and a semicircle of permanent stalls run by a family of deep gnome traders who arrived three years ago and have somehow made themselves indispensable to both sides by being aggressively neutral and extremely good at logistics. The gnome family patriarch, **Odwick Rustmantle**, knows every major player on the beach, tracks every cargo movement, and will sell that information to anyone for the right price — including the PCs.

- **Atmosphere:** The smell of brine, fish oil, cookfires, and slave-pens. The sound of two languages spoken simultaneously across a space where neither side quite turns its back on the other. Bioluminescent algae in the waterline gives the beach an eerie cold glow at night.
- **The Tension:** The drow harbormaster, **Sergeant Vethris Xilrath** (Lvl 5 Fighter), and the kuo-toa fishery elder, **Archdeacon Gluupik**, have maintained a functional if deeply hostile working relationship for eleven years. Both understand that the port requires both factions. Both are currently under pressure from above — Vethris from G1's heightened alert orders, Gluupik from Kloarek's intensifying religious demands — and are handling it by becoming less flexible with each other and with outsiders.
- **Slave Processing:** The Quay's iron pens hold 30–80 captives at any given time, sorted by destination house. A Velouryn clerk (seconded from G2) handles documentation at a stone desk inside the harbormaster's post. The same clerk is the node through which the Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route manifests are filtered before reaching Thrissik at the dock.
- **The Double-Spire Rocks:** Visible from the Neutral Strand, roughly 80 feet out in the shallows to the east — the formation marking the Drowned Vault entry (E2). Local knowledge says it is bad luck to swim near them. Both factions agree on this, which is unusual. Neither faction has a coherent explanation for why.
- **See:** [e1-the-rendering-pits.md](locations/e1-the-rendering-pits.md)

### E2 — The Drowned Vault
Not the open crossing — a collapsed cavern system accessible from the eastern shore of the Sunless Sea, below the waterline, where a partial air pocket has persisted for centuries inside a partially flooded deep gnome vault. The entry is a submerged arch visible only to anyone who dives in the shallows near a distinctive double-spire rock formation at E1's eastern edge.

- **Access:** DC 16 Perception to spot the arch from above water; the swim through is 40 feet, manageable for a prepared party but lethal without a light source or water breathing
- **Interior:** Three chambers — a flooded antechamber, a partially-air chamber with a collapsed ceiling letting in a trickle of brine, and a dry inner vault that has been sealed for at least 200 years. The svirfneblin stonework is unmistakably old: carved resonance columns, a stone table with an intact inscription in deep gnome script, a sealed chest (trapped — contact poison on the latch, DC 17 to detect)
- **The Inscription:** A transit record listing convoys that passed through this checkpoint before the vault flooded. One entry, dated in the old gnomish calendar, lists a cargo simply as *"the three voices — do not open below the third level."* No further explanation. The chest is sealed with a lock that matches the inscription's symbol.
- **Hazard:** A giant blind albino crayfish (CR 5) has nested in the flooded antechamber. It attacks anything that disturbs the silt. It is very large for the space and fighting it in water is difficult.
- **Connection:** The dry vault has a second sealed door in the far wall — stone, fitted with the same resonance-column technology Lisa uses in the Ironbell Warren. It leads somewhere below. It has not been opened. (GM note: this is the "path below" from Fragment Three of the speaking-stone prophecies at Blingdenstone — see [i-blingdenstone-deep-gnome-city.md](locations/i-blingdenstone-deep-gnome-city.md))
- **See:** [e2-the-drowned-vault.md](locations/e2-the-drowned-vault.md)

### E3 — The Kuo-Toa Pier
The kuo-toa dock complex on the far shore — a maze of rickety wooden platforms, nets, eel cages, and fish-drying racks. Actively busy; 20–30 kuo-toa at any time. Two whips (armed kuo-toa enforcers) guard the main entrance to the settlement warrens. The smell is overwhelming.

- **Key NPC:** Dock Warden Shlipik — kuo-toa whip, mercenary attitude, will accept bribes (surface metal, alcohol, live surface creatures to sacrifice) to pass travelers through with minimal hassle
- **Danger:** If the PCs damaged or stole anything at E1, Shlipik already knows
- **See:** [e3-change.md](locations/e3-change.md)

### E4 — The Blind Reach
A sea cave on the western shore of the Sunless Sea, accessible only by swimming from a submerged entry point or by a narrow ledge path from E1 that is unmarked and easy to miss (DC 18 Perception, or following someone who knows the route). No light visible from the water. No sound escapes. Seven drow are living here. They have been here for three weeks.

- **Unit:** The **Shroud** — a House Veythrae special operations cell operating under direct orders from Veythrae's senior weaponmaster, bypassing the normal Xilthraen chain of command and therefore invisible to G1's intel board. Six operatives plus one commander. They are not a patrol. They are not a caravan escort. They are a retrieval unit.
- **Commander: Velith Veythrae** — female, Lvl 8 Ranger (Gloom Stalker), cold and precise. She has the manner of someone who has been patient a very long time and is running out of patience. She knows the Drowned Vault exists. She does not yet know how to open it.
- **Operatives:** 4 drow fighters (Lvl 5, dual-blade, built for close-quarters cave work), 1 drow arcanist (Lvl 6, specializing in divination and structural analysis — here to read whatever is inside the vault), 1 kuo-toa guide who knows the E-sector waters and is being paid in something unspecified. The guide is terrified and being extremely cooperative.
- **Mission:** Locate and access the Drowned Vault. Extract the contents of the sealed chest. Do not interact with the kuo-toa settlement beyond what the guide facilitates. Leave no evidence of Veythrae presence. This is the mission Serath vel Velouryn's manifests were tracking — the "architectural diagrams" in the G2 dead drop are structural analyses of the vault's outer door, produced by the arcanist in week one.
- **Current status:** They have found the vault. The arcanist cannot open the inner door. Velith has sent one runner back toward Xilthraen with a request for a specific text from Veythrae's house archive. The runner has not returned. Velith is deciding how long she waits before she goes in without the text.
- **If the PCs arrive:** Velith will not initiate combat unless she must. She will immediately assess whether the PCs are more useful alive (as vault-openers, as a distraction for the kuo-toa, as a source of information about the door) or dead (as potential witnesses). She is genuinely curious what surface-worlders are doing this deep. She will not share her mission. She will offer safe passage through E-sector in exchange for information about the vault door — framed as a trade, with no explanation of why she wants to know.
- **What she knows that she won't volunteer:** The contents of the sealed chest are not loot. They are a weapon. Veythrae's matriarch believes it is the thing that will break the current house stalemate in Xilthraen. Velith does not know exactly what it is, only that it is old and it is dangerous and her matriarch wants it badly enough to run a black operation to retrieve it.
- **See:** [e4-the-blind-reach.md](locations/e4-the-blind-reach.md)

## Sector F — The Kuo-Toa Warrens
*D2: Shrine of the Kuo-Toa. Increasingly fanatical religious territory.*

### F1 — The Approach Warrens
The outer kuo-toa village — a crude but densely populated cavern complex of mud-and-stone structures, fish markets, slave pens (mostly grimlocks and captured surface folk), and shrines to Blibdoolpoolp on every corner. The population here is roughly sane, just alien. Trade is possible.

- **Population:** ~200 kuo-toa commoners, 15–20 whips
- **Market:** Dried fish, crude net-armor, kuo-toa glue (powerful adhesive), water-breathing unguents, minor aquatic magic items
- **Intel:** A kuo-toa trader named Glibglib has dealt with Velouryn smugglers before and recognizes House Velouryn insignia. He can provide a route through the Shrine that avoids the inner sanctum — for a price.
- **Threat:** The kuo-toa archpriest, High Servant Kloarek, considers surface-worlders to be gifts to the Sea Mother. He has standing orders to send non-drow captives to F3.
- **See:** [f1-the-approach-warrens.md](locations/f1-the-approach-warrens.md)

### F2 — The Shrine Approach
The passage narrows into a ritual corridor — walls covered floor-to-ceiling in carved kuo-toa iconography, all depicting Blibdoolpoolp consuming surface-worlders. Priestesses patrol in groups of three, singing. Torch-like bioluminescent coral provides light. The kuo-toa here are noticeably more fervent — eyes wide, muttering prayers constantly.

- **Hazard:** Several corridors are trapped with net-drops and adhesive-floor patches (DC 16 Perception)
- **Encounter:** A procession of 8 kuo-toa carrying a surface elf (alive, bound, destined for sacrifice). Intervention will trigger a full religious riot.
- **Discovery:** In a side alcove — a drow prayer-mat and the residue of a campfire. Recent. House Thalmyr spy passed through here within the last week.
- **See:** [f2-the-shrine-approach.md](locations/f2-the-shrine-approach.md)

### F3 — The Shrine of the Sea Mother
The sacred heart of D2. A vast flooded cathedral — 10 feet of brine on the floor, illuminated by enormous bioluminescent corals. The Blibdoolpoolp idol (15 feet tall, carved obsidian, lobster claws) dominates the far end. High Servant Kloarek holds court from a raised dais surrounded by kuo-toa monks and armed whips.

- **Kloarek (Lvl 10 Cleric):** Believes the drow are agents of the Sea Mother and extends them professional courtesy. He tolerates passage in exchange for tribute. He is also secretly negotiating with House Veythrae — the drow are trading arcane knowledge for kuo-toa cooperation.
- **Hostage Opportunity:** A surface party captured here 3 days ago. One survivor. Rescuing them may complicate travel but gains a potential ally.
- **The Secret:** The Veythrae-kuo-toa deal is documented in a wax-tablet in Kloarek's private chamber (DC 18 Thievery to access). The deal involves allowing Veythrae operatives to use the Sunless Sea as a covert supply route, bypassing the D3 checkpoint — meaning Veythrae is smuggling resources without Xilrath's knowledge.
- **See:** [f3-the-shrine-of-the-seamother.md](locations/f3-the-shrine-of-the-seamother.md)

## Sector G — The Final Approach
*The tunnel approach to Xilthraen. Drow-controlled throughout. Heavy patrol density.*

### G1 — The Way-Station
A permanent drow logistics post — a carved-out cavern used as a supply depot, barracks, and patrol coordination point. Multiple houses run patrols through here; the commander is House Xilrath.

- **Garrison:** 8 drow soldiers (Xilrath), 2 Xilrath priestesses, 1 senior sergeant (Lvl 7 Fighter)
- **Supply:** Ration packs, spare manacles, torch-chalk (drow marking wax), a caged quaggoth awaiting pickup by Dravun
- **Intel Board:** A carved stone notice-board with patrol schedules, missing slave reports, and a recent edict from Matron Vel'dryn ordering heightened surface-intruder watch (posted 5 days ago — they're expecting trouble)
- **See:** [g1-the-way-station.md](locations/g1-the-way-station.md)

### G2 — The Slave Road Terminus
A large holding pen — the last waypoint before Xilthraen where incoming slave caravans are processed, catalogued, and sorted by house allocation. Iron pens, a pair of drow clerks (House Velouryn), and 2–4 arriving caravans at any given time.

- **Population:** 30–50 slaves in transit, 4 drow guards, 2 Velouryn clerks (Lvl 4 Rogues)
- **Opportunity:** The clerks are bribable and have access to entry papers. A successful high-DC Deception check + bribe could get the PCs forged documentation for Xilthraen entry.
- **Danger:** A House Zhaelor inquisitor squad (3 zealots + 1 Lvl 6 Cleric) is here conducting a search for heresy. They are looking for evidence that someone freed slaves from a previous caravan. (This may or may not be the PCs depending on earlier choices.)
- **See:** [g2-the-slave-road-terminus.md](locations/g2-the-slave-road-terminus.md)

### G4 — The Sorrow Ledge
A stone shelf carved 15 feet above the main G-corridor, between G1 and G2. Used informally by House Zhaelor as a punishment post — prisoners are chained here where passing caravans can see them. No official record. Access via a hidden iron ladder behind a natural stone buttress.

- **Current Occupants:** One dead (Naleth, Zhaelor soldier, 2 days gone) and one alive (Serath vel Velouryn, Velouryn courier, day 6)
- **Intelligence:** Serath carries detailed knowledge of the Veythrae-kuo-toa supply route, the location of Velouryn's G2 dead drop, and the identity of a Zhaelor asset inside Velouryn's G2 operation. She will trade all of it for her life and her reputation intact.
- **Complication:** The Zhaelor sergeant who put them here has no fixed return schedule — 1-in-6 chance per hour the PCs spend in the area. Serath has been watching G2 from the ledge for six days; her patrol intelligence is current and accurate.
- **See:** [g4-the-sorrow-ledge.md](locations/g4-the-sorrow-ledge.md)

### G3 — The Veil of Shadows
The last natural cavern before the city gates. The tunnel ceiling rises to 80 feet and the walls fall away — the PCs emerge onto a promontory overlooking an enormous underground void. Across the void, barely visible through haze and fungal spore-cloud: the lights of Xilthraen.

- **Atmosphere:** Priestesses of Lolth bless passing soldiers from a shrine carved into the promontory wall. War banners hang from ceiling anchors. The sound of the city — drums, distant shouting, the smell of cookfires — drifts across.
- **Final Obstacle:** A 20-foot-wide rope-and-stone bridge spans the void. Two drow bridge-wardens check tokens. Beyond the bridge: the Gate of Silken Chains (entrance to Xilthraen proper — see [xilthraen-overview.md](xilthraen-overview.md))
- **Prophecy Note:** This is the threshold crossing. The Black Coil fragment: *"the blade-kiss of the Black Matrons"* — Xilthraen begins past this bridge.
- **See:** [g3-the-veil-of-shadows.md](locations/g3-the-veil-of-shadows.md)

### G4 — The Sorrow Ledge

**Type:** Punishment post / intelligence encounter
**Location:** A wide stone shelf carved 15 feet above the G-sector approach tunnel, between G1 and G2. Visible from below only as a row of iron rings bolted into the ceiling — the chains that hang from them pass through slots in the shelf's edge, keeping prisoners suspended over open air while still standing on stone.
**Visibility:** PCs with darkvision or who look up will notice the ledge and the silhouettes. PCs moving through quickly may not.

## Overview

The Sorrow Ledge is not on any official map. It predates the current G1 way-station by at least a century — carved, the stonework suggests, by whatever drow operation used this approach before Xilthraen formalized the Slave Road. Its current use is informal: House Zhaelor punishes personnel here when it wants a sentence that is public enough to be a warning and quiet enough not to require House Council acknowledgment.

The logic is cruel and efficient. Caravans passing below see the chained figures. The message is received. No paperwork.
