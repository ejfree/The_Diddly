# House Xilrath — "The Black Blades"

The war engine of Xilthraen. Two decades of preparation, a stockpile of logistics no other house can match, and a Matron who wants to burn the surface to the ground — not for Lolth's glory alone, but for deeply personal reasons she does not hide.

---

## Why the War

Matron Mother Xilrath Vel'dryn has had a long, catastrophic entanglement with the royalty of Arenal. On again. Off again. On again. Off again. Whatever it was — alliance, negotiation, something more, something less — it ended badly. Repeatedly. The royalty of Arenal made her look foolish, and nothing is more dangerous than a drow matron who has been made to feel foolish.

She does not frame it as personal. She calls it holy war. She calls it Lolth's will. But every priestess in the house knows: the Matron wants Arenal's rulers dead, their bloodline ended, and their memory ground to ash. The war against the surface is real. The logistics are real. The army is real. And underneath all of it is a woman who got her heart broken one too many times and decided to respond at scale.

Lolth approves entirely. A personal vendetta that fuels a genuine conquest is exactly the kind of mortal impulse she delights in.

## Doctrine

**Anvil and hammer under poison volleys.**

The Blade Cohorts fix the enemy and hold the line. The Quaggoths and Goblins slam the flanks. The Void-Archers cover both with poisoned bolts throughout. The Temple Cadre supports from the rear with healing and battlefield curses.

**Weakness:** If the Void-Archers are neutralized, advances stall — the poison pressure is what keeps enemies from simply enduring the frontal push.

**Darkness Tactics:** All drow units gain a circumstance advantage fighting inside magical darkness — Void-Archers suppress with bolts while Blade Cohorts advance through the dark.

---

## Personality & Politics

Pragmatic, ruthless, and presently consumed by hatred. Vel'dryn does not indulge in the cruelty-for-its-own-sake that marks lesser houses. She cares about winning. Her logistical network — fungus farms, rothé herds, slave-trains — has fed this army for twenty years. She is not interested in posturing.

The rivalry with House Veythrae is genuine and structural: she needs their magic, and she resents needing it. The alliance holds because neither house can win without the other.

**Council Stance:** Immediate war. Every delay is a personal insult layered on top of the ones she is already nursing.

**Allies:** Dravun (beasts for war), Zhaelor (fanaticism aligned with aggression)
**Rivals:** Veythrae (magical supremacy vs. military doctrine)
