## The Mindspire — Illithid Colony of the Sunless Sea

### Overview

The Mindspire rises from the center of the Sunless Sea — a volcanic rock island roughly a quarter-mile across, dominated by a single basalt spire that climbs 200 feet before terminating in an observation platform no one who goes there returns to describe. The island has no dock. The water around it is cold and still regardless of what the rest of the lake is doing. Fish do not swim near it.

The colony is home to roughly 180 mind flayers, organized in the typical illithid hierarchy around a central **Elder Brain** — a creature of indeterminate age that has been growing in the cyst beneath the spire for somewhere between 200 and 800 years, depending on whose estimate you trust. It communicates to the colony in psionic waves the duergar at the far shore describe as "a pressure behind the eyes." It has not spoken to anything outside the colony in living memory.

Until approximately sixteen months ago.

---

### Location

**Northeast Underdark, island in the Sunless Sea.** The Mindspire is visible from the eastern and northern shores as a dark needle against the cavern ceiling. The island is surrounded by deep water — the lake floor beneath it has never been sounded. Three nautical approaches:

- **Western approach** — toward the kuo-toa piers; the kuo-toa have a long-standing arrangement with the colony and will not interfere with Mindspire-flagged vessels (vessels distinguished by a black sail with no markings)
- **Southern approach** — toward the Shard Warrens shore; commercial traffic
- **Eastern approach** — deepest, least-used; leads to underwater passages that connect to flooded cave systems no surface-world map records

Getting to the island without invitation requires crossing deep open water, avoiding the things that swim below the surface, and then scaling a sheer volcanic rock face with no visible entrance.

Getting an invitation requires being something the Elder Brain wants.

---

### Scale

Illithid colonies are not measured in population the way surface cities are. The meaningful numbers:

- **Mind flayers:** ~180, organized in clusters of 8–12 called *tentacles*
- **Ulitharid** (greater mind flayer): 2 — the Elder Brain's direct extensions, rarely seen
- **Thralls (intellect devourers):** ~60 active, more in reserve
- **Indoctrinated servants:** ~400 (mostly aquatic: kuo-toa fanatics, water-breathing humanoids, captured travelers; a small number of surface-worlders taken from drow caravans)
- **Elder Brain:** 1. It is the city.

---

### The Elder Brain — Urath-Ssivix

The Elder Brain of the Mindspire has no name it has shared with non-illithids. Duergar records call it **the Pressure**. The kuo-toa call it **Ul-Blibdool** — a name that causes kuo-toa priests to react with reverence and fear in equal measure, suggesting the kuo-toa may have confused it with their own deity at some point, or been encouraged to.

Its campaign-relevant significance: **Urath-Ssivix has been in contact with Lolth.**

Not direct contact. The Elder Brain does not serve Lolth and would resist the framing. What happened was more subtle: approximately sixteen months ago, the Elder Brain detected a psionic disturbance propagating through the Underdark — a frequency it recognized as originating from Xoriat. It investigated. What it found was a pattern of psychic contamination already threading through the surface world's draconic population.

The Pale Choir. The insanity plague.

Urath-Ssivix did not create the Pale Choir. But it has been **amplifying it** — not because Lolth asked, but because a destabilized surface world with degraded draconic judgment is an environment in which illithid interests thrive. The Elder Brain recognized an opportunity and took it. This is entirely consistent with illithid ethics.

It does not know that Lolth is using the plague for a specific purpose (freeing Tiamat). If it did, it might reconsider. It does not like being used as an instrument in someone else's plan.

### The Spire Complex

#### The Base Level — The Wet Quarter

The island's shoreline is honeycombed with sea-level caves, accessible only by water. Indoctrinated servants and aquatic thralls live and work here — maintaining the colony's food supply (the illithids eat brains; the servants eat fish and subterranean fungus imported from kuo-toa markets), managing the few boats the colony keeps, and controlling traffic to and from the Shard Warrens.

#### The Middle Levels — The Thinking Halls

The carved interior of the spire. Smooth basalt corridors lit by bioluminescent organisms in ceiling tanks. The mind flayers spend most of their time here in extended telepathic communion — research, memory-sharing, the slow accumulation of everything their thralls observe in the outside world. There are no written records in the Mindspire. Everything is stored in the Elder Brain.

Notably: the Thinking Halls contain **memory-extracted archives** — physical objects (skulls, preserved brain tissue, crystal matrices) that serve as external storage for experiences too large or too old to keep in active circulation. Somewhere in these archives is a near-complete record of the Underdark's political history for the past three centuries.

#### The High Platform — The Listening Place

The top of the spire. Open to the cavern air, 200 feet above the water. The two Ulitharid use this as their observation point. It is never unoccupied. The view from the top encompasses the entire Sunless Sea and both eastern and western shores.

The platform is also where the Elder Brain sends focused psionic transmissions. Standing on it during a transmission is described by the one known survivor (a Shard Warrens trader who reached the island under extreme duress and somehow escaped) as "hearing every thought you have ever had speak at the same time, in a voice that is not yours."

#### The Deep Cyst — Where the Elder Brain Lives

Below the base level, accessible only through flooded tunnels. The cyst itself is a large natural chamber partially filled with a nutrient fluid the Elder Brain secretes and partially inhabits. The Elder Brain is not fully submerged — approximately a third of its mass floats above the fluid, densely covered in psychic receptor tissue that resembles a cluster of eyes that never close.

No one outside the illithid colony enters the cyst and leaves without fundamental alteration. The Elder Brain does not permit unauthorized visitors; it prefers conversation be conducted at a safe distance, through one of the Ulitharid.

### Subsettlements & Encounter Areas

#### Large Subsettlements

##### The Sunken Archives — Flooded Sub-Level (beneath the island)

Accessible only through flooded passages below the Deep Cyst, the Sunken Archives are a network of chambers that the Elder Brain has been filling with physical memory-storage objects for as long as the colony has existed. The water here is a few degrees warmer than the lake — the Elder Brain's metabolic processes heat the rock. Bioluminescent organisms cling to every surface, providing dim blue-green light to anyone who swims through.

**What's stored here:**

- Preserved skulls with memory-crystal inlays, containing the extracted experiences of thousands of thralls across three centuries
- Sealed stone cylinders holding preserved brain tissue — minds the Elder Brain considered worth archiving rather than consuming
- A section of intact kuo-toa architecture from a settlement that predated the current colony, submerged when the lake level rose; the Elder Brain moved its contents here and considers them of historical interest
- At the deepest point: three objects the Elder Brain has never described to any mind flayer — smooth black oblongs, each about the size of a duergar fist, that emit a faint psionic hum at a frequency slightly different from the Elder Brain's own broadcast

**Access:** Requires water-breathing, darkvision or a light source, and a successful navigation check to avoid the retrieval-thralls (aquatic indoctrinated servants who maintain the archives and treat unauthorized visitors as material to be archived).

**Role in Play:** The Sunken Archives contain the political history the PCs need. Getting there requires surviving the crossing, the retrieval-thralls, and the psychic pressure of being in proximity to thousands of stored minds. The Elder Brain is aware of anyone who enters. It does not immediately intervene, but it watches, and it will ask what was taken.

---

##### The Kuo-Toa Pilgrim Camp — North Shore of the Island

On the only flat stretch of island shoreline — a gravel beach perhaps 80 feet long on the north face, sheltered from the worst of the lake currents — the kuo-toa maintain a semi-permanent encampment for pilgrims making the crossing from the D2 warrens. Up to 50 kuo-toa at any given time, rotating in and out over a two-week cycle.

**Population:** 30–50 kuo-toa, plus 4–6 "blessed" (kuo-toa who have been brought fully under the Elder Brain's psionic influence and serve as its dedicated agents among their people)

**Structure:**

- Crude shelters of driftwood and dried seaweed
- A shrine platform — the central religious object of the camp, a carved stone bowl filled with lake water that the Elder Brain occasionally stirs with a psionic pulse, causing the water to move in patterns the kuo-toa interpret as divine messages
- A dock of lashed-together logs where the black-sailed colony boats moor when delivering goods or collecting pilgrims
- A separate, walled area where the "blessed" live apart from ordinary pilgrims — they do not speak except to relay the Elder Brain's messages, which they do in a monotone that unnerves even other kuo-toa

**Camp Warden:** Whip-Captain Shlrix (kuo-toa, Lvl 6 Cleric with Champion dedication) — appointed by Kloarek; runs the camp with religious discipline. She is not one of the "blessed" and privately finds them disturbing, which she will never say aloud and barely admits to herself.

**Role in Play:** The Pilgrim Camp is the most accessible part of the Mindspire for travelers who arrive uninvited. The kuo-toa will not immediately attack visitors; they will send word to the Thinking Halls and wait. If the PCs have Kloarek's endorsement or a token, they are received with wary hospitality. If not, Shlrix will ask pointed questions about their purpose while the "blessed" watch without blinking.

#### Smaller Encounter Areas

##### 1. The Skiff Moorings

Three black-sailed boats are kept in a sea-cave at the island's base, accessible through a low water-arch at lake level. The boats are maintained by aquatic thralls who communicate only in gesture. The boats themselves are unremarkable — flat-bottomed, single-mast designs built for the Sunless Sea's calm water — except that each has a small crystal set into the prow that glows faintly when the Elder Brain is broadcasting. When it pulses, the crystals synchronize. Anyone touching a prow crystal during a pulse must make a Will save or experience a three-second fragment of whatever the broadcast contains.

The mooring cave has two exits: the water arch (lake access) and a stone stair ascending into the Wet Quarter. The stair has a gate at the top that requires a specific psionic signature to open — the aquatic thralls generate it unconsciously. The PCs cannot generate it without assistance.

---

##### 2. The Processing Chamber

The first interior room travelers reach after ascending from the Wet Quarter — a smooth basalt chamber, oval, with drains in the floor. The ceiling has restraint hooks. There is no furniture. The room's purpose is assessment: mind flayers bring new arrivals here to determine their category.

There are currently three occupied restraint positions when the PCs arrive (adjust as needed): a duergar trader who came too close to the island's eastern shore and was collected three days ago (still coherent, terrified, has information about the east passage from Grimstone Holds); a kuo-toa who attempted unauthorized entry to the Thinking Halls (in a reduced state, not useful for conversation but might be freed as a gesture); and an empty set of restraints with recent blood on the hooks.

---

##### 3. The Amplification Node

A specific chamber in the lower-middle level of the Thinking Halls where the Elder Brain's Pale Choir broadcast is focused before transmission. The room is distinguished from others by a floor-to-ceiling crystal matrix — natural formations shaped by the Elder Brain's psionic influence over decades into an antenna array roughly 15 feet across. The crystals hum at a frequency just below audible. The broadcast travels from here down through the rock into the lake water.

Destroying or disrupting the matrix would significantly reduce the plague amplification in the Underdark corridor. The Elder Brain would be aware of this immediately and would consider it an act of war. If the PCs want to destroy it, they need either the Elder Brain's cooperation (possible if they explain the Lolth connection it doesn't know about) or a very fast plan for leaving the island afterward.

---

##### 4. The Garden

On the island's east face, where a shallow cave catches condensation from the cavern ceiling, indoctrinated servants maintain a food-production operation: fungal beds, three fish pools fed by a filtered seawater pipe, and a rack of drying frames for the preserved fish that make up most of the colony's non-illithid dietary needs. The Garden is tended by six servants who work in complete silence, moving between tasks with the mechanical smoothness of long-term thralls.

The Garden should be deeply unsettling. It is not. It is quiet, orderly, and smells of damp earth and brine. The servants look healthy. The fungus grows in organized rows. The fish are fat. Visitors who spend more than a few minutes in the Garden often report afterward that it was the calmest moment of their time on the island — and then spend several hours trying to figure out why that bothers them.

One of the servants is not a thrall. She is a surface-world human who chose to remain on the island after her group was intercepted — she was given the option, unusually, after the Elder Brain found her mind interesting. She tends the Garden by choice. She will speak to the PCs freely. Her perspective on the colony is coherent, thoughtful, and entirely alien to how any surface-worlder would expect a long-term captive to think.

---

##### 5. The Outer Watch

Four sentinel posts at the waterline, evenly spaced around the island, each a narrow stone shelf with a signal mirror angled toward the Thinking Halls' observation slits. Staffed by thralls in pairs — one with a mirror, one watching the water. The thralls do not react to most stimuli (a boat at distance, a swimmer, a noise from the lake) but respond instantly to specific triggers: anything approaching on the eastern approach route, anything submerging near the island's base, and any vessel without a prow-crystal.

The eastern watch post has been reporting a recurring anomaly for eleven days: a faint light, visible for a few minutes every night, approximately three miles into the eastern underwater passages. The Elder Brain has acknowledged the reports. It has not sent anything to investigate. The mind flayers have noticed that the Elder Brain has not sent anything to investigate, which they find unusual, and have privately discussed it in the way that mind flayers privately discuss things (which is to say, not privately at all by most species' standards, but with a careful avoidance of the subject in direct communication with the Elder Brain).

### The Kuo-Toa Arrangement

The kuo-toa of D2 have venerated the Mindspire for at least four generations. The exact origin of the arrangement is lost, but the current structure is clear: the kuo-toa High Servant Kloarek acts as the colony's primary external agent — supplying food, providing transport, passing messages, and occasionally delivering "gifts" (living beings, usually surface travelers, that the Elder Brain has expressed interest in). In exchange, the Elder Brain provides the kuo-toa with genuine divine-adjacent psychic phenomena that reinforce their religious framework. Whether Blibdoolpoolp is real is a theological question; whether the kuo-toa's prayers are sometimes answered by something is not.

This arrangement is why the kuo-toa in D2 (Sector F) are more coherent than the average kuo-toa cult — the Elder Brain keeps them functional because it finds chaos less useful than controllable devotion.

### The Pale Choir Connection

The insanity plague affecting the draconic Council is not a drow creation alone. The drow (via Xoriat tools provided by Lolth's servants) introduced the initial Xoriat-frequency contamination. The Elder Brain detected it, recognized the vector, and has been broadcasting subtle amplification pulses through the Sunless Sea's water (which transmits psionic frequencies exceptionally well).

**What the Elder Brain knows:**

- The plague originates in Xoriat-tainted material introduced into the surface world approximately two years ago
- It is targeting draconic cognition specifically — not random madness but a precisely tuned frequency
- It is working: the surface dragons are degrading faster than natural exposure would cause
- Something is using the plague as a distraction for a larger objective (Urath-Ssivix infers this but does not know what)

**What the Elder Brain does not know:**

- That Lolth intends to free Tiamat
- That the PCs are coming and are effectively immune to the plague's targeting (they are not dragons)
- That Fragment Four of the Blingdenstone prophecies describes its role as an amplifier: *"the plague is the spider's first thread — cast wide, cast early"*

**What this means for the PCs:**

If they reach the Mindspire and somehow negotiate access to Urath-Ssivix's knowledge, the Elder Brain can give them a near-complete picture of the plague's mechanism and propagation. It has no particular reason to do so — but it also has no reason to hide it from people who appear to be actively working against Lolth. The Elder Brain finds Lolth's plan more threatening than these particular travelers.

### Interacting with the Mindspire

#### Getting There

The kuo-toa will transport visitors to the island under very specific circumstances: if Kloarek endorses the visit, if the visitors carry a Mindspire token (obsidian disc, blank), or if the Elder Brain issues a direct psionic invitation (which travelers in the D1-D2 corridor occasionally receive in their dreams — a hand reaching up from deep water, no pressure, no threat, just an open palm).

#### Getting In

The base-level caves can be accessed from the water. Mind flayer thralls will intercept visitors; they do not attack unless visitors are hostile or try to ascend without guidance. They simply wait, blank-faced, until a mind flayer arrives to assess.

#### The Audience

All formal communication with Urath-Ssivix is conducted through an Ulitharid acting as voice. The Elder Brain communicates in concepts and images rather than language; the Ulitharid translates into Undercommon or (rarely) Common.

The Elder Brain is not malicious toward the PCs in the context of this arc. It is alien and self-interested. It will trade information for information. It will trade information for services (there are things it wants that mind flayers cannot obtain without attracting attention). It will not trade for coin, promises, or threats. It remembers everything and it will know if the PCs have lied to it — not during the conversation, but afterward, and it will adjust accordingly.

**Will save difficulty:** Spending extended time in the Elder Brain's presence requires periodic Will saves against psychic pressure. Failure doesn't mean thralldom — it means the Elder Brain gets a read on something the PC considers private. It will use this information, eventually, in some way they will recognize but cannot prove.

### Adventure Hooks

#### The Invitation

One of the PCs receives the dream — the open hand from the dark water — somewhere in Sector E or F of the D1-D2 approach. No context, no threat. Just the hand. When they arrive at the Sunless Sea, a kuo-toa is waiting at the pier with an obsidian token.

#### The Amplification Problem

Lisa (Blingdenstone) has observed that the insanity plague gets measurably worse in the D1-D2 corridor than it does near Blingdenstone, even accounting for the dragons' distance. She does not know why. The answer is the Mindspire's broadcast pattern — and the Mindspire is between Blingdenstone and Xilthraen. Shutting down the amplification would not cure the plague but would significantly reduce its intensity, buying time.

#### The Archive

Something the PCs desperately need to know is in the Mindspire's memory archives — a piece of political history, a record of an event, a description of the Gate of Silken Chains' mechanism. The Elder Brain knows they want it. It also wants something in return: information on House Veythrae's Xoriat research (which the PCs might obtain at Xilthraen). This is a trade offer with a timeline.
