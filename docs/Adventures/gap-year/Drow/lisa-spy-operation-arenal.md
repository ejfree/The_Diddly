## The Aelindra Deception

## Summary

The operation that started the Drow War was not a Diddly intelligence initiative. It was conceived, resourced, and directed by **Alyssa Siviridion** — ancient emerald dragon, adjunct professor of Prophecy at the Diddly, tea shop owner, and covert controller of the gnomish intelligence network known as **the Trust**.

Alyssa had read the Black Coil prophecy. She had taught it. She believed Lisa's flight were the "Young Wings" — the ones the prophecy said could break the cycle rather than simply follow it to catastrophe. She also believed the prophecy would not fulfill itself. Someone had to put the pieces in position.

So she did.

She intercepted the Aelindra correspondence through the Trust, recognized the opening it offered, and arranged for Lisa to run the operation. The Diddly's intelligence arm was informed of the operation as a courtesy — it was never theirs. Lisa was told it was a sanctioned university collection mission. It wasn't. It was her grandmother's project.

The war that followed was not an accident Alyssa failed to foresee. It was the step she needed to happen. She needed the flight to go down. She needed them to have a reason.

She did not tell Lisa any of this.

---

## The Trust

The Trust is a gnomish intelligence network of significant age and reach, operating through commercial and social infrastructure — couriers, scribes, Sivis message stations, restaurant staff, merchant contacts, and academic connections across multiple continents. It does not formally exist. Its members know they work for something; most do not know what it is called or who leads it.

Alyssa Siviridion has run the Trust's inner circle for longer than most of its members have been alive. The Viridian Salon is her primary operations hub. The "SCIF" room in the tea shop — the one private space in the building, the one Alyssa can monitor in real time through means undetectable by anything short of divine intervention — is where she reads the Trust's most sensitive traffic.

The Trust's reach into the Underdark is limited but not absent. Gnomish merchant contacts with duergar traders, svirfneblin waystation operators, and surface scouts monitoring Underdark approach tunnels feed intelligence upward through commercial cover. It was through this network that the Trust first detected Aelindra of House Silaen's tentative contact with Xil scouts.

Alyssa read the intercept herself.

---

## Why Alyssa Did This

Alyssa holds the Adjunct Chair of Prophetic Studies at the Diddly — a position she accepted not for the salary but for the access. Teaching the Black Coil and Whispered Scale fragments gave her standing to research them, annotate them, cross-reference them against historical and current intelligence, and develop a working interpretation that the Chamber of Dragons had not reached.

Her conclusion: the "Young Wings" in the prophecy were not metaphorical. They referred to a specific flight of young dragons at the specific moment when Lolth's chain-fulfillment attempt aligned with their position. She identified Lisa's flight as a strong candidate. She began watching.

When the Trust surfaced the Aelindra channel, she saw it whole: the drow war preparations were already advanced. The plague had begun — Lolth's first move. The giant chain would activate when the war went hot. If Lisa's flight could be placed inside the intelligence gap between Xil and the surface powers at the moment the war launched, they would be positioned exactly where the prophecy required.

She needed the operation to run long enough to become personal. She needed the war to launch in response to the deception rather than on its own timeline. She needed Lisa's flight to be in the upper passages when the war party came, close enough to flee into the Underdark rather than retreat to the surface.

She did not need all five of them to die. That part she got wrong.

---

## What Alyssa Told Lisa

Lisa was told:

- The Trust had intercepted a diplomatic channel between an Aerenal envoy and drow scouts from a forward city
- The Diddly's intelligence arm had cleared a collection operation using the channel as access
- Lisa's natural shapeshifting ability and espionage training made her the right operative
- The mission objective was intelligence on drow war preparations — troop strength, timeline, house politics
- She would have Trust support throughout, with a contact handler who would relay her reports back

None of this was false. All of it was incomplete.

Lisa was not told:

- That the operation was Alyssa's, not the Diddly's
- That the channel had been selected specifically because it led to Vel'dryn — the one person whose personal reaction to deception could trigger an immediate war
- That Alyssa had calculated the probable timeline of discovery and had determined it was acceptable
- That the flight's positioning in the upper passages during the war's opening hours was not coincidental

---

## Lady Aelindra of House Silaen

**Who she is:** A genuine Aerenal diplomat, mid-rank envoy of the Undying Court, with a mandate to monitor Underdark threats to Aerenal trade routes. House Silaen is minor nobility — old money, careful politics, the kind of family that survives by keeping useful secrets quietly.

Aelindra made tentative contact with Xil scouts through intermediaries roughly three years ago — cold calculation, not warmth. She wanted to know which way the war was pointing before it arrived. She maintained strict deniability and never met anyone directly.

The Trust intercepted her correspondence with those scouts. Alyssa read it within two days of the first exchange.

**Where she is now:** Alive, in Aerenal, entirely unaware that someone spent nearly two years impersonating her in private meetings with a drow Matron Mother. If the PCs ever need Aelindra as a lever — to verify identity, to exploit the gap between who Vel'dryn thinks she met and who Aelindra actually is — the real woman is accessible. She is also going to have strong opinions about what was done in her name.

## The Operation — How It Ran

### Phase One: Preparation (months 1–4)

Lisa studied Aelindra's correspondence, voice, manner, and stated positions. She built the cover over four months — transforming into an Aerenal elf form she could hold with complete physical continuity, constructing a backstory consistent with Aelindra's actual background while adding the subtle drift that would make "Aelindra" more willing to move toward direct contact.

Her Trust handler was a gnome named **Wessyl Drann**, operating under commercial cover as a gem courier in the upper passage trading posts. Wessyl did not know who controlled the operation. He knew his handler, who knew Alyssa.

### Phase Two: Direct Contact (months 5–18)

Six meetings with intermediaries. Then eight face-to-face meetings with Vel'dryn herself, at a carved waystation between the upper passages and D1 — a location Vel'dryn's side proposed and Lisa agreed to because she had already mapped it.

The intelligence return was exceptional: house politics, military readiness by unit, supply timelines, the council's fault lines, Vel'dryn's own strategic thinking. Lisa filed thorough reports through Wessyl. Alyssa read them all.

What the reports could not fully convey was the texture of those meetings. Vel'dryn in private was not Vel'dryn performing authority. She was direct, occasionally dry, honest about what twenty years of holding Xil together had cost. The relationship became something Vel'dryn understood as romantic. She did not say so explicitly.

Lisa read it. She kept reading. At some point in the later meetings she stopped using her telepathic influence at all — not sabotage, just a choice she made without examining it closely.

She filed reports. She said nothing about the rest.

### Phase Three: Discovery (month 19)

House Thalmyr's surface informants produced a physical description of the real Aelindra that did not match. Vel'dryn was informed.

She sat with it for three days. Then the war order came — not the phased mobilization, the actual order. A war party went into the upper passages.

Alyssa received word of the war order within hours through the Trust's monitoring network. She did not warn Lisa directly. She sent a single-word message through Wessyl: *run*.

The message arrived six hours after the ambush.

---

## The Ambush

The war party found Lisa's reconnaissance flight in the upper tunnels. The party was large — overkill for scouts, intentional for a manhunt. Five of the flight died. Lisa escaped using transformation, feigned death, misdirection, and tunnel knowledge she had built over two years of travelling to the waystation meetings.

She knew the passage toward Blingdenstone because she had walked it as Aelindra.

She has not contacted Alyssa since. She does not know whether the late warning was a failure of the Trust's speed or a calculated margin. She has not resolved that question. It sits under everything else she is not resolving.

---

## Alyssa's Situation Now

Alyssa received full reports of the ambush. She knows five members of the flight are dead. She knows Lisa survived — the Trust confirmed a lone survivor fleeing into the lower passages. She does not have Lisa's current location. She has been looking.

She is still teaching at the Diddly. She still runs the Viridian Salon. Solomon the phoenix appears and disappears as he always has. She picks teapots for every guest. She has not told anyone what she did.

She believes — she has to believe — that the operation placed Lisa exactly where the prophecy required. That the five deaths were the cost of a war that was coming regardless. That Lisa, wherever she is, is fulfilling something real.

She is not sleeping well.

---

## What Vel'dryn Knows

Vel'dryn knows:

- The Aelindra she met was an impersonator — extraordinary shapeshifting ability, patience sustained across nearly two years of private meetings.
- Whatever was shared in those meetings was heard by someone whose name and nature she does not know.
- The impersonator did not try to steer her. Just listened. She has not decided if that is better or worse than manipulation.

Vel'dryn does not know:

- That "Aelindra" was a dragon.
- That the operation was run by an ancient emerald dragon through a gnomish spy network.
- That the operative survived and is three passages from her forward tunnel network, running a deep gnome city.
- That the intelligence she shared in those meetings was filed in reports that Alyssa Siviridion has read in full and has not shared with anyone.

---

## What Lisa Knows

Lisa knows the shape of Vel'dryn in a way no intelligence report captures. Not doctrine, not politics — the actual person. What she values, what it costs her, what she sounded like when she stopped performing.

She has not written this down. She filed the operational intelligence. She kept the rest.

She also knows — or suspects, and has had nine months to firm the suspicion into something colder — that the operation was not what she was told it was. The warning that arrived six hours late. The specific target she was assigned. The flight's positioning. None of it adds up as coincidence.

She has not said this aloud. She does not know what she would do with the answer.
