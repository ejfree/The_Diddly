## Council of Houses — Xilthraen

## Structure

- **Seats:** 6 total (2 major houses, 4 minor)
- **Voting Power:** Weighted by house status and military contribution
  - Major Houses: 2 votes each
  - Minor Houses: 1 vote each
- **Council Hall:** A circular obsidian chamber inside the Temple-Fortress of Lolth, each house's insignia carved into stone thrones
- **Tiebreaker:** High Priestess of Lolth (neutral, outside the houses) casts the deciding vote

---

## Major Houses

### House Xilrath — "The Black Blades" (2 votes)

- **Faction Role:** Martial supremacy — disciplined heavy infantry and logistics
- **Matron Mother:** Xilrath Vel'dryn (Lvl 16 Warpriest Cleric) — council chair by tradition, though resented
- **Second:** First Sword Captain Veynar (Lvl 12 Fighter with Champion dedication) — field commander
- **Third:** Logistics Mistress Shynrae (Lvl 10 Cleric with Investigator dedication) — supply chains, slaves, provisions
- **Company:** *The Black Blades* — 650 drow + 900 auxiliaries (see reconciled TOE in house file)
- **Council Stance:** Expansionist. Constant surface raids, prioritizing logistics and supply lines
- **Allies:** Dravun (rely on beasts), Zhaelor (fanatic zealots)
- **Rivals:** Veythrae (arcane supremacy vs. military doctrine)
- **Doctrine:** Anvil (Blades) + hammer (Quaggoths) under covering poison volleys. Weakness: if Void-Archers neutralized, advances stall.

---

### House Veythrae — "The Shadowfire Cadres" (2 votes)

- **Faction Role:** Arcane dominance — shadowfire mages and demon-binders
- **Matron Mother:** Thalara Veythrae (Lvl 17 Wizard with Cleric dedication) — claims Lolth favors sorcery over brute force
- **Second:** Archmage Sybrynn Veythrae (Lvl 12 Wizard, Evocation) — battlefield spell doctrine
- **Third:** Demonmistress Elvrae Veythrae (Lvl 11 Wizard with Summoner dedication) — oversees binding pits
- **Company:** *The Shadowfire Cadres* — 520 drow + 680 auxiliaries (see reconciled TOE in house file)
- **Council Stance:** Slower buildup, more magical weapons, decisive annihilation rather than attrition
- **Allies:** Velouryn (funds research, provides poisons)
- **Rivals:** Xilrath (militarists), Zhaelor (fanatics)
- **Doctrine:** Fix with illusions and webs; assassinate command; collapse pockets. Weakness: lower staying power in prolonged frontal fighting.

---

## Minor Houses

### House Dravun — "The Fang Legion" (1 vote)

- **Faction Role:** Beastmasters — quaggoths, spiders, and shock troops
- **Matron Mother:** Yvra Dravun (Lvl 13 Cleric with Beastmaster dedication) — believes beasts are Lolth's chosen children
- **Second:** Beastlord Krael Dravun (Lvl 9 Ranger with Beastmaster dedication) — commands quaggoth and spider units
- **Third:** Mistress of Fangs Nelyth Dravun (Lvl 8 Rogue with Poisoner archetype) — venom farms and beast-training
- **Company:** *The Fang Legion* — 400 drow + 1,200 auxiliaries (see reconciled TOE in house file)
- **Council Stance:** Favors aggressive assaults — release beasts first to sow chaos
- **Allies:** Xilrath (military needs beasts)
- **Rivals:** Velouryn (mercantile types seen as weak)

---

### House Thalmyr — "The Silent Blades" (1 vote)

- **Faction Role:** Espionage and assassinations
- **Matron Mother:** Lythrae Thalmyr (Lvl 12 Cleric with Rogue dedication) — pragmatic spymistress
- **Second:** Shadow Captain Drevan Thalmyr (Lvl 9 Rogue with Assassin dedication) — leads infiltration cells
- **Third:** Whisper Adept Kyssil Thalmyr (Lvl 7 Investigator with Rogue dedication) — manages informant webs
- **Company:** *The Silent Blades* — 350 drow + 500 thrall scouts (see TOE in house file)
- **Council Stance:** Infiltration before open war; urges restraint until surface leaders are weakened
- **Allies:** Sells secrets to both sides; nominally aligned with delay bloc
- **Rivals:** Zhaelor (zealots uncover spies)

---

### House Velouryn — "The Venom Syndicate" (1 vote)

- **Faction Role:** Wealth and contraband — poison, coin, and mercantile power
- **Matron Mother:** Ulvrae Velouryn (Lvl 11 Cleric with Rogue dedication) — the purse-strings of the city
- **Second:** Trade Captain Malryk Velouryn (Lvl 8 Rogue with Mastermind archetype) — smuggling routes and surface contacts
- **Third:** Poisonmistress Thivra Velouryn (Lvl 7 Alchemist with Poisoner archetype) — manufactures toxins and alchemical weapons
- **Company:** *The Venom Syndicate* — 300 drow + 400 auxiliaries (see TOE in house file)
- **Council Stance:** Prolonged preparation; covert smuggling routes and resource accumulation preferred
- **Allies:** Veythrae (funds their research), Thalmyr (buys intel)
- **Rivals:** Dravun ("beast-brutes"), Zhaelor (inquisitors target their smuggling)

---

### House Zhaelor — "The Lash of Lolth" (1 vote)

- **Faction Role:** Fanatical religion — zealots and inquisitors of Lolth
- **Matron Mother:** Zarila Zhaelor (Lvl 14 Cleric, Warpriest doctrine, with Champion dedication) — loudest voice on council; believes she speaks for Lolth
- **Second:** Zealot Commander Rhazek Zhaelor (Lvl 9 Champion with Cleric dedication) — leads battlefield sermons and crusades
- **Third:** Firebrand Syrris Zhaelor (Lvl 7 Cleric, Fire/Destruction domain) — war-sermons and fanatic morale
- **Company:** *The Lash of Lolth* — 250 drow + 200 indoctrinated slave-fanatics (see TOE in house file)
- **Council Stance:** Holy war now; hesitation is heresy
- **Allies:** Xilrath (war doctrine, superficially)
- **Rivals:** Veythrae (dangerously heretical arcane independence)

## Council Dynamics

| Bloc       | Houses                          | Votes | Position                                               |
|------------|---------------------------------|-------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| War Bloc   | Xilrath + Dravun + Zhaelor      | 4     | Immediate war via attrition and terror                 |
| Delay Bloc | Veythrae + Velouryn + Thalmyr   | 4     | Build magical, economic, and espionage advantage first |

**Deadlock:** 4–4 split means assassination, intrigue, or divine intervention decides most major votes.

## How the PCs Arrive Here (Arc 3)

The PCs enter Xilthraen at the end of a trail beginning at the bottom of the Pit (Arc 2.5 — Seven Dooms of Sandpoint):

1. **Bottom of the Pit** — evidence of giant activity; tracks lead deeper
2. **G1 — Hill Giants** — "First fall the Children of Stone" (prophecy)
3. **G2 — Frost Giants** — "The Sons of Ice shall melt" (prophecy)
4. **G3 — Fire Giants** — "The Pyre-Lords shall be cast into their own crucibles" (prophecy)
5. **D1 — Underdark entry / troglodytes** — "the stink of the Deep Hunger" (prophecy)
6. **D2 — Kuo-toa warrens** — "the mad prayers of the Drowned Eyes" (prophecy)
7. **D3 — Xilthraen** — "the blade-kiss of the Black Matrons" (prophecy); three keys turned in the Gate of Silken Chains

The giants are not independent actors — they are links in Lolth's chain. She is using the surface war to thin her own drow forces (the strong survive), bait the PCs into completing the prophetic sequence, and ultimately free Tiamat imprisoned in Argonessen. The PCs descending through the giant chain *is* the prophecy being fulfilled. The "Young Wings" of the prophecy are the ones who can break this cycle rather than simply follow it to its catastrophic end.
