# Grimstone Holds — The Duergar Forge-City

## Overview

Grimstone Holds is the dominant duergar settlement in this region of the Underdark — a city of iron discipline, endless labor, and deeply held grievances. Carved into a cluster of mineral-rich caverns on the eastern side of the Underdark corridor, it sits roughly equidistant between the Sunless Sea to the northeast and Xilthraen to the northwest.

The city is not a single structure but a network of fortified caverns — the "Holds" — each controlled by a forge-clan and connected by tunnels wide enough for two war-wagons abreast. Every tunnel junction is a defensible chokepoint. Every cavern entrance has a portcullis. The duergar built this city to be taken one room at a time and made the rooms very expensive.

They have been here for roughly four centuries. The drow have been pressuring them for two decades. The relationship is not slavery — it is something the duergar find more insulting: *dependency*. The drow need their steel. The duergar know it. Neither side has resolved the tension.

---

## Location

**Eastern Underdark corridor**, between the Sunless Sea (northeast) and Xilthraen (northwest). Three primary tunnel approaches:
- **West Passage** — toward Xilthraen; heavily monitored; drow patrols check papers at the approach
- **North Passage** — toward the Sunless Sea and the Shard Warrens; main trade route
- **East Passage** — dead-ends in unexplored deep caverns; rumored to connect to something older

---

## Scale

- **Duergar population:** ~5,500
- **Slave/thrall population:** ~3,000 (grimlocks, captured surface folk, a significant number of duergar indentured for debt)
- **Total:** ~8,500

The city is organized into six Holds, each controlled by a forge-clan:

| Hold | Clan | Specialty |
|------|------|-----------|
| Ironhold | Clan Hrunkar | Heavy plate armor, siege weapons |
| Ashhold | Clan Greldak | Weapons — blades, axes, crossbows |
| Deephold | Clan Murthaen | Mining operations, raw ore processing |
| Cagehold | Clan Vrusk | Slave management, internal security |
| Coalhold | Clan Thannax | Fuel, alchemy, black powder (primitive) |
| Grayhold | Clan Solvrak | Diplomacy, trade, ledgers — the "soft" clan, looked down upon by others |

## Government — The Steelcourt

Grimstone Holds is ruled by the **Overking** — currently **Overking Baldrak Hrunkar** (Lvl 14 Fighter with Champion dedication), patriarch of the most powerful forge-clan. The Overking holds authority over military matters and trade agreements. His word is law in the tunnels.

Below him sits the **Steelcourt** — the six clan-lords, one per Hold. They vote on resource allocation, trade terms, and declarations of war. Unanimity is required to declare open war; a simple majority governs everything else. Clan Solvrak (diplomacy) holds slightly more influence than its military weight suggests because it controls the ledgers that everyone else depends on.

**Key figures:**

- **Overking Baldrak Hrunkar** — Gruff, patient, and quietly enraged. He has watched the drow extract favorable terms from the Holds for fifteen years by threatening to cut off access to surface-raided food and slave supplies. He knows his city could fight the drow. He believes they would win. He also believes it would cost so much that someone else — the illithids, the shard scavengers — would simply walk in afterward. He is waiting for the right moment and the right allies.

- **Clan-Lord Solvrak Greldak** (Lvl 10 Rogue with Mastermind archetype) — The trade diplomat. Manages all external contracts including the drow agreements and the Shard Warrens supply lines. Knows more secrets about every faction's finances than they are comfortable with. Has an arrangement with House Velouryn that neither party advertises. Appears cooperative with drow demands; is in fact systematically skimming from every contract.

- **Deepmistress Yurra Murthaen** (Lvl 9 Cleric with Inventor dedication) — Oversees the mines. Has recently found something in the east passage — a vein of crystal that resonates with her divination tools in ways she does not understand. She has not reported this to the Steelcourt. (It is Khyber dragonshard residue; the east passage connects deeper than anyone knows.)

## The City

### The Forge Quarter
The heart of Grimstone Holds — massive caverns lit by the glow of twenty permanent furnaces. The air is hot, thick with coal smoke and metal dust. Duergar laborers work in rotating shifts with no natural day-night cycle. Weapons, armor, tools, chains, and siege components flow out through Clan Solvrak's trade channels.

The quality of Grimstone steel is exceptional. House Xilrath buys in bulk; House Veythrae buys specific custom commissions (exotic alloys for arcane focuses, demon-binding implements). House Velouryn buys whatever they can mark up and resell.

### The Slave Pens
Managed by Clan Vrusk — ruthlessly efficient. The duergar do not sentimentalize slavery. Slaves are assets, depreciated and replaced. Vrusk maintains records of every enslaved individual in the city: origin, skills, condition, estimated remaining productive years. They sell surplus to drow caravans. They buy new stock from Shard Warrens.

The Vrusk slave-system has one notable characteristic: **duergar debt-slaves**. A duergar who fails to meet their clan's production quota, loses a trade dispute, or is convicted of a crime may be temporarily indentured to another clan. This is considered deeply shameful but legally standard. It is the source of intense inter-clan resentment.

### The Gray Quarter
Clan Solvrak's domain — the only part of Grimstone Holds that resembles a town square. Trade offices, a neutral meeting hall, a small shrine to the duergar ancestor-spirits, and three taverns that serve travelers. Non-duergar are tolerated in the Gray Quarter and nowhere else.

The **Ashen Cup** tavern is the de facto neutral ground for every faction's agents operating in the eastern Underdark. House Thalmyr keeps a permanent "observer" there. The Shard Warrens send a buyer monthly. Velouryn's trade captain has a standing room rental.

### The Deep Gates
The east passage entrance — sealed behind three successive iron doors and guarded by a permanent detail from Clan Hrunkar. Nobody goes east without Overking authorization. Deepmistress Yurra has made three unauthorized visits in the past month using a duplicate key she made herself.

## Subsettlements & Encounter Areas

### Large Subsettlements

#### The Ironspine — Forward Garrison (1.5 miles west on the Xilthraen approach)

The Ironspine is Grimstone Holds' forward military installation — a separate fortified outpost carved into a natural rock buttress that narrows the West Passage to a single-wagon choke point. Travelers approaching from the direction of Xilthraen encounter the Ironspine before they see the main city. It is not subtle about its purpose.

**Population:** ~400 duergar (rotating garrison duty from all six clans), 120 slave laborers

**Structure:**

- Three successive iron gates across the passage, each with a portcullis controlled from above
- Barracks carved into the rock wall on both sides — the garrison lives inside the chokepoint
- A signal tower at the top of the buttress, visible from both the Holds and a mile down the West Passage
- A small armory stocked with crossbows, bolts, and enough oil to make the passage impassable for a week

**Commander:** Gate-Captain Drevrak Vrusk (Lvl 8 Fighter, Clan Vrusk) — appointed by the Steelcourt specifically because Vrusk has no patience for anyone, including drow. He has turned away a Xilrath patrol once on a technicality and considers this a personal achievement.

**Role in Play:** Every significant traveler entering or leaving Grimstone Holds passes through Ironspine. Papers are checked, cargo is inspected, and everything unusual is logged. Drevrak's logs are one of the most complete records of Underdark traffic in the region. Clan Solvrak has been trying to get access to those logs for years; Drevrak reports only to the Overking.

---

#### The Smelting Depths — Deep Mining Complex (below the main city)

Three hundred feet below Ironhold and Deephold, connected by a series of ore-cart ramps and a single, very slow lift platform, the Smelting Depths is where Grimstone Holds actually gets its metal. A self-contained underground community — miners, engineers, and their slave crews who complete entire shifts without returning to the surface city. Shifts run sixteen hours; some workers spend weeks below.

**Population:** ~600 duergar miners and engineers, ~900 slave workers

**Structure:**

- Seven active ore faces along three mineral veins — iron, nickel, and a deep galena seam with silver traces
- A primary smelting chamber where raw ore is processed before lifting — twelve furnaces running continuously
- Barracks chambers for long-shift workers carved between the ore faces
- A Deephold administration post where Deepmistress Yurra maintains her office (and, three tunnels east, her unauthorized excavation)

**The Secret Level:** Yurra's unauthorized dig extends 200 feet past the easternmost mapped passage. The crystal she found — Khyber dragonshard residue seeping through a hairline fissure — is fresh, meaning it formed recently. Something below is generating it upward. She has left markers in the tunnel using a private notation system. She has not told anyone what she marked or why.

**Role in Play:** The Smelting Depths is where the "Steel Clause" crisis is most visceral — the slave workforce is already running at capacity, and another push will produce casualties. Workers in the Depths have information about Yurra's unauthorized dig. A PC who earns trust down here gets access to things no one above knows.

### Smaller Encounter Areas

#### 1. The Ancestor Vault

Below the Grayhold, sealed behind a stone door that requires a clan elder's blood to open. The duergar maintain ancestor-spirit shrines here — carved faces of clan founders in volcanic rock, each with a small perpetual flame. Duergar consult the shrines before major decisions; the spirits answer in a low vibration felt through the stone floor rather than heard.

Three weeks ago, one of the shrine-faces began answering questions the living duergar didn't ask. The responses are accurate and useful, which is why no one has reported it yet. The entity behind the answers is not an ancestor spirit. What it actually is has not been determined, but the questions it volunteers answers to all relate to the east passage.

---

#### 2. The Chain Bridge

A natural chasm, 40 feet across and 80 feet deep, splits the interior of the main cavern cluster between Ironhold and Ashhold. The only crossing is a massive iron-chain-and-plank bridge, 12 feet wide, maintained jointly by both clans. Joint maintenance means each clan blames the other for every repair that doesn't happen.

Currently: three planks are structurally unsound (both clans know; neither will pay for replacement because that would imply responsibility). A loaded ore wagon crossed three days ago and the bridge held, but the vibration loosened a chain anchor on the Ashhold side. Clan Greldak has not told Clan Hrunkar. Clan Hrunkar has not inspected the bridge since the wagon crossing. The PCs crossing with a full load at a run would require an Athletics or Acrobatics check. The PCs crossing in a fight, or while someone above drops weight on it, would require significantly more.

---

#### 3. The Debt Cells

A separate wing of Cagehold dedicated to duergar debt-slaves — kept rigorously separated from non-duergar slaves because mixing them causes violent incidents. The Debt Cells are cleaner and better-provisioned than the general slave pens; the duergar treat their own indentured as expensive equipment rather than disposable labor.

Currently holds 43 duergar in various states of indenture: 11 from Clan Thannax (production shortfall), 18 from Clan Murthaen (a disputed contract that has been in Steelcourt arbitration for four months), and 14 from smaller family groups. The Murthaen group is particularly bitter because their case should have been resolved by now; the delay is because Clan Solvrak is profiting from their labor assignment and has been quietly stalling arbitration. Deepmistress Yurra does not know 14 of her clan's people are in the Debt Cells because of Solvrak's interference. If she finds out, the Steelcourt will need to hold an emergency session.

---

#### 4. The Alchemy Vats

Clan Thannax's chemical facility in Coalhold — three large cavern chambers filled with sealed stone vats, copper piping, and the persistent smell of sulfur and coal tar. Produces the city's fuel supply, the primitive black powder compounds used in mining, caustic slags for metallurgical processes, and a line of alchemical weapons (acid flasks, burning oil, smoke canisters) sold primarily to the Shard Warrens.

The Vats are perpetually understaffed because alchemical accidents are common and the workforce turns over. Clan Thannax recruits aggressively from the slave pens, trains minimally, and accepts the losses. The current overseer, a duergar named Skurra Thannax (Lvl 5 Alchemist), is competent but aware that two of her senior chemists are actually Clan Murthaen's indentured people who were assigned here as punishment. They know enough to cause a significant explosion if they chose to. They are considering it.

---

#### 5. The West Watch Posts

Three hardened sentry stations spaced along the West Passage between the Ironspine and the point where the tunnel begins to show drow directional markings. Each post is a cut-stone room wide enough for four duergar, with arrow slits covering the approach, a signal bell connected by wire to the next post, and a collapsible barricade that can be triggered from inside.

Post One (nearest Ironspine) is manned full-time: 4 guards, 8-hour rotations. Post Two is manned during "elevated watch" periods — there have been three in the past month, following drow patrol sightings closer to the Holds than the agreement permits. Post Three is currently unmanned. Overking Hrunkar ordered it remanned two weeks ago. The order has not been carried out. Nobody has explained why to the Overking.

## Relationship to Other Factions

**Xilthraen (Drow):**
Trading partners under duress. The drow need the steel; the duergar need the food supply that drow raids provide (the Holds cannot grow enough fungus for their population). The current agreement gives drow preferential pricing and priority fulfillment — terms Baldrak Hrunkar accepted under pressure and intends to revisit. House Xilrath deals directly; House Velouryn greases everything.

The drow do not station soldiers in Grimstone Holds. They tried. One garrison disappeared. The subject has not been raised again.

**The Mindspire (Illithid):**
The duergar hate the illithids with a cold, controlled fury. Several duergar have been taken over the centuries — not killed, simply *taken*, never returned, their fate unknown. The duergar's natural psychic resistance (a racial trait) limits illithid influence but does not eliminate the threat. Grimstone Holds has standing orders: any entity approaching from the northeast sea-passages is to be detained and examined before entry.

**The Shard Warrens:**
Complicated. The Warrens buy Grimstone tools and sell Grimstone surplus slaves. Clean, transactional relationship. Both sides profit. Clan Solvrak maintains it carefully. The Overking is aware that the Shard Warrens also supply the illithids and doesn't like it, but hasn't found leverage to stop it.

**Blingdenstone (Deep Gnomes):**
The duergar and svirfneblin have a centuries-old arrangement: they ignore each other. Occasional trade via neutral intermediaries, occasional territorial disputes settled without open war. The deep gnomes are currently under drow pressure; Baldrak Hrunkar has noted this with interest. The gnomes would be useful if the Holds ever moved against the drow. He has not approached them. Yet.

## Adventure Hooks

### The Steel Clause
House Xilrath's latest contract renewal includes a clause requiring Grimstone Holds to provide ten siege weapons by a specific date — a date the Holds cannot meet without working their forge-slaves to death. Overking Hrunkar needs either an excuse to break the contract, a way to accelerate production, or something to leverage against Xilrath. The PCs arrive in the middle of these negotiations. They may have exactly the kind of information or disruption Hrunkar needs.

### The East Gate
Deepmistress Yurra found something. She needs help she can trust — which means people who are not duergar, cannot report to the Steelcourt, and have reason to keep secrets. The crystal vein in the east passage is Khyber dragonshard, and it is not dormant. Something is following it from the other end.

### The Ashen Cup Meeting
A Thalmyr agent is trying to broker a secret meeting between Grimstone Holds and a "neutral party" interested in destabilizing the drow supply chain. The agent won't say who the neutral party is. The PCs may be that party, or may stumble into the arrangement. Clan Solvrak already knows about the meeting. The Overking does not.
