# The Situation Room

*"We watch. We do not intervene. We do not contact. We watch."*
— Standing order, posted above the Viridian Salon's sub-basement door

---

## Overview

From the day each flight clears the Diddly's airspace until the day they return, the University maintains **near-constant scrying coverage** on every active gap year flight. There is no contact. There is no assistance. The tradition is sacred and non-negotiable — gap year exists precisely because dragons must prove themselves without the protection of the institution.

But watching is not contact. And so they watch.

The operation runs out of two locations simultaneously:

- **The Viridian Salon sub-basement** — Alyssa Siviridion's Trust infrastructure, handling intelligence-grade monitoring of the flights she cares about most
- **The Diddly's Observation Annex** — the University's official scrying operation, staffed by the Dean of Records and the Office of Student Affairs

These two rooms are not formally linked. They share no official channels. They occasionally share information through Alyssa's adjunct faculty status, which gives her standing to attend University briefings as an observer.

---

## The Viridian Salon Sub-Basement

The room does not appear on any floor plan of the Viridian Salon. Access is through a passage behind the SCIF, down seventeen steps, through a cold stone corridor that smells of old paper and warding incense.

### Physical Layout

The room is long and low-ceilinged, lit by amber-tinted mage lights that never fully brighten. The aesthetic is part archive, part operations center:

- **The Table** — a stone-topped map table occupying the center of the room. The surface is inlaid with an enchanted relief of the continent at the relevant scale. **Small glass markers** represent each tracked flight, moving in near-real-time as scrying updates are processed. Lines of grease pencil (renewed daily by the night analyst) show projected routes, last confirmed positions, and zones of signal degradation.

- **The Pool Array** — six scrying pools arranged in a row along the north wall, each calibrated to a specific flight or individual. The pools are oval, set into stone basins, and fed by a slow trickle of water etched with Sivis-notation binding runes. Each pool can hold a sustained scry for approximately six hours before requiring recalibration. A seventh pool — the largest — is Alyssa's personal reserve. It is never assigned to routine monitoring.

- **The Status Board** — a slate board on the east wall, divided into columns by flight. Updated at each shift change:
  - Last confirmed visual (time, location, condition)
  - Current scry status (LIVE / DARK / DEGRADED)
  - Notable events since last briefing
  - Analyst flag color: **green** (nominal), **amber** (elevated concern), **red** (critical watch)

- **The Duty Desk** — positioned so the analyst on watch can see both the table and the board without moving. Two chairs. A tin of biscuits that Alyssa replenishes personally every morning. A logbook that has never missed an entry.

### Staffing

The operation runs three shifts. Analysts are Trust staff — gnomes, mostly, with two half-elves and one human who has been with the Trust so long nobody remembers which side of the border she came from. They are not told the full picture. They are told that certain flights of young dragons require discreet monitoring as part of a sensitive academic program, that confidentiality is absolute, and that they are to log everything and flag anything unusual to their supervisor immediately.

Their supervisor is a gnome named **Corvin Tess** — small, precise, perpetually tired, absolutely unflappable. He has worked for Alyssa for forty years. He knows more than the others. He has never asked her to explain herself.

**Alyssa is not on a shift.** Alyssa is always present, in the way that weather is always present. She appears without warning, reviews the log, stares at the board for ten minutes, adjusts a glass marker on the table by a fraction of an inch, and leaves. She is there more often in the early hours before service begins.

### What She Watches For

Alyssa has specific interests. She is watching all the flights, but she is watching **Lisa's flight** — now the PCs — with an attention that is almost obsessive. She has not interfered with the operation she set in motion. She has not contacted them. She cannot.

What she is watching for:
- Movement through the giant chain zones (confirms prophecy alignment)
- Who they trust, who they fight, what choices they make under pressure
- Whether Celedyr's bloodmark is changing his behavior
- Whether Nara is reading the fragments correctly
- Signs that the Pale Choir is affecting them indirectly (not likely yet, but she watches)
- Whether they are going to get killed

She maintains a private notebook — not the official log — in which she records interpretive annotations. She cross-references their progress against the prophecy fragments. When something the party does matches a fragment she has never been able to interpret, she underlines it twice and sits very still for a while.

## The University Observation Annex

The Diddly's official monitoring operation is housed in a narrow tower room in the administrative quad, colloquially known to the staff as **the Fishbowl**. It is staffed by the Office of Student Affairs, which treats gap year tracking as an administrative function — important, but routine.

### Physical Layout

A smaller operation than the Trust's. One large scrying mirror (twelve flights, rotating coverage). A ledger. Two desks. A window. The coffee situation is, by all accounts, grim.

### What They Track

The University is monitoring for:
- **Flight status** — are the students alive and moving?
- **Serious injury** — which triggers a flag to the relevant Dean, for the record
- **Death** — which requires notification of family, filing of the academic record, and a brief ceremony in the Quiet Count
- **Gross misconduct** — theoretically. Nobody has clear guidance on what this means in the context of gap year.

The University does not track individual student choices or behavioral patterns. They are watching students, not studying them.

### Dean Argentius

Dean Argentius — Celedyr's advisor, the last one to leave the arena after the Grand Departure — comes to the Fishbowl twice a week. He stands at the mirror longer than he needs to. He never says why. The staff have learned not to ask.

## The No Contact Rule

The prohibition is not a policy. It is older than the current University structure. It derives from the **Compact of Wings** — a doctrinal agreement made after a catastrophic gap year intervention approximately 300 years prior, in which a faculty member's interference in a student flight's encounter with a rogue dragon prevented the students from developing the judgment they needed. Two of that flight later died in positions of leadership because they had never learned how to face something that outmatched them without help.

The Compact language is explicit:

> *"Those who watch shall not speak. Those who speak shall not be heard. The young wings must learn the weight of the sky before they are asked to hold it."*

Scrying is permitted because it was already being done privately before the Compact was written, and because the authors of the Compact recognized that a university that truly did not know whether its students were alive would be functionally untenable. They compromised: watch all you want. Say nothing.

The penalty for contact is severe. Faculty have been terminated. Academic records have been retroactively amended to remove gap year credits from returning students found to have been in contact with the University. Two hundred years ago, a flight that received unsanctioned advice from a professor failed to complete gap year — not because they didn't reach the end, but because the Compact was invoked and their completion struck from the record.

Alyssa is aware of this. The Trust's communication infrastructure technically falls outside the Compact's jurisdiction, as it operates through non-University channels. She has not tested that interpretation. She does not intend to.

She sends one thing, through channels that cannot be traced back to her: every time a flight transitions from one zone to the next, a gnome courier somewhere along the route finds a small parcel of tea — the Viridian Salon's private blend — left near wherever they camped. Unlabeled. Impossible to prove.

She does not know if they've noticed.

## The Mood of the Room

The Situation Room operates on a kind of professional dread. The analysts are good at their jobs. Good at not saying the obvious thing, which is: we are watching children in the dark and we cannot help them.

The amber flags go up. Occasionally they go red. The red ones come down eventually — either because the flight moved on and the signal cleared, or for the other reason.

Alyssa keeps the biscuit tin full. She asks after the analysts' families. She remembers birthdays. She is, by every visible measure, a composed and functioning ancient dragon who has done this before and will do it again.

She does not sleep during the nights when a flight goes dark and the pool shows nothing.

## GM Notes

**When to bring this into play:**
- If the PCs discover Trust surveillance infrastructure in the field (they might — gnomish waystation operators in the Underdark could be Trust-adjacent)
- If they find the tea — a recurring mystery they can eventually trace
- When they reach Xil, Alyssa will know before they do. Her preparation for that moment has already begun.
- Post-gap year: the party will eventually learn the room existed. The log is meticulous. Every decision they made is documented. Alyssa saw all of it.

**What Alyssa will not do:**
She will not intervene. Not even at the worst moments. She has decided — she is committed to this — that the prophecy only fulfills if they do it themselves. She has already paid the price of believing that once.

What she will do, if they are about to die in a way the prophecy does not account for: she will close her eyes and not look.
