# The Dragon Media Landscape — War Era

*"The flame burns clean. The story must also."*
— Masthead motto, The Argonnessen Dispatch

---

## Overview

The draconic media landscape serves a population of approximately 100,000 dragons spread across Argonnessen, Io'lokar, and outlying weyrs. It is a small audience by any mortal standard — more comparable to a well-educated city-state than a continent — and the media reflects it: intimate, ideologically charged, and staffed by dragons who know everyone they write about.

There are no mass-market operations. There is no anonymous readership. When a reporter files a piece critical of a weyr matriarch, she will hear about it personally. When a film is released depicting the drow war as a righteous crusade, the Council members who authorized it will attend the premiere.

This is 1950s media in structure and sensibility: a handful of trusted institutions, a shared assumption that certain things are not said, and a growing pressure — from the war, from the party's return, from factions that want to shape what the Fourth Age looks like in the historical record — that is beginning to crack the consensus.

## Print

### The Argonnessen Dispatch
*The paper of record.*

The oldest continuously publishing draconic newspaper. Printed in Draconic with a Common-language supplement. Alignment: **Preservationist**. The Dispatch believes in institutions, in precedent, in the long view. Its editorial voice is patrician and careful. It broke the news of the Mourning vote (heavily redacted) and still prints every Council decision verbatim before analysis.

The Dispatch does not sensationalize. It also does not rock boats that it believes are structurally important. It covered the opening of the drow war as a measured, necessary response to a documented act of aggression — the Elven Incident. It has not asked whether the Elven Incident might have been manufactured.

**Gap year angle:** The Dispatch will want the party for a formal interview. Long-form. Sober. Framed as a profile of what the institution produced, not what the students did. They will be written as evidence that the University works, not as individuals.

**Key reporter:** **Adralith Sorn** — Ancient copper dragon. Has been covering the Council for 200 years. Does not make things up, never misquotes, and will not print anything that cannot be independently confirmed. The most trustworthy journalist in draconic media. Also the most cautious.

---

### The Flame and Scale
*The popular press.*

Where the Dispatch is measured, the Flame and Scale is vivid. It prints three times a week in larger format with illustrated covers — scrying-captured images rendered as ink plates, striking, sometimes dramatic, occasionally misleading. Alignment: **Redeemer**. The Flame and Scale believes the Fourth Age is the moment dragons stop hiding and start mattering. It covers the drow war the way a sports broadsheet covers a championship season: standings, heroes, turning points, and enemy failures.

The writing is good. The sourcing is sometimes thin. The emotional pitch is always high.

When the gap year class returned, the Flame and Scale ran a cover illustration of Celedyr's flight breaking out of the Underdark approach tunnels above the headline: **THEY WALKED THE CHAIN.** No subhead. The byline was a staff illustration team who had never met any of the students.

The party has been on the cover twice without being contacted.

**Gap year angle:** The Flame and Scale wants a hero story. They will offer it framed, polished, and ready: *Young Wings of the Gap Year Walk Into the Jaws of the War and Come Back.* They are not interested in the parts of the story that don't fit that frame. A reporter will approach the party at some point, probably in a public setting, almost certainly without formal arrangement.

**Key reporter:** **Yssa Vorell** — Adult bronze dragon, war correspondent, has been embedded with the Redeemer military advance since the war opened. Young enough to still believe things. Talented enough that believing them makes for good prose. She will find the party fascinating and will write about them whether they cooperate or not. She is not malicious. She is simply on a story.

### The Diddly Dispatch
*The university paper.*

Student-run but faculty-advised. Publishes weekly during the academic year, dormant during gap year. Alignment: None — or rather, the Dispatch has a tradition of aggressive institutional neutrality that, in practice, makes it the only outlet willing to ask questions that the Argonnessen Dispatch and Flame and Scale both decide not to ask.

The Dispatch does not have the resources of the major papers. It is read obsessively by students, by many faculty, by alumni, and by anyone watching the University closely. It occasionally breaks stories that the major papers then have to cover.

When the gap year class returned, the Dispatch ran a feature asking why five members of a particular alumni flight died in the Underdark two years before gap year sent students into the same passages. The piece was careful. The sourcing was mostly documentary. No one at the University commented for the record.

**Gap year angle:** A Dispatch reporter will want to talk to the party not about heroism but about what they saw. About conditions in the passages. About Xil. About the war from the inside rather than from the official Redeemer briefings. The party may find this conversation more honest than anything they get from the major papers — and considerably more dangerous.

**Key reporter:** **Minna Chalck** — Drake-year student, History major, former Eggie who lost a cousin on the Lisa flight. She filed the alumni flight piece above as an independent investigation under her faculty advisor's reluctant supervision. She has a source she won't name. She is twenty-three years old in dragon terms and she is not going to drop this.

## Scrying Pictures

### Wyrmtone Pictures
*The newsreel and film operation.*

Wyrmtone produces what mortals would recognize as a combined film studio, newsreel operation, and propaganda office. Every major theater in Argonnessen and Io'lokar opens with a Wyrmtone Newsreel — five to eight minutes of scrying-captured footage, narrated in rolling dramatic Draconic, scored with orchestral dragonfire music. The newsreels cover the war weekly: advances, victory declarations, enemy formations driven back, dragons in heroic silhouette against cavern ceilings.

The footage is real. The framing is Council-approved. Everything shown has been reviewed by a military liaison before release. Everything the liaison says is not suitable for public morale is cut.

Wyrmtone also produces **feature-length scrying-pictures**. Think: documentary-as-propaganda, Frank Capra's *Why We Fight* mapped to a draconic war. There are currently two in production:

- **The Long Descent** — a dramatized account of the giant chain, from the first scouts to the war opening. Pre-production. Wyrmtone has been trying to contact members of the gap year class.
- **Wings of Redemption** — a shorter piece celebrating the Redeemer advance, featuring embedded Wyrmtone crews with Xilrath-facing units. Already screened at three Council events.

**Gap year angle:** Wyrmtone will approach the party about participation in *The Long Descent.* The offer will be framed as an honor. The contract, if examined by anyone with legal acumen, grants Wyrmtone full narrative control. The party's likenesses can be used with or without interview content. One of the party members will be depicted as the protagonist regardless of who actually made the decisions.

**Key contact:** **Director Orryn Vel** — Middle-aged emerald dragon, accomplished filmmaker, genuinely believes the war is just and the story of the giant chain is genuinely remarkable. He is not a liar. He makes films that are not the whole truth and does not consider those two facts to be in conflict.

---

### The Scrying Room
*The evening broadcast.*

Not a production house — a distribution network. The Scrying Room operates sixty-three broadcast crystals across Argonnessen and the Io'lokar campus, running evening reports at the sixth and tenth hours. The format is fifteen minutes of news summary, read by an anchor (rotating staff), followed by brief commentary from a Council-adjacent guest.

It is the least biased major outlet purely by format — no room for editorializing when you have fifteen minutes to cover a war. It tends toward Preservationist in its guest selection. The anchors are clean, authoritative, and never say anything that surprises anyone.

## The War Narrative and Its Cracks

The official media consensus on the drow war:

> The Elven Incident confirmed what Redeemer intelligence had been reporting for a decade — that Xil was building toward open aggression against surface powers. The Draconic Council authorized a War Footing response. The giant chain — the passage the drow used to threaten surface lands — has been contested by University-adjacent exploration. A gap year class unknowingly walked it and came back. The drow are the aggressor. Dragons are acting in their Redemption mandate.

The cracks, not yet visible in the consensus press:

- The Elven Incident involved a dragon operative running a long-term deception against a drow matron. Who authorized that operation and why is not public.
- The giant chain's existence was reported to the University by faculty-adjacent intelligence assets before gap year routing decisions were made. Whether gap year routing was influenced by this intelligence — whether students were sent toward the chain knowingly — has not been asked in print.
- The Pale Choir is not in any public reporting. The University's excess student death rate has been covered as an unexplained trend, not a plague.
- The Black Coil prophecy fragments are publicly known. Their current interpretation — that specific living dragons are the "Young Wings" — is not.

Minna Chalck has three of these four threads. She is missing the Pale Choir.

## What the Party Returns To

When the gap year class comes back, the media environment they walk into:

- **Two major papers** want them for different versions of the same heroic story
- **A student journalist** wants the version that isn't heroic and might actually be true
- **A film studio** wants their likenesses and their story, with or without their cooperation
- **A broadcast network** will mention their return as a human-interest note in the evening report and move on

Nobody in the press knows about Alyssa's role. Nobody in the press knows the Black Coil interpretation. Nobody has connected the gap year routing to the Trust's intelligence picture.

The party is, from the outside, the best gap year story in a generation: young dragons who walked into the middle of an active war, saw the enemy city, and came home. They are photogenic. They are students at the world's most prestigious university. They are going to be on the cover of the Flame and Scale again.

What they tell those reporters — and what they don't — will shape the record. The war is still going. The record matters.

## GM Notes

**Narrative uses of the media:**
- Press attention is a social pressure the party can't avoid — it will come to them, at the Viridian Salon, at the University, in the streets of Io'lokar
- Yssa Vorell (Flame and Scale) is a sympathetic character who will print whatever the party says in the most flattering possible light — which is its own kind of danger
- Minna Chalck is a pressure valve: if the party wants the truth told, she is the channel. She will protect sources. She will also keep pulling the thread whether they help or not.
- Wyrmtone's *Long Descent* will be made. The only question is whether the party shapes what it says.
- Alyssa is watching the press coverage closely. If Minna gets close to the Trust connection, Alyssa will become visible in the party's life in a new way.

**The meta-pressure:**
The drow war was sold as Redemption in action. If the party knows it was partly engineered, and if they know the plague is real and untreated in the faculty, they are sitting on information that would not just crack the media consensus — it would fracture the Council factions that the consensus is holding together.

That is a choice the players can make. Not a trap. A choice.
