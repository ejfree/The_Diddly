# The Grand Departure Ceremony

*"Go out and redeem us."*
— Traditional parting words of the Deans to departing students

---

## Overview

The **Grand Departure Ceremony** is the official university-wide sendoff held at the **Grand Old Diddly** arena on the final day before gap year begins. Every student, faculty member, and visiting dignitary attends. It is part graduation, part religious rite, part spectacle — and entirely mandatory.

The ceremony marks the moment student flights leave the safety of The Diddly and enter the real world with a mandate etched into every dragon's upbringing: **redeem dragonkind**.

The cultural weight of this phrase is enormous. Dragonkind has a complicated history — eras of dominance, of isolation, of catastrophic overreach. The Diddly exists, in part, to produce dragons who can engage with the world with wisdom, power, and purpose. Gap year is the crucible. The Grand Departure is the lighting of the flame.

---

## Setting: The Grand Old Diddly in Hippodrome Mode

The Grand Old Diddly arena shifts into **Hippodrome Mode** for the ceremony — the floor cleared of obstacles, configured as an open processional track with a central stage dais rising from the arena floor. The ley lines pulse visibly on the stone in four colors (north, south, east, west) and the seating fills by year from the bottom up:

- **Pit of Primus** (Floor Level) — Wyvern-years who completed gap year, now Primus. They stand, not sit. They are witnesses, not audience.
- **Wyvern Mezz** (2nd Floor) — 4th-year Wyverns who are *not* departing (if any). Watching.
- **Club Level** (3rd Floor) — Faculty, Deans, visiting dignitaries, parents who made the journey.
- **Drake Deck** (4th Floor) — **Departing Drake-year students** — the gap year class. This is their floor. They are the reason for the ceremony.
- **Egg Loft** (5th Floor) — 1st-year Eggies. They are here to watch what awaits them. Wide eyes. Nervous laughter. Silent math about their own odds.

## Ceremony Structure

### I. The Opening Roar

The ceremony begins with no announcement. Instead, every Primus on the floor simultaneously releases a full-throated roar — different types, different elements, different timbre — that fills the arena and shakes the upper balconies. It lasts until one by one they fall silent, leaving a single ancient Primus roaring alone. Then silence.

This is the **Roar of Return** — it means: *we came back*.

### II. Remarks of the Deans

Each of the four Deans (representing the four quads — Fire, Water, Earth, Air) gives a brief address from the central dais. By tradition, each Dean speaks for no more than three minutes, and each address follows the same structure:

1. Honor those who went before (historical gap year class called by name — a different era each year)
2. Name what the students carry with them (the Diddly's legacy)
3. Issue the charge

The final Dean to speak delivers the **Charge of Redemption** — a formal address that ends with the traditional words:

> *"The world did not make room for you. You will make room for yourselves. Go out and redeem us."*

The students on the Drake Deck are expected to respond in unison: ***"We will not fail the flame."***

### III. The Flight Rolls

Each student **flight** is called by name. When called, every member of that flight stands — visible from all levels. A brief description of the flight (their house colors, their flight name) is announced by a Herald Dragon positioned at the center dais.

The flight then performs a **formation signal**: a brief aerial display if the arena ceiling is opened (it is, for this ceremony), or a ground-level claw-strike salute if visibility is poor. The Primus on the floor respond with a single, coordinated strike of their tails on the stone — a thunderclap of acknowledgment.

**Flights called at this ceremony include (non-exhaustive):**

- The Eclipsing Ember Enclave *(Joan, Jimmy, Ciri + 2 others)*
- The Dark Marauders *(Sabellian, Mordred, Ixion, Chernobog, Belindo)*
- ...and all other flights of the departing Drake class

### IV. The Torch Passing

After the flights are called, each departing student descends from the Drake Deck in their flight group and files through the arena floor — past the standing Primus, who make no sound, no gesture. Just watching.

At the far end of the processional track, a **Primus volunteer** (chosen by lottery from among gap year returnees) stands holding a torch of **alchemical dragonfire** — the same fuel used to light the arena's eternal braziers. Each departing student lights a small personal flame-token from this torch. The tokens are small obsidian discs that hold a flame without burning — they are keepsakes, and also subtle wayfinding tools (they point toward The Diddly when held flat).

### V. The Launch

The arena roof opens fully. All four ley lines flare simultaneously.

The flights launch in order — each flight rising through the open roof in formation, banking once over the arena so the audience sees them from below, then breaking outward in their designated direction. The crowd watches until the last flight clears the sightline.

The Egg Loft goes absolutely silent.

## Tone & Details for Play

**What this looks like to Celedyr:**

Celedyr has the Bloodmark from the night before. He knows something the Eggies don't. He's stood in the Eyrie and seen the Quiet Count pillar. When the Primus roar at the opening — he understands what that roar means in a way he didn't before yesterday.

When his flight is called, he stands. The Primus on the floor hit the stone. It vibrates up through his feet.

He lights his flame-token. It feels lighter than it should.

**Environmental hooks:**

- A Primus in the floor-level pit makes eye contact with a departing student they know — and does not smile
- The Dean who delivers the Charge of Redemption is visibly old, scarred from their own gap year — they have earned the right to say these words
- Some Eggies in the loft are cheering. Some are very, very quiet. A few are doing the math.
- The last flight to launch circles the arena twice — a flourish. The crowd roars. They're showing off. The Primus on the floor do not react.

## After the Ceremony

The arena empties slowly. Faculty linger. The Dean of one quad traditionally stays until the last student has left the building — this year that role falls to **Dean Argentius** (Celedyr's advisor). He stands in the empty Pit of Primus after the Wyverns have filed out, watching the open roof.

Students are expected to depart The Diddly's grounds within 24 hours of the ceremony. There is no formal goodbye — that was the ceremony. What happens next is their own.
