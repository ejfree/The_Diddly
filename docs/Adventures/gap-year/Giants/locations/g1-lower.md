# G1 Lower Level

The masonry work is quite good — certainly superior to orcish work, except in a few places. Most stonework is old, even where recently patched. Passageway ceilings are arched and buttressed every 10 feet, with the peak about **17 feet** overhead. Ceiling height in most smallish rooms is **20 feet** (arched and buttressed). Large rooms and chambers reach **30 feet**. Natural cavern areas (Rooms 19–21) vary — passages about 10 feet, large areas 20 feet or more. Cavern 23 has a roof about **40 feet** high. All natural cave areas have stalagmites.

Access from the upper level is via the stairs in Room 18A (kitchen workroom) or the secret steps from Room 10A.

**Wandering Monsters**

Check every 10 minutes (2 exploration rounds of travel). Roll 1d6 in non-natural areas (encounter on a 1); roll 1d20 in natural areas (encounter on a 1).

## **2. Chamber of the Keeper**

### **Description**
The Keeper is a ghastly hill giant — hunchbacked, twisted, and with but one good eye. He is thoroughly wicked and evil, strong, and remorseless. He is never without one of his two pet carnivorous apes, and both are in his lair: one guarding to the north of the door, the other on a platform built above it.

His room has many skins on the floor, a bed, table, chair, two boxes, several crates and barrels, one chest, and odds and ends of armor, weapons, furniture, and tableware. None of the furnishings have any value.

## **3. Cell Block**

### **Description**
In the outer guard room are two bugbear guards armed with morningstars. If not engaged immediately, they will raise an alarm by striking a large brass gong near the door. If faced by an obviously superior enemy and prevented from sounding the gong, they will direct opponents to cell iv (a trap) and flee when the party is occupied.

The room contains a table, two stools, torches, and keys to cells i, ii, iii, and v.

**Cells:**
- **i.** A human prisoner — a merchant, now quite insane (HP 4). No combat capability; requires magical treatment to stabilize his mental state (Remove Curse, 5th level or higher, DC 20 counteract).
- **ii.** A human prisoner (HP 6, Wounded 2), an engineer kept alive to help with dungeon excavation. Cannot fight but will take service and provides a +2 circumstance bonus to checks for understanding the dungeon's construction.
- **iii.** An elf magus chained to the wall. Will serve any good-aligned character for 1 year without pay in gratitude for rescue.
- **iv. TRAP** — Three skeletons, each wearing a brass ring with a glass gem. The bugbears claim the skeletons are magical and that they have no key to this cell. The lock can be forced (DC 18 Athletics). The moment the cell door opens, the three skeletons spring to attack with swords previously hidden by dust and dirt.
- **v.** Large cell with five captured orc rebels. Three others of their number have already been tortured to death; these five are desperate. They will lead the party to Room 19 and get them safely past the guards at **B** if the PCs handle the bugbear at **W** to the west.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Torches in the outer guard room; cells are unlit (darkness).

- **Special Interactions**: The gong, if struck, alerts the entire dungeon level within 2 rounds (2 Initiative turns). Creatures in Rooms 4–8 become alerted and begin moving to investigate.

### **Smells & Sounds**

## **17A. Weird Abandoned Temple**

### **Description**
This room is of faintly glowing purplish-green stone, carved with disturbing shapes and signs which seem to stare out from the walls and columns, and to shift position when the watcher's back is turned. **Touching the walls makes one chilled. Contact with a pillar causes nausea.**

At the far west end is an altar of pale, yellow-gray translucent stone — greasy to the touch but with no mechanical effect on those who touch it. Behind the altar is a flight of low, uneven steps leading to an alcove with a concave back wall of purplish-black, glassy substance.

**If any creature stands before the wall and gazes upon it for one full round**, a writhing amorphous form of sickly mauves and violets appears, stretching formless members toward the viewer — a visual echo of Xoriat. This triggers a DC 28 Will save. On the first creature to touch the glassy wall after a successful save, a *scarab of insanity* appears on the altar. On the second creature, a **5,000 gp gem** appears.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: Faint purplish-green glow from the stone itself — dim light throughout.
- **Surfaces**: Carved purplish-green stone; columns throughout the room.
- **Objects**: Altar (pale yellow-gray translucent stone) at the west end; low uneven steps behind it leading to the alcove.
- **Special Interactions**:
  - *Touching any wall*: DC 18 Fortitude save or Enfeebled 1 for 1 hour (chilled to the bone; cannot be reduced by cold resistance).
  - *Touching a pillar*: DC 18 Fortitude save or Sickened 1 for 1 hour (nausea; save suppressed but not removed by holding breath).
  - *Gazing at the black wall for 1 round*: DC 28 Will save.
    - **Critical Success**: Unaffected; immune to this effect for 24 hours.
    - **Success**: Frightened 2 (clears normally).
    - **Failure**: Doomed 1 and Stupefied 2; these conditions are permanent until removed by *remove curse* (8th-level or higher, counteract DC 28).
    - **Critical Failure**: Permanently Confused (acts as the Confused condition continuously; permanent until removed by a 9th-level or higher effect).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Cold, sterile, and deeply wrong — like nothing natural.
- **Sounds**: A faint subsonic vibration that cannot be located; creatures with tremorsense can detect it at 60 feet.

### **Inhabitants**

- None.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot**: —
- **Magical Items**:
  - *Scarab of Insanity* (Item 12, unique, cursed) — appears on the altar after the first creature touches the black wall and succeeds its save. When **Activated** [one-action] *(Interact)*: releases a 30-foot emanation of psychic dissonance. Each creature in the area attempts a DC 28 Will save or becomes Confused for 1d4 rounds (Confused for 1 minute on a critical failure). The wielder is immune.   - **5,000 gp gem** (appears on the altar after the second creature touches the black wall and succeeds its save).

## **19. Natural Cavern (Orc Rebel Sanctuary)**

### **Description**
This is part of a large area of natural caves which the ancient dungeon builders came upon. It is now inhabited by **78 escaped orc slaves** — rebels against the hill giants — for whom this is a sanctuary. (See map position **G** for their guard posts.)

The orcs are armed with clubs, axes, daggers, and various cast-off weapons; there are 7 crossbows among them. They have 3 leader-types, 12 guards of great strength, and 63 others. They get food from raids, supplemented by fungus from a subterranean cave reached through a sinkhole in the southeast. They have an uneasy truce with the troglodytes in Area 20, trading food for water.

If approached non-hostilely and fighting bugbears or giants is mentioned, they will become agreeable and cooperative allies. Freeing the prisoners from Rooms 9–14 significantly improves their attitude (from Indifferent to Friendly, granting a +2 circumstance bonus to Diplomacy checks).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting**: A few stolen torches, kept low — dim light in the main area; darkness in the outer tunnels.
- **Surfaces**: Natural cavern with stalagmites throughout (difficult terrain in peripheral areas).
- **Objects**: Crude barricades at the entrances (positions G on the map; DC 18 Athletics to breach; guarded by 2 orc rebels each).
- **Special Interactions**: The orcs refer to the rebel orcs from the upper level (Room 20 of upper works) as kin. If the party has already spoken with those rebels, the reception here improves one step automatically.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells**: Unwashed bodies and fungus.
- **Sounds**: Hushed voices; the orcs maintain quiet discipline.
