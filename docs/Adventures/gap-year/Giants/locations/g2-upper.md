# G2 Upper Level — Glacial Rift of the Frost Giant Jarl

The Glacial Rift is a massive crack in the alpine glacier of the Eska Mountains, hundreds of feet deep and half a mile long. Wind screams through it constantly. The walls are blue-green ice ribbed with rock, and crude ice-cut stairs and platforms descend in switchbacks toward the cave complex below. Conditions are lethal to the unprepared: ambient temperature is below freezing at all times, surfaces are slick, and visibility is reduced by blowing snow and ice crystals.

A DC 16 Athletics check is required to move at full speed on the icy ledges and stairs. Failure means the character moves at half speed; a critical failure means a fall (1d6 × 10 feet of distance, damage as appropriate).

The upper rift contains a series of natural and cut-ice caves along the rift walls before the path descends to the Jarl's Hall below. The caves are lettered A through F. The great iron door to the interior complex is at the bottom of the rift.

**Burning it Down:** Fire magic melts the ice walls and floors in the upper rift, creating dangerous meltwater floods (treat as fast-moving water). This is impressive but tactically unwise.

## **1. The Rift Entrance**
The top of the rift — wind, cold, and a long way down.

### **Description**
A natural gap in the glacier, roughly 40 feet wide at the top, narrowing as it descends. Crude iron pitons and knotted rope lines have been hammered into the ice to help giant-sized feet navigate the switchbacks down. The wind is savage here and the cold bites immediately. At the bottom, 200 feet below, a faint orange-red glow hints at fires within the ice cave complex.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Natural daylight at the top, fading as one descends.
- **Surfaces:** Ice-covered stone and compacted snow; all movement is difficult terrain.
- **Special Interactions:** The rope lines are sized for giants (20-foot knots, designed for hands the size of barrels). Small folk can still use them as guide ropes. DC 14 Athletics to descend at normal speed; DC 10 if using the ropes.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Clean, sharp ice and wind.
- **Sounds:** The constant howl of wind through the rift. At DC 24 Perception: distant drums from the interior.

## **2. Guard Cave Alpha**
The first watch post, carved into the south rift wall.

### **Description**
A broad cave cut roughly into the ice wall of the rift, approximately 60 feet above the bottom. A crude wooden platform extends from the cave mouth to give a clear view down the rift. Inside are four frost giants in cold-weather furs and iron armor, warming themselves at a massive fire pit. A signal horn large enough for a giant to blow sits on an iron hook by the cave entrance. If it is sounded, all inhabitants of the rift are alerted within 1 round.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The cave mouth is open to daylight; the fire pit provides warmth and light inside.
- **Surfaces:** Ice floor with straw and hides thrown down.
- **Objects:** Two great crossbows (ballista-sized) are mounted at the cave mouth, pointing down the rift. They fire boulders and deal 4d10 damage; DC 20 to operate.
- **Special Interactions:** The guards are alert and making a DC 18 Perception check every 10 minutes.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Smoke, wet fur, boiling meat.
- **Sounds:** Giants arguing in Jotun over the watch schedule.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Frost Giants

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In the bedrolls: 1,200 sp, 320 gp (combined personal wealth).
- **Visible Loot:** A chest in the back contains the week's rations for the guard post — no value — plus 8 furs worth 50 gp each to the right buyer.

## **3. Cave Beta — Slave Pens and Storage**
Frozen supplies and miserable prisoners.

### **Description**
A large natural cave in the north rift wall, its entrance partially screened by an ice shelf. The rear two-thirds of the cave is packed with frozen provisions — haunches of mammoths and cave bears hanging from iron hooks in the ceiling, barrels of preserved fish, sacks of grain, and casks of mead frozen solid. The front third holds a crude slave pen (iron bars set into the ice) containing 20 orc laborers who have been pressed into service carrying supplies down the rift. Two frost giants oversee the stores.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two torches in iron brackets; dim.
- **Objects:** 4 chains of keys on the wall (one fits the slave pen lock, one fits the provision chest, two are dummies).
- **Special Interactions:** The orc slaves will cooperate fully if freed. They know the layout of the rift caves and the approximate strength of the Jarl's hall below, and they know the Jarl keeps a white dragon as a "pet" in Cave Epsilon. They are unarmed but know where the guards' spare weapons are stored (buried under the provision barrels).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold meat, fish, and packed bodies.
- **Sounds:** The clatter of chains; the occasional command in Jotun.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 20 Orc slaves (non-combatant; will assist PCs if freed).
- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Frost Giants

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Buried under the provision barrels: 8,000 sp and 4,000 gp (payment being held for mercenary frost giant auxiliaries).

## **4. Cave Gamma — Winter Wolf Pack**
The Jarl's hunting pack, and an alarm system.

### **Description**
A wide ice cave that serves as the kennel for the Jarl's winter wolves. Twelve winter wolves are here — some sleeping, some pacing restlessly. They are aggressive to any non-giant who enters and will howl immediately, alerting the entire rift complex. The wolves are used as hunting companions and perimeter alarms; frost giants handle them with whistles (each giant guard carries one).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — the wolves need none.
- **Surfaces:** Deep straw over ice floor.
- **Special Interactions:** A DC 24 Nature check or Animal Handling check can calm one wolf (not the pack). The wolves recognize the signal whistle tone (DC 26 Performance to mimic convincingly).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Wet fur and blood.
- **Sounds:** Growling, padding footsteps, the crack of frozen bones being chewed.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 12 Winter Wolves

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Frozen into the cave wall at the back (requires chipping out): 2 gems (500 gp each), dropped by a previous victim.

## **5. Cave Delta — Yeti Den**
Wild, dangerous, and barely controlled.

### **Description**
A deep natural cave that smells of wet animal and old blood. Six yetis have been brought here and conditioned (through starvation and beatings) to respond to giant commands — though "obedient" is generous. They are given carcasses and left alone between raids. They will attack anyone who enters who is not accompanied by a giant or who is not carrying a giant's "permission brand" (a hot iron mark carried by the giant handlers).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Special Interactions:** If the yetis are released into the rift during a raid, they are uncontrollable — they attack friend and foe alike.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Wet animal musk and rotting meat.
- **Sounds:** Deep rhythmic grunting; bones crunching.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 6 Yetis

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In a pile of debris the yetis have gathered: crude silver jewelry taken from victims (total 600 gp in raw value).

## **6. Cave Epsilon — White Dragon's Lair**
The Jarl's prize — and his most dangerous possession.

### **Description**
A vast ice cavern at the lowest accessible point of the upper rift, before the great iron door to the interior. This is the lair of Kelvakrix, a young adult white dragon the Jarl keeps as a prize and status symbol. Kelvakrix is mean, territorial, and regards the frost giants as inferiors he is merely tolerating. He is *not* under their control — the Jarl simply feeds him well enough that he stays. He considers the rift his territory and will attack intruders without warning.

Kelvakrix's breath is a 60-foot cone of cold.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Kelvakrix's pale bioluminescent scales provide a faint blue glow.
- **Surfaces:** Pure blue ice, sculpted by the dragon's breath into jagged formations. Extremely difficult terrain for non-fliers.
- **Objects:** Kelvakrix's personal hoard is piled at the back of the cave — coins and items encased in ice. Requires DC 18 Athletics to excavate without proper tools.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold, ozone, and a faint charnel smell from old kills.
- **Sounds:** The susurrus of scales on ice. A low, dismissive rumble.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Kelvakrix, Young Adult White Dragon

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** The hoard is encased in ice and partially visible: piles of coins, scattered objects, a few items clearly magical.
- **Hidden Loot (excavated):** 5,000 sp, 3,000 gp, 2 gems (1,000 gp each).
- **Magical Items:** A *ring of cold resistance* (worn on a skeletal hand still frozen into the hoard), a *scroll of fireball* (ironic — found in the hoard, readable only after thawing).

## **7. Cave Zeta — Cave Bear Den**
Trophies-in-waiting for the Jarl's hall.

### **Description**
A long narrow cave used as a holding area for cave bears the frost giants intend to train as war-beasts or slaughter for their pelts and meat. Four cave bears are penned here behind a heavy iron gate. They are hungry and in foul temper.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Special Interactions:** The gate lock is DC 20 Thievery to pick. If the bears are released, they will attack the nearest living creature — which may or may not be the party.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Bear musk, old blood.
- **Sounds:** Low, continuous growling; the clank of the gate being tested.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Cave Bears

## **8. The Great Iron Door**
The threshold between the rift and the Jarl's hall.

### **Description**
At the base of the rift, where the ice walls close to within 30 feet of each other, a massive iron door has been fitted into the rock. It is 20 feet tall, 12 feet wide, banded in cold iron, and decorated with carved images of frost giant conquests. Two frost giants stand guard here at all times. The door is barred from the inside but can be unbarred by the guards on either side. A speaking tube allows communication with the interior.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two massive braziers flank the door, kept burning at all times.
- **Objects:** The speaking tube connects to the interior guardroom. The guards will challenge anyone who approaches who is not a known frost giant.
- **Special Interactions:** If the speaking tube is used to call inside before the door is opened, whoever is on the interior side will demand a countersign (a word changed daily; the orc slaves in Cave Beta know it).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hot iron and coal smoke from the braziers.
- **Sounds:** The creak of iron in extreme cold; muffled noise from within.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Frost Giants (exterior guards)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** The guards' personal wealth: 400 gp combined.

## **9. The Provision Cave**
Frozen stores — and something valuable buried within.

### **Description**
A wide ice cave used to store frozen provisions for the rift complex. Haunches of mammoth and cave bear hang from ceiling hooks; stacked barrels line the walls; sacks of preserved grain and fish are piled near the entrance. The cold is intense even by rift standards — everything here is locked in ice.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None; the cold preserves well enough without fire.
- **Surfaces:** Ice floor packed with frost; movement is difficult terrain.
- **Objects:** Rows of barrels, many frozen shut. The third barrel from the left on the south wall conceals the hidden cache.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold air and the faint mineral smell of deep ice.
- **Sounds:** Silence, broken only by the distant wind from the rift above.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Beneath the third barrel: 3,000 gp in loose coin, 4 gems worth 1,000 gp each, and a *scroll of cure serious wounds* (cleric scroll, rolled inside a silver tube worth 300 gp).

## **10. The Ogres' Antechamber**
Petitioners waiting on the Jarl's pleasure — watchful and greedy.

### **Description**
A rough-cut cave near the descent to the iron door, currently occupied by four ogres who have traveled here seeking audience with **Jarl Grugnur**. They are impatient, bored, and suspicious of strangers. They will not attack without provocation — they need the Jarl's goodwill — but they will demand to know the party's business and will report anything unusual to the giant guards.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** A single torch jammed into a crack in the wall; dim.
- **Surfaces:** Bare ice over stone.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Unwashed bodies and rancid meat packed for travel.
- **Sounds:** Guttural argument in the Ogre tongue; the sound of something being sharpened.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Cautious Threats:** 4 Ogres (hostile if provoked or if intruders lack a convincing cover)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Each ogre carries a personal sack: 100–400 gp each, plus 1–4 gems worth 100 gp each (roll per ogre).

## **11. The South Hall**
A mob of ogres, poorly disciplined and dangerously armed.

### **Description**
A broad natural cavern deeper in the south wall of the rift, serving as a holding area for a much larger ogre force — twelve ogres encamped with their gear spread across the floor. Chests, sacks, and barrels are stacked against the walls; skins are piled for sleeping. The ogres are quarrelsome and restless but not currently on alert.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two fire pits, smoky and irregular; dim in the corners.
- **Objects:** Two chests, four sacks, and a large barrel visible from the entrance.
  - **Chest #2** contains an ear collection — dozens of preserved humanoid ears. No monetary value.
  - **Chest #4** holds coin: 1,300 cp / 2,111 sp / 792 ep / 5,300 gp.
  - **Sack #9** holds silver jewelry (6 pieces, 100–600 gp each) and gold jewelry (8 pieces, 200–800 gp each).
  - **Barrel #1** is filled with skulls. No monetary value.
  - Beneath the tenth skin pile: 2 *potions of healing* and a gold armband set with ivory and amber in a bear motif (worth 2,000 gp as jewelry). The armband functions as a **pass token** granting audience with the Jarl.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Smoke, rotting trophies, and the sour reek of packed ogres.
- **Sounds:** Dice games, shouted arguments, something being sharpened on stone.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 12 Ogres

### **Loot & Rewards**

- See objects listing above.

## **12. The Guard Choke**
A hammer-and-anvil killing ground designed to destroy anything that gets past the iron door.

### **Description**
Two connected ice caves flanking a narrow corridor — the passage all traffic must use coming down from the rift. Eight frost giants are posted here in two squads of four: one squad in the north cave, one in the south. The arrangement is intentional. When intruders enter the corridor, the north squad engages from the front while the south squad hits from behind, pinching attackers between them with no room to maneuver.

The giants in the north cave (Area 9) are on full alert at all times. The giants in the south cave (Area 10) wait behind a concealed ice bulkhead until the moment to strike.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches in the caves; the corridor between is dim.
- **Surfaces:** Narrow passage — Medium creatures can move two abreast at most. Larger creatures must squeeze.
- **Special Interactions:** The south squad's position (Area 10) grants them a surprise attack on the first round if the party has not detected them (DC 22 Perception to notice the concealment before the trap springs).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Iron armor, torch smoke, frost.
- **Sounds:** The creak of cold iron; heavy breathing barely audible through the ice wall.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 8 Frost Giants (4 per cave; coordinated ambush)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **No Treasure:** The north cave (Area 9) giants carry nothing of value on watch.
- **Hidden Loot (south cave, Area 10):** 8 bags left with the off-duty squad, each containing 200–1,200 gp. A stone box on a ledge above the fire holds 6 gold armbands and 21 gems at 100 gp each.

## **13. The Corpse Gallery**
Eight victims frozen in time — and in ice.

### **Description**
A high-ceilinged ice cavern where eight humanoid figures stand (or float, or kneel) suspended in blocks of transparent ice like terrible statues. Each is perfectly preserved: clothing, equipment, and all. Someone — presumably the Jarl or his predecessors — has arranged them facing the entrance as a display.

The figures are:
1. A **dwarf** in battered armor, clutching a *battle axe +1*.
2. An **elf** in traveler's robes, holding a *wand of cold* (16 charges).
3. A **human** in merchant's clothes, wearing a jeweled belt worth 7,000 gp.
4. A **human** warrior, one hand pressed to a *scroll of protection from elementals* tucked into their chest.
5. A **dwarf** in mining gear, a belt pouch with 37 gems at 10 gp each sewn into the lining.
6. A **human** in scorched robes, wearing a *ring of fire resistance*.
7. A **half-elf** — young, by the look of them — carrying a sack of 471 sp.
8. A **human** in what appears to be ornate plate armor, seemingly in fine condition. The armor is *armor of vulnerability* (AC −2, though it appears to be +2 until the wearer is first struck in combat).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The translucent ice blocks catch and refract available light, filling the room with shifting blue-white refractions. Quite beautiful.
- **Surfaces:** Smooth ice floor. Difficult terrain.
- **Special Interactions — Fire Hazard:** Any fire spell or effect triggers catastrophic ceiling collapse. A *fireball* destroys all items in the room. Chipping at the ice blocks with lesser heat (torches, *burning hands*, etc.) has a 10% cumulative chance per block of triggering a ceiling collapse for 6d10 damage to everyone in the cave.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold and clean — utterly odorless.
- **Sounds:** Silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Accessible (with careful chipping):** Items as listed per figure above. Fire destroys everything.

## **14. The Bone Caves**
Something worships here — and it will defend its idol.

### **Description**
Two connected chambers (Areas 11 and 12) used as a disposal site for the larger complex: bones, broken equipment, and discarded refuse cover the floor in a deep, uneven carpet. The chambers reek of old death. Five ice toads inhabit these caves, emerging from Area 12 (the lower bone cave) to investigate any noise. They are otherwise motionless on their ledges, 8–12 feet above the floor, watching.

The toads are not aggressive unless their idol is threatened: a large amethyst toad carved from a single stone, resting on a rocky protuberance in Area 11. It is worth 5,000 gp. The moment any creature touches it, all five toads attack simultaneously.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Bones and rubble — extremely difficult terrain. Searching Area 11 takes 1 turn per 10-foot square.
- **Special Interactions:** The toads on the ledges are nearly invisible in the dark; DC 22 Perception to notice them before they move. They hear through vibration in the floor — moving quietly (DC 20 Stealth) may prevent them from investigating.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Old bone, old blood, and something amphibious and wet.
- **Sounds:** Silence from above; a faint wet slapping from Area 12 if listening.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 5 Ice Toads (attack if idol is touched; otherwise neutral)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** The **amethyst toad idol** (5,000 gp) on the protuberance — easily seen, dangerously claimed.

## **15. The Misty Passage**
Warm air from below collides with the glacier cold — and the footing suffers for it.

### **Description**
A long connecting passage where volcanic warmth seeping up from the lower levels meets the perpetual cold of the glacier. The result is a dense, churning fog that fills the passage floor-to-ceiling. Visibility is reduced to 3 feet in all directions. The floor beneath the fog is slick from condensation and meltwater.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None visible through the fog.
- **Surfaces:** Ice-coated stone, wet. Every 10 feet of movement requires a DC 14 Acrobatics check; on a failure (2-in-6 chance), the character slips and falls prone.
- **Special Interactions:** Any item dropped in this passage has a 1-in-4 chance of sliding into an unseen crack in the floor and being permanently lost.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Warm, mineral-rich steam; almost sulfurous.
- **Sounds:** The drip of water and the hiss of vapor.

## **16. The Snow Leopard Den**
Silent, swift, and already watching.

### **Description**
A cave off the main passage, its floor a raised ledge 8 feet above the entry. Two snow leopards — pets of the yetis in Room 5 — spend most of their time on that ledge, motionless and invisible against the white ice. They drop on anything that enters without warning.

The leopards always attack with surprise (1–3 on a d6) or complete surprise (4–6 on a d6). They are sleek, fast, and perfectly adapted to the ice.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Smooth ice below; rocky ledge above.
- **Special Interactions:** The leopards will not pursue out of the cave. If both are killed or driven off, the yetis in Room 5 become immediately agitated (DC 18 to notice the behavioral change if observing them).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Almost nothing — snow leopards are fastidiously clean.
- **Sounds:** Nothing until they land on you.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Snow Leopards (always surprise; ledge position)

## **17. The Barracks Complex**
The main reserve force of the rift — spread across four interconnected caves.

### **Description**
Four large interconnected caves (Areas 16–19) form the primary barracks for the frost giants posted to the rift. Eighteen giants total are distributed across the complex: four in Area 16, four in Area 17, six in Area 18, and four in Area 19. All are armed with clubs, spears, and four boulders apiece. They are off-duty but not unaware; noise or alarm from elsewhere in the rift will bring them to full readiness within 1 round.

Area 18 — the central and largest cave — has a natural spring in the floor surrounded by a crust of 272 rock crystals, each worth 10 gp.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Fire pits in each cave; smoky and dim in the connecting passages.
- **Surfaces:** Straw and hides over ice floor.
- **Special Interactions:** The four caves are connected by short passages. Alert in one cave spreads to all within 1 round. Fighting in the barracks is a war of attrition — giants can retreat and regroup between caves.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Unwashed giant, torch smoke, boiled meat.
- **Sounds:** Snoring, dice rattling, occasional argument.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 18 Frost Giants total (4/4/6/4 across Areas 16–19)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** 272 rock crystals in Area 18's spring crust — 10 gp each (2,720 gp total if collected; takes 30 minutes to fully harvest).
- **Hidden Loot:** Personal wealth scattered in sleeping piles: 200–1,200 gp per giant (18 giants total).

## **18. The Visitors' Caves**
Emissaries from three different giant clans — politics in ice.

### **Description**
Three adjoining caves have been set aside for visitors attending the Jarl's gathering. Each hosts a different delegation, and the diplomatic implications of what is assembled here are significant.

**Area 24 — Hill Giants:** Five hill giants who have been summoned by the Jarl and are awaiting their audience. They are rough, loud, and bored. Each wears a fur cloak worth 1,000–6,000 gp. Among their baggage, **Bag #4** holds a gold-inlaid skull containing a written report from **Nosnra** (the hill giant chief, now dead) and a **pass token** granting entry to **King Snurre's Hall**.

**Area 25 — Stone Giants:** Five stone giants here as observers — they have not yet committed to the coalition and are studying everything. They carry a **jeweled crown** worth 15,000 gp tucked inside a rolled skin. They will not fight unless directly attacked; if attacked, they will defend themselves but not pursue.

**Area 26 — Fire Giant Messengers:** Three fire giant messengers preparing to depart. Cave C is lit by a dim volcanic-red glow from the heat they radiate. They carry proof of the Jarl's fealty to **King Snurre**: a solid silver rampant bear statuette with topaz eyes (500 gp each × 2) and ruby fangs (8 gems at 500 gp each), worth 6,000 gp total as a piece. Additionally, three sacks each holding 3,000 gp for transport to Snurre.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches in Areas 24 and 25; Area 26 is lit only by the fire giants' own heat.
- **Special Interactions:** The stone giants will describe the assembly to any party that manages to talk with them — they are observing, not participating, and are not hostile. The fire giants are departing and will not engage unless blocked.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hill giant musk in Area 24; stone dust in Area 25; volcanic heat and sulfur in Area 26.
- **Sounds:** Hill giant carousing; stone giant silence; fire giant heavy footsteps preparing to move.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Area 24:** 5 Hill Giants (hostile if pushed)
- **Area 25:** 5 Stone Giants (neutral; will fight if attacked)
- **Area 26:** 3 Fire Giant Messengers (will leave if allowed; hostile if blocked)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Area 24:** Fur cloaks (1,000–6,000 gp each); Bag #4 contains the **Nosnra report** and **pass to King Snurre's Hall**.
- **Area 25:** Jeweled crown worth 15,000 gp hidden in skins.
- **Area 26:** Silver bear statuette worth 6,000 gp; three sacks of 3,000 gp each (9,000 gp total).

### **Additional Notes**
The **pass to King Snurre's Hall** from Bag #4 is a key handout — along with the stone tablets in Room 18 and the map in Room 11 (lower level), it provides independent confirmation of the route to G3.

## **19. The Brown Mold Corridor**
A warning in runes — and something worse past the bend.

### **Description**
A narrow ice passage marked at the entrance by frost giant runes carved into the floor. The runes are a warning: *"Turn back. Death lives here."* A DC 16 Society check (or any character who reads Giant) deciphers them. Ten feet past the westward bend in the passage, the walls are covered floor-to-ceiling in **brown mold** — a vast, well-fed colony that has expanded to fill the corridor completely.

The skeletal remains of at least three frost giants lie at the edge of the mold growth. They were not clever enough to heed their own warning.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Ice passage; the mold zone begins 10 feet past the bend.
- **Special Interactions — Brown Mold:** Brown mold feeds on heat. Any fire or heat source brought near it causes the mold to grow rapidly (doubling in 1 round). Cold damages or kills it. Creatures within 5 feet of the mold take 3d6 cold damage per round; fire immunity does not help.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** A faint organic dampness past the bend, wrong in context of the clean ice.
- **Sounds:** Silence.

### **Additional Notes**
The passage is a dead end beyond the mold. There is no treasure here and no reason to push through — the warning exists for good reason.

## **20. The Remorhaz Dome**
Recent and terrible — a new apex predator has moved in.

### **Description**
A snow-covered dome of ice at the periphery of the rift, its ceiling naturally formed into a hemisphere thirty feet high. Something has recently claimed this space: the bones of previous occupants (frost giant, orc, and unidentifiable beast) are scattered across the icy floor, gnawed and cracked. A **remorhaz** — 30 feet long, its back ridges currently dimmed to orange as it rests — has moved into this space within the last tenday. The frost giants have not yet discovered it.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The remorhaz's heat ridges provide faint orange-red light.
- **Surfaces:** Ice floor, slick with meltwater from the creature's heat.
- **Special Interactions:** Fire magic used in this enclosed space will destroy items near the creature (including those on the bones). The domed ceiling conducts heat poorly — fire spells will not collapse the dome, but sustained heat will melt the floor into a shallow pool.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Sulfur and cooked bone; markedly warmer than the surrounding rift.
- **Sounds:** A rhythmic clicking from chitin on ice; the hiss of meltwater.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 1 Remorhaz (30 feet long; always hungry)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot (on the bones):** On one human skeleton: a *ring of three wishes* on the bony finger of an outstretched hand; a *bastard sword +2 / giant slayer* lying in the ice nearby. Fire magic near the skeleton destroys both items.

## **21. The Sinkhole**
The way down — if you survive the descent.

### **Description**
A circular sinkhole in the rift floor, 12 feet in diameter, its walls coated in black ice. The shaft drops 100 feet straight down, opening into the lower level of the complex. There is no ladder; the frost giants descend by rope (coiled near the edge — two giant-sized ropes, each anchored to iron spikes hammered into the ice).

The shaft walls are not empty. Two **white puddings** — one near the top, one approximately midway down — cling to the ice walls, nearly invisible against the pale surface. They are currently inactive (feeding mode, barely moving), but anything descending the shaft will disturb them.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None in the shaft; a faint orange glow from fires below is visible at the bottom.
- **Surfaces:** Ice-coated shaft walls. DC 18 Athletics to climb without a rope; DC 12 with the giant-sized ropes (awkward but possible for smaller folk).
- **Special Interactions:** The white puddings on the shaft walls can be spotted with DC 20 Perception before descending. If not spotted, they attack when a creature reaches their level on the shaft.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold stone and the faint warm-air updraft from the lower level.
- **Sounds:** The distant sound of voices and fires from 100 feet below.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 White Puddings (one near top, one at midpoint of shaft)

### **Additional Notes**
The sinkhole connects the upper rift to the lower level. It is an alternative to the main route through the Great Iron Door (Room 8) and bypasses the interior guard room (lower Room 9). Arriving this way puts the party in the lower complex without passing through the standard entry — a significant tactical advantage if the main entrance is alerted.
