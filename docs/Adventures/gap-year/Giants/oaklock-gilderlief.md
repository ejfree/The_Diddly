# Oaklock Gilderlief

**Elf (Aerenal) Magus 7** — Court Mage and Field Intelligence Officer, House Silaen
**Hybrid Study**: Laughing Shadow
**Alignment**: NG
**Age**: 247 (young for an Aerenal elf; he has not yet petitioned for undying status)

---

## Who He Is

Oaklock Gilderlief is the intelligence officer of Lady Aelindra of House Silaen. His formal title within the house is *Cipher Attendant* — an Aerenal court position that translates, roughly, to "the person who knows things no one else in the house should have to know."

House Silaen survives by keeping useful secrets quietly. Oaklock is the mechanism for that. He monitors threats before they become crises, files reports the house can deny receiving, and travels the uncomfortable routes that Lady Aelindra cannot take while maintaining deniability. He has been doing this for forty years.

He is meticulous, controlled, and politically fluent in the understated way of minor Aerenal nobility — the kind of person who delivers bad news without inflection and receives it the same way. His detachment is genuine but not callous. He cares about House Silaen's survival. He cares about Lady Aelindra, who he has served for four decades. What has happened — he doesn't yet know the full shape of it, but what he suspects sits under everything.

---

## Why He Was Here

Lady Aelindra became aware of Xil scout activity in the upper passages roughly two years ago through careful intermediary contact. She never went direct. Strict deniability was doctrine for House Silaen. What she wanted was information: which way was the war pointing, and how fast.

She sent Oaklock.

He has been operating in the upper passage networks for eight months, filing reports back through Sivis message stations under commercial cipher. He mapped patrol routes, observed supply-caching patterns, and tracked the pace of drow forward-positioning in the tunnels between the surface and D1. His reports were meticulous. His conclusions were grim: the drow were not preparing to patrol. They were preparing to push.

He was captured by a hill giant hunting party four months ago, returning from a forward observation point. The giants weren't looking for him specifically — he was simply on the wrong route when they were ranging north. His equipment was stripped and distributed through the dungeon. His spellbook was taken and is currently in the possession of **The Keeper** (Room 2), tucked under the loose floor stone with the rest of his valuables — the Keeper found it incomprehensible but suspected it was worth something.

He has been in Cell iii since.

---

## What He Knows

**Confirmed intelligence (operational)**:
- Drow forward-positioning in the upper passages has been accelerating for at least six months prior to his capture — supply caches, scout rotations, tunnel clearing
- There is a waystation carved into the rock between the upper passages and D1 that saw regular, scheduled traffic for at least two years; the pattern suggests diplomatic or recurring covert contact, not supply runs
- The pace of activity suggested war was not speculative — it was being prepared for

**What he has pieced together in captivity**:
- He can hear things from the cell. Monsters come and go. Orc prisoners talked before being taken to Room 17. He knows a war started. He does not know the trigger or the details.
- He suspects House Thalmyr's scouts noticed him — his last two routes were compressed by patrols that moved wrong. Whether that contributed to his capture or was coincidence, he cannot say.

**What he does not know**:
- That someone spent two years impersonating Lady Aelindra in private meetings with Matron Vel'dryn
- That the impersonator was a dragon operating on behalf of an ancient emerald dragon professor at a dragon university
- That the discovery of the impersonation triggered the war order
- That Operation Arenal exists at all

When he learns any of this, his first response will be controlled. His second will be to identify every way it could destroy House Silaen — and there are several.

---

## Personality in Play

- Speaks carefully. Does not speculate aloud. Asks clarifying questions before drawing conclusions.
- Visibly shaken on rescue but will not perform relief; he'll say "thank you" once, mean it, and move on
- Becomes sharp and focused the moment he understands the players know something about the upper passages or Xil
- When told about the impersonation: goes very quiet. Then: *"Lady Aelindra never met the Matron directly. She was explicit about that. She was never going to meet anyone directly."* Then he starts asking questions.
- Will not lie to the party once he decides they're trustworthy. Getting there takes one honest exchange.
- Has no illusions about House Silaen's position. Small house, minor nobility, caught between a drow war and a dragon intelligence operation. He is already thinking about how to mitigate.

---

## What He Wants

**Immediate**: His spellbook (The Keeper's treasure, Room 2). Without it he can prepare new spells after a full rest, but the encoded intelligence records in the margins are irreplaceable.

**Short-term**: Get a message to Lady Aelindra. She needs to know her identity was stolen, that a war was triggered in her name, and that House Silaen needs to establish its own account of events before someone else does it for them.

**If the party is heading deeper**: He will offer to accompany them. He knows the upper passage routes, the waystation, and the approach to D1 better than anyone currently above D1. He has personal reasons to see Xil.

---

## His Value to the Party in D3

Oaklock is potentially the most useful corroborating witness the party can carry into Xil.

- He can confirm the real Lady Aelindra's mission and methods — she was gathering intelligence through intermediaries, she was never going direct, she would never have initiated a two-year personal relationship with a Matron Mother
- He knows the approximate timeline of House Silaen's contact initiation and can place it against whatever Vel'dryn believes she experienced
- He has no reason to lie about any of this; if anything, the truth helps his house more than any cover story
- He can be sent to Aerenal as a party asset — if the PCs want to bring the real Aelindra into the picture, he is the right courier
