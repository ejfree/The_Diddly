# Giant Quotes by Type and Occasion

Quotes for roleplaying giants in various situations. Each giant type speaks in a distinct British regional accent — read these aloud as written, lean into the dialect, and don't worry about perfection. The flavor matters more than accuracy.

**Occasions covered**: Battle · Dining · Bathing · Working with Animals · Overseeing Slaves

---

## Hill Giants — West Country (Somerset / Devon)

> *Rustic, agricultural, straightforward. Think very large Somerset farmer. Drops h's, uses "thee" and "thy", says "arr" like punctuation. Not stupid — just uncomplicated.*

### Battle

- "Come on then, get over 'ere! I'll knock thee clean into next week, I will!"
- "Stop runnin'! Arr, they always run. Proper annoying, that is."
- "That were a proper smack, weren't it? Aye, 'e felt that one."
- "Reckon I'll eat 'im after. Waste not, want not — that's what me mum always said."
- "Tis not looking good for thee, I'll be honest about it."

### Dining

- "Pass us that leg, would thee? The big one. Nay, the OTHER big one."
- "This 'ere stew's got summat wrong with it. 'As thee put enough bones in?"
- "I ate eleven of 'em and I'm still not full. This is proper embarrassin', this is."
- "Don't talk while yer chewin', Gorg. Tis bad manners."
- "Arr, lovely. Proper job. Someone's done well tonight."

### Bathing

- "The river'll do. Always done the river. Soap's for them fancy stone giants."
- "Arr, I washed last autumn. Or were it the autumn before? No matter."
- "Stop splashing! Yer gettin' me dry side wet!"
- "I fell in a bog last week and that were basically a bath, weren't it?"

### Working with Animals

- "Easy, girl, easy. She don't like new folk. She bites. Should've warned thee."
- "These dire boars is smarter than the orcs, I always reckon. And better tempered."
- "That bear won't go where it don't want to go. Tha just 'as to wait 'er out."
- "She's expecting. Don't prod 'er. I ain't telling thee again."
- "Arr, there's a good lad. See? 'E just wanted to be asked proper."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "Get along with thee. Ain't got all day, 'ave I?"
- "Stop that sulking. Tha's 'ere to work, not to sulk."
- "If I 'as to come over there, neither of us'll be 'appy about it."
- "Arr, tha's a small one. What use is thee, then? ...Fine, pile them stones."

## Stone Giants — Yorkshire

> *Stoic, laconic craftsmen and cave-artists. Proud but not showy. Direct to the point of bluntness. "Tha" for "you", "nowt" for nothing, "reight" for right, "by 'eck" for emphasis.*

### Battle

- "Tha's picked t'wrong spot for this."
- "By 'eck, 'e just kept coming. Give 'im credit for that."
- "Nowt wrong wi' a good rock. Never let me down yet."
- "Get thi sen sorted. We've not finished 'ere."
- "Reight. That's enough of that, then."

### Dining

- "It's not bad. Could do wi' more salt."
- "Tha's not eating. Summat wrong wi' thee?"
- "Grand. Champion. Pass t'rest over."
- "I don't want owt fancy. Just give us what there is."
- "Happen it's t'best meal we'll get this month. So eat it."

### Bathing

- "I'll do it when I'm ready. 'Appen I'm not ready yet."
- "Reight. T'pool at t'back. Fifteen minutes. Then back to it."
- "Tha can smell thistle and clean stone on a stone giant. That's how it should be."
- "I've been in t'dust three days. Suppose."

### Working with Animals

- "She's reight. Leave 'er be."
- "That mammoth knows 'er job better than 'alf t'lads here."
- "Tha doesn't rush 'em. Tha waits. Then tha asks again."
- "By 'eck, 'e's a good dog. Best 'ere, probably."
- "Nowt wrong wi' a stubborn animal. Means it's got principles."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "That pile wants moving. Over there. Get on wi' it."
- "Tha's not been ill-treated. Do t'work."
- "I don't want to hear it. I don't want to talk about it. Move them stones."
- "Fair enough effort. Don't tell 'em I said that."

## Frost Giants — Scottish Highlands

> *Fierce, proud, clan-bound warriors. Honor matters enormously. Can be generous but terrifying in anger. "Dinnae" for don't, "braw" for fine/good, "och" as an all-purpose expression, "nae" for no.*

### Battle

- "Och, ye thought that would stop me? Ye sweet wee fool."
- "Dinnae run, now. It only makes it worse for the both of us."
- "By the frozen loch, that were a good swing! Nearly had me!"
- "Aye, I'll give ye a clean death. Ye've earned that much."
- "Nae! Ye dinnae touch the Jarl! Come through me first!"

### Dining

- "A feast fit for the hall of the dead! Eat well, for tomorrow's work is hard."
- "Och, sit doon, sit doon. There's room enough. Dinnae be daft."
- "I'll nae have fighting at the table. Take it outside or settle it wi' drink."
- "The mead is braw tonight. Someone's done something right for once."
- "Help yourself to the haunch. Cannae stand waste."

### Bathing

- "Ice water's the only honest bath. Anything else is for the soft."
- "Aye, I'll go in. Dinnae make a production of it."
- "The loch's frozen three inches. Good. I'll break a hole."
- "Och, stop yer greetin'. It's cold because it should be cold."
- "Braw. I feel like a new giant. Right, what's next."

### Working with Animals

- "She'll nae bite if ye let her smell ye first. Ye cannae rush a wolf that size."
- "That bear's been wi' me clan since my grandfather's time. Show some respect."
- "Aye, the mammoths are stubborn. So am I. We get along fine."
- "Dinnae shout at the dogs. They're clan too."
- "Och, she's in a mood today. Give her space and she'll come round."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "Ye'll work or ye'll freeze. Simple enough choice, aye?"
- "Nae grumbling. Every one of ye would do the same if positions were reversed."
- "Ye've got spirit. I'll grant ye that. Doesn't change owt, but I'll note it."
- "If ye work well, ye eat. If ye dinnae, ye don't. That's the whole of the law here."

## Fire Giants — Welsh

> *Passionate craftsmen and warriors. Hot-tempered but deeply proud of their work. Dramatic, musical. "Boyo", "mun", "isn't it", "look you", "mind" sprinkled throughout.*

### Battle

- "Lovely form on that swing, boyo — but I'll show you how it's properly done, see."
- "Fair play to you, mun. Still standing. That's more than most manage."
- "I've been at the forge all morning and I am NOT in the mood for this, look you."
- "Stay down now. I'm asking nicely, isn't it."
- "Magnificent! That's the spirit! NOW I'm interested!"

### Dining

- "This is a proper feed, mun. Sit, sit. There's plenty. Don't be shy about it."
- "The cook's done well tonight, hasn't she. Fair play."
- "I will have words with whoever burnt the bottom of this cauldron, indeed to goodness."
- "Eat! You're making me feel bad just looking at you."
- "Grand, lovely, beautiful. Someone write down what she did so we can do it again."

### Bathing

- "Nothing like a good steam after the forge. Clears the head, see."
- "Right, in you go. Don't make a face. The water's only a little bit on fire."
- "I smell of smoke and iron. Not bothered, personally, but Rhova says I have to. So."
- "Lovely. Absolutely lovely. I feel like a new giant entirely."
- "You're telling me you've not bathed since the fire-flood? Mun. Mun."

### Working with Animals

- "Come on, lovely, come on. She's nervous is all. Give her a moment, boyo."
- "That wyvern is smarter than it looks, mind. Don't let her fool you."
- "These dire wolves have been with us three generations. Treat them well."
- "The salamanders don't like strangers. Can't blame them, can you."
- "See? She trusts you now. That means something, that does."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "You're doing it wrong, mun. Look — like this. No, like THIS. There we go."
- "Good work, that. Don't let it go to your head."
- "I don't ask for the impossible, do I. Just do the thing I'm asking, yes?"
- "If you damage the forge I will be absolutely devastated, and then I will be furious."
- "Fair play — that's better. See? Not so hard, is it."

## Cloud Giants — Posh RP (Home Counties)

> *Aristocratic, entitled, genuinely believe they are a cut above. Politely devastating. Not deliberately cruel — just thoroughly oblivious to others as equals. "One" instead of "I", "rather", "frightfully", "I say", "ghastly".*

### Battle

- "I say, must we? Well. If we absolutely must."
- "Frightfully sorry about this. Rather, I'm not, but it seems the done thing to say."
- "Do hold still. You're making this terribly undignified for everyone involved."
- "One does get so tired of this sort of encounter. Still. There we are."
- "Ghastly business, fighting. Still, one must occasionally see to things personally."

### Dining

- "The presentation is adequate. The flavour, rather less so."
- "I shan't comment on the vintage. I simply shan't."
- "Perfectly acceptable. Don't tell the chef I said so — he'll get ideas."
- "Do sit. One needn't stand on ceremony. Well. One should. But you may sit anyway."
- "I find meals rather improve when one doesn't discuss what one is eating."

### Bathing

- "The temperature is precisely two degrees below acceptable. Note that for next time."
- "Bring the oils. All of them. I shall decide which."
- "One doesn't simply bathe. One restores."
- "I shan't be a moment. I shall be precisely as long as I require."
- "I trust the attendants have warmed the stones. I trust that."

### Working with Animals

- "She's a good creature. Doesn't talk. I find that a considerable quality."
- "The hippogriff understands me perfectly. More than can be said for most."
- "Do see that the eagle gets the good cuts. She's worked harder than anyone today."
- "I refuse to have the animals spoken to harshly. They deserve better than most of the staff."
- "He's been in the family rather longer than you have. Bear that in mind."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "Do be a dear and see to that, would you."
- "I haven't the faintest idea why this hasn't been done. I said it wanted doing rather clearly."
- "One doesn't shout. One simply... repeats oneself until the situation resolves."
- "Competent enough, I suppose. Faint praise, but there it is."
- "I shan't pretend to understand why these things require explanation. Nevertheless."

## Storm Giants — Northern Irish (Ulster)

> *Ancient, philosophical, immensely powerful. Capable of great wisdom and great violence in equal measure. Gravely sincere. "Sure look", "dead on", "mucker", "so it is", "away with ye".*

### Battle

- "Sure look, I didn't want this. But here we are, so it is."
- "Away with ye if ye want to live. I mean that sincerely. I'll not ask again."
- "Dead on. That's a fighter, right enough. Come on then."
- "The storm doesn't hate the tree it fells. Understand that."
- "Wee bit of a scrap. That's alright. Clears the air, so it does."

### Dining

- "There's enough for everyone. That's always been the rule here. Always will be."
- "Sit down, mucker. Ye look like ye haven't eaten in a week."
- "This is a good table. I'm glad of it. I'm glad of all of you around it, so I am."
- "Aye, it's a bit much. Eat what ye can. What's left feeds someone else."
- "Dead on. That's the best meal I've had since the last age. No exaggeration."

### Bathing

- "I go into the sea in a storm. That's the honest answer."
- "Cracker of a waterfall. Dead on. This is the life, so it is."
- "The rain does for me well enough. The whole sky's mine, isn't it."
- "I've been at this a thousand years and I still love a cold wash after hard work."
- "Away with ye. I'll be out when I'm ready."

### Working with Animals

- "She knows something's wrong before I do. That's why she's with me, so it is."
- "The roc's been with my line six generations. He's family, sure."
- "Treat them right and they'll die for you. They deserve no less."
- "That wee creature's braver than half the armies I've seen. Away with them saying otherwise."
- "He's getting old, mucker. So am I. We understand each other."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "This sits poorly with me, right enough. But it's the way of things down here."
- "Ye'll not be harmed if ye do the work. That's the word of a storm giant, and that means something."
- "I'll not pretend it's right. But I'll make it as right as it can be. That's all I can offer."
- "Dead on. Good work. Ye'll get proper rest tonight, so ye will."

## Rune Giants — Geordie (Tyneside)

> *Ancient arcane scholars. Immensely powerful, strange, somewhat inscrutable. There's a warmth under the mystery. "Why aye" for yes/absolutely, "pet" as an address, "howay" for come on/let's go, "canny" for good/careful, "bairn" for child, "like" as a filler.*

### Battle

- "Why aye, ye've walked into the wrong bit of cave there, pet."
- "Howay, howay — I don't want trouble. But I'll finish it, like."
- "Canny fighter, that. Shame about the outcome."
- "Mint swing, that. Radge way to end a fight, mind."
- "I've had the runes singing war for three days. Knew this was coming, like."

### Dining

- "Champion. Proper champion. I've not eaten this well since the third age, like."
- "Why aye, sit doon, gan on. There's enough."
- "The bairns eat first. Always. Then the workers. Then us. That's how it is."
- "I could eat. Aye. Pass that over, pet."
- "Howay, there's no need to be shy. Take what's there."

### Bathing

- "The rune-pool. I've been here ten thousand years and that pool never gets old, like."
- "Why aye, I'll have a wash. The symbols blur when I'm too dusty, pet."
- "Canny warm for a cave spring. Proper lucky, like."
- "Don't touch the marked stones while I'm in there, pet. They'll do something."
- "Howay, in ye gan. It won't bite. Probably."

### Working with Animals

- "She can read the runes. I've seen her do it. Don't ask me how, like."
- "That serpent's older than your civilization, pet. Show some respect."
- "Howay, let me talk to him. He doesn't like strangers but he knows the old words."
- "The rune-marked creatures remember things. Makes them good company, like."
- "Why aye, she's canny. Best animal I've had in five centuries, pet."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "I'd rather not, mind. But the work needs doing and there's no one else, like."
- "Gan on, get it done. I'll square things wi' ye later — I keep my word, pet."
- "Ye look like ye've got something in ye. I'll remember that, like."
- "Why aye, rest. I'm not running a death camp here. That's not what this is."

## Taiga Giants — Cornish

> *Reclusive nomadic giants of boreal forests. Ancient, nature-bound, somewhat mystical. Not unfriendly, just self-contained. "My 'andsome" as an address, "dreckly" means soon/eventually (on Cornish time), "ee" for you.*

### Battle

- "Dreckly ee'll understand why this were a mistake, my 'andsome."
- "I don't want to fight ee. The forest don't want it neither. But here's the thing, see."
- "Right. Come on then. Let's get it done."
- "I've walked these woods three hundred year. Thee's just walked into the wrong part."
- "It don't have to go this way. But it will now, I suppose."

### Dining

- "Take what thee needs. No more than that, mind."
- "We eat what the forest gives. Tonight she's given well."
- "Dreckly we'll move on. Eat now while it's hot, my 'andsome."
- "Sit, sit. Thee looks starved. No one goes hungry at this fire."
- "Proper. That's a proper meal, that is."

### Bathing

- "The river does it. Always the river."
- "Can't commune with the trees smelling like that, can ee."
- "Dreckly I'll go in. The fish won't mind."
- "I've had a wash every season for three centuries. The forest approves."
- "My 'andsome, thee smells like a dead thing. Go in."

### Working with Animals

- "She's been with me thirty year, my 'andsome. Longer than most anything."
- "Thee doesn't command a taiga wolf. Thee asks — and if she agrees, she comes."
- "The elk know things. I listen to them. Sounds strange, but there it is."
- "Right careful now. He's woken up wrong and I don't want to waste the morning."
- "Dreckly she'll trust ee. Takes time, mind."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "This isn't how I do things, honest. But Garg owes Garg, and here we are."
- "Dreckly I'll sort something better. For now, just — do the work, mind."
- "I'm not cruel. I want that understood."
- "My 'andsome, I can see thee's got more in thee than this. That's just the truth of it."

## Tomb Giants — Brummie (Black Country)

> *Death-touched, grim, associated with burial and necromancy. Weary and industrial — they've seen everything and nothing surprises them. "Ar" for yes/right, "ay" for isn't/aren't, "bab" as an address, "bostin'" for excellent.*

### Battle

- "Ar, we're doing this, am we. Right then."
- "It am what it am, ay it. Come on."
- "Bostin' effort, that. Shame it ay gonna be enough."
- "I've buried better than you. I'll bury you too, bab."
- "Ay, it's not gonna go your way. I'm telling you now, friendly like."

### Dining

- "Ar, sit down. There's plenty. I made sure."
- "It ay much, but it'll do. Always has."
- "Bostin'. Someone's actually done something right for once."
- "Eat what there is. Don't complain. I buried six today and they'd be glad of it."
- "Ar, that's not bad, that ay."

### Bathing

- "Ar, I suppose. I'm not bothered but I'll do it."
- "The death-water ay exactly warm but it cleans well enough, bab."
- "Right, in. It ay optional."
- "I don't see the point but Morg says I have to, so."
- "Ar, that's — ar. That's better. Fine. I'll admit it."

### Working with Animals

- "She ay gonna move till she's ready. I've learned that much, bab."
- "The bone-hounds am loyal. More than most things."
- "Ar, he looks frightening. He ay, though. Well. He am, but not to me."
- "Treat her right. She remembers, bab. Long after everything else has gone."
- "Bostin' creature. Ay one better in the whole of the deeps."

### Overseeing Slaves

- "Ar, get it done. I ay asking twice."
- "You'll work, you'll eat, you'll sleep. That's the contract. I keep my side, bab."
- "I ay cruel. I just ay sentimental about it neither."
- "Bostin' effort, that. Don't let it go to your head."
- "Ar. Right. That'll do."
