# G3 Deep Level — The Natural Caverns

Level 3 is entirely natural — tunnels and caverns showing no marks of being hollowed out except for the entranceway from above and the exit beyond the River of Lava in the northwest corner. The areas are dark and still. Only a few are lit: the lava cavern and where the fire giants have gathered at area 5.

All passages are at least 15 feet high. Small caves are 20 feet floor-to-ceiling. Large caverns are anywhere from 25 to 75 feet high (areas 7 and 19–20 should be treated as among the highest). A few patches of phosphorescent growth can be added — enough to see movement across them but not enough to illuminate an area. If the party stands still, they might hear water dripping and perhaps a far distant echo of stone striking stone — but only once.

Do not be too precise about direction or distance when describing this level. The twists, turns, and irregularities make accurate mapping nearly impossible under stress conditions. General directions and descriptions will suffice. If the party has made it this far, they do not need handholding from the DM.

**Wandering Monsters** (1 in 12 per turn):

1. 3–12 wandering trolls
2. 2 fire giants and 2 hell hounds (HD 6; THACO 13), patrolling
3. 4 male drow watchers — 3 in 4 chance to pass the party unseen

Patrol Leader Calimar (House Eilserv; Level 7/6 Fighter/Magic-User; hp 42; MR 64%; AC 2; THACO 14). Spells: charm person ×2, magic missile, spider climb / knock, mirror image / lightning bolt, protection from normal missiles. Equipment: chain +1, shield +1, shortsword +1, dagger +1.

Drow Fighters (3): MR 54%; AC 3; HD 2; THACO 19. Equipment: chain +1, shortsword +1, dagger.

## **1. Cavern**
Three ropers — patient and colorful.

### **Description**
Three ropers dwell here and conceal themselves among the natural stalagmite formations. They wait until prey is toward the center of the area (75% likely) before attacking. Each has a number of gems worth 100 gp — one gem per Hit Die — in its internal digestive organ.

The cavern is fairly colorful: reddish, pale yellow, and shining blue-grey rock formations glisten in the light of torches or lanterns.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — total darkness.
- **Surfaces:** Natural cavern floor with many stalagmite formations that conceal the ropers.
- **Doors & Exits:** Main tunnel from Level 2 area 1 (the secret tunnel). Passage continues south.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Damp mineral smell, faintly organic.
- **Sounds:** Silence — until the ropers strike.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 3 Ropers (HD 10 hp 39, HD 11 hp 48, HD 12 hp 60; THACO 10/10/9).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Inside each roper's digestive organ: gems worth 100 gp each, one per Hit Die (10, 11, and 12 gems respectively).

## **2. Glowing Cave**
Luminous and lethal from above.

### **Description**
A small offshoot of the main cavern running north and south, filled with luminous plant growth. Contains 12 hungry fire beetles. Between 1 and 4 of their number will be on the ceiling and drop upon any creature entering their lair — always surprising unless at least one party member is specifically looking upward. These creatures served as a source of gifts for the frost giants in the past. No treasure.

Note: The secret tunnel from Level 2 area 1 exits near here.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Dim phosphorescent glow from the plant growth — enough to see movement but not detail.
- **Doors & Exits:** Off the main north-south cavern. The Level 2 secret tunnel exits into this area.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 12 Fire Beetles (hp 10, 10, 9, 8, 7, 7, 7, 6, 6, 5, 5, 4). 1–4 on the ceiling will drop with surprise.

## **3. Cavern**
Two fire lizards — easily mistaken for dragons.

### **Description**
As this long cavern hooks eastward and terminates, it serves as a lair for 2 large fire lizards — a mated pair of "false dragons" with a clutch of three eggs. Both are quite old. Their skins are reddish-grey with large mottlings of red-orange, so at first glance they are 80% likely to be mistaken for red dragons. Their nest has several dozen shiny rocks, but none are gems.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Objects:** Three eggs in the nest (the lizards defend these furiously). Shiny but valueless rocks.
- **Doors & Exits:** Hooks east from the main cavern — dead end.
- **Special Interactions:** 80% chance party mistakes them for red dragons on first sight — this may trigger premature retreat or over-preparation.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Fire Lizards (hp 66, 54).

## **5. Cavern**
The fire giants' reserve — and the emergency refuge.

### **Description**
Eight fire giants are stationed here as a guard and reserve by Snurre's order (the order came from the drow). One of their number watches each end of the cavern — positions marked G. Each giant is armed with sword, spear, and several boulders nearby. No personal treasure — only piles of hides for bedding, a hamper or two, some personal wear, and eight bags.

**In crisis situations**, this is where King Snurre, Queen Frupy, and other important fire giants and guests will retreat. In that event, whatever goods they salvage with them constitute the treasure.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None except what the party brings.
- **Objects:** Bedding hides, hampers, personal gear only.
- **Doors & Exits:** Two ends watched; multiple tunnel connections.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 8 Fire Giants (hp 63, 62, 59, 57, 56, 55, 53, 49).

## **6. Great Vaulted Cavern**
Not what it appears — at all.

### **Description**
In the middle of this place is a **permanent illusion** of a huge sleeping red dragon atop a mound of treasure. What is actually there is a **gorgon** that has been charmed and instructed to ignore hell hounds, fire giants, and trolls. It obeys the drow. If any creature speaks to it in the Common tongue or in the language of red dragons, it has been instructed to stand quietly — but to breathe on the creature as soon as it comes within range. No treasure in the cavern.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Objects:** The permanent illusion of a red dragon and treasure hoard — *detect magic* or *true seeing* reveals it; physical interaction dispels it locally.
- **Special Interactions:** The gorgon's petrification breath. Creatures within range must save or be petrified. The illusion is intended to make adventurers charge in confidently.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Gorgon (hp 43) — appears as a red dragon until revealed.

## **7. Treasure Trove Cave**
The real dragon — and he is awake.

### **Description**
Here sleeps a real red dragon. **Brazzemal** is a very large ancient male who can both speak and use spells. A large boulder partially blocks the entrance — if moved, Brazzemal will certainly awake and immediately use his ESP spell to learn who intrudes.

If cornered and in desperate straits, he will swear to anything to save his life — he is persuasive and deceitful, and his real desire is to stay and devour the party. Do not let his negotiating succeed: he fights the moment the odds improve.

The cave appears strewn with coins of all sorts (about 20,000 each of copper and silver, 9,000 each of electrum and gold, no platinum) and a few dozen gems. All worthwhile treasures are covered by the coins. Brazzemal also has 666 gems (1 gp each) pressed into his stomach scales to protect them. If struck by cold or electricity, 60–90% of these will be destroyed.

Searching the pile: one randomly determined item found per turn per person searching.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — but Brazzemal's scales faintly glow with retained heat.
- **Objects:** Coin piles (deep), buried treasures, the dragon himself.
- **Doors & Exits:** Entrance passage from area 6. No other exits.
- **Special Interactions:** Brazzemal will ESP the party before they see him if possible. He will roleplay cooperation until the moment is right.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Heat, scorched stone, the acrid tang of dragon breath residue.
- **Sounds:** Deep rhythmic breathing — DC 16 to hear from the entrance before moving the boulder.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Brazzemal, Red Dragon (HD 11; hp 88; THACO 10; breath weapon). Spells: detect magic, erase / ESP, mirror image / invisibility 10' radius, protection from normal missiles / confusion, polymorph other.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Loose Coin and Gems:** 19,830 cp, 21,010 sp, 8,700 ep, 8,475 gp, 222 gems (10 gp each).
- **Buried Under the Coins:**
  - 17 large containers: 8 chests, 1 coffer, 8 jars
  - 1 suit of human-sized *plate mail +4*
  - 11 jeweled man-sized weapons (assorted, 300–1,800 gp each)
  - 1 *longsword +2 of red dragon slaying* in a jeweled scabbard (6,000 gp)
  - 2 silver mirrors (300 gp each)
  - 1 *gem of controlling fire elementals* (as stone of earth elementals)
  - 31 pieces of jewelry (200–800 gp each)
  - 12 ivory statues with gem/metal inlays (200–2,400 gp each)
  - 1 idol of bloodstone (1,900 gp)
  - 11 gold service pieces (500–3,000 gp each)
  - 17 silver or electrum service items (100–1,000 gp each)
  - 4 small containers: 1 coffer, 1 ivory case, 1 silver egg, 1 malachite box, 1 crystal basket
- **Container Contents:**
  - 5 Chests: 5,000 sp each
  - 2 Chests: 7,000 gp each
  - 1 Chest: cheap blankets + 4 carved jade figurines (1,000–8,000 gp each)
  - 8 Jars: rare unguents and perfumes (200–2,000 gp each)
  - Coffer: 4 *potions of fire resistance*
  - Ivory case (400 gp): jeweled necklace (2,000 gp)
  - Silver egg (500 gp): 51 gems (100 gp each)
  - Malachite box (1,000 gp): 800 pp
  - Crystal basket (3,400 gp): 1 scroll of 7 magic-user spells (any)

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — the drow prefer darkness.
- **Objects:** Bedrolls and gear only.
- **Doors & Exits:** Entry passage with 2 sentries. Connects to the main cavern network.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Leaders (3):** AC −2; HD 6 (hp 35, MR 62%) and HD 4 (hp 24, 21, MR 58%). Equipment: chain +2, shield +2, shortsword +1, dagger +1, hand crossbow with 9 poisoned darts.

Piercers from above — and a glint in the bones.

### **Description**
Twelve piercers make this wide passage their home and patiently await prey. The floor is strewn with shattered skulls and bones. Amidst these gleaming white remains glints a gold necklace set with 5 gems (1,100 gp intact; total gem value 800 gp).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Bone-strewn floor — difficult to move through silently (DC 20 Stealth).
- **Special Interactions:** Piercers drop individually each round; check for each creature passing under them.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 12 Piercers (HD 4: hp 22, 19; HD 3: hp 16, 13, 12, 12; HD 2: hp 12, 11, 10, 10; HD 1: hp 5, 3).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Gold necklace set with 5 gems (1,100 gp intact; gems alone 800 gp).

## **10. Singing Chamber**
Music and slime.

### **Description**
This small chamber has excellent acoustics. Drops of water falling into the pool along the southwestern wall make a pleasant musical sound — audible from 60 feet in quiet conditions, 30 feet under normal conditions. The pool edge is lined by a huge grey ooze; another grey ooze lies on a ledge 11 feet high in the southeast.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Objects:** The pool (no treasure — the ooze has corroded everything that fell in).
- **Doors & Exits:** Off passage from area 9.
- **Special Interactions:** The grey ooze is nearly indistinguishable from wet stone. DC 22 Perception to identify it before touching. Corrodes metal and wood on contact.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Damp stone, faintly mineral.
- **Sounds:** The beautiful musical dripping — which is ironic given what's waiting.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Grey Oozes (hp 24, 17).

## **11–13. Stinking Caverns**
Troll territory — and they cooperate.

### **Description**
All three of these caverns are inhabited by troll servants of King Snurre. The stink from these creatures, their nests, and what they leave about is noticeable from the corridor leading southwest from area 9. The trolls support each other in case of attack — those in areas 11 and 12 will respond to disturbances in each other's caves by splitting into two groups and converging.

### **11. Cavern**

Thirteen trolls, each with the typical messy mound of sticks and bones and other noisome things. Each nest contains 5–50 of each coin type. If they hear a disturbance at area 12, they split into two groups and move there immediately.

**Hostile Threats:** 13 Trolls (hp 37, 35, 33, 33, 30, 30, 30, 26, 36, 24, 22, 20, 19).

### **12. Cavern**

Sixteen trolls as above. If they hear combat from area 11, they move there in 2 groups.

**Hostile Threats:** 16 Trolls (hp 36, 34, 34, 33, 32, 31, 30, 29, 29, 29, 28, 28, 25, 22, 17, 14).

### **13. Cavern**

Twenty-seven trolls nest here. The largest is the chieftain; two other large trolls are his lieutenants. If they hear suspicious noises or are assaulted, the trolls use the multiple passages to surprise and surround attackers, leaping upon them to tear them apart. The three leader types nest in the southeastern spur. Each leader nest contains 100–400 of each coin type. The chieftain has 5–30 gems (100 gp each) and a **jeweled mace worth 6,500 gp**.

**Hostile Threats:** 27 Trolls (hp 41 Chief, 39, 38, 36, 33, 33, 32, 32, 32, 32, 31, 31, 29, 28, 28, 27, 24, 24, 23, 20, 18, 17, 17, 17, 16, 15, 12).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Areas 11 and 12: 5–50 of each coin type per nest. Area 13 leader nests: 100–400 of each coin type. Chieftain: 5–30 gems (100 gp each) + jeweled mace (6,500 gp).

## **14. Narrow Cavern**
Green slime and agates.

### **Description**
This cavern is striated with layers of blue, green, and greenish-blue deposits. Green slime grows on the passage roof and in the pool at its end. The slime on the ceiling covers about 40 feet of space just south of the pool — roll for each party member under it each round (1 in 6 = dropped on them). 36 agates (10 gp each) lie in the pool. Any creature reaching in to retrieve them has a 50% chance of the pool slime attacking. The pool slime blends with the greenish rock.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Striated walls of blue-green mineral deposits.
- **Special Interactions:** Ceiling slime (40-foot zone): check each party member each round. Pool slime: 50% attack chance when reaching in.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** 36 agates (10 gp each) — visible in the pool, but getting them is the problem.

## **15. Gray Cavern**
The drow strongpoint.

### **Description**
This dark-colored cavern is the drow strongpoint on this level. Two guards watch each of the six passage ways meeting here — 12 guards total on the perimeter. Six additional guards are in the main cavern. All 18 male drow guards are commanded by three higher-level types in the main eastern section: a **female guard commander**, **female mercenary Greyanna**, and **male noble Bruherd** of House Tormtor.

Each drow carries 1–10 pp per level of experience. Each above 4th level carries one gem per level (100 gp each), or double if multi-classed.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — the drow operate in total darkness.
- **Doors & Exits:** Six passage junctions controlled by paired guards. The Level 2 stairway from area 8 exits near here.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 18 Male Drow Guards (Level 3; hp 18 each; MR 54%; AC 0; THACO 18). Equipment: chain +1, shield +1, shortsword +1, dagger +1, hand crossbow with 10 poisoned bolts.
- **Female Guard Commander** (Level 5; hp 30; MR 60%; AC −4; THACO 16). Equipment: chain +2, shield +2, shortsword +3, dagger +1, hand crossbow with 10 poisoned bolts.
- **Female Mercenary Greyanna** (Level 9; hp 54; MR 68%; AC −5; THACO 12; #AT 3/2). Equipment: chain +3, shield +3, shortsword +3, mace +3, atlatl with 13 poisoned javelins.
- **Male Noble Bruherd** (House Tormtor; Level 4/6 Fighter/Magic-User; hp 30; MR 62%; AC −3; THACO 18). Equipment: chain +2, ring of protection +3, shortsword +2, dagger +3, wand of viscid globs (79 charges). Spells: charm person ×2, shield, ventriloquism / mirror image, ray of enfeeblement / lightning bolt, slow.

### **Loot & Rewards**

## **16. Small Cavern**
Nedylene and her guard — rival drow politics made flesh.

### **Description**
A metal spear trap is located north of the entry passage. Furthermore, a fine trip wire triggers a torrent of small iron spheres (¼ inch diameter) which pour into the last 20 feet of the passageway and first 10 feet of the cavern. These balls make a loud noise; any creature in the area has a 5 in 6 chance of falling for the first 3 rounds, and 2 in 6 for the next 3 rounds (unless they stand completely still). Fallen creatures cannot attack and lose shield and Dex benefits. All targets are easier to hit.

Nine female drow are in the cavern. Eight are 4th-level fighters — special guards from the female fighter's society protecting **Nedylene of House Despana**, a noble generally opposed to everything Eclavdra attempts. She is here to monitor her rival. She will be suspicious of any creature that enters — but she will not object to seeing Eclavdra's plans go wrong. If things go badly on the upper levels and Eclavdra appears slain, Nedylene will rally the remaining drow in areas 8, 15, 16, and 20 for an organized tactical retreat.

Nedylene wields a **demon staff** — a black leather-like staff carved with disgusting scenes and vile runes (Dmg 4–24; *fear* with no save; damage heals only 1 hp/day by rest only; once per day: summon one Type I demon; once per day: shapechange into Type III demon for 5 rounds).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Objects:** The spear trap (north passage). Trip wire for the iron ball flood.
- **Special Interactions:** The iron ball flood is loud — it will alert area 15 and area 20 immediately.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 8 Female Drow Guards (Level 4; hp 28, 26, 25, 24, 24, 24, 22, 21; MR 58%; AC −3; THACO 18). Equipment: chain +2, shield +2, shortsword +2, dagger +2, hand crossbow with 10 poisoned bolts, atlatl with 3 poisoned javelins.
- **Nedylene of House Despana** (Level 8/7 Cleric/Fighter; hp 48; MR 66%; AC −6; THACO 14; #AT 3/2). Equipment: chain +5, buckler +3, mace +3, demon staff. Spells: curse, cure light wounds ×3, protection from good / detect charm, find traps, hold person ×2, silence 15' radius / blindness, dispel magic, prayer, remove curse / cure serious wounds, neutralize poison.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Magical Items:** Nedylene's demon staff. Her chain +5, buckler +3, mace +3. Guards' equipment.

## **17. Jeweled Cavern**
Illithids — observers, not combatants (yet).

### **Description**
When light strikes the mineral formations here, the deposits make the place glitter and sparkle as if sown with jewels — breathtakingly beautiful. This is the current lair of three illithids (mind flayers) who have come to observe what is going on with their "friendly enemies," the drow. They plan only to observe; the dark elves ignore them.

Each illithid carries 2–12 gems (100 gp each) as bribe money. They also have an *amulet of the planes* and a *tome of clear thought* in a small black metal box that can only be opened by creatures of 18 or greater Intelligence.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The mineral formations create their own sparkle in any light source — stunning visual effect.
- **Objects:** The locked metal box (Intelligence 18+ to open).
- **Special Interactions:** The illithids will not attack unprovoked — they are here to watch. However, if the party attacks or threatens them, they respond with mind blasts and psychic assault. They will attempt to flee into the cavern passages if losing.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Slightly organic, faintly unpleasant beneath the mineral smell.
- **Sounds:** Near-silence; the illithids communicate telepathically.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Neutral Creatures (hostile if threatened):** 3 Illithids (hp 44, 41, 38).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** 2–12 gems per illithid (100 gp each). The metal box: *amulet of the planes* + *tome of clear thought*.

## **19–20. Red Cavern Inferno**
The lava river — and Eclavdra's command post.

### **Description**
This huge space is lit in reddish light by a bubbling, steaming river of molten lava flowing through it. The cavern stinks of sulphur and heated rock; the temperature is very hot, though a strong draft cools it somewhat. The ceiling is no less than 60 feet high.

### **19. Venting Gases**

Gases of flame and smoke illuminate this area in wavering bands of light and shadow. Two salamanders are here, enjoying a change of climate. The flames and smoke make them impossible to see beyond 30 feet. They are totally neutral regarding whom they attack — though they know and respect the drow. No treasure.

A **rope bridge** connects this area to area 20, suspended about 15 feet above the lava river.

**Hostile Threats:** 2 Salamanders (hp 45, 38).

### **20. Eclavdra's Council Chamber**

The secluded section serving as the meeting place and council chamber for Eclavdra and her minions. Currently here: six male fighters, three female fighters, and **Viconia** — an 8th-level female commander particularly favored by Eclavdra, who carries three *bolts of power*.

Eclavdra will be here with as many of her followers as survive if things go badly on Level 2. Otherwise Nedylene will be rallying the troops here.

A constant watch is kept on the east entry. If intruders come, the drow will know. If threatened by powerful characters, they flee north-northwest — protected at location **A** by a huge charmed lurker above that falls on any creature passing under it after Eclavdra gives the command.

**Location A — Lurker Above** (hp 63; Dmg 2–8; smothers in 2–4 rounds).

In addition to bedding and personal gear, a **chest** holding 2,080 pp and 200 gems (100 gp each) is hidden under an illusion making it look like a rock formation in the far northeastern corner.

Viconia has a small **coffer** with 2 *potions of speed* and 2 *potions of extra-healing* — to be used by the drow if necessary.

**Special Note:** The party will find an **adamantite tube** (200 gp) containing a *scroll of wish* and a **players' map of the Depths of the Drow**. This tube was left behind by Viconia so that — if Eclavdra's scheme is foiled and Eclavdra is slain — the attackers will learn of the drow homeland and, it is hoped, attack Eilserv's rival factions.

Equipment for a **pair of hoists** (derrick-like machines with straps for cargo, even large animals) can be found in nooks on either side of the lava river. Setting them up takes several hours, but once done they allow the party to move supplies and pack animals across the lava — ready for the next part of the adventure.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Reddish glow from the lava — dim but present throughout.
- **Objects:** Rope bridge (area 19 to 20). The hoist equipment in nooks beside the river. The chest hidden under illusion in the NE corner.
- **Doors & Exits:** East entry (watched). Rope bridge between 19 and 20. The north-northwest passage — guarded by the lurker above at location A — leads deeper into the Underdark toward the D series.
- **Special Interactions:** The lurker above at location A is triggered only by Eclavdra's command. The illusion hiding the chest requires *detect magic* or *true seeing* to penetrate, or physical interaction.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Sulphur, heated rock, the mineral tang of lava.
- **Sounds:** The constant low roar of the lava river. Hissing steam. The salamanders' movements in area 19.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 6 Male Drow Troops (hp 14, 12, 11, 11, 11, 10; MR 52%; AC 1; THACO 20); 3 Female Drow Troops (Level 4; hp 30, 27, 23; MR 58%; AC 1; THACO 18). All equipment: chain +1, buckler +1, shortsword +1, dagger +1, hand crossbow with 10 poisoned bolts.
- **Viconia of House Eilserv** (Level 8 Fighter; hp 60; MR 66%; AC −5; THACO 14; #AT 3/2). Equipment: chain +5, shield +3, shortsword +4, 3 bolts of power, hand crossbow with 10 poisoned bolts.
- **Lurker Above at location A** (hp 63) — triggered by Eclavdra's command only.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Chest (under illusion, NE corner): 2,080 pp + 200 gems (100 gp each). Viconia's coffer: 2 potions of speed + 2 potions of extra-healing.
- **Magical Items:** The adamantite tube: *scroll of wish* + players' map of the Drow Depths. Viconia's 3 bolts of power. Her shortsword +4. Her chain +5 and shield +3.

### **Additional Notes**

The north-northwest passage beyond location A leads to the Underdark. The hoist equipment allows the party to cross the lava river with their gear and mounts when ready to continue the adventure. This is the transition point between the G series and the D series.
