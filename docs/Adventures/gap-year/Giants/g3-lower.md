# G3 Lower Level — The Deep Dungeons

Below the main Hall of King Snurre is a network of worked passages and natural caverns that serve as dungeon, storage, and — most critically — the connection to the Underdark and the drow below. The temperature here drops compared to the levels above: still warm by most standards, but the volcanic heat fades quickly as the passages descend into bedrock. The walls are black basalt, undecorated, and the ceilings run 20–25 feet.

This level has a dual purpose: it is both a prison and an interface point. The drow use it as a neutral ground when visiting the fire giants, maintaining a small suite of rooms at the far end. The passage at the level's deepest point leads, over many days of underground travel, to the Underdark and eventually to Xilthraen and the D series.

**The lower level is quieter than the upper.** Wandering encounters (1 in 12 per turn):
1. 2 fire giants escorting a prisoner to Room 18
2. 1d4 drow warriors moving silently between Room 19 and the stairs
3. A troll that has wandered up from the deeper tunnels (it will be confused and hostile)
4. An ogre overseer checking the prison cells

## **13. Lower Entry and Guard Post**
Four fire giants hold the base of the stairs.

### **Description**
The bottom of the staircase from Room 12 opens into a wide, low guardroom. Four fire giants stand watch here — two at the stair foot, two at a table playing dice. Unlike the upper level guards, these giants are nervous: they know what is below them, and even fire giants are uncomfortable with their drow neighbors.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two braziers; adequate.
- **Objects:** An iron gong (DC 18 Athletics or a weapon to strike it — alerts all rooms on this level within 1 round).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hot stone and the faint incense smell drifting up from deeper rooms.
- **Sounds:** Dice; quiet muttering in Giant. DC 20 Perception to hear footsteps from the lower passages.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Fire Giants

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Personal wealth: 400 gp combined.

## **14. Troll Cave**
Three trolls — organic garbage disposal.

### **Description**
A foul-smelling cave off the main corridor, its entrance blocked by a crude iron grate the fire giants lift once or twice a day to throw in refuse (and the occasional uncooperative prisoner). Three enormous trolls inhabit the cave, well-fed and sluggish. They react to the smell of blood or fresh meat instantly.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Natural rock covered in accumulated filth.
- **Special Interactions:** The iron grate can be lifted from the outside with DC 22 Athletics, or dropped from above with DC 14. If the grate is opened, the trolls rush through immediately.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Overwhelming rot. The worst thing in this dungeon.
- **Sounds:** Wet sounds of feeding if the trolls were recently given something.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 3 Trolls

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In the refuse at the back of the cave, buried under organic matter: 3,000 gp worth of mixed coin and crude jewelry (thrown in with previous victims).

## **15. Ogre Quarters**
The dungeon overseers.

### **Description**
Six ogres who serve as the dungeon overseers live here — they run the prison cells, feed the trolls, and (most critically) manage the fire giants' side of the relationship with the drow by serving as go-betweens. They know more about the lower level than any non-drow in the complex. Their chambers are crude but functional: sleeping pallets, a table with food remains, and a locked weapon rack.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two wall torches; dim.
- **Objects:** A key ring with the keys to all prison cells (Rooms 17 and 18) is worn by the lead ogre (the largest one, who sleeps in the corner bunk).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Ogre quarters.
- **Sounds:** Snoring or argument, depending on time of day.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 6 Ogres

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** The lead ogre's locked chest (DC 18): 900 gp, a ring taken from a prisoner (worth 200 gp), and a crude sketch in charcoal that shows the rough layout of the lower level — the ogre drew it as a memory aid.

## **16. Deep Storage**
Where the fire giants put things they don't want the drow to see — and vice versa.

### **Description**
A large storage room split by a central dividing wall with an iron door in it (never locked). The fire giant side holds excess war materials: barrels of oil, stacked iron ingots for the forge, preserved food. The drow side holds crates of unidentified goods — trade goods brought up from below, sealed with wax and the spider insignia. These crates have never been opened by the giants.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — no one spends time here.
- **Objects:** The drow crates are sealed. Opening one (DC 14 Athletics or a blade) reveals: alchemical materials for the forge (specific components for making fire giant-grade iron), rolls of spider silk rope (worth 200 gp per roll; 6 rolls), and — in the smallest crate, wrapped in spider silk — a second *Letter of the Matron*, this one addressed to King Snurre personally and signed by a drow matron. It confirms in unambiguous terms: the giant raids are ordered, the drow will "collect what is owed" once the surface is softened, and Snurre is expected to have his forces ready to move within one month.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** 6 rolls of spider silk rope (200 gp each).
- **Hidden Loot:** In the fire giant's half, buried under oil barrels: 4,000 gp in emergency coin — Snurre's "escape fund" according to the crude label in Giant.
- **Magical Items:** Among the drow goods: a *potion of fire resistance* (clearly meant for the drow visitors navigating the hot upper level) and a *scroll of wall of stone* (drow travel seal — they use these to close off tunnels behind them).

### **Additional Notes**
The second Letter of the Matron is perhaps the most damning document in the entire giant arc. It explicitly names the drow house behind the conspiracy (using the web insignia of one of the Xilthraen noble houses — cross-reference with the drow city documentation) and gives a timetable. If found, this letter makes the urgency of reaching D1 completely clear.

## **17. Prison Cells — General**
The Hall's standard holding area.

### **Description**
A corridor with six iron-barred cells on each side. Most are occupied. Prisoners here are those awaiting judgment by Snurre — merchants, minor nobles, captured soldiers, and travelers who stumbled too close. Their conditions are bad but survivable.

Current notable prisoners:
- **Cell 3:** A dwarven envoy (Thordak Ironmantle) sent to negotiate with the fire giants on behalf of a surface coalition — captured on arrival three weeks ago. He knows that the surface kingdoms have begun to suspect organized giant aggression.
- **Cell 7:** A young elf scholar who was researching drow legends and got too close to the right answer. She has independently pieced together that "something below" is driving the giants.
- **Cell 9:** A mercenary captain (human, 5th-level fighter) with 3 of her surviving soldiers. They were hired to escort a trade caravan and were captured when the caravan was raided.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** One torch at each end of the corridor, guttering.
- **Objects:** Each cell lock is DC 20 Thievery; the key ring is in Room 15.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 14 prisoners of various races; the notable ones above can fight if armed (the mercenary captain in particular). The dwarf envoy and the elf scholar have information that supplements documents found elsewhere in the complex.

## **18. Prison Cells — Deep**
The important prisoners — and the worst conditions.

### **Description**
A second, more secure cell block at the far end of the dungeon corridor. Fewer cells, heavier iron, and two fire giants standing permanent guard. This is where Snurre keeps prisoners of actual significance — people the drow have requested be held, or captives whose value is high enough to warrant special treatment.

Current prisoners:
- **Cell 1:** **Arendyl Swiftbranch**, a surface elf ranger and agent of a major surface intelligence organization. He was captured trying to get into the Hall through the lower tunnels from the Underdark side (he was working his way up from D1). He knows the first section of the tunnel route to the Underdark and has basic information about what lies below (troglodytes, kuo-toa, and then the drow — he never got that far). He is in poor condition but alert.
- **Cell 2:** Empty — but recently occupied. Claw marks on the wall are not giant-made. Something was held here briefly and then taken below by the drow.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Dim — a single torch at the corridor entrance.
- **Objects:** These cell locks are DC 26 Thievery. The fire giant guards each carry a key.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** The incense smell from the drow rooms is stronger here.
- **Sounds:** DC 18 Perception: faint sounds of movement from Room 19, very close.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** Arendyl Swiftbranch (Ranger 7, currently at 8 HP; has been fed but not treated)
- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Fire Giants (permanent guards)

### **Additional Notes**
Arendyl can provide the party with a *partial map* of the tunnel route from the Hall's lower exit to the Underdark — covering the first three days of travel. His map ends just before the troglodyte territory (D1). He will guide them if freed and healed to at least 50% HP.

## **19. Drow Embassy Suite**
The masterminds revealed — in person.

### **Description**
The drow maintain a suite of three chambers at the lowest accessible point of the Hall. Unlike every other room on this level, the drow suite is cool, dark, and furnished with the dark elegance typical of drow work: spider-silk hangings, glowstone lanterns in violet, obsidian writing surfaces. The contrast with the rest of the Hall is jarring.

The current drow delegation:
- **Vraxis Thal'nyr** — senior female drow priestess, representative of House Veythrae (the dominant arcane house in Xilthraen). Level 13 Cleric of Lolth. She wears black chain mail +3, carries a +2 whip and a *wand of magic missiles* (12 charges), and has a spider brooch (2,000 gp).
- **3 Drow Elite Warriors** — house guards in black chain mail, hand crossbows with poisoned bolts, rapiers.

Vraxis has been here three days finalizing the invasion timetable. She has the final piece of the conspiracy: her orders, her route back below, and a sending stone that connects to the Matron of House Veythrae.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Violet glowstone — dim for humans, comfortable for drow.
- **Objects:** Three obsidian writing surfaces covered in documents. An iron coffer (DC 26 lock). A *sending stone* (one of a matched pair; the other is in Xilthraen with Matron Thalara Veythrae).
- **Doors & Exits:** One door back to the main corridor. A concealed iron panel in the south wall (DC 20 Perception) conceals the **passage to the Underdark** — a natural tunnel reinforced with drow stonework, leading downward toward D1.
- **Special Interactions:** If Vraxis is captured alive (not killed), she will not cooperate but she will radiate enough arrogant contempt that PCs who speak Elvish or Undercommon can gather intelligence from her responses to questions. She considers the giants cattle and the surface world already conquered. She is not afraid — she considers herself beyond the reach of consequences.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Burnt silk incense; utterly alien.
- **Sounds:** The scratch of a stylus on obsidian when writing.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 3 Drow Elite Warriors
- **Unique NPCs:** Vraxis Thal'nyr, Drow Priestess of House Veythrae (will activate *darkness* and attempt to flee via the south passage if losing; she is not suicidal)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** The obsidian writing surfaces (too large to carry) contain the complete invasion plan, names of all six drow noble houses, and a map of Xilthraen's district structure.
- **Hidden Loot:** The iron coffer: 8 gems (2,000 gp each), 2,000 pp, and a *Letter of Transit* — a drow document that identifies the bearer as a drow house agent and instructs any drow encountered in the tunnels to grant passage. Potentially useful for the D series.
- **Magical Items:** The *sending stone* (matched pair; the other is with Matron Thalara Veythrae — activating it will connect directly to the Matron, which is a risk and an opportunity). Vraxis's *wand of magic missiles* (12 charges). A *potion of spider climb* on her person.

## **20. The Path Below**
The tunnel to the Underdark — the beginning of the D series.

### **Description**
The concealed iron panel behind the drow suite opens onto a passage descending steeply into natural rock. The drow stonework ends after 100 feet; below that point it is raw Underdark tunneling. The passage has been used regularly — the floor is worn smooth, and drow glowstones mounted at intervals provide faint violet light for the first quarter-mile. Beyond that: darkness, cold, and the slow descent toward D1.

Carvings in the Drow dialect of Elvish mark the tunnel walls at regular intervals — directional markers, warnings, and what appear to be ownership claims. A single phrase appears three times:

*"By order of the Web, this passage is the property of the Matrons of Xilthraen. Trespassers are claimed."*

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Drow glowstones for the first quarter-mile; total darkness beyond.
- **Surfaces:** Worn smooth; not difficult terrain. The descent is gradual.
- **Special Interactions:** Three days of travel from this point reaches the beginning of D1 (troglodyte territory). Arendyl's map covers the first day; beyond that point, the party is navigating unknown Underdark.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold stone, deep earth, and the faint smell of something biological — faint, and wrong.
- **Sounds:** Distant dripping. At DC 28 Perception, the faintest possible echo of drums — far below, steady, and patient.

### **Additional Notes**
This is the transition point between the G series and the D series. The drums are the first indication of organized life in the Underdark below — but their source will not be identified until D1. The drow glowstones marking the passage can be removed (they remain active for 1 week removed from the wall) and serve as a limited light source for the descent.
