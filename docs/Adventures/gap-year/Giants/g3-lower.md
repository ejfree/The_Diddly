# G3 Lower Level — The Dungeon

The dungeon level below the Hall is primarily worked passages connecting to natural chambers. Temperature remains punishing but slightly less extreme than the upper level. Ceilings run 20–30 feet. Walls are black basalt. Most corridors are unlit; light sources listed per room.

This level is dual-purpose: it serves as dungeon and prison for the King's important captives, and as a religious and diplomatic space for the drow visitors who come and go through the passages connecting to Level 3.

**Wandering Monsters** (1 in 10 per turn):

1. 2 fire giants and 2 fire giantesses on a "romantic stroll"
2. 2 trolls escorting 3 hill, 2 stone, or 2 frost giants
3. 4 trolls with 4–16 armed gnolls
4. Drow Istorvir (Level 7/7 Fighter/Magic-User; hp 40; MR 64%; AC 3) with 2–8 wererats

## **1. Hall of the Dead Kings**
A tomb — already looted.

### **Description**
This dark hall contains 20 huge sarcophagi (lettered A–T) standing upright along the walls, and 4 even larger ones (U–X) lying on the floor. Floor sarcophagi are stone; U and V bear carved likenesses of fire giant kings, W and X are blank. The wall sarcophagi are stone (A, B, C, F, G, H, J, O, P, Q), iron (D, E), bronze (I, K, L, M, N, R), and brass (S, T). All bear likenesses of fire giant kings and queens (13 kings, 11 queens).

Examination reveals only the remains of each ruler — moldering garments, a few corroded weapons, similar things. Wererat grave robbers have taken anything of value long ago.

A **secret tunnel** in the southeast corner is about 2 feet in diameter. It twists and turns so that anyone using it loses all sense of direction. It exits on Level 3 at area 2.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — total darkness.
- **Objects:** 24 sarcophagi. The secret tunnel entrance (DC 24 to find).
- **Doors & Exits:** Main corridor entrance. Secret tunnel SE → Level 3, area 2.
- **Special Interactions:** The secret tunnel is only 2 feet across — requires crawling; anyone in armor must remove it first.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Old stone, dust, faint decay.
- **Sounds:** Complete silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Nothing — wererats have been thorough.

## **2. Ettin Guards' Chamber**
Off-duty guards — two alert, two not.

### **Description**
The four ettins here are not on duty but generally serve as guards at Level 1, area 2. Two are asleep; the other two are quite alert. Each is armed with a spear and *morning star +1*, though the sleeping ones have their weapons set aside. The chamber has a rude table, a bench, two stools, and four cots. Torches light the place. Nine pegs hold clothing and six bags. In the far corner is a hamper with six sacks of copper — each holding 5,100–5,800 cp. This is the ettins' accumulated pay.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches — dim.
- **Objects:** Six coin sacks in the hamper.
- **Doors & Exits:** Off the main corridor near area 1.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Ettins (hp 53, 49, 48, 46; THACO 10/spear, 9/morning star; Dmg 2–16 spear, 4–19 morning star).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Six sacks of cp (5,100–5,800 each) in the hamper.
- **Magical Items:** 4 morning stars +1 (one per ettin).

## **3. Visitors' Chamber**
Four stone giant engineers.

### **Description**
Four stone giants who have been working for the King as engineers are lodged here. Each has a club; there are also 11 boulders in the place. The room is torchlit and contains four cots, four stools, four lockers, a table, a big barrel of beer, and pegs holding clothing and four sacks. A haunch of meat is on the table with mugs and platters of tin. Each giant carries 1,000–4,000 gp and 3–12 gems (100 gp each).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches.
- **Objects:** 11 boulders. Personal lockers.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Stone Giants (hp 47, 45, 44, 40) — will fight if attacked but are here under contract, not by loyalty.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Personal wealth: 1,000–4,000 gp each + 3–12 gems (100 gp each) per giant.

## **4. Storage Room**
Converted to quarters — for guests from G1.

### **Description**
This area has been cleaned out to serve as quarters for either **five hill giants** (armed with clubs) or **Chief Nosnra** of the Hill Giants, his wife Solka, and their cave bears Throatripper and Snookums — if they survived the party's visit to G1.

*If hill giants only:* Five heaps of skins for bedding, a table, two benches, and two chests. Each has only 200–1,200 gp.

*If Nosnra is present:* Two cots, two trunks, a chair, two stools, a table, and a coffer. The Chief will have brought whatever he could salvage and carry from the Steading.

In both cases the room is illuminated by four torches in wall cressets.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Four wall torches.
- **Doors & Exits:** Off the main corridor.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats (if hill giants):** 5 Hill Giants (hp 46, 40, 38, 37, 36).
- **Hostile Threats (if Nosnra):** Chief Nosnra, Solka (use G1 stats), Cave Bears Throatripper and Snookums — all hostile on sight.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Hill giants: 200–1,200 gp each. Nosnra: whatever he salvaged from G1.

## **5. Community Quarters**
A fire giant family unit — and they all fight.

### **Description**
One leader (sergeant), three males, six females, and eight young are housed here. Two large beds, five cots, seven bunks, two large cabinets, two tables, four chairs, four stools, three buckets, four chests, six hampers, a crate, and three small boxes. Torches on the walls; pegs hold clothing and seven bags. Miscellaneous gear and eating utensils scattered about. 20 throwing rocks in the chamber. Each male has 200–400 gp in his bag; each female has 100–300 gp hidden in her personal area. All are armed — even the young.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches.
- **Objects:** 20 throwing rocks.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Fire Giants (hp 66 sergeant, 60, 58, 55); 6 Fire Giantesses (hp 53, 50, 47, 44, 41, 40); 8 Young Fire Giants (hp 37, 36, 33, 28, 25, 23, 20, 17 — fight as ogres).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Males: 200–400 gp each (in bags). Females: 100–300 gp each (hidden in personal areas).

## **6. Smithy**
The weapon smith — and a unique magical weapon.

### **Description**
The sound of metal hammering occasionally drifts from here. The hall outside is tinged bloody red from the lava bed and gas jets inside. A knotty-limbed, burly fire giant works here as weapon and iron smith. His exceptional strength gives +2 to hit and +4 damage. The molten lava heats his work. Two trolls serve as his assistants. About him are three giant swords, various armor pieces, and several axe and spear heads.

He is doing repair work on a special giant-sized *mace of black metal* — a *+4 magical weapon* (Dmg 5–16/5–12). **If the encounter is going against him, there is a 2 in 6 chance per melee round that he will toss the mace into the lava bed and destroy it.**

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Blazing from the lava bed and forge fires.
- **Objects:** The mace +4 (under repair). Giant swords and armor pieces (giant-sized, of no value to PCs).
- **Doors & Exits:** Off the main corridor.
- **Special Interactions:** The mace destruction mechanic — check each combat round once it's going badly for the smith. If destroyed, it is gone permanently.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hot metal, coal, the mineral smell of melting rock.
- **Sounds:** Hammer on metal; audible throughout the adjacent corridor.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Fire Giant Smith (hp 70; THACO 7; Dmg 5d6+4); 2 Trolls (hp 40, 38).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Magical Items:** Giant-sized *mace +4* (Dmg 5–16/5–12) — if it survives the encounter.

## **7. Torture Chamber**
The King's interrogation room — and the deep cells.

### **Description**
Steps lead down to this cluttered room with a 50-foot ceiling (allowing very tall victims to be hung in chains above the floor). Vision range is 15 feet in the murky depths.

The **King's Torturer** and **Royal Headsman** are playing knucklebones — their stakes are 7 pieces of jewelry (1,000–4,000 gp each) and two piles of gems (68 worth 10 gp each, 39 worth 50 gp each). The Headsman's gigantic axe gives him +2 to hit and damage (only when wielded by a creature as strong as he). On a natural 20, roll 1d6: 1–2 = severs an arm, 3 = severs a leg, 4–6 = decapitation. Dmg 10d4+2.

**If both are in melee:** The Torturer grabs his opponent and attempts to throw him into the **iron maiden** (position A) and slam it shut — 10–100 damage and trapped. Requires two successful hits (second at +4). The Headsman grabs and tosses opponents down the **well** (position B) — 9d6 damage (discard any 6s rolled, since water 90 feet below breaks the fall — actual range 0–45 damage). Both will systematically clear the room this way.

The chamber contains: large rack, smaller rack, five other appropriate implements, the iron maiden, and the well. Chains, bats, irons, whips, ropes, wires festoon the room. A table, two chairs, a stool, and a large barrel of ale.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches on the stairway and in the chamber — dim.
- **Objects:** Iron maiden (position A). Well (position B, 90 feet deep — water at bottom). Standard torture equipment.
- **Doors & Exits:** Stairs from main level. Passage to cell block.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Blood, ale, and hot iron.
- **Sounds:** Dice clatter; rough laughter. DC 14 to hear from the corridor.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Fire Giant Headsman (hp 63; THACO 9); Fire Giant Torturer (hp 59; THACO 7; Dmg 10d4+2 with axe).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Knucklebones stakes: 7 jewelry pieces (1,000–4,000 gp each), 68 gems (10 gp each), 39 gems (50 gp each).

### **Cells (1c–14c) and Guardroom G**

All cells are marked C on the map. Each has rings set in the walls, chains, a bucket, and a straw heap. Guardroom G controls access to the cell block.

**1c.** **Lady Aelindra of House Silaen** — a genuine Aerenal diplomat, intended for sacrifice at the Temple of the Eye (area 9–11). Normal elf, hp 8. She is of high noble birth and a legitimate envoy from Aerenal; her face and identity were used without her knowledge by a Diddly intelligence operative for over a year. When House Thalmyr exposed the deception, Vel'dryn's people took the real Aelindra as proof of concept — to verify the impersonation had been perfect, and to punish someone for it. She knows nothing of the spy operation and is baffled why the drow speak of her as if they know her intimately.

If rescued and allowed to return to Aerenal, she will send her rescuers (1–2 months later): 10,000 gp, 20 *arrows +1*, an *arrow of giant slaying*, and a set of *cloaks and boots of elvenkind*. She will also, if the party tells her the truth about the impersonation, have extremely complicated feelings about it — and will ask questions about whoever wore her face.

**2c.** Eight male elves also meant for temple sacrifice. Normal elves only (hp 7 each). They will follow Aelindra.

**3c.** Androcor, a merchant held for ransom. Normal human, hp 6. He cannot pay his own ransom.

**4c.** Empty cell. A one-way invisible door at the back opens only with a key held by the drow at area 12.

**5c.** Two noble centaurs — Sedgar and Chaunsleaf — imprisoned and bound for torture and execution. They will offer minimal aid to any who free them. (hp 30, 27.)

**6c.** Empty cell.

**7c.** Three gnolls punished for insubordination. One is dead.

**8c.** Empty cell.

**9c.** A troll being used for torture practice. Mindless and enraged — attacks instantly if given any opportunity, otherwise sits motionless. (hp 36; THACO 12; Dmg 6–9/6–9/3–13.)

**10c.** Seven gnolls caught stealing gold. They will not help any creature; if freed they run away immediately. (hp 14, 13, 13, 12, 11, 11, 9.)

**11c.** Two inanimate human skeletons.

**12c.** Gareth, a female human thief chained to the wall (Level 11; hp 61; S 15 I 15 W 8 D 18 Co 16 Ch 17). She admits freely to being a thief caught trying to find the King's treasure room. She will help the party faithfully in exchange for escape — but will steal as much in gems and magic as possible and slip away at the first opportunity. She is casing every character from the moment they meet.

**13c.** Boldo, a fire giant (hp 82) hanging in chains as punishment for failing to show proper deference to Snurre. He was the King's chief lieutenant. He will lie to the party, claiming he tried to prevent Snurre's aggression and can get other fire giants to help overthrow the King. He will betray the party at his earliest opportunity. He is quite intelligent and will not be rash about it.

**14c.** Kendar, an unfortunate titan, chained and drugged. He can employ cleric spells up to 6th level when conscious. Only *neutralize poison* removes the drug before 12 hours pass. If aware, he would help any party destroy the Hall's inhabitants — though he would not mind seeing evil party members die too. He cares little for the drow once they are driven underground. (AC −1; HD 20; hp 126; THACO 7; Dmg 7–42.)

**Guardroom G:** One fire giant (hp 58) and three wererats (in humanoid form, hp 22, 19, 18) are conversing here. The giant has sword, spear, and two boulders nearby. Each wererat has a *shortsword +1* (black metal, drow-made) and a normal dagger. The room holds a table, chair, stool, bench, and several kegs. On the walls: a bag, cape, shield, **cell keys**, and a shirt. Keg #1: small beer. Keg #2: water. Keg #3: mead. A smaller keg on the table holds excellent wine — and also the drug stupefying the titan in 14c (a mere quart is sufficient).

A loose stone east of the door hides 321 ep, 608 gp, and 212 pp. The fire giant wears a brooch set with 6 gems (50 gp each) around a black pearl (500 gp).

**If the encounter goes badly for the wererats:** They turn into rats and escape down the drain at point X, alerting the drow. If this happens, the drow will never be surprised by the party. The secret door to the east opens into a passage about 1 foot wide and 1.5 feet high — a man in armor cannot fit; even a halfling must worm through it.

## **8. Secret Room**
The wererat lair — pitch dark and deadly.

### **Description**
Pitch dark. Seven wererats are armed as those in Guardroom G. The strongest has poison on his dagger; wererats #3 and #7 have poison on their swords. They will gain surprise (1–3) or complete surprise (4–6) unless the darkness is countered (*light*, *detect invisibility*, etc.). If the encounter goes badly, survivors flee down the stairway to Level 3, area 15, to warn the drow.

Three **very heavy iron chests**, all locked, each with a poisoned needle in the latch (hits on 12+).

**Chest #1:** 3,200 gp; 7 pieces of jewelry (1,000–4,000 gp each); 9 pieces of jewelry (1,000–10,000 gp each).
**Chest #2:** 8,000 gp; a *potion of poison*; a *scroll of protection from lycanthropes*.
**Chest #3:** Fires two poisoned darts upward when opened (THACO 16; save at −1). The chest is empty — but an invisible inscription inside the lid shows where a stone in the stairway can be removed to reveal: a *ring of shooting stars*, a *rod of cancellation*, a *scroll of seven cleric spells* (random), and 6 potions (diminution, healing, and four random). A very well-hidden compartment in the lid of the box holding the potions contains *pipes of the sewers*.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — absolute darkness.
- **Objects:** Three iron chests. The stair-stone cache (revealed by invisible inscription in Chest #3).
- **Doors & Exits:** Hidden entrance from Level 2 main area. Stairway down to Level 3, area 15.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 7 Wererats (hp 24, 21, 19, 18, 16, 15, 14; Dmg 2–9 with drow swords +1; #1, #3, #7 also have poison).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Three iron chests as detailed above.
- **Magical Items:** *Potion of poison* (Chest #2). *Scroll of protection from lycanthropes* (Chest #2). *Ring of shooting stars*, *rod of cancellation*, cleric scroll, 6 potions, *pipes of the sewers* (all from the stair cache).

## **9–11. Temple of the Eye**
The drow's place of worship — and one of the most dangerous rooms in the module.

### **Description**
Illusionary walls screen this area. The space is illuminated by a strange swirling light that seems part of the very air — eddies of luminosity drift here and there, making the scene strange and uncertain. Distances and dimensions are tricky to determine in the shifting light of rusty purple motes and lavender rays. Globs of mauve and violet seem to seep and slide around. The ceiling is out of visual range — at least 50 feet at its lowest, over 65 where it vaults upward.

### **9. Giants' Worship Area**

Each pillar radiates unease and insecurity in a 5-foot radius (simulate by unsettling players). The west wall is a mural showing giants bowing to a cairn of black, offering sacrifices and gifts. The floor is porphyry; the pillars are serpentine; their polished surfaces clash with each other and the swirling light. The scenes on the west wall grow more horrific toward the altar (north) end — showing human and giant sacrifice.

### **10. Servants' and Thralls' Worship Area**

The polished floor of red and black hornblende seems to flow between the obsidian pillars closing off this area. Each pillar radiates **mild fear** in a 2-foot radius — any creature touching a pillar must save versus fear or flee in absolute panic. Anyone **passing between two pillars** takes 2–8 points of electrical damage (double in metal armor).

The east wall shows various creatures crawling toward huge vaguely squid-like beings with 10 hairy tentacles. Elves and men are in the forefront — those near the monsters are being torn apart and eaten. Three of these ghastly things are depicted, mottled in shades and tints of purple and violet.

### **11. Priest's Area**

The north wall of cloudy purple stone bears an amber-like inlay of a huge inverted triangle with a Y enclosed in it. Hanging on chains from the ceiling: a black metal triangle and cylinder.

First tier (black stone shot with violet veins): A great drum of blackened skin and chitinous material on the west third. On the east third: a rack with **nine silver chime cylinders** (hollow; worth 1,000 gp each).

Second tier (dark gray stone with lilac/orange/purple specks): A huge stone **altar block** of dull, porous-looking rusty-black mineral. To either side: large corroded bronze braziers. In triangular formation around the braziers: two sets of three candelabra with three branches each — bronze green with age, each holding a fat black candle burning with leaping lavender and deep purple flame (never grows smaller).

Third tier (dull black stone with plum/lavender whorls): Nothing but the metal triangle.

**The Altar:** If touched by living flesh or struck, it begins to fade in color; in 3 rounds it becomes translucent amethyst with a black amorphous center. Any further touch while transformed paralyzes the creature for 5–20 turns. If the drum is beaten, chimes rung, and triangle struck while the altar is changed, a glowing golden eye swims into view. Roll 1d12 for each creature seeing it:

| 1d12 | Effect |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Death |
| 2 | Insanity (remove curse to cure) |
| 3 | Rage — attacks own party (remove curse to cure) |
| 4 | Fright and 50% strength loss (remove curse to cure) |
| 5 | Age 1–20 years |
| 6–12 | No effect (looked away in time) |

**If all three tentacle rods (from area 12) are present and the braziers are lit:** The altar becomes transparent heliotrope, the black mass grows showing purple veins, and the eye becomes fiery red-orange. A tentacle reaches from the altar, grabs the nearest living creature, and draws it completely into the stone — destroyed utterly. Atop the altar appears the thing most desired by the party — or something enabling them to attain what they most desire (specifically: help in locating and fighting drow who follow Lolth).

**Second summoning on the same day:** Roll 1d12: 1 = seize and devour 1–4 creatures, grant nothing; 2 = strike everyone present blind, grant nothing; 3 = raise each ability score by 1, take no sacrifice; 4–12 = ignore the whole thing.

The large **malachite pillar** on the east first tier is covered with graven signs and sigils. Two specific ones, if touched together, transport the creature to Level 3, area 18. (The DM should devise the 24 glyphs and select which two are the trigger.)

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The strange swirling luminosity — dim, shifting, unnatural.
- **Objects:** Altar, drum, nine chime cylinders (1,000 gp each), malachite pillar.
- **Doors & Exits:** Illusionary walls screen the approach. Exit to area 12 (drow clerics) is through the inner section.
- **Special Interactions:** The altar mechanics, the fear pillars in area 10, the electrical damage between pillars.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Something ancient and alien — neither stone nor organic but both at once.
- **Sounds:** A subsonic resonance audible only as a feeling of unease rather than actual sound.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Nine silver chime cylinders (1,000 gp each) on the rack in area 11.

## **12. Drow Clerics' Area**
The true masterminds — and the most dangerous non-giant room.

### **Description**
The entrance is protected by a **wall of tentacles** through which drow can freely pass. It appears as rough brown-purple stone. The wall can only be harmed by: *dispel magic* (removes 50 hp), *disintegrate* (destroys 100 hp), or *symbol of persuasion* (allows all of the same alignment as caster to pass unharmed). It has 20 tentacles (up to 4 against one opponent) and 2 beaks. If any non-drow touches it, it grasps the victim and sets off hissing and champing to alert those beyond. If forcibly attacked and damaged or hit by any spell/wand, it creates *darkness 20' radius* on the intruders' side and begins biting.

**Wall of Tentacles:** AC −2; HD 10 (hp 200); THACO 10; 20 tentacle attacks (Dmg 1–20 each), 2 beak attacks (Dmg 1–10; poison); SD darkness 20' radius.

The **inner chamber** is lit by sconces with non-self-consuming black candles — dim and eerie. Walls hung with purple cloth; floor thickly carpeted in black. A brocaded hanging of black with orange, gold, lilac, and mauve screens the inner room.

**Antechamber** (two lesser priests' quarters): Two low black couches inlaid with silver. Round table and two armchairs. Low tables with jasper basin and ewer sets (3,000 gp each set). Small stools and enameled coffers. Two chests (personal effects). A huge ebony wardrobe with clerics' vestments. Coffer #1: a cleric scroll (*word of recall*) and three bars of adamantium (worth 3,500 gp each, weighing 100 gp/bar). Coffer #2: 100 of each coin type in black leather pouches plus 100 gems (50 gp each). A copper brazier near the door gives off sweet, cloying, decaying, simultaneously disgusting and euphoric incense smoke.

**Inner Sanctuary** (Eclavdra's room): Candle-lit plus two small black-and-silver braziers. Walls with tapestries of suggestive nature. Large bed, two small tables, three coffers, buffet, cabinet, large wardrobe, two divans with a low chest between them, dressing table and chair, large silver mirror (500 gp), two hampers and two chests — all furnishings ebony or black stone with silver inlay.

The wardrobe holds Eclavdra's vestments (mauve, black, and plum stitched with gold) set with 10 violet garnets, 10 topaz (500 gp each), 10 black opals, 10 oriental amethysts (1,000 gp each). The dressing table holds 10 toiletry items of onyx and silver set with tiny gems (200–800 gp each).

Coffer on the chest between the divans: poison gas trap (fills the whole area) + a scroll of 3 spells (*gate*, *unholy word*, *restoration*).

Coffer near the bed: 6 potions — *fire giant control*, *human control*, *philter of love*, *philter of persuasiveness*, *poison*, *polymorph (self)*.

On the small bedside table: ewer, basin, and goblet of lapis lazuli (5,000 gp each).

A small ebony and amber screen (1,500 gp) in the SE corner sets off a dressing area near the wardrobe — a lowbacked chair holds a gown thrown over it. **This conceals the secret door.**

**Three drow clerics are present. Each wields a tentacle rod (see Appendix B). They speak all giant tongues plus troll and gnoll.**

**Amalica** (House Eilserv, male Level 4 Cleric; hp 24; AC 0): Armed with *chain +1*, *shield +1*, purple *tentacle rod* (3 tentacles; THACO 13; 3 attacks; Dmg 3 each; if all hit, double damage and slow 9 rounds). Spells: cause light wounds, darkness, fear ×2, resist cold / chant, hold person, silence 15' radius, snake charm.

**Orgoloth** (House Eilserv, male Level 4 Cleric; hp 24; AC −1): Armed with *plate +1*, *shield +1*, russet *tentacle rod* (3 tentacles; if all hit, total weakness in one arm for 9 rounds). Secretly in vassalage to Nedylene (Level 3, area 16) — sent to spy on Eclavdra. Spells: cause light wounds ×2, curse, fear, resist cold / chant, hold person ×2, silence 15' radius.

**Eclavdra** (Ruler of House Eilserv, female Level 10/10 Cleric/Fighter; hp 60; AC −8): The primary force behind the entire giant uprising. *Chain +5*, *shield +3*, *mace +4*, violet *tentacle rod* (6 arms; THACO 7; 6 attacks; Dmg 6 each; if 3 hit: −4 to hit for 3 rounds; if all 6 hit: 6 rounds numbness + permanent −1 Dexterity). Wears ring of amber set with amethyst (2,500 gp) to control her rod. Spells: cause light wounds, curse, darkness, fear, resist cold ×2 / hold person ×2, know alignment, silence 15' radius ×2 / blindness, cause disease, dispel magic, prayer / cause serious wounds, cure serious wounds, poison / flame strike, true seeing.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Non-consuming black candles — dim throughout.
- **Objects:** The wall of tentacles. The concealed secret door in the sanctuary SE corner.
- **Doors & Exits:** Entrance from area 11. Secret door in Eclavdra's sanctuary → downward passage to Level 3.
- **Special Interactions:** The wall of tentacles cannot be bypassed without the listed spells or drow lineage.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** The cloying incense from the antechamber brazier — an almost physical presence.
- **Sounds:** Quiet within; DC 18 to hear anything from outside.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 3 Drow Clerics as detailed above. Eclavdra is the priority target — she is the conspiracy.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Eclavdra's vestments (heavily gemmed, substantial gp value as detailed). Jasper sets in antechamber (3,000 gp each). Lapis lazuli set by the bed (5,000 gp each piece). The ebony-amber screen (1,500 gp).
- **Hidden Loot:** Coffer by bed: 6 potions. Coffer on chest between divans: scroll of 3 spells (behind poison gas trap). Adamantium bars in Coffer #1 (3 × 3,500 gp). Coin and gem pouches in Coffer #2.
- **Magical Items:** Eclavdra's *chain +5*, *shield +3*, *mace +4*, violet *tentacle rod*. Amalica's purple *tentacle rod*, *chain +1*, *shield +1*. Orgoloth's russet *tentacle rod*, *plate +1*, *shield +1*. Scroll of *word of recall* (Coffer #1). Scroll of *gate/unholy word/restoration* (divan coffer).

## **13. Guest Chamber**
The Frost Giant Jarl — if he survived G2.

### **Description**
If the Jarl of the Frost Giants survived the party's visit to G2, he is here with his wife and two guards posted by the door. The room is otherwise occupied by six frost giant messengers from the Jarl. The place is only dimly lit by a few torches. Eight cots, a long table, two benches, two chairs, three stools, two buckets, six chests, a large box, and a cabinet. Hides on floors, pelts on cots. Pegs hold clothing and several bags. Each giant has 100–400 gp. The Jarl has whatever loot he managed to salvage and carry from his former stronghold.

### **Features & Terrain**
