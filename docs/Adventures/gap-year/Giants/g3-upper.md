# G3 Upper Level — Hall of the Fire Giant King

The pile of lava, slag, and jutting black rock housing the Hall is a steeply rising hill about 300 feet high. The entrance is through two great obsidian valves — each 29 feet tall, 10 feet wide, and 3 feet thick — requiring fire giant strength, a *chime of opening*, or a *knock* spell to open. On the second and any successive raids there is a 50% chance the gates are ajar, with a guard watching.

The upper two levels are lit by torches, braziers, natural gas jets, and molten lava. Most lights will be extinguished if the place is heavily attacked. Passageways are vaulted, about 30 feet high. Halls and chambers are 40–60 feet high. Floors, walls, and ceilings are black, reddish-black, dark gray, and dull brown rock — hewn in places, heat-fused in others. All doors are iron plate; opening a resisting door is at half normal probability.

**Wandering Monsters** (1 in 12 per turn):
1. 2 fire giant guards
2. 2 fire giantesses with 12 gnoll workers
3. 4 young fire giants with 1–4 young hell hounds (HD 4)
4. 3 giant visitors (1 cloud, 1 frost, 1 stone)

## **1. Entry Passage**
The first thing the party sees — and the first warning system.

### **Description**
The floor is polished obsidian. Great tapestries hang between the torches, done in bloody colors showing victorious fire giants. The passage runs the length to the Grand Hall (area 2). One alcove — **Alcove A** — is screened by a tapestry loosely woven to allow the guard inside to see clearly while remaining hidden.

**Alcove A:** If intruders are spotted, the guard winds a great bronze horn to warn the Hall (area 2). He has a club and four boulders.

**After a severe defeat:** A ballista (from area 10A) will be set at the far end of the hallway on any subsequent raid. A trip wire is strung 30 feet into the passage (1 in 6 chance to spot if using lights; one check for the whole party). The ballista fires 6 spear missiles, THACO 2 vs. AC 10 (no Dex adjustments), each dealing 2–16 damage.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torchlit throughout.
- **Objects:** Tapestries (worthless) between torches. The tapestry at Alcove A is loosely woven (DC 20 Perception from inside to see without the tapestry appearing disturbed).
- **Doors & Exits:** Leads south to the obsidian gate; opens north to the Grand Hall (area 2).
- **Special Interactions:** Horn in Alcove A alerts area 2 immediately.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Volcanic rock, hot iron, faint sulfur.
- **Sounds:** Distant bass rumble of the Hall beyond.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 1 Fire Giant Guard (hp 56) in Alcove A.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** None in the passage proper.
- **Hidden Loot:** Guard's personal belt pouch: 2d6 × 100 gp.

## **2. Grand Hall**
The kill box — the first real fight of the Hall.

### **Description**
The floor is reddish-black, highly polished stone. The great pillars are carved into the shapes of dwarves straining to hold up the figures atop them. Light flickers weirdly from burning gases spurting from the walls. A tapestry on the south wall screens the entrance to the Royal Apartment (areas 4–6). Two ettins guard this chamber at all times using all four heads to watch every direction.

If the gate guard sounds the alarm, the ettins move to hold the entryway mouth until help arrives from areas 3, 18, and/or 21–25.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Bright — burning gas jets in the walls, wall-mounted braziers.
- **Objects:** Carved dwarven pillars throughout. The south tapestry conceals the Royal Apartment corridor.
- **Doors & Exits:** North to area 1. East to area 3 (Throne Room). West to area 14. South tapestry → areas 4–6.
- **Special Interactions:** The ettins' four heads mean they cannot be surprised from any direction.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Burning gas, hot stone.
- **Sounds:** The ettins muttering to themselves in their two-headed way. DC 16 to hear specific words.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Ettins (hp 49, 44); THACO 10 (spear/mace) or 9 (morning star); armed with huge morning stars of black metal (+1 to hit and damage) and spears.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Personal belts and pouches: 400–800 gp combined.

## **3. Throne Room and Audience Chambers**
The heart of the Hall — where Snurre rules.

### **Description**
Two steps of white-veined black marble lead up to this area. The floor is deep red polished stone, the great pillars of polished obsidian. Between the pillars stands a massive throne of jet and black-and-white banded onyx, set with 12 pieces of sard (1,000 gp each), 12 fire opals (1,000 gp each), and 12 rubies (5,000 gp each). The walls show inlaid scenes of Snurre's victories over opponents. Behind the throne the wall shows his device — a flaming skull — flanked by fire giants with clubs.

**Secret Door:** One of the flanking giant inlays is actually a door. Pull the cresset to its left downward and it swings inward.

**King Snurre Iron Belly** sits on the throne. He is 13 feet tall, massively built, bald beneath his iron helmet with bright orange side whiskers and protruding tusk-like teeth. He wears black iron armor and a white dragon-hide cloak (+3 saves vs. cold, half damage from cold). His two-handed sword flames when swung (+4 to hit, +6 damage). He fights as a storm giant with the sword, cloud giant without it. He wears a necklace of coral skulls (15,000 gp) and a girdle set with 66 garnets (100 gp each). His iron crown (when on throne only) holds 6 rubies, 6 diamonds (1,000 gp each), and a huge jacinth (10,000 gp).

Four fire giants in chain shirts guard the King from positions shown on the map. Two large hell hounds crouch beside the throne arms.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Huge torches in bronze cressets; bright throughout.
- **Objects:** Throne (value as gemmed object: 80,000+ gp, but immovable). Secret door in west wall.
- **Doors & Exits:** South to area 2 (down marble steps). Secret door west → leads toward area 7. Corridor east → area 12.
- **Special Interactions:** Snurre will not remain seated if an attack is in progress anywhere in the Hall. He will assess the tactical situation and move to where he is most effective.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Burning torches, hot iron of Snurre's armor.
- **Sounds:** Snurre's voice echoes throughout the room when he speaks; any audience is held in this chamber by force of personality.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Snurre Iron Belly (hp 92; THACO 4/9 with/without sword; Dmg 7d6+6/6-36); 4 Fire Giant Guards (hp 63, 61, 59, 58; THACO 9; Dmg 5-30 or 5-20); 2 Hell Hounds HD 7 (hp 49, 46).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Throne gemstones (sard, fire opals, rubies). Snurre's crown (on throne only). Snurre's necklace and girdle.
- **Magical Items:** Snurre's two-handed sword (flaming, +4 hit, +6 dmg). His white dragon-hide cloak (+3 cold saves, half damage from cold).

## **4. Chamber of the Queen's Serving Maids**
The firewall between the Grand Hall and Queen Frupy.

### **Description**
The hall and chamber are covered with hide rugs and wall hangings. Torches burn on the walls. Six chairs and three small tables line the hallway. The end chamber has four beds, eight chests, two wardrobes, and four stools. Four fire giantesses wait in the hallway at all times; four more are in the end chamber. All fight fiercely to protect the Queen — those in the end chamber rush to aid the hallway group immediately.

Among the 81 hides, pelts, skins, and furs in the end chamber, numbers 17, 24, 40, 59, and 77 are each worth 1,000–4,000 gp.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Wall torches throughout.
- **Objects:** Tapestry screening the entrance to the Queen's chamber (area 5). Wardrobes contain giantess clothing only.
- **Doors & Exits:** Leads from area 2 corridor. Opens to area 5 (Queen's Chamber). End chamber is a dead end.
- **Special Interactions:** Hallway giantesses will fight to the last to delay entry to area 5.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Furs, perfumed oil, female giant musk.
- **Sounds:** Quiet conversation in the hallway; can be heard from outside (DC 18).

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 8 Fire Giantesses — hp 44, 42, 40, 39 (hallway), 45, 40, 38, 35 (end chamber). Fight as frost giants.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Each wears 2–5 pieces of jewelry worth 500–2,000 gp each.
- **Hidden Loot:** Five valuable hides among the 81: numbers 17, 24, 40, 59, 77 (1,000–4,000 gp each). No other treasure.

## **5. Queen Frupy's Chamber**
Cunning concealed in ugliness.

### **Description**
Floors and walls covered with rich rugs and tapestries. Well lit by torches and a large brazier — very hot. **Queen Frupy** is a harridan of extraordinary ugliness and unusual intelligence. Her mass of yellow-orange hair resembles a fright wig; her face is jowls and wrinkles; her body lumpy and bristled. Her little pig eyes are bright with calculation. She wears garments of black dragon hide with iron studs (AC 2). She fights as a normal fire giant male with +2 to hit and +4 to damage, wielding an iron scepter. Hidden out of sight are her two pet giant weasels, Redant and Rogue, which obey her every command.

Any intruder commanded by Frupy to kneel in her "August Presence" who complies will be struck immediately: she gains +4 to hit, +8 to damage, and decapitates on a natural 20. She will then call her serving maids (area 4).

The chamber contains: huge bed with 12 furs (100–600 gp each), small table and two chairs, stool, dressing table with huge silver mirror, ebony and mother-of-pearl wardrobe, one iron chest, two bronze caskets, a chest of 12 drawers, and small copper coffers on each table.

**Bronze Casket #1:** Contains an asp. Holds 4,000 cp.
**Bronze Casket #2:** Contains an asp. Holds 4,000 pp — under a spell making them appear as copper until removed from the chamber.
**Iron Chest:** Trick opening in the *side* — if the lid is opened instead, all within 10 feet are englobed in fire (6–36 damage, no save). Inside: a jade box (5,000 gp) with a hidden compartment (detectable only by X-ray vision or *true seeing*). The compartment holds a scroll of three randomly determined 7th-level spells matching the class of the first such character who examines it.
**Copper Coffer #3:** Six pieces of very fine gem-set gold jewelry (7,000–10,000 gp each) and a jeweled pendant from whose inky depths come flashes of color. *This is a wizard eye device from House Tormtor of the Drow — it allows them to see through it.*
**Chest Drawer #5 (from bottom):** Three potions — fire giant control, delusion, and mammal control.
**Silver Mirror:** Worth 1,000 gp; shows invisible or magically changed creatures in their true form.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Multiple torches and a large brazier — bright and very hot.
- **Objects:** See description above. The dressing table mirror is a significant magic item.
- **Doors & Exits:** From area 4 hallway. No other exit.
- **Special Interactions:** The wizard eye pendant is a drow surveillance device. If the party carries it, drow in the Hall below can see through it.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Perfumed oil and something faintly caustic — she brews her own concoctions.
- **Sounds:** Frupy's imperious voice if she is present; the rustle of the giant weasels in their hiding place.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Queen Frupy (AC 2; hp 74; THACO 8; Dmg 5d6+4); 2 Giant Weasels (Redant hp 24, Rogue hp 19).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Bed furs (12 total, 100–600 gp each). Silver mirror (1,000 gp). Ebony and mother-of-pearl wardrobe (furnishing value only).
- **Hidden Loot:** Bronze casket #2 (4,000 pp under illusion). Copper coffer #3 (jewelry + drow wizard eye pendant).
- **Magical Items:** Scroll in iron chest's jade box (3 × 7th-level spells). Three potions in chest drawer #5. The drow wizard eye pendant.

Off-duty guards — sleeping lightly.

There are 12 boulders in the entry hall, six along either wall.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Sounds:** Snoring from multiple cots. DC 14 to hear it from the corridor.

### **Inhabitants**

## **7. King Snurre's Private Quarters**
The King's personal hell — complete with resident monster.

### **Description**
The great iron doors bear the blazon of the flaming skull. Six hell hounds roam the outer hall (forehall). The walls are set with torched cressets and draped with crude tapestries. Six chairs and two benches line the walls; three tables each hold a keg of ale, beer, or mead with leather or horn drinking vessels. Eight normal shields and eight normal swords decorate bare patches on the walls. A step up at the reddish stone pillar marks the entry to the bedchamber proper.

**The Bedchamber** contains: a huge bed, table, small ebony throne and three lesser chairs, tall cabinet, four trunks, iron chest at the foot of the bed, and a bench. Pelts cover the floor; trophies and tapestries hang on the walls (all worthless). A ledge 9 feet up on the south wall holds 39 skulls, eight helmets, and five sets of armor (2 human, 1 dwarven, 2 elven) of no particular worth. The eight fur pelts on the bed are worth 1,000–4,000 gp each. All containers hold only Snurre's personal gear. On the table: bits of carved ivory, animal teeth, bits of wood, stones — including one carnelian worth 1,000 gp. Snurre uses these as a (worthless) divination device.

**Secret Door (north wall):** Extremely well concealed. Located only 1 in 10 by magical means; not findable by elven searching or normal means. A projecting stone 12 feet above the floor to the left activates the 4-foot-thick portal (it pivots, forming a 4-foot-wide, 10-foot-high opening on either side). Leads toward area 9.

**Secret Door (to area 8):** Only 1 foot thick; magical means locate it 1 in 4. Leads to the pyrohydra's cave.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Flaming gas jets and a huge iron brazier full of glowing coals — eerie orange light.
- **Objects:** Six hell hounds in the forehall (loose). The two secret doors.
- **Doors & Exits:** Great iron doors from area 6 corridor. Secret door north → area 9. Secret door → area 8.
- **Special Interactions:** The hell hounds in the forehall will attack anything that enters that is not Snurre, a fire giant, or specifically ordered to be admitted.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hell hound sulfur, burning coals, hot iron.
- **Sounds:** The hounds pacing. DC 20 to hear the pyrohydra from area 8 through the concealed door.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 6 Hell Hounds (HD 6, 5, 4 — 2 of each; hp 33, 30, 25, 22, 18, 17; THACO 13/15/15) in the forehall.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Eight fur pelts on the bed (1,000–4,000 gp each).
- **Hidden Loot:** Carnelian on the divination table (1,000 gp). The trunks and cabinet contain only Snurre's personal clothing.

## **8. Cave**
The King's pet — and the King's punishment device.

### **Description**
A natural cave accessible through the secret door from area 7. A 10-headed pyrohydra lurks here. It is vicious because Snurre beats and torments it for fun and it takes revenge on virtually any other living thing. It lurks to the north (1–3 on d6) or south (4–6) if it hears creatures approaching up the steps. It attacks by surprise on a 3 in 6.

It attacks by biting with as many heads as possible. It will breathe fire for 10 points per head; 1–10 heads will breathe in a single round, though any one head may only breathe twice per day.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None — absolute darkness.
- **Surfaces:** Natural cave floor, rough stone.
- **Objects:** Nothing — the pyrohydra has cleared the cave of anything breakable.
- **Doors & Exits:** Secret door to area 7. Passage south to area 9.
- **Special Interactions:** The pyrohydra cannot be reasoned with. It attacks all non-fire-giants on sight.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Sulfur, char, and the acrid stench of its breath.
- **Sounds:** A low bubbling hiss when it breathes. DC 18 Perception to hear it before entering if it isn't moving.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 10-headed Pyrohydra (HD 10; hp 8 per head; THACO 10; Dmg 1–10 each head).

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** None — the pyrohydra has no interest in treasure.

## **9. King Snurre's Treasure Cave**
More wealth than most kingdoms — poorly organized.

### **Description**
A chimney runs up the southeastern wall. The cave is filled with a pile of 28,000 cp, three mounds of 4–16 worthless tapestries and furs, several dozen bales of valueless cloth, scores of vessels and containers (pewter, brass, bronze), plus 12 silver and 6 gold urns worth 200–1,200 gp each. There are also **8 iron trunks**, **6 chests**, and **5 large coffers**. Locks are large, rusty, and impossible to open with thieves' tools — require *Bend Bars* or *knock* spells.

**Trunk #1:** Locked, no trap. 72,000 sp.
**Trunk #2:** Locked, no trap. Empty.
**Trunk #3:** Unlocked, no trap. 60,000 cp; under which, a sack with 11 pieces of jewelry (1,000–10,000 gp each).
**Trunk #4:** Unlocked, trapped — blade in lid chops down (THACO 10, Dmg 3–24; if it misses, ruins 2/3 of the treasure). 3 cubic feet of silk (3,000 gp per cubic foot).
**Trunk #5:** Locked, no trap. Eight pieces of rare wood inlaid with mother of pearl (1,000 gp each; each the size of a footman's mace).
**Trunk #6:** Unlocked, no trap. 13,000 ep.
**Trunk #7:** Locked, no trap. 12 fine ivory tusks (2,000 gp weight, 1,500 gp value each).
**Trunk #8:** Unlocked, trapped — 9 large *invisible* asps (hp 6 each; save vs. poison at +2). Six pieces of jewelry (*invisible* until touched, 1,000–4,000 gp each).

**Chest #1:** Unlocked, trapped — gas trap of *weakness*, 10-foot radius cloud. Empty.
**Chest #2:** Locked, no trap. Rocks only.
**Chest #3:** Unlocked, trapped — squirts acid 12 feet forward, 3 feet wide when opened (Dmg 3–12; save or equipment also damaged). Contains 12 potions and 8 scrolls (randomly determined; no poison, delusion, or cursed items).
**Chest #4:** Locked, trapped — spike trap (4 forward, 4 backward, all 4 sides; 15-foot range; THACO 10; Dmg 2–12 each). 17,300 gp.
**Chest #5:** Locked, no trap. Empty.
**Chest #6:** Unlocked, trapped — contact poison on lock and handles (save at −6 or die). One black cloak and one pair of black boots (man-sized): together give a 75% chance to be invisible/move silently in dungeons.

**Coffer #1:** Locked, trapped — poisoned needles shoot inward from sides when treasure is lifted (25% chance of hitting any body part put inside). Contains 72 gold rings (20 gp each): **#4** is a *ring of protection +3*; **#8** is a *ring of contrariness*; **#21** is a *ring of commanding water elementals*; **#26** is a *ring of delusion* with contact poison inside (die, no save); **#56** is a *ring of three wishes*.
**Coffer #2:** Locked, no trap. 1,000 cp covering 1,325 pp.
**Coffer #3:** Locked, no trap. Packets of hot spices (9,000 gp for the lot).
**Coffer #4:** Unlocked, no trap. 7 highly crafted strange idols of unknown mineral and workmanship. Each brings a cumulative −1 curse to its possessor (affects all hits, damage, saves). All radiate magic and a dim evil force.
**Coffer #5:** Locked, no trap. 500 gems (1 gp each), then 266 average gems (10 gp each), then 39 fine gems (1,000 gp each).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Objects:** As detailed above. The chimney is not a viable exit.
- **Doors & Exits:** Passage from area 8. Secret door north → area 7.
- **Special Interactions:** The invisible asps in Trunk #8 cannot be seen even with *detect magic* (they're natural). They strike as soon as the lid opens.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Copper and silver coin, dust, old cloth.
- **Sounds:** Complete silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** 28,000 cp pile. Silver and gold urns. Mounds of tapestries/furs (mostly worthless).
- **Hidden Loot:** Everything in the trunks, chests, and coffers as detailed above.
- **Magical Items:** Ring collection in Coffer #1 (multiple magical rings). Scroll collection in Chest #3. Black cloak and boots in Chest #6. Coffer #4 idols (cursed magic). Ring of three wishes (#56).

## **12. Advisor's Chamber**
The most dangerous room in the Hall for reasons of intellect.

### **Description**
Eight gnoll servants/bodyguards lounge in the outer chamber. They obey only the Advisor, the King, or the Queen. **Obmi**, the Advisor, is in his study (Room A). The outer chamber has a mixture of small, medium, and large furniture: a small table, plush chair with footstool, and couch (small scale); a long table, two benches, two chairs, and 12 man-sized chests; four huge giant-scaled chairs, table, and footstool. Rugs on the floor, tapestries on the walls, bronze cressets with flaming torches.

The chests hold the bedrolls, personal gear, and treasure of each gnoll (200–800 gp each). Chests #3, #8, #10, and #12 contain bottles of wine and spirits and are locked.

**Room A — Obmi's Study:** Door appears locked from outside. Obmi is at his plain wooden desk working on several scrolls. Against the north wall: a case with maps and scrolls (all in fire giant or dwarvish). A plain wooden box serves as a footstool — it appears to hold old clothes, but in a false bottom are hidden 92 gems: 2 diamonds (5,000 gp each), 8 opals (1,000 gp), 13 aquamarines and 10 topaz (500 gp each), 12 amethysts, 13 spinels, 16 tourmalines (100 gp each), 2 bloodstones, 3 citrines, 8 onyx, 5 zircons (50 gp each). A rude wooden pallet for occasional rest.

If Obmi hears noise in the outer area, he uses his peephole to watch. If the PCs are winning, he calls out to them claiming to be a prince held captive for 10 years — that the giants tried to torture information from him, that humans in black robes aid the giants' questioning, and that if given his weapons he knows exactly where the King is. If disbelieved, he bolts for Room B.

**Obmi (currently unarmed):** AL CN; Level 9/11 Fighter/Thief; hp 79; AC 9; base THACO 12; S 18/94 I 18 W 18 D 16 Co 17 Ch 16.

**Room B — Obmi's Inner Chamber:** Soft bed, small stand, table and chair, cabinet, two iron chests, arming rack, food and ale stores. His *plate +2* and *shield +2* are on the rack. His *axe +1* and *hammer +3* hang on the wall above. Underneath the armor: his *gauntlets of ogre power* (+3 to hit, +6 damage). His *ring of invisibility* is on the stand by his bed.

Obmi arms in 5 rounds (armor 2, gauntlets 1, axe 1, shield and hammer 1). He will attack the party at the first opportune moment when he believes help can arrive. He knows about the drow and will bargain information to save his life. Faced with no quarter, he fights to the end.

**Chest #1:** 8,493 gp. **Chest #2:** 904 pp, a silver ewer and bowl each set with 9 red garnets (100 gp each) and 3 violet garnets (500 gp each) — the set worth 6,000 gp intact.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches and bronze cressets throughout.
- **Objects:** Multiple scales of furniture. The wooden footstool/box in Room A. Arming rack in Room B.
- **Doors & Exits:** From area 3 corridor. Room A off outer chamber. Room B off Room A.
- **Special Interactions:** Obmi's deception attempt. He is extremely persuasive and genuinely intelligent — he will say exactly what the party wants to hear. He carries a dagger hidden in his boot even when "unarmed."

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Parchment and ink from Obmi's work. Gnoll musk from the outer room.
- **Sounds:** Scratching of a quill from Room A (DC 16). Gnolls murmuring.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures (initially):** Obmi (see above) — will fight if cornered or when equipped.
- **Hostile Threats:** 8 Gnolls (hp 15, 14, 13, 12, 12, 11, 11, 10).

## **13. Council Room**
Empty — but the cabinet holds the war.

### **Description**
The King and his council meet here, but the place is now empty. Long table, five great chairs around it. A stepped platform against one wall with a small chair beside it. Hides on parts of the polished black floor; rude tapestries on walls. Several torches — none lit.

**Alcove A:** A table with a flat chest on it. If the chest is lifted or moved, six poisoned arrows fire from the north wall (fire at the same probability as fire giants; Dmg 2–8 each; arrows shatter if they miss). The chest is locked and full of stones. A map of the area hangs on the wall. Two large chests against north and south walls: north holds 8,438 sp; south holds 4,188 gp and 6 bone batons with fire giant runes — each is a pass reading *"Official Business On Behalf Of KING SNURRE THE FEARSOME."* Both large chests are locked.

**Alcove B:** Four extra chairs stored here. A huge cabinet holds 83 scrolls and 367 pieces of paper or parchment. Most are worthless. The **68th scroll tube** contains instructions to Snurre: gather forces of hill, stone, frost, and fire giants plus ogres, ogre magi, cloud giants, and others for an all-out attack on provinces to the east and northeast. *"Powerful help from drow"* is promised. Signed **"Eclavdra."** The papers are message copies to and from various giants and others. One note in dwarvish reads: *"The fire giant of stone left and left elbow."*

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches present but unlit — complete darkness unless the party brings light.
- **Objects:** The flat chest trap in Alcove A. The crucial Eclavdra letter in Alcove B.
- **Doors & Exits:** From area 2 corridor. Alcoves A and B are off the main room.
- **Special Interactions:** The trap in Alcove A fires if the chest on the table is moved even slightly.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Dust and old parchment.
- **Sounds:** Silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Map on wall in Alcove A (tactical intelligence). Six bone batons (official passes).
- **Hidden Loot:** 8,438 sp (north chest), 4,188 gp (south chest). Eclavdra's letter (Alcove B) — the clearest evidence of the drow conspiracy, explicitly naming her and detailing the plan.
