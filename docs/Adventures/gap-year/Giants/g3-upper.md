# G3 Upper Level — Hall of the Fire Giant King

The Hall of King Snurre Iron Belly is carved into and beneath the volcanic heart of the Broken Blade Mountains. The stone is black basalt and red granite, hot to the touch throughout. Fires burn everywhere — in great iron braziers, open hearths, channels of running lava visible through iron grates in the floor, and the forge that never cools. Temperatures are punishing for non-fire-adapted creatures: treat the entire complex as a severe heat environment (DC 15 Fortitude save every hour or suffer 1d6 fire damage and become fatigued; magical protection from fire negates this).

All doors are iron — some rusted shut (DC 24 Athletics), most unlocked. Ceilings run 40 feet in the main halls, 25 in the side chambers. The floors are fitted black basalt, worn smooth by decades of giant feet.

**King Snurre Iron Belly** is a legendary fire giant — massive even for his kind, scarred from a hundred battles, wearing blackened iron plate and wielding a custom blade. He is short-tempered, brutal, and paranoid. He has been in contact with the drow for three years and knows enough to be dangerous.

**Queen Frupy** is equally dangerous but in a different way — she is manipulative, highly intelligent for a giant, and has her own correspondence with the drow independent of her husband. She knows more about the conspiracy than Snurre does.

## **1. Main Gate**
Four fire giants hold the pass.

### **Description**
The entrance to the Hall is a natural pass between two volcanic spires, sealed by a massive iron portcullis and flanked by carved basalt towers. Four fire giants stand watch here, armed with iron-shod clubs and wearing plate. A lever mechanism to raise and lower the portcullis is inside the right tower, accessible only to those already inside. A fire pit the size of a wagon burns between the towers, providing light and warmth that is somewhat redundant in this environment.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Bright — the fire pit and numerous torches.
- **Objects:** The portcullis is iron, 20 feet tall, and requires DC 32 Athletics to lift manually or the interior lever to operate.
- **Special Interactions:** A signal horn is mounted in the left tower. If sounded, all rooms within the Hall are alerted. The guards will sound it at the first sign of hostile intent.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hot iron, sulfur, coal smoke.
- **Sounds:** The roar of the fire pit; distant hammering from the forge (Room 10).

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Fire Giants

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In the guard towers: 400 gp combined personal wealth, 12 javelins (giant-sized, useless to PCs).

## **2. Outer Guard Room**
The kill box inside the gate.

### **Description**
The first room inside the portcullis is designed to trap anyone who makes it past the gate. The ceiling is low (only 25 feet) and the walls are fitted with arrow-slits leading to a murder gallery above. Six fire giants are stationed here with spears positioned to attack through the slits. In the room itself, three more fire giants are on immediate reaction duty. Twelve hell hounds are penned in iron cages along the south wall, released on command.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Bright from wall-mounted braziers.
- **Surfaces:** The floor has a drain grate — below it, a channel of lava (DC 18 to notice; if the drain grate is opened, a column of heat vents upward dealing 3d6 fire damage to everything within 10 feet).
- **Special Interactions:** The murder gallery can be accessed from Room 3.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hell hound breath (sulfur and brimstone), scorched metal.
- **Sounds:** The hell hounds pacing and growling; the creak of their iron cages.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 6 Fire Giants (in gallery), 3 Fire Giants (floor level), 12 Hell Hounds (caged; released as action)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In the guard lockers on the east wall: 600 gp combined personal wealth.

## **3. Hell Hound Kennels**
The Jarl's secondary alarm system.

### **Description**
The main kennel area adjacent to the guard room. When not deployed or penned in Room 2, the hell hounds live here in connected runs of iron-bar enclosures. The kennel master is a scarred fire giant named Krug who carries the only key to the main kennel and speaks to the hounds in a dialect of Ignan that commands their obedience. Without Krug's presence, released hell hounds are indiscriminate.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Dim — the hounds prefer it.
- **Objects:** Krug's private chest (DC 22 lock) is hidden under a layer of filthy straw in the kennel master's alcove.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Sulfur, burned meat, and hot iron.
- **Sounds:** Constant growling and the clank of chains; Krug's commands in Ignan.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** Krug (Fire Giant, kennel master; non-hostile if PCs enter without aggression, but will not allow passage)
- **Hostile Threats:** 4 additional Hell Hounds (off-duty, loose in the kennel runs)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Krug's chest: 1,800 gp, a stolen sapphire (600 gp), and a crude drawing — a map of a secret back exit from the Hall (leads to Room 6 of the Lower Level).

## **4. The Great Hall**
Snurre's throne room — and the most dangerous room in the Hall.

### **Description**
A hall 300 feet long and 100 feet wide, its ceiling supported by black basalt columns carved with fire giant battle histories. Three great fire pits run the length of the room. The walls are hung with the trophies of Snurre's conquests: dragon skulls, the banners of fallen human kingdoms, the chains of enslaved nobles. At the far (north) end, on a dais of red granite, sits **King Snurre Iron Belly** on a throne of welded iron swords.

Present in the hall during a normal session:
- **King Snurre** — fire giant king, hp 80, wearing blackened *+2 fire giant full plate*, wielding his personal greatsword
- **Queen Frupy** — fire giantess, hp 60, wearing black chainmail, seated to Snurre's left, watching everything
- **4 Stone Giant advisors** (seated at a side table; neutral, prefer not to fight if avoidable)
- **1 Efreeti** (bound and standing behind the throne, forced to serve as a bodyguard; it despises Snurre)
- **8 Fire Giant nobles** at the long tables
- **4 Ogre servants** clearing food

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Blazing bright — the three fire pits, dozens of braziers, and channels of lava beneath grates in the floor all contribute.
- **Objects:** The throne itself is an iron construction incorporating 200 swords taken from fallen heroes (an artistic statement and a 4,000 gp object d'art). Behind the throne: a great iron door leading to Snurre's personal chambers.
- **Special Interactions:** The bound efreeti will not assist the party unprompted. However, if someone speaks to it in Ignan and offers it freedom from Snurre's binding circle (inscribed on the floor beneath the throne dais), it will provide one round of assistance and then depart. The binding circle can be disrupted with *dispel magic* (DC 28) or by physically defacing it (takes 2 rounds, provokes attack of opportunity from Snurre).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Roasting meat, coal, and the iron-and-sulfur smell that permeates the whole Hall.
- **Sounds:** The King's voice carries — he is currently lecturing one of his nobles on a recent failed raid. The stone giants speak quietly among themselves.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** King Snurre Iron Belly, Queen Frupy, 8 Fire Giant Nobles
- **Passive Creatures:** 4 Stone Giant Advisors (will fight if the Hall is attacked, but prefer to avoid conflict), 4 Ogre Servants (flee immediately)
- **Unique NPCs:** The Bound Efreeti (see Special Interactions above)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Snurre's crown — a circlet of black iron set with 10 rubies (total 8,000 gp). Frupy wears a necklace of black iron and fire opals (5,000 gp). The stone giants carry no treasure but wear worked-stone jewelry (total 2,000 gp).

## **5. King's Chambers**
The private domain of Snurre Iron Belly.

### **Description**
Snurre's personal chambers are a study in contradictions — the room is enormous and filled with expensive loot, but all of it is jumbled together without care. Dragon skulls sit next to fine elvish tapestries (badly singed). A massive bed of iron rails and stone platforms is mounded with furs. Three iron chests are padlocked and chained to the wall. The room reeks of hot iron and blood.

A weapons rack holds Snurre's secondary armory — backup weapons he keeps here in case of treachery.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** A lava channel visible through a grate in the floor provides constant orange light.
- **Objects:** Three iron chests (each DC 26 Thievery). The weapons rack holds two additional giant-sized swords and a set of javelins.
- **Special Interactions:** One of the iron chests has a poison needle trap (DC 24 Perception to spot; DC 22 Thievery to disarm; failure triggers 4d6 poison damage).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hot iron, old blood, and the faint sulfur that pervades everything here.
- **Sounds:** None. The room is heavily insulated.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** A dragon skull (decorative; the horn alone is worth 1,200 gp). Two elvish tapestries (badly singed; worth 800 gp each if repaired).
- **Hidden Loot:** The three iron chests contain: **Chest 1** — 20,000 gp in mixed coin. **Chest 2** — 100 gems (mix of values totaling 50,000 gp). **Chest 3** — 5 art objects and pieces of exceptional jewelry (individual values 2,000–8,000 gp each, total 28,000 gp).
- **Magical Items:** On the weapons rack: *+3 fire giant plate armor* (sized for Snurre; a smaller smith could potentially resize it over weeks of work). A *belt of giant strength (fire giant tier)* hanging on a peg. A *ring of fire resistance* on a stand.

## **6. Queen Frupy's Chambers**
Cunning dressed as vanity.

### **Description**
In sharp contrast to the King's chambers, Frupy's rooms are organized, comfortable, and somewhat elegant for a fire giant's quarters. Woven hangings of black and crimson cover the walls. A writing desk of polished obsidian holds correspondence — including several items sealed with the spider motif of a drow house. Her jewelry is displayed openly on a carved iron stand. She is intelligent and dangerous; her rooms reflect it.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Glowstone lanterns in iron brackets — warm orange light.
- **Objects:** The obsidian writing desk holds correspondence. Several letters are in Giant, but three are in an unrecognized script (Drow dialect). A DC 18 Arcana or Linguistics check confirms the script is Elvish dialect; a DC 26 to read it without a translation. The letters detail the drow's instructions to Frupy specifically — she has been in private communication with the drow matrons, separate from Snurre's chain of command. This is extremely significant.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Perfumed oil and something faintly chemical (she brews her own poisons).
- **Sounds:** None while empty.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Her jewelry stand: 6,000 gp in gems, rings, and bracelets (giant-sized but gems removable).
- **Hidden Loot:** Behind a removable section of the wall hanging (DC 20 Perception): a locked iron coffer (DC 26) containing 3,000 pp and a *necklace of fireballs* (6 beads).
- **Magical Items:** On her vanity table: a *potion of invisibility* and a *scroll of charm monster* — clearly intended for use against her husband if needed.

### **Additional Notes**
The drow correspondence is the hardest evidence yet of the wider conspiracy. The letters reveal that Frupy was recruited separately from Snurre, that the drow plan to dispose of both giants once their purpose is served, and that there is a "city below" to which she has been invited after the conquest of the surface. This should drive the party urgently toward the D series.

## **7. Stone Giant Advisors' Quarters**
Not enemies — unless made to be.

### **Description**
Four stone giants — **Brog, Makantha, Velk**, and **Thurir** — serve as Snurre's senior military advisors. They are here under a contract of honor and would leave if they could. They know the Hall's full layout, the existence of the lower level, and that the drow come and go through the lower tunnels. They are not loyal to Snurre — they are loyal to their contract and to stone giant honor.

If the party approaches peacefully and does not attack, Brog (the eldest) will parley. He will not give up tactical information freely, but if the drow conspiracy is revealed to him, he becomes increasingly uncomfortable. He will not betray Snurre actively, but he will "fail to notice" the party moving through the hall if given a compelling argument.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Stone giant preference — dim.
- **Objects:** Stone tablets of their own — personal records in stone giant script, not relevant to the adventure.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Stone dust and the mineral smell of stone giants.
- **Sounds:** Quiet, rumbling conversation in Terran.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 4 Stone Giants (hostile only if the Hall is attacked or they are directly threatened)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Combined personal wealth: 4,000 gp, 4 gems (200 gp each). Each stone giant wears a worked stone ring (250 gp each, purely aesthetic).

## **8. Fire Giant Barracks**
The bulk of Snurre's military might.

### **Description**
A massive sleeping hall with 20 iron-framed beds, a central fire pit, and racks of weapons along every wall. Sixteen fire giants are here, rotating off their guard duties. Some sleep, some play at cards using plates of beaten iron, some sharpen weapons. The room is loud and smells of smoke and sweat.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Bright from the central fire pit.
- **Objects:** 16 weapons racks, each holding the personal arms and armor of the resident giant.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Giant sweat and hot iron.
- **Sounds:** Snoring, argument, the rasp of whetstones.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 16 Fire Giants

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Personal wealth distributed among them: approximately 4,800 gp total.

## **9. Armory**
The Hall's weapon reserve — and some useful trophies.

### **Description**
Racks of giant-sized weapons fill this chamber floor to ceiling — iron clubs, spears 20 feet long, shields the size of wagon beds, and enough swords to equip a small army. None of it is useful to human-sized adventurers. However, along the back wall, hung as trophies from defeated enemies, are a number of human-scale weapons and shields.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two braziers; dim in the corners.
- **Objects:** The trophy weapons are hung between larger pieces. DC 18 Perception to notice any specific item among the chaos.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** 30 helmets, 22 shields, 18 spears (all giant-sized).
- **Magical Items:** Among the trophies: a *+2 longsword* in a plain scabbard hung between two giant-sized axes, a *+1 shield* decorated with the arms of a fallen noble house, and a *mace +2* on a bracket. All human-sized. A DC 22 Perception check is required to find any given item before the party is interrupted.

## **10. The Forge**
Three fire giant smiths, never idle.

### **Description**
The forge never stops. Three fire giant smiths work in rotating shifts at three separate anvils, producing the iron weapons and armor the Hall requires. The forge itself is enormous — a furnace the size of a house, fueled by a lava channel beneath it that can be opened by a valve, making the forge effectively inexhaustible. The noise is deafening.

The lead smith, **Ironscale**, is highly skilled — he has produced work that even the drow have praised. He is apolitical and cares only about his craft.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Blazing from the forge fires.
- **Surfaces:** Flagstone blackened with decades of coal dust and metal scale.
- **Special Interactions:** The forge's lava valve, if opened, floods the forge area with a surge of lava — treating the entire room as an extreme heat environment (4d6 fire per round, DC 22 Reflex for half). The valve takes a DC 22 Athletics check to operate.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Coal, hot metal, and the acrid burn of metal scale.
- **Sounds:** Deafening hammering. Normal conversation is impossible. Shout-range is needed for 10 feet.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 3 Fire Giants (smiths; will fight if attacked but are not soldiers — they are untrained for combat, meaning they attack and defend as Level 12 instead of Level 14)
- **Unique NPCs:** Ironscale (Lead Smith; will parley if not threatened; knows the layout of the lower level and has heard the drow visitors)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Raw iron and steel ingots ready for work (2,000 gp if transported — very heavy). Ironscale wears a gold armband (600 gp, his only personal wealth).
- **Magical Items:** On the cooling rack — a half-finished *+2 fire giant battleaxe* (blade is complete, handle not yet fitted; a competent smith could finish it in a week).

## **11. Kitchen**
Orc labor, human prisoners, and the back passage.

### **Description**
A large, hot kitchen serving the Great Hall. Four ogres act as head cooks, managing 20 human and dwarf slaves who do the actual work. The kitchen has direct access to the Great Hall via a passage, and a separate delivery entrance on the east wall that leads to the slave quarters. The slaves are aware of the lower level and know that "dark elves come up from below sometimes."

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Bright from multiple cooking fires.
- **Objects:** Large stores of preserved food, barrels of cooking oil (flammable), enormous iron pots and roasting implements.
- **Special Interactions:** If the party can speak quietly to the slaves, they provide information: the lower level entrance is in Room 12; the drow visitors always arrive at night and go directly to the lower level; there are prisoners kept below who have been there for weeks.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Roasting meat, baking bread, and onions — almost pleasant if not for the circumstances.
- **Sounds:** The clatter of enormous pots, the crack of fire, voices giving orders.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 20 human/dwarf slaves (non-combatant; know about the lower level; will flee if fighting breaks out)
- **Hostile Threats:** 4 Ogres (cooks; will fight, but no armor)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** The silver serving implements for the King's table are stored here between uses (1,200 gp as a set).

## **12. Slave Quarters and Lower Level Entrance**
Where the evidence of the conspiracy literally lives.

### **Description**
The slave quarters proper — 30 slaves of various races penned here. Most have been here for months. Among them are three former adventurers (a 4th-level fighter, a 3rd-level rogue, and a 2nd-level cleric) who came here specifically to investigate the giant raids and were captured. They know a great deal.

In the northeast corner, behind a heavy iron door (DC 22 Thievery; the ogre overseer carries the key), stairs descend into the lower level.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Dim — a single torch.
- **Objects:** The iron door to the stairs is thick enough that sounds from below are muffled but not entirely absent — a DC 26 Perception check in the slave quarters can hear faint sounds (footsteps, occasional words in a strange language) from below.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Unwashed bodies and misery.
- **Sounds:** Quiet resignation; the former adventurers will try to quietly get the party's attention.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 30 mixed slaves, including 3 former adventurers (Fighter 4, Rogue 3, Cleric 2). The adventurers know: the lower level layout (partially), that the drow visitors descend there, and that there is a significant prisoner held deep below.
- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Ogre Overseers with the key

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Nothing — all possessions were taken. The adventurers can name where their items were stored (Room 9, the armory).
