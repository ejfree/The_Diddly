# G2 Lower Level — The Jarl's Hall

Beyond the Great Iron Door the temperature drops further. The interior of the Jarl's hall is cut into solid glacial ice over bedrock — walls, floors, and ceilings are blue-green translucent ice, faintly glowing from the massive fires and braziers burning throughout. The place is enormous in scale: ceilings run 40–60 feet in the halls, lower in the side chambers. The floors are covered in straw, hides, and scattered bones.

The Jarl, **Grugnur**, is a frost giant of exceptional age and power — larger than his subjects, with ice-crusted grey hair and a beard braided with iron rings. He is cunning and has been in contact with the fire giants to the south (through the drow intermediary chain). A map found anywhere in the Jarl's hall that points toward the fire giant kingdom in the Broken Blade Mountains is the hook into G3.

**Wandering Encounters (1 in 8 per turn):**
1. 1d4 frost giants heading between rooms
2. 2 winter wolves on patrol with a frost giant handler
3. 1d3 frost giant sub-chiefs returning from or heading to the feast hall
4. A group of 6 orc servants hurrying with food or supplies

## **9. Interior Guard Room**
The first line of defense inside the door.

### **Description**
Immediately inside the Great Iron Door, a guard room cut from the ice with six frost giants on rotating watch. Three are at the door, three are resting on ice-benches covered in furs. A fire pit the size of a wagon burns in the center. The room has murder holes in the north wall that cover the entrance corridor.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The massive fire pit; bright throughout.
- **Objects:** A rack of reserve weapons — ice-encrusted spears 18 feet long and clubs the size of small trees.
- **Special Interactions:** The murder holes are connected to a pressurized water system (frozen melt); in an emergency the guards can flood the entrance corridor with a burst of ice-cold water (DC 18 Fortitude or slowed 1 for 1 minute).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Coal smoke and boiling broth.
- **Sounds:** The crackle of the massive fire; distant feasting noise from deeper in.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 6 Frost Giants

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In the sleeping benches: 800 gp combined personal wealth.

## **10. The Great Feasting Hall**
The heart of the Jarl's power — and his most dangerous room.

### **Description**
A vast hall 200 feet long and 80 feet wide, cut into the glacier's bedrock. Three enormous fire pits run down the center. Long ice tables are laden with whole mammoth haunches, frozen fish, barrels of mead, and crude clay serving ware. The hall is FULL — the Jarl has called a gathering of his sub-chiefs and allies.

At the head table, raised on an ice dais at the north end:
- **Jarl Grugnur** — frost giant king, hp 72, wearing *+2 frost giant chain*, bearing his personal weapon
- **Estia**, his wife — frost giantess, hp 58, wearing a platinum crown worth 4,000 gp
- **A cloud giant visitor** (neutral, here to negotiate; will not fight unless attacked)
- **4 frost giant sub-chiefs** (captains of his war bands)

Around the hall: 16 additional frost giants feasting. 8 orc servants run food and drink.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Blazing bright from three fire pits and dozens of torches.
- **Surfaces:** Flagstone floor under the central fire pits; ice elsewhere.
- **Objects:** The Jarl's throne (ice carved into a throne form, inlaid with silver worth 3,000 gp).
- **Special Interactions:** This room is a catastrophe to fight directly. The cloud giant will not join either side unless its personal safety is threatened.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Roasting meat, mead, and the reek of 20+ giants.
- **Sounds:** Deafening — roaring conversation, laughing, a frost giant beating a drum.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Jarl Grugnur, 16 Frost Giants, 4 Frost Giant Sub-Chiefs, Estia (frost giantess)
- **Unique NPCs:** Cloud Giant visitor (neutral; will not initiate combat)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** The Jarl's crown is a circlet of iron set with 8 sapphires (total 6,000 gp). Estia's platinum crown (4,000 gp). The sub-chiefs wear jewelry totaling 3,000 gp combined.

## **11. The Jarl's Bedchamber**
His most personal space — and his most valuable loot.

### **Description**
The Jarl's private chamber is extravagant by frost giant standards: walls hung with the pelts of white dragons and polar bears, a bed the size of a barn door mounded with furs, an iron war chest secured with three padlocks, and a weapons rack holding his personal collection. Carved ice sculptures of frost giant battle scenes line the walls. The Jarl is in the feasting hall (Room 10) during a gathering, but he returns here to sleep.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** A single brazier, dimly lit.
- **Objects:** The iron war chest has three locks (each DC 24 Thievery; all three must be picked). Destroying it requires DC 30 Athletics or 50 points of bludgeoning damage.
- **Doors & Exits:** A concealed door (DC 20 Perception to spot) behind the largest pelt leads to a short tunnel connecting to Room 12.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold stone, rich furs, the faint smell of metal polish.
- **Sounds:** Near silence — this room is well insulated from the feasting hall.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** A giant otter pelt hanging on the wall (worth 3,000 gp to the right collector). 6 polar bear pelts (200 gp each).
- **Hidden Loot:** The iron war chest contains: 10,000 gp in assorted coin, 8 gems (mix of values, total 12,000 gp), and a rolled map of the region south — showing the route to the Broken Blade Mountains and a fire giant symbol marking a location labeled "Snurre."
- **Magical Items:** Mounted on the weapons rack: **Frostbrand** — a *+3 longsword* (+6 vs. fire creatures) that sheds a 10-foot radius of cold, extinguishes non-magical fires it touches, and grants the wielder immunity to cold damage while held. Also a *javelin of lightning* (3 charges remaining).

## **12. Estia's Chambers**
The Jarl's wife — not to be underestimated.

### **Description**
Estia's chambers are warmer and more lavish than the Jarl's — she has covered every surface with woven tapestries (stolen from human kingdoms to the south), and her dressing table is piled with jewelry and cosmetic items sized for a giantess. A carved ice vanity holds a polished steel mirror (human-sized, looted) and an enormous ivory comb set with moonstones.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Glowstone lanterns in silver brackets — actually quite attractive.
- **Objects:** The dressing table is covered in jewelry. Hidden beneath a false bottom in the table's main drawer: her personal coffer.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Perfumed oil (stolen from a merchant caravan) — almost pleasant.
- **Sounds:** Silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Jewelry on the dressing table: 5,000 gp in gems, rings, and bracelets (all giant-sized, but the gems can be removed). The ivory comb (400 gp).
- **Hidden Loot:** The false-bottom coffer: 3,000 gp in platinum pieces, a *wand of frost* (20 charges).

- **Magical Items:** One sub-chief's chest (the largest, farthest room) contains a *+2 throwing hammer* sized for a giant.

## **15. Kitchen and Servants**
Where the food comes from.

### **Description**
A long, hot kitchen cut into the ice — the constant fires here have melted the walls into smooth, blackened curves. Two frost giant cooks manage a rotating staff of 30 human, dwarf, and orc slaves who do the actual labor. The kitchen is always in motion; food constantly being prepared, cooked, and sent to the feasting hall.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Hot meat, boiling fat, baking bread.
- **Sounds:** The roar of fires, the clatter of enormous cooking implements, the constant low talk of slaves.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 30 mixed slaves (non-combatant; will flee if fighting breaks out; some know about the dungeon below).
- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Frost Giants (cooks; will fight to protect their kitchen)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** Silver serving implements meant for the Jarl's personal table (800 gp as a set).

## **16. Storm Giant's Prison**
An ally waiting for rescue.

### **Description**
A cell cut deep into the ice, sealed behind an iron door with three massive padlocks. Inside, chained to the wall with cold iron chains, is a storm giant named Vaelstrix — ancient, massive, and furious. He was captured six months ago while attempting to warn surface kingdoms about the frost giant mobilization. He has been here since, fed just enough to keep him alive as a trophy.

Vaelstrix is *not* hostile to the party. If freed, he will not fight alongside them (he is exhausted and injured, at one-quarter HP), but he will:
- Provide the full layout of the Jarl's hall
- Name the fire giant king (Snurre Iron Belly) and the location of his hall in the Broken Blade Mountains
- Confirm that the drow are behind the giant coalition, describing a drow priestess who visited the Jarl two months ago
- Promise to send word ahead to surface allies on the party's behalf

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Total darkness.
- **Objects:** Three padlocks (each DC 26 Thievery; Vaelstrix's chains are a separate set, DC 28 or the right key, which the Jarl carries).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold stone, old blood, and something electric — storm giant ozone.
- **Sounds:** A deep, exhausted breathing.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Unique NPCs:** Vaelstrix, Storm Giant (weakened; non-combatant while injured). Speaks Common, Giant, and Elvish fluently.

## **17. Remorhaz Caves**
The deep end of the complex — and the most lethal.

### **Description**
Beyond the main inhabited area, a series of natural lava-warmed caves connects the ice complex to the deep rock below. Two remorhaz have denned here — drawn by the heat emanating from an ancient volcanic fissure at the cave's base. The frost giants are aware of them and leave them entirely alone. The passage through these caves is the only path to the lower tunnels connecting G2 to G3.

The remorhaz are massive — 30 feet long, their back ridges blazing white-hot. They are always hungry.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** The heat ridges of the remorhaz provide a faint orange light.
- **Surfaces:** Natural rock, warm to the touch; wet from meltwater flowing from above. Not difficult terrain, but slick (DC 14 Athletics to run).
- **Special Interactions:** Remorhaz swallow creatures whole. A swallowed creature takes acid damage each round and can attempt to cut their way out.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Sulfur and hot stone — startlingly warm after the rest of the complex.
- **Sounds:** A deep, rhythmic clicking from chitinous legs.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Remorhaz

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** In the remorhaz nest (crushed gear from previous victims): 2 gems (500 gp each), a *+2 spear* (shaft cracked but blade intact), and assorted ruined equipment.

## **18. The Jarl's Treasure Vault**
The accumulated wealth of decades of raiding.

### **Description**
A chamber sealed behind the most complex lock in the complex (DC 30 Thievery; the Jarl carries the only key), this vault holds Grugnur's accumulated hoard — tribute from lesser giants, loot from raided kingdoms, and gifts from his drow advisors. The room is stacked floor-to-ceiling with iron-bound chests, loose coin heaped in drifts, and objects of obvious value hung on hooks or resting on stone shelves.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Glowstone crystals mounted in the ceiling — the only room in the complex with this luxury.
- **Objects:** The vault also contains documentation: a set of stone tablets inscribed in Giant, detailing the coalition of giants being assembled under drow direction. Named: Hill Giant Chief (slain), Frost Giant Jarl (here), Fire Giant King Snurre. The tablets confirm the drow connection and point south to G3.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** 30,000 cp, 50,000 sp, 20,000 gp, 500 pp, loose across the floor and in open chests.
- **Hidden Loot:** 30 gems (mixed values; 8 × 100 gp, 12 × 500 gp, 8 × 1,000 gp, 2 × 5,000 gp), 8 pieces of art objects and jewelry (each 1,000–6,000 gp).
- **Magical Items:** A *+1 shield* mounted on the wall (human-sized, taken from a slain hero), a *potion of storm giant strength*, and a *necklace of fireballs* (6 beads remaining) hanging on a hook alongside other looted necklaces.

### **Additional Notes**
The stone tablets naming Snurre and pointing to the Broken Blade Mountains are the mandatory hook into G3. Without them, PCs may find written confirmation of the same information via the map in Room 11 or testimony from Vaelstrix in Room 16.

## **19. The Grand Entry Cavern**
The formal face the Jarl shows to his visitors.

### **Description**
A grand cavern carved into the living rock at the base of the lower complex, its walls covered in elaborate bas-relief carvings depicting frost giants slaying enemies, hunting great beasts, and standing triumphant over fallen kingdoms. Torches burn at intervals in iron sconces, casting the carved figures in leaping shadow. The space is deliberately impressive — the giants' attempt at architectural intimidation.

A massive boulder in the western passage appears to be a natural part of the cavern wall. It is not. Pressed in the correct sequence of three points (known to all frost giants here), it rolls aside to reveal a hidden door into the complex interior.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches at intervals; dim between them.
- **Objects:** The bas-relief carvings are non-removable but historically interesting. A DC 18 Perception check notices that several figures in the carvings depict a robed, non-giant figure directing the frost giants — a subtle detail the giants' masters apparently had the Jarl include.
- **Special Interactions:** The boulder door (DC 24 Perception to identify as a door; DC 20 Athletics to force open without the combination; the combination is known to all frost giant inhabitants).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Torch smoke and the faint mineral smell of deep stone.
- **Sounds:** The drip of water; the distant sound of activity deeper in.

## **20. The Dragon Vault**
Two ancient white dragons, a hoard measured in tens of thousands, and a ceiling that hides the more dangerous one.

### **Description**
A vast vaulted cavern — the largest single chamber in the lower complex. The floor is heaped with the accumulated plunder of decades: coin drifts, piled weapons and armor, boxes and urns and bottles stacked against the walls. In the center of the floor, coiled on the main treasure mound, sleeps an **ancient male white dragon**. On a ledge 30 feet above the floor, curled in shadow, a **very old female white dragon** watches the entrance.

Neither is the Jarl's pet. The Jarl tolerates their presence because they are effectively unmovable and guard the hoard by existing. The arrangement is mutual contempt masquerading as alliance.

The male surprises on a 3-in-6. The female, from her elevated position in deep shadow, surprises on a 4-in-6.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Pale bioluminescent glow from the dragons' scales; dim throughout.
- **Surfaces:** Coins and loose debris make the floor difficult terrain. The ledge is accessible by a natural ramp behind the female's position — she will use it to gain altitude if combat begins.
- **Special Interactions:** The female has concealed her personal cache in a crack in the ledge wall behind her: 12 gems, 8 opals, and 4 diamonds worth 1,000 gp each.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold, ozone, and charnel — old kills preserved by the perpetual cold.
- **Sounds:** The slow, enormous breathing of sleeping reptiles.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** Ancient Male White Dragon; Very Old Female White Dragon

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Female's Hidden Cache:** 12 gems, 8 opals, 4 diamonds at 1,000 gp each (concealed in ledge crack).
- **Main Hoard (floor):** 72,000 sp / 17,320 ep / 2,966 pp; 8 silver boxes filled with ivory (each weighing the equivalent of 3,000 gp ivory + the box worth 1,500 gp = 4,500 gp per box); 1 alabaster statue worth 4,000 gp; 7 white marble statues (no significant value); 1,900 loose gems at 1 gp base value each; 11 small pewter pieces (minor value); 24 weapons including a *dagger +2*; 9 shields; 8 suits of armor including a *silvered black chainmail +3*; 27 urns (minor value); 61 bottles — 1 poison (undetectable by sight), 1 *potion of fire resistance*, 1 *potion of diminution*, 1 *potion of polymorph self*.

## **22. The Deserted Cavern**
Old death, scattered gold, and a lie embedded in the floor.

### **Description**
A cavern that appears to have been abandoned following some violent event: three frost giant skeletons are visible in the open, their bones scattered as though they died mid-motion. Broken giant furniture — shattered chairs, a toppled table, smashed chests — is strewn across the floor. Approximately 4,000 gp in coin is scattered among the debris, along with a *shield +1* lying near the largest skeleton.

At location B, six ice toads crouch motionless on the floor. They are not aggressive unless attacked.

Skeleton A is partially buried in rubble near the east wall. One skeletal hand clutches an iron tube. Inside the tube is a **false map** of the lower complex — carefully drawn and deliberately misleading: it labels the Grand Entry Cavern as a storage area, labels another chamber as the "Great Hall of the Jarl," and shows the southern passages leading to the barracks, the Jarl's bedchamber, and the treasure vault. Every major designation is wrong. This map was planted by the giants' hidden masters as a ruse for anyone who penetrated this far and survived.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None.
- **Surfaces:** Rubble and debris; difficult terrain.
- **Special Interactions:** A DC 20 Society check or DC 24 Perception while studying the false map reveals inconsistencies with what the party has already observed. The map appears old but the ink has not faded the way it should have.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold stone and old bone.
- **Sounds:** The occasional soft movement of the ice toads.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 6 Ice Toads (non-aggressive unless attacked)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** ~4,000 gp in scattered coin; *shield +1* near the large skeleton.
- **Special Item:** The **false map** (iron tube, Skeleton A) — a deliberate plant intended to misdirect the party. Sharing it as truth with allies could have consequences.

### **Additional Notes**
The false map is a significant intelligence handout in either direction: if the party is deceived by it, their tactical planning will be wrong; if they recognize it as a ruse, it tells them someone sophisticated is managing these giants from behind the scenes.

## **23. The Emissaries' Cavern**
Ogre magi diplomats — courteous, lethal, and carrying a treaty that changes the scale of the war.

### **Description**
Five **ogre magi** have been sent here as formal emissaries from their lord, bearing a treaty scroll signed by **Jarl Grugnur**. The treaty offers 100,000 gp in gems to the Lord of Ogre Magi in exchange for military alliance. The emissaries are dressed formally, well-armed, and watching everything. They are not immediately hostile — they are on a diplomatic mission — but they are dangerous, and if the party appears to be spies or intruders they will attack without hesitation.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two torches; functional.
- **Objects:** Each emissary carries a pouch of 10 gems worth 500 gp each. The largest ogre mage carries a small **iron trick casket** — a puzzle-box holding a *box of holding*.
  - **Casket contents:** 18 platinum plates (100 gp each), 18 electrum plates (10 gp each), 18 silver plates (2 gp each). The plates are the mechanism: slide them in the correct order (left-top-right in platinum-electrum-silver sequence on each end, reverse on top) to open the inner compartment — a *box of holding* (3 cubic feet / 3,000 gp weight limit) holding 2,000 gp, a *potion of frost giant control*, and a *potion of cloud giant strength*.
  - **Trap:** Using the wrong order drives a poisoned pin into the opener's hand (hits any AC on a roll of 12+; −4 penalty to the Fortitude save against the poison).
- **Special Interactions:** The leader wears a *necklace of missiles* with 5 fireballs remaining (7-die / 5-die / 5-die / 3-die / 3-die).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** The faint incense of formal ogre mage ceremony — unexpectedly refined.
- **Sounds:** Quiet, deliberate conversation in a dialect of Jotun.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 5 Ogre Magi

### **Loot & Rewards**

- 5 pouches × 10 gems at 500 gp each = 25,000 gp in gems.
- Iron trick casket containing the box of holding (see above).
- *Necklace of missiles* (5 fireballs: 7/5/5/3/3 dice) on the leader.
- The **treaty scroll** — a significant political document confirming the scope of the giant coalition.

## **24. The Cloud Giant's Chamber**
A new ally for the Jarl — Thordak has just said yes.

### **Description**
A large, well-appointed guest chamber prepared for a visiting cloud giant. **Thordak** — the cloud giant visitor noted in Room 10's feasting hall — has just concluded negotiations with the Jarl and agreed to serve as his chief henchman. He has returned here from the feast to review the terms before they are sealed. He is pleased with himself, well-fed, and therefore in an expansive mood. He is not expecting trouble.

Thordak is large even by cloud giant standards, wearing a silver belt set with gems as a mark of his status.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Surfaces:** The chamber has been fitted with furniture scaled to cloud giant size — oversized chairs, a bed like a barge.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Rich food and the cold-air scent of high altitudes that clings to cloud giants.
- **Sounds:** The scratch of a quill — Thordak is making notes.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** **Thordak**, Cloud Giant (hostile if threatened; will call for aid if the fight goes against him)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Visible Loot:** A chest in the corner containing 11,000 gp.
- **Worn:** Thordak's **silver belt** set with 1 ruby, 2 violet garnets, 4 fire opals, and 12 zircons — worth 3,000 gp total.

## **25. The Servants' Quarters**
Sixteen ogres — the Jarl's domestic labor force.

### **Description**

Each has a sleeping hide pile, a wooden box of personal effects, a peg for their gear, and a small sack. They are not fighters. If combat breaks out, they will run.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Sounds:** Snoring, murmured gossip in Ogre.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Passive Creatures:** 16 Ogres (non-combatant; will flee if fighting starts)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Hidden Loot:** Each ogre's wooden box contains 1–100 each of sp, ep, and gp (roll separately per box; 16 boxes total).

## **26. The Great Hall of the Jarl**
The ceremonial heart of the complex — apparently empty, actually a killing ground.

### **Description**
A huge ceremonial hall carved from the deep ice and bedrock: the formal seat of the Jarl's power. Tables and benches have been pushed to the walls. Dim light comes from caged fire beetles mounted at intervals. The floor is clear. The hall appears empty.

It is not.

Two ledges run along the north wall, each 30 or more feet above the floor. On each ledge, hidden in shadow, frost giant guards crouch behind **ballistae** — mounted crossbow-artillery that fire spear-sized bolts. Each guard can fire once per round and has 4 spear-missiles, 2 boulders, and a club for close range. From their position they command the entire hall floor.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Fire beetles provide dim, greenish light. The ledges are in deep shadow (DC 24 Perception to spot the guards before they fire).
- **Surfaces:** Clear flagstone on the floor; the ledges are only accessible via the passages connecting to the upper complex.
- **Special Interactions:** The ballistae can target anyone on the floor with no cover. Taking cover requires reaching the walls (the pushed tables provide partial cover, DC 14).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold stone and the faint chemical glow of fire beetles.
- **Sounds:** Silence — the guards are disciplined.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Frost Giant Guards (on ledges; each with ballista, 4 spear-missiles, 2 boulders, club; no treasure)

## **27. The Throne Dais**
The Jarl's seat of power — lavish, dangerous, and full of deceptive gems.

### **Description**
An alcove off the Great Hall containing the Jarl's formal throne: a massive construction of ivory and bone, decorated with skulls, silver inlay, and gemstones. Behind the throne hangs a **white dragon hide**. Before it, a huge **polar bear skin** covers the dais floor. The space is furnished with an alabaster table and three ivory stools for supplicants. Currently unlit and empty — the Jarl uses Room 10 for informal gatherings and returns here for formal audiences.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None; the throne room is dark when unoccupied.
- **Objects:** The throne is not portable — 600 pounds of silver in its construction. A DC 20 Perception check while examining the inlaid gems reveals that most of the "diamonds" are quartz. Only a few stones are genuinely valuable.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Old bone, cold silver, and the faint animal smell of the dragon hide.
- **Sounds:** Silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Throne Valuables (requiring examination and extraction):** The throne's silver construction is worth 600 pounds of silver. Set into the throne: 1 small diamond worth 5,000 gp; 40 quartz pieces disguised as diamonds (10 gp each if sold to the uninformed, essentially worthless otherwise); 8 topaz (3 oriental topaz at 1,000 gp each, 5 regular topaz at 500 gp each); 10 amber pieces at 100 gp each; 20 citrines at 50 gp each.

## **28. Guard Area**
Two giants, one gong, and an alarm that reaches every room in the complex.

### **Description**
A narrow guard station covering the intersection of two major passages. Two frost giants stand watch here at all times — one covering the eastern approach, one covering the western. On the north wall hangs a massive iron **gong**; a single strike can be heard throughout the lower complex and brings every inhabitant to full alert within 2 rounds.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Two torches; bright enough that approaching with stealth requires DC 22.
- **Objects:** The gong is mounted 12 feet up the north wall — easily reached by a giant, difficult for smaller creatures to strike quietly.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Torch smoke and iron.
- **Sounds:** The steady drip of meltwater from the ceiling; the giants' breathing.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Frost Giants (clubs and 2 boulders each; no personal treasure)

### **Additional Notes**
If either giant is killed or seriously wounded without being silenced, they will attempt to strike the gong as a free action on their dying breath. A DC 20 Athletics check to physically restrain the giant's arm; otherwise the gong sounds.

## **29. The Weapons Cache**
The Jarl's reserve armory — scaled for giants.

### **Description**
A long, dry cave stacked with weapons and armor maintained for the frost giant garrison. Everything here is frost giant-sized and therefore unwieldy or unusable for smaller folk, with one exception. Racks and floor stacks hold: 62 throwing rocks, 16 shields, 29 spears, 10 clubs, 9 helmets — all standard giant-issue — plus 9 battle axes specifically balanced for hurling, and 1 suit of giant-sized chainmail.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** One torch near the entrance; dim throughout.
- **Surfaces:** Dry stone floor — well-maintained.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Cold iron and tallow from the weapon maintenance grease.
- **Sounds:** Silence.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **No Monetary Loot:** The weapons and armor are giant-sized and have limited resale value.

## **30. The Kennel**
Four polar bears — surprisingly quiet, terrifyingly fast.

### **Description**
A cave fitted as a kennel for the Jarl's polar bears: four bears, two males and two females, kept here as pets and status symbols. The males wear jeweled collars; the females' collars are slightly less ornate. The bears are very quiet — unnervingly so for their size. They can be missed entirely until they are already attacking.

The bears surprise on a 4-in-6 and attack immediately upon detecting an intruder.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** None; the bears need none.
- **Surfaces:** Straw over ice; the bears have worn paths from their pacing.
- **Special Interactions:** The bears' collars are fastened with a standard buckle sized for giant fingers (DC 14 Thievery for a Small or Medium creature; cannot be removed from a living, struggling bear without restraint).

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Bear musk and old fish.
- **Sounds:** Almost nothing. A faint exhalation. Then the impact.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 2 Male Polar Bears, 2 Female Polar Bears (all attack immediately; surprise 4-in-6)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Male collars:** 8 gems each at 100 gp each = 800 gp per collar, 1,600 gp total.
- **Female collars:** 6 gems each at 100 gp each = 600 gp per collar, 1,200 gp total.
- **Total collar value:** 2,800 gp.

## **31. Caverns of the Frost Giant Gentry**
Family quarters for the Jarl's extended household — not soldiers, but dangerous if provoked.

### **Description**
Two large interconnected caverns (Areas 18 and 19) housing the frost giant gentry — the Jarl's extended family, lesser nobles, and their households. These are not the barracks giants; these are frost giants who consider themselves above the common soldiery. Each giant has a cot, chest, chair, stools, and a pile of hides. Torches burn in brackets alongside caged fire beetles. Pegs on the walls hold cloaks, capes, and personal bags. Shared wardrobes and hampers line the walls: 4 huge lockers, 3 hampers, and 6 wardrobes distributed between the two caves.

**Area 18:** 9 male frost giants, 11 female frost giants, 8 young (non-combatant).
**Area 19:** 3 male frost giants, 4 female frost giants, 5 young (non-combatant).

The young are not a combat threat but their screaming will bring every adult in both caves within 1 round.

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Torches and fire beetles; dim but functional throughout.
- **Surfaces:** Straw and hides over ice floors.
- **Special Interactions:** The gentry are not on formal watch duty. They will not attack without cause, but they respond immediately to perceived threat and fight to protect the young.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Domestic giant life — food, furs, the faint smell of giant-sized children.
- **Sounds:** Low conversation; a young giant crying somewhere in Area 19.

### **Inhabitants**

- **Hostile Threats:** 12 Male Frost Giants, 15 Female Frost Giants (27 adults combined)
- **Passive Creatures:** 13 Young Frost Giants (non-combatant; will flee and scream)

### **Loot & Rewards**

- **Male giants:** Each carries 2,000–8,000 gp and wears an **armband** (a pass token granting access to the Jarl's private areas; 27 males total across both caves but not all will have armbands — DM's discretion).
- **Female giants:** Each carries 1,000–4,000 gp.
- **Young:** No treasure.

## **32. The Jarl's Trophy Hall**
Decades of conquest on the walls — and several deadly surprises among the trophies.

### **Description**
A two-part chamber accessed through a short corridor. The western spur is a **private audience hall**: a table, four chairs, shelves with worthless scrolls (administrative notes, supply lists), two non-magical shields, a **heavy crossbow of speed**, two non-magical two-handed swords, and a pair of decayed ivory tusks worth 10 gp for the ivory.

The main hall is the **Trophy Hall proper**: rugs on the floor and south wall, every surface covered in mounted hunting and combat trophies. The collection includes:

- Pelt of a **cave bear** / hide of a **su-monster** / skin of a **wyvern**
- Rack of a **giant stag** / mounted **umber hulk** teeth and claws / mounted **giant scorpion** claws
- Horn of a **woolly rhino** / pair of **mammoth tusks** / pair of **mastodon tusks**
- Quiver with 16 normal arrows
- Head of a **giant boar** / skin of a **griffon**
- Two man-sized shields mounted side by side — the first is non-magical; the **second is a *shield +2***. Removing it releases a spear trap: the spear hits AC 5 or better automatically for 3d18 damage.
- Mounted **dwarven skull** wearing an iron crown (no monetary value)
- Two crossed spears and a flail
- **Tapestry** depicting the Jarl's defeat of a storm giant (worth 1,875 gp; **Vaelstrix** will destroy it if he sees it — it depicts his own capture)
- Case of 25 crossbow bolts — 11 of them are *bolts +2*
- Wings of a **giant eagle** / great axe / human-sized **field plate armor**
- Mounted **walrus tusks** — one is actually an **iron horn of Valhalla**. If touched, a shedu skull mounted nearby screams "ALARM! ROBBERS!" in Frost Giant continuously until smashed (DC 16 Athletics).
- Human-sized **Yeomancy insignia shield** / dwarf-sized **splint mail**
- Light crossbow (non-magical) / **tapestry** of a giant raid on human lands (worthless)
- Huge iron mace (normal) / pelt of a **giant lynx** / jaws of a **subterranean lizard**
- Elf-sized ***plate mail +2***
- Human-sized buckler (non-magical)
- **Tapestry** of the Jarl in battle with a giant eagle (worth 500 gp)

At the eastern end, steps approximately 3 feet high lead through leather hide curtains into the Jarl's private cavern (connects to Room 11, the Jarl's Bedchamber).

### **Features & Terrain**

- **Lighting:** Dim; the same torch sconces as the rest of the lower level.
- **Special Interactions:** The spear trap (second shield) fires automatically when the shield is removed without first locating and disabling the trigger (DC 22 Perception to find; DC 18 Thievery to disable). The shedu skull alarm activates on contact with the walrus tusk and cannot be quieted except by smashing the skull.

### **Smells & Sounds**

- **Smells:** Old leather, dust, cold iron, and the preserved-animal smell of taxidermy work.
- **Sounds:** The creak of dried skins; the faint echo of the feast hall beyond.

### **Loot & Rewards**

- *Shield +2* (second wall shield; spear trap on removal)
- *Bolts +2* × 11 (in the bolt case)
- *Iron horn of Valhalla* (the walrus tusk; triggers alarm on touch)
- *Plate mail +2* (elf-sized)
- *Heavy crossbow of speed* (in the western audience hall)
- **Tapestry** (Jarl defeating storm giant) — 1,875 gp; Vaelstrix will destroy it
- **Tapestry** (Jarl vs. giant eagle) — 500 gp

### **Additional Notes**
The steps at the east end lead through hide curtains to Room 11 (the Jarl's Bedchamber). This is the private back-access used by the Jarl himself — anyone who finds it bypasses the main corridors into his personal quarters.
