## G1 to G2 Connector — The Steading to the Glacial Rift

**From:** The Steading of the Hill Giant Chief (G1 exit — main gates or side exits)
**To:** The Rift Entrance (Room 1, G2 Upper Level)
**Distance:** Approximately 100 miles overland through the Sundering Peaks
**Travel Time:** 10 days at normal pace (10 miles/day); 20 days at half pace (+5 stealth); 5 days at double pace (−5 stealth, greater encounter chance)
**Route Map:** ![Route map: Pit to G1 to G2 to G3](../../../../assets/images/import/path-pit-G2-G2-G3.png)
**Terrain Type:** Highland mountain — temperate-to-alpine transition, high pass, glacial moraine

### Overview

The party does not know where G2 is when they leave G1. That is by design.

Somewhere in G1 — in a prisoner's bargaining, a document in the chief's treasury, a drunken boast overheard in the great hall — they learn the direction: north and west, into the teeth of the Sundering Peaks, where the tallest peaks split into twin horns. The Rift is between them. That is all they get. Distance, exact bearing, and what they will find there are for the journey to reveal.

The Steading sits in a highland valley at the feet of the mountains. Leaving it, the party climbs. The air thins. The trees thin first, then disappear entirely above the snowline. By Day 4 they are above the world — nothing but rock, ice, wind, and the white silence of the high peaks. By Day 6 the temperature is lethal without preparation. By Day 9 the blue-green light of the glacier is visible from the ridgelines.

The journey is approximately 100 miles as the passes run. At normal pace (10 miles per day) with full cold-weather equipment: 10 days. Without proper gear, survival checks begin on Day 1.

---

### The Trail of Evidence

- **Days 1–3 (Highland Approach):** The valley floor gives way to steep highland terrain — scrub pine, broken shale, thin soil over bedrock. Giant boot impressions appear in softer ground along the passes. The party passes two large campsites sized for frost giants — enormous fire pits, scattered with charred bones and smashed pottery. One site has a crude hide map scratched with charcoal: the twin-horned peaks are labeled in a single Jotun word. DC 14 Society (Giant) reads it: *"The Jarl's Road."*

- **Days 4–7 (The High Pass):** The tree line falls away and the world becomes rock, snow, and sky. The giant-road is ancient — worn into the stone by centuries of travel, wide enough for two giants abreast. Iron pitons have been hammered into steeper sections as handholds. The cold is serious now; wind drives sleet horizontally across exposed ridges. A series of crude stone cairns marks the route, each with a Jotun glyph scratched into the topmost rock. The cairns are maintained, not ancient — someone has been through recently. One cairn has a frost giant's axe leaning against it, blade broken, left as a grave marker. A crude skull is carved into the stone beneath it.

- **Days 8–10 (The Glacial Approach):** The glacier: vast, blue-green, groaning. Crevasses open without warning. Exposed skin hurts within minutes. The twin-horned peaks are visible ahead — the Rift is a dark vertical slash between them. The cairn markers continue, now supplemented by rope lines hammered into the ice. Far above, something large moves along the rim of the Rift, silhouetted against the grey sky.

### Survival DCs by Zone

Check frequency: once per day of travel. On a failure, apply the listed consequence. Critical failure escalates one step.

| Zone | Days | Navigation DC | Cold Hazard DC | Detect Ambush DC | Exhaustion Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Highland Approach | 1–3 | 14 | — | 16 | 4 failed days |
| High Pass | 4–7 | 18 | 16 | 18 | 3 failed days |
| Glacial Approach | 8–10 | 20 | 20 | 14 | 2 failed days |

**Cold Hazard DC:** Replaces Forage in cold zones. Any character without cold-weather gear (heavy winter clothing, furs, or magical protection) must succeed at a DC 16/20 Fortitude save or gain sickened 1. Critical failure also adds fatigued. Failed cold hazard checks count toward the Exhaustion Threshold.

**Forage:** Highland Approach DC 18. High Pass DC 24. Glacial Approach DC 28 — assume the party has packed from G1 or this is a forced hardship moment.

**Navigation failure in the Glacial Approach:** The party descends into the wrong crevasse field. DC 22 Athletics to climb out; a wandering monster check occurs immediately.

**Securing camp:** DC 16 Survival to find a windbreak. In the High Pass, an additional DC 18 Athletics to clear enough snow to avoid a snowslide. Failure means each character must succeed at a DC 14 Fortitude save or lose the benefits of their long rest.

### Waypoint Encounters (Roll or Choose)

Use 1–3 over the journey, scaling to party pacing.

**1. The Intel Stop (Days 1–3)**
Six Mror Holds dwarves — a contract survey team mapping giant movements for House Kundarak, watching the alliance form with alarm. They have a detailed map of the high pass route, know the approximate Rift location ("a slash of darkness between the twin horns, can't miss it from the right ridgeline"), and have noted frost giant patrols extending further south than usual. They will share everything in exchange for two things: safe passage out through G1 territory, and a promise to notify House Kundarak of what the party finds in the Rift. They have cold-weather gear to sell — they always bring extra for casualties.

**2. The Cairn Cache (Days 3–5)**
One cairn on the High Pass route has a hollow base — the topstone conceals a rough iron box. Inside: 300 gp in mixed coin and a handwritten note in Jotun (DC 14 Society or Lore: Giant): *"Three hills, north face, below the cracked horn. Watch for the wolves. Bring the response before the next moon."* A dead drop, part of a giant-to-giant communication chain. The note is unsigned. Someone in G2 has been exchanging messages with someone in G1, bypassing the Jarl's official channels. Internal politics or drow coordination — leave it ambiguous.

**3. The Avalanche and the Giant Beneath (Days 4–6)**
A massive avalanche from the past week has buried a section of the pass under 15 feet of compacted snow. Working around it requires DC 18 Survival (otherwise 4 hours lost). DC 16 Perception notices something large beneath the snow — a frost giant, killed by the avalanche, preserved by the cold. Dead 4–5 days. His gear is intact: 400 gp, a crude map of G2's exterior approach routes, and a sealed iron scroll tube. The tube contains an order in frost giant runes signed with the Jarl's personal mark: a list of G1 giants identified as "unreliable" who are to be "corrected" on his next visit. Someone in the alliance has an informant network inside the Steading.

**4. The Dwarven Waypost (Days 3–5)**
A crumbling stone waypost marks a pass junction. The original markers have been chiseled off, but a Mror Holds survey notation in chalk (recent, last six months) points north with a distance in leagues to a dwarven refuge shelter. The shelter: a small stone hut, ancient, with a House Kundarak cache inside — six days of rations, a lantern with 12 hours of oil, a *potion of resist cold* (cold resistance 10 for 1 hour), and a logbook. The logbook records three previous survey teams' observations of giant movement. The last entry is 11 days old: *"Frost giant patrol count: 12, heading south toward the Hill Giant Steading. They were carrying something large, wrapped in black cloth. Neither team wanted to get close enough to see what."*

**5. The Jarl's Hunting Party Returns (Days 7–9)**
Two frost giants, a frost giant sub-chief, and six winter wolves on chains heading back to the Rift after a successful hunt — two dead woolly mammoths slung on a massive pole between them. They are loud and satisfied. The pass is 40 feet wide; hiding requires DC 18 Stealth, but the wolves may smell the party regardless (DC 22 Stealth). If spotted, the sub-chief demands to know who they are in Giant. He assumes they are some allied creature group heading to the Rift with a report or tribute; DC 18 Deception maintains the fiction long enough to pass. He volunteers directions — "the shadow between the two horns, follow the cairns past the third turn" — and mentions, irritably, that the Jarl is "in a foul mood — the dark ones were here again last week and they always leave him angry."

**6. The Ascent at Night (Days 9–10)**
The final approach to the Rift must be timed around patrols. DC 16 Perception to map the patrol schedule. The best window is a 3-hour stretch before dawn — patrols overlap poorly and the cold is worst. During this approach: a single frost giant sentinel on a high ledge above the Rift entrance is barely visible, a hulking shape against the sky, motionless. DC 20 Perception reveals she is asleep at her post, huddled into her furs. She can be bypassed without incident on a DC 18 Stealth check. If she wakes, she sounds an alarm horn; the entire Upper Rift is alerted within one round.

### Arrival: Room 1 — The Rift Entrance

The twin-horned peaks are exactly as described — two massive spires of black and grey granite rising above the glacier, between which the world simply drops away. A vertical slash of darkness, 40 feet wide and hundreds of feet deep, descending into blue-green ice.

**Read aloud (arrival):**

> *You smell it before you see it: coal smoke and something animal — wet fur and old meat — rising on the updraft from below. Then you are at the edge, and the Rift opens beneath you. It is enormous. The walls are ice, translucent and blue-green, and they descend further than your light reaches. Far below, impossibly far, a faint orange glow moves — a torch, or a fire, carried by something very large. The wind here is not wind; it is a roar, a constant scouring that drives ice crystals into exposed skin and tears tears from your eyes. Rope lines — knotted, each knot the size of your head — drop down the near wall into the dark. Someone has been using them recently. The ice around them is fresh-broken.*

The descent begins here. Room 2 (Guard Cave Alpha) is 60 feet below, and the frost giant sentries there make active Perception checks every 10 minutes.

### Notes for the GM

- The party should leave G1 with a direction and a landmark description, nothing more. The journey is a resource drain and a tension ramp toward G2.
- Cold-weather gear matters. If the party pillaged G1 without thinking about it, they pay for it in Fortitude saves. Winter clothing, furs, and fire-making supplies are available from G1's storerooms.
- The cairn cache (Waypoint 2) and the buried giant's sealed letter (Waypoint 3) foreshadow internal giant politics and drow coordination. These seeds can be harvested in G2 or left as background texture.
- The Mror Holds are watching. House Kundarak's interest in the giant alliance is a thread for political connections once the arc is complete.
