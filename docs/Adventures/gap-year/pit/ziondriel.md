## Overview

Ziondriel is a drider exile from Xil, the drow staging city at the end of this adventure. She fled when city agents — investigating a smuggling ring — looked too closely at her heretical devotion to the Traveler. For years she has wandered upward through the Darklands, nursing plans for revenge on Xil while gradually falling in love with exploration instead.

She has made this cavern her temporary home, but she is **trapped**: a seugathi she barely survived occupies the passage to the north (area G11), and the tunnel to the south leads somewhere unknown. She has been here long enough that her food sources have run dry.

She wears her holy symbol of the Traveler openly — a four-faced coin on a chain, each face different, none the same. She is not what the PCs expect a drider to be.

**She is neutral toward the PCs. She is also hungry. These two facts are in tension.**

---

## The Prophecy on the Wall

One entire wall of Ziondriel's silken hut is covered in Drow script. If the PCs enter her space or she lets them close enough to see it, they can read it (Drow language required) or recognize its prophetic form (Occultism or Religion, moderate DC).

This is the **Pit Prophecy** — see [pit-prophecy.md](pit-prophecy.md) for the full text and GM notes.

Ziondriel did not consciously write it. She woke with the carving stylus in her hands three times before she realized what was happening. The script is hers but the compulsion was not. This is deeply disturbing to her: the Traveler's faithful prize cunning, self-determination, and change wrought by their own hand. The Traveler teaches that deception is a gift — something you choose to give. Being made a vessel without consent, used against her will for someone else's god, is a desecration of everything she believes in. It is a violation she hasn't processed yet. She is furious and frightened in equal measure and does not want to discuss it.

**She will lie and say she carved it deliberately if confronted directly.** A successful Sense Motive or Insight check against her Deception reveals the lie. If pressed or if the PCs demonstrate they know what it means, she may crack — but only if she trusts them.

## Encounter Structure

### Phase 1 — First Contact

Ziondriel emerges from her silken hut as soon as she detects the PCs. She squeezes through a low opening in the silk and calls out in Elven from the central ledge. Her voice is sonorous and unhurried.

Her opening attitude is **indifferent** — she is curious, not hostile. But:

- She is condescending. She will refer to the PCs as "leg-addled," "flat-toothed," "cave-stumblers," or similar. This is not malice; it is habit. She genuinely does not think surface-walkers are impressive.
- She is **eyeing the PCs as food.** A PC who succeeds on a Perception check (moderate DC) notices that her gaze keeps moving across them with the focus of something estimating weight and warmth, not just assessing threat.

> *"Leg-addled. Surface-born, by the dust on you. You've come a long way down to stumble into my cavern. Tell me why I shouldn't simply eat you, and we may have a conversation."*

She means this as an opening gambit, not a declaration of intent — but it is not entirely a bluff.

---

### Phase 2 — The Hunger Clock

Ziondriel is managing herself, but her hunger is real and worsening. Treat this as a **pressure mechanic on the social encounter.**

Every time the PCs stall, are unhelpful, or provoke her (failed social rolls, hostile posturing, insulting the Traveler), advance the clock by one step. Every time they give her a concrete reason to cooperate — feed her, offer a deal, demonstrate useful skills, or ask about the prophecy in a way that intrigues her — hold the clock or push it back.

**Hunger Clock (4 steps before she attacks or demands tribute):**

| Step | Her Behavior |
| ---- | ----------- |
| 1 | Controlled. She's managing it. Only sharp eyes notice. |
| 2 | She asks, almost casually, if they brought anything to eat. "Long tunnel, leg-addles. You must have provisions." If they share food (even trail rations), reset to Step 1 and gain a +1 circumstance bonus to further Diplomacy checks with her. |
| 3 | The pretense drops slightly. She stops pretending. "I will be honest with you. I have not eaten in eleven days. I am still more useful to you alive and cooperative than dead at the bottom of this ledge — but that calculus changes if you waste my time." |
| 4 | She gives the party one final chance to offer her something — a deal, food, an agreement. If they do not, she attacks. She is not mindless; she will target the smallest or most isolated PC first. |

**Feeding her resets the clock.** She is a pragmatist. She will accept rations without shame. She will not thank them warmly, but she will visibly relax, and her next responses will be sharper and more cooperative.

---

### Phase 3 — What She Wants

Ziondriel will eventually work the conversation toward her actual goal: she wants the PCs to kill the seugathi in area G11.

She will not lead with this. She will:

1. Ask what they're doing in the caves (if there was fighting nearby, she heard it and is curious)
2. Probe whether they're here to clean out the dungeon ("You're picking fights down here. You have the look of people being paid for it.")
3. Share that she has information about the creatures in this area — and offer to trade it for a service

**What she offers in exchange for killing the seugathi:**

- A map of the passages she knows, including G11's layout
- Her assessment of the other creatures in the dungeon
- Safe passage through her cavern without harassment
- (If the PCs earned real trust) A warning about the prophecy and what little she understands of it

She will not volunteer the prophecy. She considers it a liability and is ashamed of the fugue states. But if the PCs see the wall and ask — she cannot unsay what they have already read.

---

### Phase 4 — The Prophecy Conversation

If the PCs investigate the wall carvings, Ziondriel's reaction depends on where the clock is and how the relationship has developed:

**Hostile or neutral (no trust built):**
> *"That is my work. A meditation on theology. It is not your concern."*
She is lying. She knows she is lying poorly. She crosses two of her arms over the coin at her throat.

**Guarded (some rapport):**
> *"I carved it. I don't — I don't entirely remember carving all of it. It happens sometimes. In the night. I wake and it is there, and my hands ache."*
She says this quietly and does not elaborate unless pushed.

**Trusting (clock held back, she has been fed or helped, they have treated her with some respect):**
> *"I serve the Traveler. I choose my own deceits. I do not pray to the Spider Queen — I have not, since before I fled Xil. And yet — three times, I woke in the dark with this stylus in my hands and new script on the wall. Someone used me. Someone reached into my skull while I slept and wrote their prophecy in my home, in my script, with my hands.*
>
> *I do not know what it says. I can read it, but I cannot interpret it. It speaks of plague and chains and something waking. If you can tell me what it means, I would consider that a debt worth repaying."*

This is the hook. She is not the seer — she was the instrument. She wants to know who did this to her and why.

## Resolution Paths

**Full Alliance:** The PCs feed her, treat her with basic respect, and agree to deal with the seugathi. She gives them everything she knows, warns them about the prophecy, and may even offer to accompany them temporarily (she wants that seugathi dead more than she wants to be left alone). She remains in her cavern when they leave and could be a recurring contact.

**Transactional:** The PCs make a deal — kill the seugathi for the information. She keeps her word. She serves the Traveler; a bargain freely made is a contract, and the Traveler prizes clever deals honored. She does not betray people who honor agreements.

**Combat:** If the PCs attack first, she fights with glee — she is bored and frustrated and a fight is a relief. She surrenders if reduced to fewer than 30 HP, offering her treasures for mercy. If she surrenders, she will still trade information, but she has lost respect for the party and her tone shifts from condescending-curious to coldly contemptuous.

**Provoked Fight (hunger clock hits 4):** She gives one clear warning, then attacks. If the PCs respond immediately with food or a concrete offer, she stops. She is not proud of the situation and would rather not kill them — they are more useful cooperative. But she will not apologize.

## Key Roleplaying Notes

- **She is not a Lolth drider.** Her Traveler symbol — the four-faced coin — is visible. Any PC who recognizes it (Religion check or knowledge of the Dark Six) can use that as an immediate point of common ground or at least curiosity. A Lolth cleric who sees it will be visibly unsettled.
- **She is intelligent and bored.** She will enjoy a PC who matches her wit. Clever insults aimed back at her land better than earnest diplomacy.
- **She hates being pitied.** If a PC expresses sympathy about her exile, her transformation, or her situation in the cave, she becomes colder. She does not want sympathy. She wants useful people.
- **The fugue writing is her deepest wound.** She won't show it easily, but it is there. A PC who treats it with genuine gravity — not as a curiosity or a plot point, but as a violation of her autonomy — will break through her defenses faster than anything else.
- **She refers to the seugathi with more emotional weight than her other enemies.** She was mentally manipulated by it. For a servant of the Traveler — who prizes being the one who chooses when and how to deceive — having her mind touched and her will overridden is unforgivable. She doesn't hide this.

## Stat Block Reference

Use the Drider stat block from the Pathfinder Bestiary. She is a spellcasting drider (not warrior variant), reflecting her devotion to the Traveler. Her spells should lean toward enchantment, illusion, and transmutation rather than Lolth-flavored necromancy — she deceives, misleads, and escapes rather than curses and destroys.

**Key modifications:**

- She has access to web spells (her silken hut demonstrates this)
- Her Wisdom save is lower than average — the fugue states have left her psychically raw and she is more susceptible to mind-affecting effects than a healthy drider would be
- She is **not** vulnerable to holy/sacred damage aligned against Lolth's undead or divine champions — she is an apostate, not a Lolth servant

## GM Notes

**The prophecy she carries is larger than this dungeon.** If the PCs are running the GDQ chain because they are Drake-year students from The Diddly, they may already know that the Pale Choir plague is spreading through their university. The prophecy on Ziondriel's wall, if decoded, tells them that the plague is not an accident — it is Lolth's tool for silencing the elder dragons and breaking Tiamat's seals.

Ziondriel does not know this. She knows she was used. She knows the prophecy speaks of plague and a "House of Wings" and something being freed. She cannot place it.

If the PCs piece it together in front of her, she will be furious — not at them, but at the scope of it. She was made a message-carrier for the unmaking of the world, and no one even asked her.

**That is very on-brand for a servant of the Traveler to eventually demand revenge about — on her own terms, in her own time, in a form no one will see coming.**
